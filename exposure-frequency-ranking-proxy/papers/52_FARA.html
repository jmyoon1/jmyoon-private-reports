FARA: Future-aware Ranking Algorithm for Fairness Optimization

Yang et al. · CIKM 2023 · 2023

arXiv:2305.16637 · PDF · SP: SP5

P4Track C

한 줄 요약

다음 ΔT개 리스트에 대한 exposure를 볼록 QP로 공동 계획하고 슬롯을 수직으로 채우는 미래 인지 ranking(FARA). 반응형 제어기를 동일 불공정성에서 상위 랭크 효용으로 지배한다(cNDCG@1 129.0 vs FairCo 85.5).

Problem해결하려는 문제

Existing exposure-fair ranking algorithms are greedy: they construct the locally optimal ranklist for the next immediate session only. When an item carries accumulated exposure debt, greedy repayment must boost it aggressively within one list (e.g. an irrelevant item to rank 1), producing an unmitigated conflict between relevance and fairness and a suboptimal long-run frontier. No prior method plans exposure allocation over future sessions jointly.

Solution / Contribution해결책 / 기여

Two-phase future-aware planning, recomputed every $\Delta T$ sessions per query (Algorithm 2):

  1. Phase 1 — future exposure planning (QP): maximize $\Delta\widehat{\text{fair.}} = \vec{G}\cdot\Delta\vec{E} - \frac{1}{2}\Delta\vec{E}^T H \Delta\vec{E}$ over per-item planned marginal exposure $\Delta E(d)$, subject to (13b) total exposure of $\Delta T$ lists is conserved, (13c) DCG of the plan $\ge (1-\alpha) \cdot$ IDCG (relevance guarantee), (13d–e) $0 \le \Delta E(d) \le \Delta T \cdot P_1$. The expansion is exact (fairness is quadratic in exposure) and $H$ is PSD, so the QP is well-posed. An online variant (Eq. 14) adds slack variables $s(d)$ with weight $\beta$ enforcing a minimum-exposure exploration floor $E_{min}$ while relevance $\widehat{R}(d) = \text{cumC}^t(d) / E^t(d)$ is still being learned.
  2. Phase 2 — vertical allocation: construct the $\Delta T$ ranklists rank-major, greedily placing the most relevant item with remaining planned exposure; guarantees allocation error $\le P_{k_s}$ for all but $k_s$ items (Theorem 5.1) and optimal aver-NDCG at every cutoff (Theorem 5.2).

Served lists are popped one per session; the plan is re-solved when exhausted.

Key Results주요 결과
  • Effectiveness–fairness frontier: FARA dominates all fair baselines (FairCo, MCFair, MMF, PLFair, ILP, LP) on MSLR-10k, Istella-S, MQ2008 in both post-processing and online settings (Fig. 2, higher cNDCG@3 at equal unfairness tolerance).
  • At maximum fairness pressure ($\alpha$ max), FARA reaches the same unfairness lower bound as FairCo/LP but with much higher top-rank relevance: MSLR-10k cNDCG@1 129.0 vs FairCo 85.5 (+51%); Istella-S cNDCG@1 135.5 vs 63.3; MQ2008 cNDCG@1 196.3 vs 179.0.
  • Runtime: ~0.9s per 1k ranklists vs ILP ~1200s and LP ≥10 days on MSLR-10k — >1000× faster than programming-based baselines, comparable to controller-based FairCo (0.73s).
  • Increasing planning horizon $\Delta T$ (1→100) monotonically boosts cNDCG@3 for FARA (vertical) with unfairness flat; no boost for horizontal allocation — the gain comes specifically from planning + vertical fill.
  • Online exploration ablation: slack-variable exploration both raises the frontier and lowers the achievable unfairness floor (Fig. 4).
5Cs Analysis5Cs 분석

Category: new method (fair ranking algorithm) with theoretical optimality claims. Context: builds on exposure-based fairness (Singh & Joachims 2018 LP; Biega et al. 2018 ILP/equity of attention; Morik et al. 2020 FairCo; Oosterhuis 2021 PLFair; the authors' own MMF (WWW'21), MCFair (WSDM'23), and vertical-allocation idea from Yang et al. 2022 offline top-k amortization). Directly relevant to this survey's amortized-exposure thread (SP5). Correctness: the exactness of the second-order expansion and PSD Hessian are correct and cleanly argued; experiments average 5 runs with Fisher randomization tests ($p<0.05$). Caveat: all evaluation is on semi-synthetic click simulation with known examination probabilities. [SKIM - proof details of Theorems 5.1–5.3 spot-checked only] Contributions: (1) reframes fair ranking as joint multi-list future planning rather than per-session greedy repayment; (2) exact QP formulation over continuous exposure with $O(n)$ decision variables (vs $O(n^2)$ for LP/ILP → >1000× faster); (3) vertical allocation achieving cutoff-wise optimal NDCG. Contribution vs Claim기여 대 주장
  • Stated claims: FARA is theoretically optimal for joint relevance-fairness optimization; efficient; adaptable to recommendation tasks.
  • Demonstrated: optimality theorems are conditional — Theorem 5.2 shows NDCG-optimality given the exposure plan, and the QP is exact for this specific quadratic disparity measure; empirical wins on three semi-synthetic LTR simulations with known position bias.
  • Gap: no real-user or real-click evaluation (simulation only, examination probabilities assumed known); recommendation adaptation asserted but deferred to future work; fairness optimality holds for the squared-disparity metric — transfer to other exposure-fairness definitions untested. Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

Future-aware (planned) exposure amortization

  • What it computes: at time $t$, jointly precomputes the next $\Delta T$ ranklists $\mathcal{B}^* = [\pi_{t+1}, ..., \pi_{t+\Delta T}]$ for a query, maximizing marginal fairness $\Delta\widehat{\text{fair.}}(q, t, t+\Delta T)$ over all $\Delta T$ lists at once, instead of producing one locally optimal ranklist per session.
  • Why it was created: prior fair-ranking algorithms (FairCo, ILP, LP, MMF, MCFair) are greedy/myopic — they only optimize the next immediate session, so repaying an item's exposure debt forces it to the top rank of a single list, creating a relevance–fairness conflict.
  • Concrete example: 3 items with relevance A > B > C and C exposure-starved; greedy repayment yields one ranklist [C, A, B] (bad relevance), while joint planning yields [[A,C,B], [A,C,B], ...] — C is boosted smoothly at rank 2 across many lists while A stays on top.
  • How it is used in this paper: the core objective (Eq. 10) that the two-phase algorithm optimizes.

Exact second-order Taylor expansion of the fairness objective

  • What it computes: expresses fairness change as $\Delta\widehat{\text{fair.}} = \vec{G} \cdot \Delta\vec{E} - \frac{1}{2}\Delta\vec{E}^T H \Delta\vec{E}$ in the planned marginal exposure vector $\Delta E(d)$ per item.
  • Why it was created: to make long-horizon fairness planning tractable — the ranklist space is discrete/discontinuous, but exposure space is continuous.
  • Concrete example: because the squared-disparity unfairness (Eq. 8) is a degree-2 polynomial in exposure $E$, the expansion is an equality, not an approximation, and $H$ is positive semi-definite — so the planning problem is an exact convex QP.
  • How it is used in this paper: Phase 1 solves this QP (with total-exposure, min-NDCG constraint $(1-\alpha)$, and per-item bounds; via qpsolvers) to get optimal exposure planning $\Delta E^*$.

Vertical allocation

  • What it computes: given $\Delta E^*$, fills the $\Delta T$ ranklists rank-by-rank (all lists' rank 1 first, then all rank 2, ...), each slot taking the most-relevant item that still has planned exposure left, rather than list-by-list (horizontal).
  • Why it was created: many ranklist sets realize the same $\Delta E^*$ (same NDCG@$k_s$, Theorem 5.3); vertical order additionally optimizes NDCG at every cutoff $k_c \le k_s$ (Rearrangement-Inequality argument, Theorem 5.2).
  • Concrete example: FARA beats its own ablation FARA-Horiz. significantly at cNDCG@1 and @3 (e.g. MSLR-10k cNDCG@1: 129.0 vs 90.7) while both match at cNDCG@5.
  • How it is used in this paper: Phase 2 (Algorithm 1) constructs the served ranklists.

Exposure-based fairness via squared disparity — reuses the pairwise disparity measure $\left(E^t(d_x)R(d_y) - E^t(d_y)R(d_x)\right)^2$ from Oosterhuis 2021 (merit-proportional exposure: $E/R$ equal across items), chosen over Singh & Joachims 2018's $E(d)/R(d)$ form to avoid zero-denominator issues for irrelevant items.

Implementation Feasibility구현 가능성
  • Needs: any LTR dataset with relevance labels (or a click model), a QP solver (qpsolvers, Python), exposure/click bookkeeping per item; compute is light — $O(n)$ decision variables per query, ~1ms/ranklist.
  • Code: available at https://github.com/Taosheng-ty/QP_fairness/
  • Modifications: for our proxy-analysis setting, replace known $P_j$ with estimated exposure (the paper's exactness argument breaks if exposure is misestimated — the QP would plan against a wrong ledger); extend from per-query planning to shared item pools across queries.
  • Unknowns: missing link: sensitivity of the plan to relevance-estimator error $\widehat{R}$ (online setting studies exploration but not misestimation robustness); missing link: behavior when the same query does not recur $\Delta T$ times.
Follow-up Papers관련 후속 논문
  • Yang, Xu & Ai, "Effective Exposure Amortizing for Fair Top-k Recommendation" (arXiv 2022) — the offline vertical-allocation predecessor; contrast: requires all queries known in advance.
  • Morik et al., "Controlling Fairness and Bias in Dynamic Learning-to-Rank" (SIGIR 2020) — FairCo, the canonical past-amortizing (debt-repayment) baseline FARA is defined against; central to SP5's past-vs-future amortization axis.
  • Biega, Gummadi & Weikum, "Equity of Attention" (SIGIR 2018) — ILP-based amortized individual fairness; the original exposure-amortization framing this paper reframes as future planning.
Condition Delta — transfer to our setting조건 차이 — 우리 설정으로의 전이

Paper's setting: inputs = query + candidate items with (true or click-estimated) relevance and full exposure ledger $E^t(d)$ per item; outputs = batch of $\Delta T$ stochastic-in-order ranklists; conditions = repeated identical queries, known position-bias curve $P_j = 1/\log_2(j+1)$, $k_s=5$, semi-synthetic click simulation on MQ2008/MSLR-10k/Istella-S, provider-side merit-proportional fairness target. Our setting (SP5, exposure frequency as ranking proxy): we study exposure/impression frequency as a proxy signal in ranking — where accumulated-exposure bookkeeping itself drives ranking decisions and may distort relevance ordering. Differences:

  • Repeated-identical-query assumption (planning only pays off if the same query recurs $\Delta T$ times): missing link — real query streams are heavy-tailed; per-query plans for rare queries never amortize.
  • Known examination/position-bias probabilities: reference only — our setting treats exposure measurement itself as the noisy proxy under study; FARA assumes it is given exactly.
  • Fairness as the objective vs exposure-as-proxy analysis: resolved: FARA is a forward-planning use of the exposure ledger, giving SP5 a contrast point to debt-repayment (past-amortizing) methods like FairCo/equity-of-attention.
  • LTR benchmarks with graded relevance labels vs our general ranking-proxy setting: reference only.
Root-cause vs. Symptom근본 원인 대 증상

Direct — the paper identifies the root cause of the relevance–fairness conflict (per-session myopia of greedy amortization) and removes it structurally by planning exposure jointly over future sessions with an exact (not approximated) convex reformulation, rather than tuning the greedy repayment strength.
