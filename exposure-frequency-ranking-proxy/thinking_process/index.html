Objectives → SP mapping

O1 (taxonomy of exposure-frequency interventions) → SP1, SP3, SP4, SP5, SP6, SP7, SP8 — each mechanism family (exposure fairness, post-hoc re-ranking, stochastic policies, amortization, bandits, capping/pacing, popularity debiasing) contributes one branch of the taxonomy.

O3 (2024–2026 state of the art) → SP2 (dedicated recency probe, year-pinned 2024–2026), SP7 (recent feed-level work), SP9 (2023–2026 ecosystem line).
O4 (open research questions) → SP2, SP6, SP9 — frontier lines, theory-guarantee gaps, and strategic/ecosystem failure modes are the likeliest gap sources.

SP set (9 sub-problems)

SP1 Fairness of exposure in rankings under fixed scores — densest on-point field; contains all seed papers; taxonomy backbone.
SP2 2024–2026 frontier: exposure control & LLM-era re-ranking — dedicated recency probe (O3 is P1; stopping early is a failure condition).
SP3 Post-processing re-ranking over black-box scorers — the closest relaxation of the score-immutable constraint; papers state access assumptions explicitly.
SP4 Stochastic ranking policies & randomized exposure allocation — the formal mechanism realizing target exposure over repeated impressions.
SP5 Amortized fairness / equity of attention over time — temporal form of exposure-frequency control (cumulative impression equalization).
SP6 Bandit & online learning for exposure allocation — score-free candidates with regret guarantees under exposure constraints.
SP7 Impression capping, frequency control, pacing in feeds/ads — the literal industrial instantiation of the user's only knob.
SP8 Popularity debiasing & calibrated exposure vs. relevance — the controlled-objective axis beyond fairness proper.
SP9 Content ecosystems, creator incentives, platform exposure mechanisms — exposure as the platform's policy lever; likeliest O4 source.

Ranking rationale

SP1 leads because it directly answers both P1 objectives O1 and O2 and anchors on the seed papers (Singh & Joachims 2018, FairRec, Equity of Attention) — its vocabulary ("exposure", "expected exposure", "provider fairness") is what every later SP's queries and synthesis reuse. SP2 sits at rank 2 rather than last because recency is itself priority-1: probing the 2024–2026 frontier early lets its findings revise the queries of all seven remaining SPs instead of arriving after they have already run. SP3–SP6 then cover the mechanism families in decreasing directness to the user's constraint (read-only re-ranking → stochastic realization → temporal amortization → score-free online allocation). SP7–SP9 close with the industrial and ecosystem views that mostly feed O3/O4 rather than the core taxonomy.

Field assumptions

Before any papers are read, the ranker assumes: (1) the exposure-fairness line (2018–) is mature and well-cited, so SP1 will over-deliver; (2) an explicit "black-box scorer, exposure-frequency-only" framing matching the user's exact constraint is rare — most papers will assume at least read access to scores, making O2's feasibility map non-trivial and the purely score-free subset small (mainly bandits and capping/pacing); (3) the 2024–2026 frontier is dominated by LLM-ranker fairness audits and ecosystem/ incentive papers rather than new classical-style allocation algorithms; (4) industrial frequency-capping literature is thinner in academia than its practical importance suggests, so SP7 may under-deliver. Phase 1 search results will confirm or contradict each of these.

Conceptual surprises

(1) The proxy question is repeatedly flagged as open by the papers themselves: nearly every skim's Condition Delta hits the same wall — methods assume true relevance/merit is known and exposure follows from it, while the survey's setting treats logged exposure frequency as a contaminated proxy. Equity of Attention assumes relevance=worthiness; FairPGRank calls it out of scope; ExpectedExposure needs a known browsing model. (2) "Scoring Is Not Enough" (2026) proves score-then-sort is Pareto-suboptimal for any non-decomposable exposure objective — a structural argument FOR frequency-level intervention. (3) Production evidence of proxy failure: Meta's VRS achieves impression-share parity while reducing unique-user reach ~9.8% (FAccT 2025 external audit) — impressions ≠ people; the Timee field experiment shows engagement flat while matches rose. (4) FELIX and the Expohedron line are the most direct formalizations of the user's constraint (stochastic policy over frozen scores realizing target exposure).

Phase 3 ✓ Synthesis SP1: Fairness of exposure in rankings under fixed scores 2026-07-15 KST
Prior belief ◈

The densest, most on-point field; expected to supply the O1 taxonomy backbone and the first O2 access-level labels, with most methods assuming at least score read access.

Dominant approach found

Score-read-only post-processing over a fixed merit vector, in two formally-identical realizations: within-query stochastic policies (convex program over marginal rank probabilities → distribution over rankings) and cross-query amortization (cumulative exposure ledger + dual/pacing variables). Because utility and exposure are both linear in the marginal rank-probability matrix, exposure control under fixed scores reduces to convex optimization — and a distribution over rankings served across repeated impressions IS an impression-frequency schedule (FELIX states the equivalence explicitly).

Surprise / Confirmation

Confirmed: 9/15 papers are score-read-only (feasible under immutable scores). Surprised: (a) nearly all read-only methods still assume per-instance slate-reordering rights — only FairRec operates on pure inclusion counts, and its EF1/MMS guarantees provably do not transfer to position-weighted exposure; (b) the 2026 SOTA (HEA) moves AWAY from black-box feasibility (needs internal embeddings); (c) post-processing on group-corrupted scores can become LESS fair as the fairness weight rises (FairPGRank) — the trusted-scores assumption is load-bearing and untested everywhere.

6 claims added (1 mechanism, 2 findings, 1 gap, 1 limitation, 1 hypothesis — the pacing≡dual-targeting bridge). knowledge_base.json initialized. 7 RQs extracted; 4 follow-up seeds (1 missing_sp candidate: policy-weights→impression-budget discretization).

Phase 3 ✓ Synthesis SP2: 2024–2026 frontier — exposure control and LLM-era re-ranking 2026-07-15 KST
Prior belief ◈

Recency probe for O3; revised after SP1 to check whether frontier papers relax the two universal assumptions (known position-bias curve, trusted scores). Pre-loop search assessed WWW 2026's FUDLR as an "almost verbatim" match to the user constraint.

Dominant approach found

The frontier empirically demolishes the trusted-scores assumption for LLM/generative scorers via three identified mechanisms (prompt-position bias; pre-training content priors; gradient starvation + tokenization amplification) — and scaling does not fix it. But the strongest repairs abandon black-box feasibility (FUDLR needs gradients, Ghost retrains the tokenizer, CRAB edits the codebook). The known-position-bias assumption is untouched, and LLM re-rankers stack a second, unmeasured internal position-bias curve (prompt slots) on top of the display one.

Surprise / Confirmation

Corrected a pre-loop belief: FUDLR is NOT black-box feasible (needs gradients + training data). The true near-verbatim match to the user's setting is TEC (Timee 2026 production field experiment) — purely frequency-level, exposure-debited eligibility control over an unmodified scorer, the first production successor to FairRec's show/don't-show pattern (KB claim 3 refined). Also new: three independent 2026 papers converge that exposure is a lossy proxy for provider utility when exposure→utility conversion is heterogeneous — a THIRD load-bearing assumption identified. Inference-time exposure correction is non-monotone in correction strength (fully removing the exposure signal hurts; γ-tuning required).

Phase 3 ✓ Synthesis SP4: Stochastic ranking policies and randomized exposure allocation 2026-07-16 KST
Prior belief ◈

The user's exposure-frequency knob formalized; synthesis organized around the realization axis with two inherited lenses: policy-weights→impression-budget discretization (SP1) and realized-vs-target concentration bounds (SP3).

Dominant approach found

Three realization families with a strict ordering: LP+BvN (exact in expectation, O(n⁵)); expohedron+Carathéodory (exact, O(n² log n), support ≤ n, whole Pareto front, numerics cap n≈100); PL/Gumbel sampling (cheapest per impression but under fixed scores only a one-parameter temperature family — Pareto-dominated). Headline structural finding: decomposition and delivery are SCORE-FREE — scores enter exactly once, at target selection. The immutable-scores constraint does not bind on the realization axis at all.

Surprise / Confirmation

(a) Exactly one per-realization mechanism exists in the whole line — balanced-word (stride) delivery, deterministic, ~10× empirically tighter than i.i.d. sampling — but NO paper states a concentration theorem in position-weighted exposure units. (b) The discretization question is solved only for the single-repeated-query case; the production mapping remains open, and stride scheduling ≈ ad pacing's smooth delivery (strengthens the pacing≡dual-targeting hypothesis). (c) The realization machinery has been FROZEN since 2022 — every 2024–2026 paper changes targets or scale, never the mechanism. (d) Controller adequacy diverges across papers (near-Pareto vs unfairness 0.93 on MSLR) — unadjudicated, sent to SP5.

Total 17. 7 RQs; seeds: concentration-theorem gap (→SP6 regret bounds), discretization+smooth-delivery (→SP7), target-redefinition reading lens (→SP8).
