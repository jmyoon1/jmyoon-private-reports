Online Restless Multi-Armed Bandits with Long-Term Fairness Constraints

Wang et al. · AAAI 2024 · 2024 · 16 citations

arXiv:2312.10303 · PDF · SP: SP6

P0Track A

한 줄 요약

장기 공정성 제약 하의 온라인 restless bandit을 다룬다.

Problem해결하려는 문제

Basic RMAB maximizes long-term reward under a per-epoch budget ($\le B$ arms activated) but provides no guarantee on how activation is distributed among arms — low-priority arms can be starved indefinitely. Prior fairness-in-RMAB work (Li & Varakantham 2022a/b; Herlihy et al. 2023) covers only finite-horizon or discounted settings, has no finite-time regret analysis, and (Herlihy et al.) is restricted to 2-state MDPs. Prior online-RMAB RL (colored-UCRL2, Thompson sampling) is computationally exponential in state-space size and ignores fairness entirely.

Solution / Contribution해결책 / 기여

Introduce RMAB-F (minimum long-term activation fraction $\eta_n$ per arm) and solve its online version with Fair-UCRL:

  1. Per episode $k$: build plausible-MDP confidence set $\mathcal{M}^k$ from empirical transition/reward means with Hoeffding radius $\delta_n^k(s,a)$.
  2. Relax the instantaneous budget constraint to a long-term one; rewrite the relaxed optimistic problem as an extended LP over occupancy measures $z_n^k(s,a,s')$, with the fairness constraint appearing as LP row $\sum_{s,s'} z_n^k(s,1,s') \ge \eta_n$.
  3. Convert the LP solution into the FairRMAB index $\omega_n^{k,*}(s)$ (probability of activation in state $s$ under the LP solution) and activate the top-$B$ arms each epoch — restoring exact per-epoch budget feasibility.

Theorem 1: reward regret $\tilde{O}(B\epsilon\log T + \sqrt{SANT})$ and per-arm fairness violation regret $\tilde{O}(\eta_n\epsilon\log T + C_0 T^n_{\text{Mix}}\sqrt{SANT})$, both with high probability. The FairRMAB index policy needs no Whittle indexability condition and is asymptotically optimal (Theorem 3).

Key Results주요 결과
  • Reward regret bound: $\tilde{O}(B\epsilon\log T + (\sqrt{2}+2)\sqrt{SANT\log\frac{SANT}{\epsilon}})$ with probability $\ge 1-(\frac{\epsilon}{SANT})^{1/2}$ (Theorem 1) — first such bound for online RMAB with fairness constraints.
  • Fairness violation regret: $\tilde{O}(\eta_n\epsilon\log T + C_0 T^n_{\text{Mix}}\sqrt{SANT}\log\frac{SANT}{\epsilon})$ per arm.
  • G-Fair-UCRL: zero fairness violation, reward regret picks up an extra $\lceil\frac{\sum_n \eta_n}{B}\rceil\sqrt{T}$ term (Theorem 2).
  • Simulations (300 arms, $B=100$): lowest accumulated reward regret vs. FaWT-U and Soft Fairness; fairness fractions converge to required $\eta$ while FaWT-U repeatedly violates them. [Exact regret numbers are figure-only, no table.]
  • Validated on 3 real-data settings: CPAP therapy adherence ($N=20$, $B=5$), PASCAL-RTE crowdworker assignment ($N=10$, $B=3$, $\eta=0.05$), land mobile satellite channels ($N=4$, $B=2$, $\eta=0.03$).

Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

RMAB-F (RMAB with long-term fairness constraints)

  • What it computes: a restless multi-armed bandit optimization — maximize $\liminf_{T\to\infty} \frac{1}{T}\mathbb{E}[\sum_{t=1}^T \sum_{n=1}^N r_n(t)]$ subject to (i) instantaneous activation constraint $\sum_n a_n(t) \le B$ at every epoch and (ii) a long-term fairness constraint $\liminf_{T\to\infty} \frac{1}{T}\mathbb{E}[\sum_t a_n(t)] \ge \eta_n$ for every arm $n$.
  • Why it was created: basic RMAB gives no guarantee on how activations are distributed across arms — an arm with low index can be starved forever, which is unacceptable in healthcare/resource-allocation uses.
  • Concrete example: 3 classes of 100 arms each, budget $B=100$, minimum activation fractions $\eta = 0.1, 0.2, 0.3$ per class — each arm must be activated at least that fraction of epochs on long-run average.
  • How it is used in this paper: the problem definition (Eqs. 1–3); all algorithms and regret definitions are built on it.

Fair-UCRL

  • What it computes: an episodic model-based RL algorithm — per episode: build Hoeffding confidence balls around empirical transition/reward estimates → solve an extended LP over state-action-state occupancy measures with the fairness constraint as an LP row → derive a "fair index" $\omega_n^{k,*}(s) = \sum_{s'} z_n^{k,*}(s,1,s') / \sum_{a,s'} z_n^{k,*}(s,a,s')$ per arm-state → activate the $B$ highest-indexed arms each epoch.
  • Why it was created: off-the-shelf RL for online RMAB (colored-UCRL2, Thompson sampling) has exponential compute and regret in state-space size, and no prior method handles the fairness constraint at all.
  • Concrete example: with $\epsilon \in (0,1)$, confidence radius $\delta_n^k(s,a) = \sqrt{\frac{1}{2C_n^k(s,a)}\log\frac{SAN(k-1)H}{\epsilon}}$.
  • How it is used in this paper: the main contribution; Theorem 1 proves both regrets sublinear.

Fairness violation regret ($\Delta_T^{n,F}$)

  • What it computes: $T\eta_n - \mathbb{E}_\pi[\sum_{t=1}^T a_n(t)]$ — the per-arm cumulative shortfall between the required activation fraction and the actually delivered activations.
  • Why it was created: conventional RMAB regret analysis bounds only reward regret; with a fairness constraint one needs a second regret to quantify how much the learned policy under-serves each arm during learning.
  • Concrete example: bounded as $\tilde{O}(\eta_n \epsilon \log T + C_0 T^n_{\text{Mix}}\sqrt{SANT}\log\frac{SANT}{\epsilon})$, i.e. $\tilde{O}(\sqrt{T})$ per arm.
  • How it is used in this paper: one of the two quantities Theorem 1 bounds; the proof (via mixing times) is claimed as the main theoretical novelty.

G-Fair-UCRL — variant that greedily force-pulls each arm $H\eta_n$ times at the start of every episode, achieving zero fairness violation at the cost of an extra $\lceil\sum_n \eta_n / B\rceil \sqrt{T}$ term in reward regret. [SKIM - verify proof details in deep read]

Implementation Feasibility구현 가능성
  • Needs: an LP solver (the extended LP has $O(N S^2 A)$ variables), per-arm transition/reward counters; no deep learning, modest compute.
  • Code: not available — no repository link found in the paper. [SKIM - confirm in deep read]
  • Modifications: for a ranking/exposure setting, replace binary activation with position-dependent exposure weights in constraints (16)–(17) and redefine the budget as a slate/slot constraint; state spaces beyond small finite $S$ would need function approximation, voiding the current regret analysis.
  • Unknowns: missing link: sensitivity to the feasibility assumption (Assumption 1) when $\sum_n \eta_n$ approaches $B/N$ capacity; missing link: how mixing time $T^n_{\text{Mix}}$ (unknown in practice) affects usable confidence-parameter choices.
Follow-up Papers관련 후속 논문
  • Li & Varakantham, "Efficient Resource Allocation with Fairness Constraints in Restless Multi-Armed Bandits" (UAI 2022) — the FaWT-U predecessor/baseline; finite-horizon fairness-in-RMAB without regret analysis.
  • Herlihy et al., "Planning to fairly allocate: Probabilistic fairness in the restless bandit setting" (KDD 2023) — probabilistic fairness guarantees but restricted to 2-state MDPs; the limitation this paper generalizes past.
  • Xiong, Wang & Li, "Learning Infinite-Horizon Average-Reward Restless Multi-Action Bandits via Index Awareness" (NeurIPS 2022) — the LP-based index-policy machinery Fair-UCRL builds on, without fairness.
  • Yin et al., "Long-Term Fairness with Unknown Dynamics" (2023) — cited motivation for long-term fairness under learning; relevant to SP6's dynamic-exposure angle.
Condition Delta — transfer to our setting조건 차이 — 우리 설정으로의 전이

Paper's setting: inputs = $N$ arms each an unknown finite unichain MDP (state space $S$, binary activate/passive action), per-epoch budget $B$, per-arm minimum activation fractions $\eta_n$; outputs = an activation subset each epoch; conditions = infinite-horizon average reward, episodic online learning, feasibility assumed. Our setting (SP6 — exposure frequency as a ranking proxy): items in a ranking/recommendation system whose exposure frequency must meet minimum guarantees while ranking quality is maximized; exposure is position-dependent and graded rather than a binary activate/passive action; item "state" (relevance, freshness) may not follow a small known-structure MDP. [SKIM - verify SP6 framing against synthesis docs] Differences:

  • Binary activation vs. graded position-based exposure in rankings: the fairness constraint $\frac{1}{T}\sum_t a_n(t) \ge \eta_n$ is exactly a minimum long-term exposure-frequency guarantee, so the constraint form transfers; the action space does not — missing link (extension to slot/position-weighted exposure needed).
  • Finite small state space per arm assumed ($S$ enters regret as $\sqrt{S}$): ranking items with rich feature states break this — missing link.
  • Reward only when activated ($r_n(s,0)=0$): analogous to "clicks only when exposed" in ranking — resolved: matches the exposure-proxy premise.
  • Infinite-horizon average criterion vs. typical finite-horizon ranking evaluation: reference only.
  • Provides the regret-vs-fairness-violation two-regret framework that SP6 can borrow for analyzing exposure-frequency guarantees during learning — resolved: directly reusable conceptual template.
Root-cause vs. Symptom근본 원인 대 증상

Direct — the paper embeds the fairness requirement as an explicit constraint in the optimization (an LP row on occupancy measures) and proves finite-time bounds on its violation, rather than post-hoc patching an unfair policy (as the FaWT-U baseline does by force-activating starved arms); the root cause — fairness absent from the RMAB objective — is addressed structurally.
