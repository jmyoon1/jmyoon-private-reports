Creator Incentives in Recommender Systems: A Cooperative Game-Theoretic Approach for Stable and Fair Collaboration in Multi-Agent Bandits

Krishnamurthy et al. · AISTATS 2026 (Oral) · 2026 · 1 citations

arXiv:2604.08643 · PDF · SP: SP6

P4Track B

한 줄 요약

multi-agent bandit에서 협조 게임 이론으로 creator 인센티브의 안정적/공정한 협업을 설계한다(AISTATS 2026 Oral). creator 복지를 exposure가 아닌 regret 배분으로 측정하는 사례.

Problem해결하려는 문제

Feedback on one creator's content changes what the platform's learner infers and therefore how much exposure other creators get, coupling creators' economic outcomes. Prior strategic-recommender work (Ben-Porat & Tennenholtz 2018; Jagadeesan et al. 2023; Yao et al. 2023) fixes the recommendation algorithm and studies content-creation games; none answers whether cooperative participation (data-sharing among creators, i.e. the platform's centralized learning viewed as a coalition) is individually rational, stable, and fairly compensated.

Solution / Contribution해결책 / 기여

Model creators as agents in a multi-agent stochastic linear bandit (shared unknown $\theta^*$, per-agent action sets $X_{a,t}$), and coalitions as full data-sharing groups running a joint bandit algorithm. Define the TU collaboration game $v(C) = -\sum_{a\in C} R^C_a$. Then:

  1. Homogeneous agents, fixed shared action set (Thm 1): if the black-box single-agent algorithm has strictly concave cumulative regret that flattens no faster than logarithmically (Assumption 1), the meta-algorithm Mul (buffer-based reduction to a single agent run for $mT$ steps, à la Howson et al. 2024) induces a convex game → non-empty core containing the Shapley value → grand coalition stable and fair.
  2. Heterogeneous action sets (Thm 2): if the algorithm uses only anonymized pooled samples (Assumption 2) and no agent's regret increases when the coalition grows (Assumption 3), the game is balanced → non-empty core (via Bondareva–Shapley), but convexity/Shapley-in-core no longer guaranteed.
  3. Payout rule (Thm 3): $p_a = -R^M_a$ satisfies efficiency, dummy-player, symmetry, and core membership (not linearity). [SKIM - proof details in appendix D; deep read to verify assumption usage]
Key Results주요 결과
  • Thm 1: homogeneous fixed-action-set game is convex for sufficiently large $T$ (given concave, at-least-logarithmic regret of the black-box learner) → Shapley value in the core.
  • Thm 2: heterogeneous game has a non-empty core whenever regret is monotone non-increasing in coalition size (Assumption 3) — grand coalition always stable.
  • Thm 3: payout $p_a = -R^M_a$ satisfies efficiency + dummy-player + symmetry and is in the core; linearity argued unattainable (App. D.3).
  • MovieLens-100k: payouts track empirical Shapley values closely for the occupation-split instance; for the geography split, some agents (NY, PA users) contribute far more than they are compensated — engagement-based reward is inequitable there.
  • Greedy (myopic OLS) beats LinUCB in regret on both instances and yields higher Shapley values, attributed to inadvertent exploration from heterogeneous action sets (consistent with Kannan et al. 2018).
5Cs Analysis5Cs 분석

Category: theoretical (cooperative game theory applied to multi-agent bandits) with small empirical validation. Context: builds on collaborative multi-agent bandits (Wang et al. 2019, Howson et al. 2024), greedy-with-heterogeneity results (Kannan et al. 2018, Raghavan et al. 2018), and strategic content-creator models (Hron et al. 2023, Yao et al. 2023). Within this survey it is the creator-side incentive complement to SP6's user-side exposure-allocation bandits. Correctness: theorems come with full appendix proofs; assumptions are explicitly stated and defended (App. B) — Assumption 3 is checked empirically and fails for 3–22 of 2816 agent–coalition pairs on MovieLens, an honest caveat. Experiments report error bars over 5 repetitions. [SKIM - proof soundness not independently verified] Contributions: (1) first TU-cooperative-game formulation of creator interdependence in recommender learning; (2) convexity/core results under mild algorithmic assumptions; (3) a computation-free payout rule with 3/4 Shapley axioms + core membership. Clarity: model, algorithms (Mul, M-ETC), and proofs are stated precisely; reproducible in principle from the description plus MovieLens-100k setup details in App. E.

Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

Collaboration game (TU game over coalition regrets)

  • What it computes: input = a multi-agent stochastic linear bandit instance $I=(\theta^*, X)$, a bandit algorithm Alg, horizon $T$; operation = for each coalition $C \subseteq M$ of agents (creators), compute $v(C) = -\sum_{a \in C} R^C_a(\text{Alg}, I, T)$, the negative sum of cumulative regrets when coalition members pool their action–reward data; output = a transferable-utility (TU) characteristic function $v: 2^M \to \mathbb{R}$.
  • Why it was created: prior strategic-recommender work fixes an algorithm and analyzes creator content choices; there was no formal way to quantify how one creator's feedback data helps or hurts another creator's exposure/reward, i.e. no incentive analysis of data-sharing coalitions among creators.
  • Concrete example: two MovieLens gender-group agents — solo regrets 1085 and 1106 under LinUCB, but 752 and 672 in the two-agent coalition, so $v(\{0,1\}) = -1424 > v(\{0\}) + v(\{1\}) = -2191$.
  • How it is used in this paper: the entire analysis object — Theorems 1–3 establish convexity / non-empty core / axiom compliance of this game.

Grand-coalition-regret payout rule

  • What it computes: input = the grand coalition $M$ run of Alg; operation = pay each agent $p_a = -R^M_a(\text{Alg}, I, T)$, the negative of their own regret inside the grand coalition; output = an allocation profile $p \in \mathbb{R}^M$.
  • Why it was created: for heterogeneous agents the Shapley value may fall outside the core and is anyway impractical (exponential in $M$, requires counterfactual regrets of all $2^M$ coalitions); this rule needs no computation or post-hoc redistribution.
  • Concrete example: in the MovieLens occupation instance, the payout of each occupation-group agent is simply its observed negative regret (e.g. roughly −150 to −250), plotted against its empirical Shapley value.
  • How it is used in this paper: Theorem 3 shows it satisfies 3 of the 4 Shapley axioms (efficiency, dummy-player, symmetry — not linearity) and lies in the core, so the grand coalition is stable under it.

Core / Shapley value / convex TU game — standard cooperative game theory (Shapley 1953/1971, Bondareva–Shapley). Core = allocations no coalition can block; convex game = supermodular value function, which guarantees the Shapley value is in the core. Recapped in the paper's Appendix A; not new here.

Implementation Feasibility구현 가능성
  • Needs: MovieLens-100k, a linear-bandit implementation (paper extends LinUCB from Meta's Pearl library), exhaustive $2^M-1$ coalition runs to compute empirical Shapley values (feasible only for small $M \le 8$).
  • Code: not available (no repository link found in the paper). [SKIM - check appendix/openreview for code]
  • Modifications: to serve this survey, the value function would have to be re-defined over exposure counts instead of regret, and the platform reinstated as the allocating agent.
  • Unknowns: missing link — whether Assumption 3 (regret monotone in coalition size) holds for exposure-constrained allocation mechanisms; the paper itself finds violations in 3–22 of 2816 pairs.

  • Fedorova, Kitch & Podimata (2025), "Altruistic Collective Action in Recommender Systems" — demand-side (user) collectives strategically shaping recommendations; complements the supply-side view here.

Condition Delta — transfer to our setting조건 차이 — 우리 설정으로의 전이

Paper's setting: inputs = multi-agent stochastic linear bandit, shared true $\theta^*$, creators as agents pooling action–reward data; outputs = coalition stability results and per-agent payouts (negative regret); conditions = platform abstracted away, transferable utility, offline game-theoretic analysis, MovieLens-100k simulation with $d=100$, $T=4096$. Our setting (SP6 / exposure-frequency-as-ranking-proxy survey): inputs = an exposure/impression-allocation mechanism over items, with exposure frequency used as a proxy signal for ranking quality; outputs = score-free exposure allocation with fairness/minimum-exposure guarantees (O1, O2, O4); conditions = the platform is the decision-maker, item-side exposure is the quantity of interest. Differences:

  • Fairness object: paper's fairness is over creator payouts (regret shares), our SP needs fairness over exposure counts/frequencies: missing link — the paper never converts regret allocations into exposure allocations.
  • Decision-maker: paper abstracts the platform away and lets creators "choose" actions; our setting keeps the platform as the allocator: reference only.
  • Exposure-frequency proxy: the paper's reward is user engagement $\langle\theta^*,x\rangle$, not frequency of exposure; no proxy analysis: reference only.
  • Bandit machinery (regret decomposition, LinUCB/greedy comparison, data-pooling monotonicity): resolved — directly reusable analytic vocabulary for SP6's regret-under-constraints column.
  • Fixed creators/content: paper takes content as given (no strategic content creation), matching our static-item assumption: resolved by shared assumption.
Root-cause vs. Symptom근본 원인 대 증상

Mixed — for the homogeneous case it addresses the root cause (proves structural convexity of the induced game, so stability/fairness follow from first principles), but for the realistic heterogeneous case it substitutes an easy-to-compute payout that satisfies only 3 of 4 Shapley axioms and empirically diverges from Shapley fairness for outlier agents, patching the fairness question rather than resolving it.
