Optimising Budget Management via Primal-Dual Approximation with Constrained Polynomial Weights Update

Moor et al. · KDD 2025 · 2025

Problem해결하려는 문제

Existing budget-spend optimization for online allocation (sponsored-search ad campaigns, promotional-content slots in recommender systems) splits into two camps with complementary failures: (1) worst-case approximation algorithms for the online knapsack problem, which underperform in practice because extreme adversarial scenarios are rare; (2) online-learning methods that optimize average performance but lack robustness in highly non-stationary environments. No existing method delivers both worst-case guarantees and good average allocative efficiency.

Solution / Contribution해결책 / 기여

[SKIM - verify] From the abstract: frame optimal budget allocation as online learning where the learner's strategy set is constrained by a primal-dual approximation algorithm that defines the environment. The paper (a) identifies a feedback loop between the learner's strategy and the environment's response, (b) proposes a method to break this loop, yielding an online budget-pacing algorithm ("constrained polynomial weights update") that improves average allocative efficiency while preserving the primal-dual worst-case guarantees. Exact algorithm steps, the guarantee statement, and the loop-breaking construction require the full paper.

Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

Note: no PDF or arXiv version is available (KDD 2025, citation_count 0, S2 record only). All concepts below are identified from the abstract; every definition is [SKIM - verify in deep read] at the mechanism level.

Online budget pacing

  • What it computes: a stream of arriving impression/allocation opportunities + a fixed budget → per-opportunity accept/bid decision → a spend trajectory that exhausts the budget smoothly over the horizon rather than early
  • Why it was created: naive greedy spending exhausts budgets early and misses high-value later opportunities; pacing spreads spend to match opportunity arrival
  • Concrete example: an advertiser with a $1,000 daily budget facing impressions arriving all day; pacing throttles participation so spend at hour 12 is roughly $500, not $1,000 [SKIM - verify in deep read]
  • How it is used in this paper: the core problem — the paper's algorithm is a pacing rule for promotional-impression budgets in a recommender system

Primal-dual approximation for online knapsack

  • What it computes: an arriving item's value/cost → comparison against a dual variable (a threshold price maintained from the LP dual of the knapsack) → accept iff value exceeds the dual price; dual updated after each decision
  • Why it was created: gives worst-case competitive-ratio guarantees for online packing without distributional assumptions
  • Concrete example: classic online knapsack primal-dual achieves $O(\log(U/L))$ competitive ratio where $U/L$ is the value-density range [SKIM - verify: which guarantee this paper preserves]
  • How it is used in this paper: defines the "environment" that constrains the online learner's strategies — the safety layer that preserves worst-case guarantees

Constrained polynomial weights update

  • What it computes: per-round losses of candidate strategies → a polynomial-weights (online-learning) update over strategies, restricted to the strategy set the primal-dual approximation admits → the next round's strategy
  • Why it was created: pure online learning lacks robustness in highly non-stationary environments; constraining its strategy space by a worst-case-safe algorithm bridges the two [SKIM - verify in deep read]
  • Concrete example: [SKIM - verify in deep read] — the exact update rule and constraint set require the methods section
  • How it is used in this paper: the paper's named mechanism (title); it is the learner layer sitting inside the primal-dual environment

Learner–environment feedback loop

  • What it computes: the online learner's chosen strategy changes the environment's (primal-dual algorithm's) response, which changes the losses the learner observes, closing a loop that can destabilize learning
  • Why it was created: identified by this paper as the obstacle to combining online learning with a primal-dual safety layer
  • Concrete example: [SKIM - verify in deep read]
  • How it is used in this paper: the paper "proposes a method to break this loop," which is the key technical contribution enabling average-efficiency gains without losing worst-case guarantees
