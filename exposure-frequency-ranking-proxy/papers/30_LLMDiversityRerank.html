Enhancing Recommendation Diversity by Re-ranking with Large Language Models

Diego Carraro et al. · arXiv · 2024 · 62 citations

arXiv:2401.11506 · PDF · SP: SP3

P2Track A

한 줄 요약

LLM을 re-ranker로 사용해 추천 다양성을 높이는 프롬프트 기반 접근을 제안한다. 제어 대상이 다양성인 re-ranking 사례로 O1의 목표 축을 채운다.

Problem해결하려는 문제

Existing diversification methods for recommender systems (greedy re-rankers such as MMR, xQuAD, RxQuAD) require explicit item features and hand-designed objective functions. LLMs have been shown to re-rank for relevance in RS/IR, but no prior work tests whether general-purpose LLMs can perform beyond-accuracy (diversity) re-ranking zero-shot, nor what the performance/cost trade-offs against traditional re-rankers are.

Solution / Contribution해결책 / 기여

Frame diversification re-ranking as zero-shot text generation: prompt the LLM with a relevance-ranked candidate list $CL$ ($m$=40/50 items with rank numbers) and instruct it to output a top-10 list that balances or maximizes diversity, using 8 prompt templates varying the goal string ($\lambda \in \{0, 0.5\}$ analogue) and optional per-item features (genres, plot summaries). Parse output; discard invalid items and random-fill. Compare 4 LLMs (ChatGPT, InstructGPT, Llama2-7B/13B-Chat) against Random, MMR, xQuAD, RxQuAD over an MF base recommender on Anime (17M ratings) and Goodreads (8M ratings).

Key Results주요 결과
  • Anime, avg over 8 templates: ChatGPT loses 10.1% NDCG vs MF baseline while gaining +4.7% ILD / +7.8% SRecall; traditional re-rankers lose only 4.6–6.5% NDCG (xQuAD: +7.9% α-NDCG, best). Best-template ChatGPT loses just 2.8% NDCG, beating all traditional re-rankers on that metric.
  • Goodreads is much worse for LLMs: ChatGPT −33.4% NDCG, InstructGPT −53.5%, Llama2-13B −61.3% (vs traditional −4.7 to −8.1%); ILD actually drops for all LLMs (−1.1 to −4.8%).
  • Invalid generations: 2.3% (Anime) vs 13.1% (Goodreads); feature-rich templates T7/T8 worst (18.0%/22.4%). LLMs also draw lower-ranked items from $CL$ (e.g., avg lowest rank 43.1 for Llama2-13B on Goodreads vs 15.9 for RxQuAD).
  • Ordering among LLMs: ChatGPT > InstructGPT > Llama2-7B > Llama2-13B (13B worse than 7B, unexplained). Feature-aware templates (T5–T8) help on average despite more invalid outputs.
  • Cost: traditional re-rankers ~0.1s/user vs 12.6–31.6s/user (Llama2) or <25s (OpenAI API, upper bound); total OpenAI API cost ~$161 for 500 users x 8 templates x 2 datasets.
  • RQ1 preliminary study: ChatGPT re-ranks by popularity with rank-biased-overlap 0.5–0.8 against ground truth, and by release date at 0.35–0.7 — direct evidence the LLM has internalized item popularity/exposure information from pretraining.
5Cs Analysis5Cs 분석

Category: benchmark / empirical evaluation (no new model; a feasibility study of LLM-based diversity re-ranking) Context: builds on greedy re-ranking (Carbonell & Goldstein MMR 1998; Vargas xQuAD/RxQuAD) and LLM-as-ranker work (Dai et al. 2023; Hou et al. 2024; Sun et al. 2023 RankGPT). In this survey it covers the O1 diversity-objective axis for SP3. Correctness: 95% CIs reported (Appendix B) but explicitly wide — only 500 test users, so many comparisons lack confirmed significance; the authors flag this honestly. Preliminary study (RQ1) is explicitly informal. Contributions: (1) first LLM zero-shot diversity re-ranking methodology with 8 templates + explicit invalid-output handling; (2) quantitative comparison showing LLM re-rankers beat Random but trail traditional greedy re-rankers; (3) cost/trade-off analysis (money, latency, control, cold-start, valid-output guarantees). Clarity: reproducible — prompts, hyperparameters, and code are given (github.com/cdiego89phd/chat-reranking).

Contribution vs Claim기여 대 주장
  • Stated claims: LLMs "can interpret diversification re-ranking"; LLM re-ranking will "become more competitive soon" as costs fall.
  • Demonstrated: LLM re-rankers statistically beat Random on relevance-aware metrics; they trail traditional re-rankers, with significance confirmed mainly where CIs don't overlap (Goodreads gaps; ILD wins for OpenAI models).
  • Gap: the "will become competitive" claim is extrapolation from cost trends, not demonstrated. The domain-difference explanation (books harder than anime due to title similarity/prompt length) is explicitly labeled speculation. Only gpt-3.5-class and Llama2-class models tested (Oct 2023–Jan 2024); no GPT-4. Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

Greedy diversification re-ranking (framework the paper builds on)

  • What it computes: candidate list $CL$ of $m$ relevance-ranked items → iteratively select the item maximizing $f_{obj}(i, RL) = \lambda \cdot rel(i) + (1-\lambda) \cdot div(i, RL)$ → final list $RL$ of $n < m$ items balancing relevance and diversity.
  • Why it was created: relevance-only top-$n$ lists are redundant; post-processing lets any base recommender be diversified without retraining.
  • Concrete example: $m=40$ anime candidates from MF, $n=10$, $\lambda=0.5$; MMR sets $div(i,RL)$ = negative of $i$'s max similarity to items already in $RL$.
  • How it is used in this paper: the conceptual frame that LLM prompts "implement" implicitly — key terms ("balance", "maximize", "diversity") stand in for $\lambda$, $rel$, $div$.

Zero-shot prompt-based diversity re-ranking (the paper's contribution)

  • What it computes: prompt containing instructions + output format + the ranked $CL$ (titles, optionally genres or plot summaries) → LLM text generation → parsed top-$n$ re-ranked list.
  • Why it was created: prior LLM-in-RS re-ranking work (Dai et al. 2023, Hou et al. 2024, Sun et al. 2023) targets relevance only; this is claimed as the first LLM re-ranking work targeting a beyond-accuracy objective (diversity).
  • Concrete example: 8 templates T1–T8; T1 = "balance relevance and diversity" ($\lambda \approx 0.5$), T2 = "maximize the items' diversity" ($\lambda \approx 0$), T5–T8 are feature-aware (append genres or plot summaries per item).
  • How it is used in this paper: the entire experimental object, evaluated on Anime and Goodreads datasets against MMR/xQuAD/RxQuAD/Random.

Invalid-generation handling by random fill

  • What it computes: LLM output items not matching any $CL$ title (hallucinations, title mismatches like "Naruto" vs "Naruto:Shippuden") are discarded; the incomplete $RL$ is padded with random items from $CL$ until $|RL| = n$.
  • Why it was created: most LLM-RS papers ignore invalid outputs; this makes the evaluation methodology explicit and quantifies the problem.
  • Concrete example: invalid-generation rate 2.3% (Anime) vs 13.1% (Goodreads); worst template T8 = 22.4%.
  • How it is used in this paper: named as one of three factors explaining why LLM re-rankers lose relevance vs. traditional ones.

Diversity metrics ($\alpha$-NDCG, ILD, EILD, SRecall, rSRecall) are reused from Vargas & Castells' framework — no new metrics introduced.

Implementation Feasibility구현 가능성
  • Needs: Anime Recommendation Database + Goodreads datasets (public); RankSys (Java) for MF + traditional re-rankers; OpenAI API (~$161 for the full study at 2023 prices) or 2x A40 48GB GPUs for Llama2.
  • Code: available at https://github.com/cdiego89phd/chat-reranking
  • Modifications: for our exposure-frequency question, replace the genre-diversity objective with per-item popularity/frequency covariates and correlate LLM re-rank positions with item exposure proxies (e.g., dataset popularity, which the RQ1 study shows the LLM tracks at RBO 0.5–0.8).
  • Unknowns: missing link — whether the "lower-ranked items drawn by LLMs" effect (Table 5) is driven by item popularity in pretraining rather than list position; the paper does not decompose this.
Follow-up Papers관련 후속 논문
  • Hou et al., "Large Language Models are Zero-Shot Rankers for Recommender Systems" (ECIR 2024) — the closest relevance-ranking counterpart; documents position bias and popularity bias in LLM rankers, directly relevant to SP3.
  • Dai et al., "Uncovering ChatGPT's Capabilities in Recommender Systems" (2023) — ChatGPT ranking of unordered candidate sets; baseline for LLM-as-ranker behavior.
  • Sun et al., "Is ChatGPT Good at Search? Investigating LLMs as Re-Ranking Agents" (2023) — IR-side re-ranking methodology this paper's prompt/output format follows.
  • Li et al., "A Preliminary Study of ChatGPT on News Recommendation" (2023) — handles a relevance-vs-fairness (popular/unpopular item groups) trade-off via prompting, the closest existing exposure-frequency-aware LLM re-ranking instruction design.
Condition Delta — transfer to our setting조건 차이 — 우리 설정으로의 전이

Paper's setting: inputs = MF-generated relevance-ranked candidate list ($m$=40/50) of anime/book titles; outputs = re-ranked top-10; conditions = zero-shot prompting, genre-based diversity metrics, 500 test users, 2023-era LLMs, items identified by natural-language titles the LLM plausibly saw in pretraining (post-2021/2018 items filtered out to ensure exposure). Our setting (SP3, exposure-frequency-as-ranking-proxy): we ask whether an item's exposure frequency in the LLM's training corpus acts as an implicit ranking prior when LLMs rank/re-rank, and whether diversity objectives interact with that popularity prior. Differences:

  • The paper deliberately maximizes item exposure (filters unfamiliar items) rather than measuring exposure-frequency effects: missing link — no per-item popularity/frequency analysis of LLM ranking behavior.
  • Popularity signal is only probed in the informal RQ1 study (RBO 0.5–0.8 for popularity re-ranking): reference only — usable as evidence LLMs encode popularity, but not a controlled measurement.
  • Diversity objective (genre coverage) vs our exposure/frequency axis: resolved — the paper supplies the O1 diversity-objective baseline behavior (LLMs sacrifice relevance for diversity, drawing lower-ranked = less "relevant-popular" items) against which exposure effects can be contrasted.
  • Fixed upstream recommender (MF) supplies relevance; LLM never scores relevance itself: reference only for our setting where the LLM's own ranking prior is the object of study.
Root-cause vs. Symptom근본 원인 대 증상

Symptom — the paper diversifies lists post-hoc over a fixed recommender and patches LLM failure modes (hallucinated titles) by random-filling, without addressing either why the base recommender under-diversifies or why LLMs hallucinate/degrade on less-exposed domains (the Goodreads gap is left as speculation); as an honest feasibility benchmark this is by design, but no underlying mechanism is resolved.
