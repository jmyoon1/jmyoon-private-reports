Conclusion

Frequency-only control of an immutable scorer is feasible, production-validated, and in one respect axiomatically necessary — build a show/don't-show stack, denominate in reach and position-adjusted units, and run the exploration floor persistently. Every formal guarantee currently available is count-denominated; the seven gaps are exactly where the theory this setting needs does not yet exist.

Answers to Research Questions

결론

불변 scorer의 빈도 전용 제어는 실행 가능하고 production에서 검증되었으며 한 측면에서는 공리적으로 필요하다 — show/don't-show 스택을 구축하고, 목표를 reach와 position 조정 단위로 표기하며, 탐색 floor를 지속 가동하라. 현재 존재하는 모든 형식적 보장은 count 단위이고, 일곱 개의 gap은 이 설정이 필요로 하는 이론이 아직 없는 지점들이다.

연구 질문에 대한 답변

O1 (P1): Map the landscape of exposure-frequency techniques

The taxonomy is complete on both requested axes. By intervention mechanism: (i) within-query stochastic ranking policies — convex optimization over marginal rank probabilities realized as a distribution over rankings, which is an impression-frequency schedule ([01] FoE, [05] IncompleteExposure, [28] Expohedron); (ii) cross-query amortized ledger control — reactive dual-variable controllers, provably one algorithm family with ads pacing ([46] FairCo, [53] LongTermConstraints, [04] PMMF), succeeded by future-aware planners ([52] FARA); (iii) capping = deterministic exposure ceilings with tight 3/4 and 1−1/e competitive ratios ([80] FreqCapTheory, [70] SoftFreqCap); (iv) boosting/floors = guaranteed-display contracts, inclusion floors, and cold-start budgets ([13] FairRec, [92] RepurposeLongTail, [62] ItemCentricExploration); (v) bandit exposure allocation, split between merit-proportional targets that replace the scorer and score-free quota floors ([55] FairExposureBandits, [66] FairSMAB); (vi) exposure-aware re-ranking, unified as taxation ([32] TaxRank). By controlled objective: fairness, diversity, calibration, popularity debiasing (four defective reference-distribution classes, [89] CalibSurvey), and ecosystem/creator welfare — where [104] StrategicProviders proves withholding exposure is axiomatically required for a stable creator ecosystem, giving the survey's core object a normative foundation.

O1 (P1): exposure-빈도 기법의 지형도

taxonomy는 요청된 두 축 모두에서 완결되었다. 개입 메커니즘 축: (i) 질의 내 확률적 ranking 정책(주변 순위 확률 위 볼록 최적화 — 그 자체가 impression-frequency 스케줄), (ii) 질의 간 amortized ledger 제어(반응형 dual 제어기 — ads pacing과 하나의 알고리즘 계열 — 와 미래 인지 planner), (iii) capping(tight 3/4·1−1/e 경쟁비의 결정론적 exposure 상한), (iv) boosting/floor(보장 표시 계약, 포함 floor, cold-start 예산), (v) bandit exposure 배분(scorer를 대체하는 merit 비례 목표와 score-free quota floor로 양분), (vi) taxation으로 통일된 exposure 인지 re-ranking. 제어 목표 축: 공정성, 다양성, calibration, popularity debiasing(네 가지 결함 있는 기준 분포 계열), 그리고 생태계/creator 후생 — StrategicProviders는 안정적 creator 생태계에 exposure 보류(show/don't-show)가 공리적으로 필요함을 증명하여 조사의 핵심 대상에 규범적 기초를 부여한다.

O2 (P1): Which techniques survive the strict constraint set

Every surveyed technique carries an access label, and the strict frequency-only (show/don't-show) subset is explicit and larger than the classical literature suggests: FairRec inclusion allocation (EF1/MMS on counts, [13] FairRec); TaxRank/ElasticRank, whose decision variable is top-K inclusion via an item-level exposure vector e* with the first predictable-cost guarantees — Lipschitz knob and POT bound ([32] TaxRank, [34] ElasticityTradeoff); FairSync retrieval-stage dual floors at billion scale ([48] FairSync); Fair-Learn's deterministic anytime count floors over any black-box learner ([66] FairSMAB); hard frequency caps ([80] FreqCapTheory); and production systems TEC ([20] SpotWorkTEC), InvR ([92] RepurposeLongTail), item-centric exploration ([62] ItemCentricExploration), and HMT ([99] WelfareCompetingCreators), plus the Shapley mediator as a score-read-only stable display rule ([104] StrategicProviders). Two structural results sharpen feasibility: the realization pipeline (target → ranking distribution → delivery) is score-free given the target, so the immutability constraint binds only on target selection ([28] Expohedron, RQ4.2); and no per-item re-scoring can reach the optimal utility–fairness frontier for composition-dependent objectives ([50] ScoringNotEnough), which structurally elevates list-construction and frequency-level control over the entire re-scoring family. The binding caveats — count-vs-position denomination, unvalidated position-bias curves, dynamic immutability — are recorded as O4 gaps, not coverage holes.

O2 (P1): 엄격한 제약 하에 생존하는 기법

조사된 모든 기법이 접근 수준 라벨을 갖고, 엄밀한 빈도 전용(show/don't-show) 부분집합이 명시적으로 확정되었다: FairRec(count 기준 EF1/MMS), TaxRank/ElasticRank(item 수준 exposure 벡터 e*로 top-K 포함 결정, Lipschitz knob과 POT 상한이라는 최초의 예측 가능 비용 보장), FairSync(10억 규모 retrieval 단계 dual floor), Fair-Learn(임의 black-box 학습기 위 결정론적 anytime count floor), hard frequency cap, 그리고 production 시스템 TEC·InvR·item 중심 탐색·HMT와 Shapley mediator. 두 구조적 결과가 지도를 날카롭게 한다: 실현 파이프라인(목표→ranking 분포→전달)은 목표가 주어지면 score-free이므로 불변성 제약은 목표 선택에만 구속되고, 어떤 per-item re-scoring도 합성 의존적 목표의 최적 frontier에 도달할 수 없어(ScoringNotEnough) 빈도 수준 제어가 구조적으로 우위에 선다. 구속력 있는 주의 사항(count 대 position 단위, 미검증 position-bias 곡선, 동적 immutability)은 커버리지 구멍이 아니라 O4 gap으로 기록되었다.

O3 (P1): The 2024–2026 frontier

Every classical line has named 2025–2026 advances, and the frontier's central finding is that scores deserve less trust, not more: LLM/generative scorers violate the trusted-scores assumption structurally (prompt-position sensitivity, pre-training content priors, gradient starvation amplified by tokenization — unremoved by scaling 0.6B→8B; [18] LLMPositionBias, [22] EchoesFilterBubble, [23] CRAB), and the strongest cures go white-box, away from the user's constraint ([16] FairLLMRec). The frequency-level line, by contrast, gained production validation: TEC's exposure-debited eligibility thresholds raised the platform's true objective while engagement stayed flat ([20] SpotWorkTEC); Meta's VRS audit showed impression-parity compliance with −9.82% unique reach — and naive per-group budget-splitting, a pure frequency intervention, dominated the score-side fix ([74] MetaVRSEval). Two new lines emerged via spawned SPs: LLM-era exposure feedback loops (hallucination contamination, incremental-fine-tuning acceleration, generation-space support collapse; 3-month production A/B, [115] BanditDriftLLMRec) and strategic stability of exposure rules (concavity boundary, [122] PropRankingConvergence). One field experiment changed the survey's framing: a temporary exposure boost durably retrained the production recommender, inverting its goal (+14.75%, persistent) — immutability fails dynamically ([78] FeedInterventionFieldExp).

O3 (P1): 2024–2026 frontier

모든 고전 라인이 2025–2026년의 진전을 명명받았고, frontier의 중심 발견은 score를 '더'가 아니라 '덜' 신뢰해야 한다는 것이다: LLM/생성형 scorer는 trusted-scores 가정을 구조적으로 위반하며(prompt 위치 민감성, pre-training 내용 prior, tokenization으로 증폭되는 gradient 결핍 — 스케일링으로 제거 불가), 가장 강한 치료법은 white-box로 가서 사용자 제약에서 멀어진다. 반면 빈도 수준 라인은 production 검증을 얻었다: TEC의 exposure 차감 eligibility 임계값은 engagement 불변으로 플랫폼의 진짜 목표를 올렸고, Meta VRS 감사는 impression parity 준수와 고유 도달 −9.82%를 — 그리고 순수 빈도 개입인 집단별 예산 분할이 score 측 수정을 지배함을 — 보였다. 스폰된 SP를 통해 두 신생 라인(LLM 시대 exposure feedback loop; exposure 규칙의 전략적 안정성)이 확보되었다. 한 필드 실험이 조사의 프레임을 바꿨다: 일시적 exposure boost가 production recommender를 지속적으로 재학습시켜 목표를 반전시켰다 — immutability는 동적으로 실패한다.

Recommended Approach — the six-step frequency-only control stack
  1. Choose a merit-blind or one-sided exposure target — define targets as floors/ceilings/proportions (minimum inclusion counts, per-user frequency caps, catalog-share floors), never “exposure ∝ score”. robustness splits by target: merit-blind constraints never reference the unvalidatable black-box scores, so constraint satisfaction survives arbitrary score error.
  2. Adopt the two-stage architecture: item-level exposure vector e* → eligibility/OT assignment — compute e* by progressive taxation or directly from the floors; assign via eligibility thresholds (TEC pattern) or Sinkhorn OT (TaxRank pattern) — never additive per-item score boosts. e* is literally a per-item impression budget, and this is the only architecture with predictable-cost guarantees (Lipschitz knob, POT bound); additive boosts are proven non-Lipschitz and re-scoring is Pareto-capped anyway.
  3. Enforce with closed-loop dual-variable pacing over a cumulative exposure ledger — maintain per-item/group cumulative delivered-exposure counters; run Dual Mirror Descent for budgets/ceilings and a Fair-Learn debt-priority override for hard floors. every finite-T realized-exposure guarantee in the survey comes from closed-loop ledger feedback (DMD O(√T); deterministic anytime floors; 3/4 and 1−1/e cap ratios) — and fairness dual targeting, ads pacing, and virtual queues are one algorithm family.
  4. Denominate targets in unique-user reach and position-adjusted units — set budgets over unique users reached; log a position-adjusted ledger (debit γ(k) per impression at slot k) alongside the count ledger; estimate the position-bias curve via low-impact swaps. impression-count parity is satisfiable by repeat exposure to the same users — Meta's VRS passed its impression metric while cutting unique reach 9.82%; equal counts are also not equal value.
  5. Run the exploration floor persistently and evaluate everything closed-loop — reserve a constant fraction of impressions as a relevance-targeted, scorer-independent exploration floor that never switches off; evaluate with pre/during/post windows and a held-out unbiased slice. the exposure knob writes the scorer's future training data; four theory lines prove a constant loop-independent fraction bounds drift at α⁻¹ while a vanishing fraction accumulates error linearly — yet every deployed floor switches off.
  6. Treat the intervention layer as a mechanism facing strategic creators — aggregate floor/boost budgets at creator level; gate “new item” predicates with near-duplicate detection; audit beneficiaries with cumulative promise-vs-delivery statistics; prefer proportional/smooth exposure splitting. a floor is a guaranteed exposure payout for a cheaply-satisfiable predicate — exactly the allocation shape Sybil-proofness theory proves splitting-attractive; no strategy-proof intervention exists to adopt (gap 4), so these are mitigations.
권장 접근법 — 6단계 빈도 전용 제어 스택
  1. merit-blind 또는 one-sided exposure 목표를 선택한다 — 목표를 floor/ceiling/비율(최소 포함 횟수, 사용자별 빈도 cap, catalog 점유 floor)로 정의하고 “exposure ∝ score”는 피한다. 강건성은 목표에 따라 갈린다: merit-blind 제약은 검증 불가능한 black-box score를 참조하지 않으므로 제약 만족이 임의의 score 오차를 견딘다.
  2. 2단계 구조를 채택한다: item 수준 exposure 벡터 e* → eligibility/OT 배정 — e*를 누진 과세나 1단계의 floor에서 직접 계산하고, eligibility 임계값(TEC 패턴)이나 Sinkhorn OT(TaxRank 패턴)로 배정한다 — item별 가산 score boost는 금지. e*가 곧 item별 impression 예산이며, 이것이 예측 가능 비용 보장(Lipschitz knob, POT 상한)을 가진 유일한 구조다; 가산 boost는 비Lipschitz가 증명되었고 re-scoring은 어차피 Pareto 천장에 막힌다.
  3. 누적 exposure ledger 위의 폐루프 dual 변수 pacing으로 집행한다 — item/집단별 누적 전달 exposure 카운터를 유지하고, 예산/상한에는 Dual Mirror Descent를, hard floor에는 Fair-Learn debt-priority override를 가동한다. 조사의 모든 유한 T 실현 exposure 보장이 폐루프 ledger feedback에서 나온다(DMD O(√T), 결정론적 anytime floor, 3/4·1−1/e cap 경쟁비) — 그리고 공정성 dual targeting, ads pacing, 가상 큐는 하나의 알고리즘 계열이다.
  4. 목표를 고유 사용자 도달과 position 조정 단위로 표기한다 — 예산을 도달한 고유 사용자 기준으로 설정하고, count ledger 옆에 position 조정 ledger(슬롯 k의 impression마다 γ(k) 차감)를 기록하며, 저영향 swap으로 position-bias 곡선을 추정한다. impression count parity는 같은 사용자에게 반복 노출하는 것으로도 만족된다 — Meta VRS는 impression 지표를 통과하면서 고유 도달을 9.82% 깎았다; 같은 count가 같은 가치도 아니다.
  5. 탐색 floor를 지속적으로 가동하고 모든 것을 폐루프로 평가한다 — impression의 상수 비율을 relevance 표적·scorer 독립 탐색 floor로 예약하고 절대 끄지 않는다; pre/during/post 창과 held-out unbiased 슬라이스로 평가한다. exposure knob은 scorer의 미래 학습 데이터를 쓴다; 네 이론 라인이 상수 비율의 루프 독립 데이터가 드리프트를 α⁻¹로 묶고 소멸 비율은 오차를 선형 누적시킴을 증명한다 — 그런데 배포된 모든 floor는 꺼진다.
  6. 개입 레이어를 전략적 creator를 상대하는 mechanism으로 다룬다 — floor/boost 예산을 creator 계정 수준에서 합산하고, “신규 item” 술어에 근접 중복 탐지를 걸고, 누적 약속 대비 전달 통계로 수혜자를 감사하며, 비례/평활 exposure 분할을 선호한다. floor는 값싸게 만족 가능한 술어에 대한 보장된 exposure 지급 — Sybil-proofness 이론이 분할 유인적이라 증명한 바로 그 배분 형태다; 채택할 strategy-proof 개입은 존재하지 않으므로(gap 4) 이는 보장이 아닌 완화책이다.

Method: a layered show/don't-show frequency-control stack over the untouched scorer — target selection (merit-blind or one-sided), two-stage realization (item-level exposure vector → eligibility/assignment), closed-loop dual-paced enforcement, correct denomination, persistent exploration floor, and gaming defenses. Concretely:

  1. Choose a merit-blind or one-sided exposure target; do not build a merit-proportional target from the black-box scores.

Action: define the target as floors/ceilings/proportions (minimum inclusion counts per item/group, per-user frequency caps, catalog-share floors) rather than "exposure ∝ score." Reason: robustness to score corruption splits by target, not by method — merit-blind targets never reference scores in the constraint, so constraint satisfaction survives arbitrary score error; merit-proportional targets import score error into the definition of "fair" ([37] FairRankingSurvey, [14] FairPGRank, KB uuid 12), and the black-box scores cannot be validated (that is gap 5). Assumption: the platform can state its objective as floors/ceilings; if exposure-proportional-to-merit is genuinely required, one-sided caps (only limit above-merit exposure, [14] FairPGRank) are the fallback. Failure mode: merit-blind floors over-penalize genuinely relevant items when relevance decays steeply — the utility cost is then governed by the elasticity mechanism ([34] ElasticityTradeoff); mitigate with a per-user utility floor (binary search on the fairness weight, [09] FairSort) and validate the empirical cost curve before committing.

  1. **Adopt the two-stage architecture: solve an item-level exposure vector e*, then assign to users by eligibility/OT — never additive per-item score boosts.**

Action: compute e* by progressive (utility-difference) taxation or directly from the floors of step 1; assign via eligibility thresholds (TEC pattern) or Sinkhorn OT (TaxRank pattern). Reason: e* is a per-item impression budget — the frequency-only decision variable — and this is the only architecture in the survey with predictable-cost guarantees for inclusion-level control (Lipschitz knob, POT ≤ 1 − O(|U|^{−t/(1+t)}), [32] TaxRank); item-level additive score boosts are proven non-Lipschitz with unbounded, erratic accuracy cost ([32] TaxRank Thm 2), and per-item re-scoring is Pareto-suboptimal for composition-dependent objectives anyway ([50] ScoringNotEnough). Assumption: top-K inclusion rights exist (the stack decides who gets shown; within-list ordering stays score-driven). Failure mode: if even inclusion control is unavailable at ranking time, move the enforcement point to stage-1 retrieval — FairSync-style dual-price floors, which are also necessary for any downstream guarantee ([48] FairSync).

  1. Enforce with closed-loop dual-variable pacing over a cumulative exposure ledger — not open-loop sampling.

Action: maintain per-item/group cumulative delivered-exposure counters; run Dual Mirror Descent (probabilistic throttling with a deficit-updated dual price) for budgets/ceilings and a Fair-Learn debt-priority override for hard floors. Reason: every finite-T realized-exposure guarantee in the survey comes from closed-loop ledger feedback, never from open-loop concentration (KB uuid 18): DMD gives O(√T) adherence regret over heterogeneous live traffic ([71] PercentilePacing), Fair-Learn gives deterministic anytime count floors (⌊r_i t⌋ − N_{i,t} ≤ α at every round, learner-independent, O(1)/round, [66] FairSMAB), hard caps are anytime ceilings with 3/4 and 1−1/e worst-case value bounds ([80] FreqCapTheory). And this is one algorithm family, not two: fairness dual targeting = ads pacing = drift-plus-penalty queues (KB uuid 6), so the ads literature's production hardening (unsmoothness index, percentile-space duals) transfers directly. Assumption: quotas are feasible (Σr_i < 1) and small enough that natural traffic can absorb them — floors are free until the quota exceeds an item's organic exposure, after which the price is linear in the excess ([66] FairSMAB Thm 4). Failure mode: i.i.d.-arrival assumptions in the regret proofs break under drift/endogenous traffic; mitigate with percentile-space duals ([71] PercentilePacing) and treat the deterministic floor override (which needs no distributional assumption) as the safety layer.

  1. Denominate targets in unique-user reach and position-adjusted units; audit position-weighted exposure separately from count compliance.

Action: set per-group/per-item budgets over unique users reached, not raw impressions; log a position-adjusted ledger (debit γ(k) per impression at slot k, the [72] LinkedInJobsPacing practice) alongside the count ledger; estimate the position-bias curve once via low-impact swap interventions, over-estimating small propensities ([43] UnbiasedLTR). Reason: impression-count parity is satisfiable by repeat exposure to the same users — Meta's VRS passed its impression-variance metric while cutting unique reach 9.82% and leveling down; a naive per-group frequency-budget split dominated the score-side fix on reach and cost for every group ([74] MetaVRSEval). Equal counts are also not equal value: match quality dominates quantity (random slot-filling: +181% exposure, ~0% CTR gain; relevance-targeted: +261–300% CTR, [92] RepurposeLongTail, [62] ItemCentricExploration). Assumption: the position-adjusted ledger is a monitoring/accounting layer; no theory yet guarantees it (gap 2). Failure mode: if the position-weighted audit shows count-satisfied floors moving no attention, tighten within-slate placement rules (insertion at fixed visible slots, InvR pattern) — and treat this as gap 2 evidence worth publishing.

  1. Run the exploration floor persistently and evaluate everything closed-loop — the scores are only statically immutable.

Action: reserve a constant fraction of impressions as a relevance-targeted, scorer-independent exploration floor that never switches off; normalize per-round budgets by the position-weighted cumulative ledger (DM^dynamic pattern, [95] DynamicExposure); evaluate interventions with pre/during/post windows and a held-out unbiased slice. Reason: the exposure knob writes the scorer's future training data — a temporary boost durably retrained a production recommender and inverted its goal ([78] FeedInterventionFieldExp); amplification compounds mechanically (θ^t, [86] FeedbackLoop); and four independent theory lines prove a constant fraction of loop-independent data bounds the drift (at α^{-1}) while a vanishing fraction accumulates error linearly (KB uuid 29, [111] RecursiveStability, [112] IterativeRetrainStability). Every deployed floor in the survey switches off (InvR, TEC, cold-start boosts) — forfeiting exactly the property the theory says matters. Memoryless per-round-fair constraints also fail cumulatively inside a retrain loop ([95] DynamicExposure). The coupling is exploitable too: temporary relevance-targeted floors kick-start tail items into organic recommendation (+18% unique items, [92] RepurposeLongTail). Assumption: the platform retrains the scorer on interaction logs (if the scorer is truly frozen forever, this step reduces to monitoring); the generation→reweighting theory transfer is unproven (gap 3), so α is a principled default, not a certified threshold. Failure mode: loop-blind offline metrics will certify a degrading system (relative metrics show ~100% accuracy while true accuracy collapses, [113] FairnessFeedbackLoops); if the scorer is an LLM/generative recommender, add catalog-validity filtering on logs and expect that a serving-side floor cannot re-expose an item the policy no longer generates ([115] BanditDriftLLMRec) — support collapse needs training-side anchoring, outside the frequency knob.

  1. Treat the intervention layer as a mechanism facing strategic creators.

Action: aggregate floor/boost budgets at creator/account level rather than per item; gate "new item" boost predicates with near-duplicate detection; audit boost beneficiaries with cumulative promise-vs-delivery statistics (the OptGTM pattern); run frequency-anomaly detection on the engagement signals any adaptive reweighting consumes. Reason: a floor is a guaranteed payout for a cheaply-satisfiable predicate, and per-unit floors are exactly the allocation shape Sybil-proofness theory proves splitting-attractive — total guaranteed exposure linear in reported unit count ([121] SybilProofMech, [118] CostOfSybils); the attack literature shows the input signals are forgeable and defenses lag ([124] PUDA, [125] RecSysPoisoningSurvey). Also keep the base allocation on the stable side of the concavity boundary (prefer proportional/smooth exposure splitting over winner-take-all; [122] PropRankingConvergence, [104] StrategicProviders). Assumption: no strategy-proof frequency intervention exists to adopt (gap 4) — these are mitigations, not guarantees. Failure mode: identity aggregation fails under true Sybil registration; re-entry defeats grim-trigger audits. If floor-farming is observed, shrink per-creator total floor mass sublinearly in unit count (pie-shrinking form), accepting the per-unit guarantee decay it causes.

Evidence (core recommendations to papers):

  • [20] SpotWorkTEC: production field experiment — frequency-only eligibility control over an unmodified scorer raised the true objective (+9.0 matches/prefecture-day) with engagement flat; the pattern step 2 generalizes.
  • [32] TaxRank / [34] ElasticityTradeoff: the only inclusion-level controllers with predictable-cost guarantees (Lipschitz, POT bound; elasticity theory forecasting the utility cost of any target).
  • [71] PercentilePacing / [66] FairSMAB / [80] FreqCapTheory: the enforcement layer's guarantees — O(√T) DMD adherence at billion-request scale; deterministic anytime floors over any black-box learner; tight competitive ratios for hard ceilings.
  • [48] FairSync: retrieval-stage floors at <0.25 ms overhead, and the proof that stage-1 inclusion is necessary for any downstream exposure guarantee.
  • [74] MetaVRSEval: why the denomination in step 4 is load-bearing — impression parity with −9.82% reach in production, budget-splitting dominating the score-side intervention.
  • [90] CalibBandits / [94] CDN / [89] CalibSurvey: the double-win evidence — exposure reallocation over a popularity-miscalibrated scorer improved accuracy in five independent online/field tests, so the expected cost of this stack is lower than offline curves suggest.
  • [78] FeedInterventionFieldExp / [111] RecursiveStability / [112] IterativeRetrainStability: why step 5's floor is persistent — field evidence of durable retraining plus the constant-α stabilization theorems.
  • [104] StrategicProviders: the normative anchor — only an incomplete-display (show/don't-show) mediator can be simultaneously fair, stable, and efficient for strategic creators.

Conditions under which this holds:

  • Static immutability approximately holds over each control window (the scorer is not retrained mid-window), with step 5 handling the cross-window coupling.
  • Top-K inclusion (or retrieval-pool) rights exist; within-list ordering may remain entirely score-driven.
  • Quotas/ceilings are modest relative to organic exposure (the "floors are free until binding" regime), and the relevance distribution is not degenerate-steep (flatness law).
  • Targets are expressible as counts/reach/inclusion — the units frequency control provably delivers.

Key assumption: count/reach-denominated control, with score-driven within-list ordering, moves position-weighted attention in the intended direction. This is empirically supported everywhere (TEC, InvR, HMT, budget-splitting) but formally unproven — it is exactly gap 2, the count→position transfer.

Invalidation criterion: a position-weighted exposure audit showing that satisfied count floors leave attention shares unmoved (or moved perversely), or a post-intervention drift audit showing the retraining loop inverting the intervention within the control horizon. Either finding invalidates the count-denominated stack as specified; the response is not to abandon frequency control (no alternative exists under the constraint set — score modification is ruled out by assumption and re-scoring is capped by [50] ScoringNotEnough) but to move enforcement inside the slate (fixed visible insertion slots) and shorten retraining windows while gaps 2 and 3 remain open.

Testable Hypothesis

Based on the SP2/SP3/SP5/SP7 DIR evidence ([20] SpotWorkTEC, [32] TaxRank, [48] FairSync, [71] PercentilePacing, [74] MetaVRSEval, [90] CalibBandits), we hypothesize that **a frequency-only control stack — a reach-denominated per-item exposure target e*, enforced by eligibility floors/ceilings at retrieval plus DMD-paced impression budgets with a closed-loop cumulative ledger — attains its exposure targets within O(√T) count-denominated regret at zero or positive engagement cost on a production black-box scorer**, under the conditions that the base scorer is popularity-miscalibrated in the corrected direction (the adjudicated double-win mechanism, KB uuid 13) and the relevance distribution is not steeply decaying (the flatness law, KB uuid 5).

This would be confirmed if an online A/B shows target-exposure attainment (cumulative ledger within tolerance) with flat-or-positive true-objective metrics, replicating the TEC/CalibBandits/HMT pattern. This would be refuted if (a) a position-weighted exposure audit shows attention unmoved behind satisfied count floors (gap 2 biting), or (b) engagement cost exceeds the elasticity-predicted transfer ratio (KB uuid 5), or (c) post-intervention scorer drift inverts the intervention's direction within the control horizon (gap 3 biting, the [78] FeedInterventionFieldExp failure mode).

검증 가능한 가설

SP2/SP3/SP5/SP7의 DIR 증거에 기반해 다음을 가설로 세운다: 빈도 전용 제어 스택 — 도달(reach) 단위의 item별 exposure 목표 e*를 retrieval 단계 eligibility floor/ceiling과 폐루프 누적 ledger를 갖춘 DMD pacing impression 예산으로 집행 — 은 production black-box scorer 위에서 engagement 비용 0 또는 양(+)으로, count 단위 O(√T) regret 내에 exposure 목표를 달성한다. 조건: 기저 scorer가 교정 방향으로 popularity miscalibrated일 것(double-win 메커니즘), relevance 분포가 가파르게 감쇠하지 않을 것(flatness 법칙). 온라인 A/B에서 누적 ledger가 허용 오차 내로 목표를 달성하고 진짜 목표 지표가 유지·상승하면 확증(TEC/CalibBandits/HMT 패턴 재현); (a) position 가중 감사에서 count floor 만족에도 attention이 움직이지 않거나(gap 2), (b) engagement 비용이 탄력성 예측 전이 비율을 초과하거나, (c) 개입 후 scorer 드리프트가 제어 지평 내에서 개입 방향을 반전시키면(gap 3, [78]의 실패 모드) 반증된다.

Ranked by (1) how directly each blocks the recommended approach for the user's setting, (2) number of SPs exposing it. Each is stated precisely enough to seed a research project, with its completed-search evidence.

  1. The degenerate regime: exposure-only control over fixed, noisy, exogenous scores — exposed by SP6, SP7, SP8 — blocks O2's guarantee layer [P1]

Problem: design an exposure-frequency-only allocator with regret/constraint-violation guarantees when merit is a fixed, immutable score with bounded (or adversarial) error — the user's literal setting. Why open: with trusted scores the bandit problem degenerates (the merit-proportional target π* ∝ f(score) is closed-form, [55] FairExposureBandits); with untrusted scores every method replaces the scorer with its own estimates; ads pacing assumes known values or self-estimated pCTR ([80] FreqCapTheory, [71] PercentilePacing); debiasing either retrains or trusts ([88] CausalEPP, [84] CalibratedRec). Completed search spans three literatures (KB uuid 23, O4-FINAL). Seed: a regret/violation analysis where merit is an exogenous score with error bound ε and the only decision variable is impression allocation; the FairX necessity conditions (γ, L) are the natural starting sensitivity parameters.

  1. Count→position-weighted transfer for frequency-only control — exposed by SP1, SP3, SP5, SP6, SP7 (five literatures) — blocks the meaning of every feasible guarantee [P1]

Problem: no guarantee anywhere relates inclusion/impression counts (the only units frequency-only control provably delivers) to position-weighted exposure (the unit that measures attention). Sharpest form: the position-weighted delivery ledger already exists as undisclosed engineering practice — LinkedIn Jobs debits position-adjusted expected charges at impression time with zero theory ([72] LinkedInJobsPacing). Seed (two theorems): (a) a transfer bound "inclusion frequency f + score-driven within-slate ordering ⇒ position-weighted exposure ∈ [l(f), u(f)]" under a known bias curve; (b) a DMD pacing theorem where the per-impression debit is the position-bias weight γ(k) of the occupied slot, with regret/violation in the same units (KB uuid 22).

  1. Reweighting-loop stability: the exposure-denominated loop theorem — exposed by SP7, SP8, SP9, SP10 — blocks any long-horizon deployment [P1]

Problem: all loop-stability theory covers generation loops (synthetic samples re-enter training); the exposure knob creates a reweighting loop (biases which real interactions are observed), and the scorer demonstrably retrains on it ([78] FeedInterventionFieldExp, [86] FeedbackLoop). The theory's central lever — the self-influenced data fraction λ — is unobservable in a live recommender, and top-k selection breaks the Wasserstein-Lipschitz sensitivity the contraction proofs require ([112] IterativeRetrainStability, [111] RecursiveStability). Seed: re-derive the contraction/generalization analysis with an exposure-biased subsampling operator over a fixed catalog, the persistent floor fraction as the clean-data lever α, and error in position-weighted units (KB uuid 30). This is also where two channels (creator content reverts on removal; log contamination persists) await co-modeling.

  1. Strategy-proofness of frequency-level interventions (splitting-proof floors) — exposed by SP9, SP11 (dedicated three-literature sweep) — blocks deployment against strategic creators [P2]

Problem: a floor/boost converts a statistical attack surface into a contractual one — a guaranteed exposure payout for a cheaply-satisfiable predicate ("new item", "under-exposed item"), gameable by re-upload and content splitting. Sybil-proofness theory affirmatively predicts an impossibility shape (ex-post Sybil-proofness collapses monotone rules to second-price; resistance costs exponential welfare, [121] SybilProofMech, [118] CostOfSybils) but every theorem is payment-load-bearing and one-shot; the constructive mechanism that exists (OptGTM's promise-vs-delivery grim trigger, [123] StrategicBandits) is defeated by re-entry. Seed: does a payment-free, repeated, divisible exposure-floor mechanism exist that is splitting-proof, non-trivial per creator, and polynomial-welfare — exploiting the one asset auctions lack, repeated interaction with observable delivery history? Bayesian (population-level) splitting-proofness is the unexplored escape route (KB uuid 28). Residual check before absolute finality: the replication-proof bandit line (Esmaeili et al. 2023), surfaced via related work only, not ingested.

  1. Bayes-optimal exposure-fair post-processing over elicited black-box scores — exposed by SP2, SP3 (dedicated 15-paper pass) — reduces confidence in target selection [P2]

Problem: the classification result exists (elicited output probabilities are sufficient statistics for the optimal fair post-processor, [17] BlackBoxFairPost); its ranking/exposure analogue does not — every optimality statement in the post-processing literature (LP optimality, POT bound, elasticity transfer ratio) conditions on scores being the truth (KB uuid 10). Seed: a theorem identifying which statistic of a black-box scorer suffices for the optimal exposure-constrained ranking policy, with a regret bound under elicitation error.

  1. The pacing↔fairness dual reduction (a one-page-proof-shaped bridge paper) — exposed by SP1, SP4, SP5, SP6, SP7, SP9 — blocks guarantee transfer between the two richest literatures [P2]

Problem: ads budget pacing and fairness-side exposure targeting are the same algorithm — Dual Mirror Descent on a cumulative-deficit subgradient, differing only in constraint sign (budget ceiling vs exposure floor), denomination, and slot granularity ([71] PercentilePacing, [53] LongTermConstraints, [48] FairSync) — yet the two clusters have zero citation overlap after checks in SP7 and SP9, and no paper states the reduction (KB uuid 6). Seed: prove the sign-flip reduction, then port the ads side's drift-robustness devices (percentile-space duals, conformal forecast calibration) to exposure floors and the fairness side's slate/position structure to pacing.

  1. Position-weighted concentration for open-loop realization — exposed by SP3, SP4, SP5 — avoidable by architecture choice, so lowest priority [P2]

Problem: open-loop sampling from a target ranking distribution has no realized-vs-target concentration bound in exposure units; balanced-word delivery gives a deterministic ranking-frequency discrepancy (m = N−1 per window, [28] Expohedron) but no exposure-unit theorem, no heterogeneous-stream coverage. SP5/SP6 established that all existing finite-T realized-exposure bounds come from closed-loop ledger feedback, never concentration (KB uuid 18). Seed: a Hoeffding/discrepancy theorem "after T impressions, realized position-weighted exposure deviates from target by ≤ f(n, support, γ, T)" for sampled and stride-scheduled delivery. Practical note: using closed-loop control (recommended below) sidesteps this gap, which is why it ranks last.
