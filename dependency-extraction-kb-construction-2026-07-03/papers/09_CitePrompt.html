CitePrompt: Using Prompts to Identify Citation Intent in Scientific Papers

Lahiri et al. · JCDL 2023 · 2023 · 22 citations인용 22회

arXiv:2304.12730 · PDF · SP: SP1

P0Track A

Problem
해결하려는 문제

Prior citation-intent classifiers (Jurgens et al.'s feature-engineered classifier; Cohan et al.'s Structural Scaffolds multi-task BiLSTM+ELMo) require either heavy manual feature engineering or auxiliary scaffold tasks and large labeled corpora. No prior work had applied prompt-based learning to citation intent classification, and no prior work had addressed few-shot/zero-shot citation intent classification, despite labeled citation data being scarce.

인용 의도 분류는 라벨 데이터가 희소하여, 대규모 지도학습 없이 적응 가능한 방법이 필요하다.

Solution / Contribution
해결책 / 기여

Prompt-based learning with SciBERT. [X] It has a citation of type [MASK]. prefix prompt → SciBERT MLM → knowledgeable verbalizer (section-anchored label words expanded via cosine similarity over S2ORC section text, refined with KPT in low-data settings) maps [MASK] distribution to intent labels. Only base-task data + external KB (S2ORC) are used; no scaffold tasks, no explicit feature engineering.

prompt-learning과 knowledgeable verbalizer로 인용 의도 분류를 masked-token 예측으로 재구성하며, few/zero-shot을 최초로 시연하고 SciBERT 교체 시 ACL-ARC가 12점 하락함을 통해 도메인 사전학습의 결정성을 재확인한다.

Key Results
주요 결과
  • ACL-ARC fully-supervised: accuracy 78.42%, macro-F1 68.39% — new SOTA (vs Cohan et al. 67.9 F1, Jurgens et al. 54.6 F1).
  • SciCite fully-supervised: accuracy 87.56%, macro-F1 86.33% — beats all baselines except ImpactCite/XLNet (Mercier et al. 88.93 F1).
  • SciCite zero-shot: macro-F1 53.86% (accuracy 60.52%); 5-shot 63.61 F1; 10-shot 66.99 F1. (Note: abstract's "53.86% zero-shot" figures are SciCite, not ACL-ARC — ACL-ARC few/zero-shot gave very low results due to class imbalance.)
  • Ablation: replacing SciBERT with vanilla BERT drops ACL-ARC F1 by 12.02 pts (68.39→56.37) and SciCite F1 by 2.11 pts — domain-pretrained PLM is critical.
Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

Prompt-based learning for citation intent classification (CitePrompt framework)

  • What it computes: input citation sentence $x$ → wrapped by a manual template $T$ into a prompt $x_T$ containing a [MASK] token → SciBERT MLM produces a distribution over vocabulary for [MASK] → a verbalizer maps a subset $L_y$ of label words to each intent label $y \in Y$ → most probable label selected. Reformulates classification as conditional (masked) text generation.
  • Why it was created: prior SOTA (Cohan et al.'s Structural Scaffolds) needs multi-task training on auxiliary scaffold tasks and rich external structural/lexical features; CitePrompt removes that requirement, needing only base-task data plus an external KB for the verbalizer.
  • Concrete example: template [X] It has a citation of type [MASK].; for $x=$ "This task has been shown to have the same features as in [10]", target $y=$ "Background". A prefix prompt (mask at end), found to beat cloze prompts.
  • How it is used in this paper: the core method; evaluated fully-supervised on ACL-ARC and SciCite, plus few-shot/zero-shot on SciCite.

Knowledgeable verbalizer construction (section-anchored label-word expansion)

  • What it computes: for each label, choose a few anchor words → for each label's associated paper sections (Table 1 mapping, e.g. Background→Introduction/Related Work/Motivation) parse S2ORC text → compute cosine similarity between anchor words and section words → take top-100 closest words per (anchor, section) pair → union = label-word set $L_y$.
  • Why it was created: standard verbalizers use one/few label words with high subjective bias; broadening the label-word space with section-grounded words gives extensive coverage and captures document structure that signals citation intent.
  • Concrete example: anchor words for "Method" = {technique, procedure, method}; 100K words sampled per section from S2ORC CS papers; 100 keywords per (anchor, section) pair.
  • How it is used in this paper: builds the verbalizer that maps MLM output to intent labels in all settings.

KPT (Knowledgeable Prompt-tuning) refinement — reused from [Hu et al. 2022, ACL].

  • What it computes: four post-hoc optimizations on the expanded label-word set: (1) remove label words rare in the LM, (2) remove irrelevant label words, (3) contextual calibration of the predicted distribution, (4) assign a learnable weight per label word.
  • Why it was created: unsupervised label-word expansion introduces noise; in low-data (few/zero-shot) regimes there is no training signal to suppress it.
  • Concrete example: applied to the "Method" label-word set to drop out-of-vocabulary or off-topic words before prediction.
  • How it is used in this paper: added only for the few-shot and zero-shot settings on SciCite.

5Cs Analysis5Cs 분석

Category: new method (prompt-learning application to an existing task). Context: builds on OpenPrompt toolkit, KPT verbalizer (Hu et al. 2022), SciBERT (Beltagy et al. 2019); baselines against Jurgens et al. 2018, Cohan et al. 2019 (Structural Scaffolds), ImpactCite (Mercier et al. 2021). Directly relevant to SP1 (citation intent classification) and to LLM-adaptation concept group. Correctness: results averaged over 5 seeds; reports accuracy + macro-F1. No confidence intervals/error bars given. Ablations (SciBERT vs BERT, w/o scaffold comparison) support claims. Contributions: (1) first prompt-learning approach to citation intent classification; (2) SOTA on ACL-ARC without scaffold tasks; (3) first few-shot/zero-shot formulation of the task. Clarity: template, verbalizer procedure, datasets, and hyperparameters (seq len 512, batch 40, 5 epochs, OpenPrompt) are given; reproducible in principle. Verbalizer label-word lists not fully published.

Contribution vs. Claim기여 대 주장

  • Stated claims: better-than/comparable-to SOTA with much less exterior information; first prompt-learning + first few/zero-shot for the task.
  • Demonstrated: SOTA on ACL-ARC only; on SciCite it is beaten by ImpactCite. Few/zero-shot works only on SciCite (3 coarse labels); on ACL-ARC (6 labels) it "gave very low results" — so the few/zero-shot claim does not generalize to the finer-grained schema.
  • Gap: "less exterior information" is somewhat overstated — the verbalizer still consumes a large external corpus (S2ORC, 100K words/section) and a hand-built label→section mapping (Table 1), which is a different, not necessarily smaller, form of external knowledge than scaffold tasks.
Implementation Feasibility구현 실현가능성
  • Needs: SciBERT weights, OpenPrompt toolkit, S2ORC corpus access for verbalizer construction, ACL-ARC + SciCite datasets; modest compute (seq 512, batch 40, 5 epochs).
  • Code: no repository link stated in the paper; built on public OpenPrompt + KPT + SciBERT, so reconstructable.
  • Modifications: for our KB task, swap SciBERT for a modern LLM verbalizer/prompt, remap the label schema to derivation-lineage relations, and add a sentence-label→paper-edge aggregation step.
  • Unknowns: missing link — exact label-word lists / Table 1 mapping details not fully published; missing link — no code artifact confirmed.
Follow-up Papers관련 후속 논문
  • Prompting Strategies for Citation Classification (Kunnath et al., CIKM 2023) [ref 10] — parallel/contemporary prompting approach to the same task; direct comparison for SP1.
  • Knowledgeable Prompt-tuning / KPT (Hu et al., ACL 2022) — the verbalizer-refinement method reused here; needed to understand the few/zero-shot pipeline.
  • Structural Scaffolds for Citation Intent Classification (Cohan et al., NAACL 2019) — the SciCite dataset + prior SOTA baseline this paper unseats on ACL-ARC.
Condition Delta — transfer to our setting
조건 차이 — 우리 환경으로의 전이

Paper's setting: inputs = single citation sentence; outputs = one intent label from a fixed schema (ACL-ARC 6-way, SciCite 3-way); conditions = SciBERT MLM, OpenPrompt, S2ORC-derived verbalizer; supervised + few/zero-shot. Our setting: extract derivation-lineage dependencies between papers from reference lists (O1) — need to classify why a paper cites another (background / method-use / extension / comparison) as a dependency signal for KB construction. Differences:

  • Label schema: paper's ACL-ARC "Extends/Uses/Compare" labels map well onto our derivation-lineage intents — resolved: directly usable label vocabulary.
  • Output granularity: paper emits sentence-level intent, we need paper→paper dependency edges — missing link: aggregation from citation-sentence labels to a directed dependency relation not addressed here.
  • Model era: SciBERT MLM prompting vs modern LLM prompting (our "LLM adaptation" concept group) — reference only: CitePrompt predates instruction-tuned LLMs; approach is portable but not an LLM demonstration.
  • Data regime: few/zero-shot shown, matching our low-resource KB-construction constraint — resolved: provides adoptable low-resource recipe (but only validated on coarse 3-way schema).
