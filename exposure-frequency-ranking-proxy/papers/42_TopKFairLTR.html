Learning to Rank with Top-$K$ Fairness

Zhang et al. · arXiv · 2025

arXiv:2509.18067 · PDF · SP: SP4

P4Track B

한 줄 요약

top-K 공정성을 학습 목표에 넣는 learning-to-rank(TMLR 2025). 목표 재정의 계열의 2025년 사례.

Problem해결하려는 문제

In-processing fair learning-to-rank methods (DELTR, Zhu et al. 2020, Robust/Memarrast et al. 2023, MCFR) enforce exposure parity over the entire ranked list, but in top-$K$-driven decisions (interview shortlists, resource allocation to hotspots) only the head of the list receives any exposure — whole-list parity does not guarantee parity within top-$K$. Post-processing top-$K$ methods (FA*IR) exist but yield suboptimal relevance–fairness trade-offs and require group labels at test time. No prior in-processing framework targets top-$K$ exposure fairness with a convergence guarantee.

Solution / Contribution해결책 / 기여

A list-wise LTR objective: $\min_w \frac{1}{|S|}\sum_q L_q(w) + \frac{C}{N}\sum_q U_q^{topK}(w)$, where $L_q$ is an NDCG-surrogate or ListNet loss and $U_q^{topK}$ is the squared top-$K$ exposure disparity. The non-differentiable top-$K$ selection is converted to a differentiable threshold comparison via the $\lambda_q(w)$ formulation above, and efficient stochastic algorithms optimize the composite objective. Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

Top-$K$ exposure disparity

  • What it computes: model scores $h_q(x;w)$ over items in two groups → restrict the classic softmax-exposure average to items inside the top-$K$ set via an indicator $I(x_i^q \in S_K^q)$ → squared difference between the minority-group and majority-group averages of top-$K$-masked exposures (Eq. 9 of the paper).
  • Why it was created: existing list-wise exposure-disparity metrics (Singh & Joachims 2018; DELTR/Zehlike & Castillo 2020) average exposure over the entire ranked list, but in resource-allocation uses (hiring shortlists, disaster hotspots) only the top-$K$ positions matter, so whole-list parity can leave the top-$K$ segment unfair.
  • Concrete example: with exposure $e(w,x_i^q,S_q) = \exp(h_q(x_i^q;w)) / \sum_j \exp(h_q(x_j^q;w))$ (a softmax over scores, interpretable as the reciprocal of an exponential surrogate rank), only items whose score exceeds the $(K{+}1)$-th largest score contribute to each group's average.
  • How it is used in this paper: added as a squared-disparity regularizer $U_q(w)$ alongside an NDCG or ListNet ranking loss, weighted by trade-off parameter $C$, and optimized during training.

Differentiable top-$K$ selection via $\lambda_q(w)$

  • What it computes: scores of $N_q$ items → solves $\lambda_q(w) = \arg\min_\lambda \frac{K+\varepsilon}{N_q}\lambda + \frac{1}{N_q}\sum_i (h_q(x_i^q;w)-\lambda)_+$ (Eq. 10) → $\lambda_q(w)$ equals the $(K{+}1)$-th largest score, so top-$K$ membership becomes $I(h_q(x_i^q;w) - \lambda_q(w) > 0)$, then smoothed to be differentiable.
  • Why it was created: naive sorting for top-$K$ selection costs $O(n \log n)$ per query per step and is non-differentiable; older surrogates $\psi(K - \bar g(w;x,S_q))$ (Wu et al. 2009; Qin et al. 2010) stack two approximation errors (rank estimate + indicator smoothing). This single-threshold formulation, adapted from K-SONG (Qiu et al. 2022), reduces that error.
  • Concrete example: for $K=10$ over $N_q=10{,}000$ items, membership check reduces to a comparison against one learned scalar threshold $\lambda_q(w)$ per query rather than a full sort. [SKIM - verify optimization details in deep read]
  • How it is used in this paper: replaces the top-$K$ indicator inside the disparity metric, enabling stochastic optimization with a claimed provable convergence guarantee.

Softmax exposure $e(w,x,S_q)$ itself is the DELTR-style exposure (Zehlike & Castillo 2020) — reused, not new.

Follow-up Papers관련 후속 논문
  • K-SONG (Qiu et al., 2022) — source of the differentiable top-$K$ selection technique; relevance-only counterpart.
  • DELTR (Zehlike & Castillo, 2020) — the whole-list exposure-disparity in-processing baseline this paper generalizes.
  • FA*IR (Zehlike et al., 2017) — post-processing top-$K$ group-representation constraint; the contrasting top-$K$ fairness formulation.
Condition Delta — transfer to our setting조건 차이 — 우리 설정으로의 전이

Paper's setting: supervised list-wise LTR — inputs = query–item embeddings with graded relevance labels and binary group labels at training time; outputs = a scoring function $h_q(x;w)$; conditions = group labels needed only during training (in-processing), exposure defined as softmax over model scores, fairness enforced at top-$K$ positions. Our setting (SP4 — exposure/frequency as a ranking proxy): concerned with how exposure metrics behave as proxies for ranking quality/frequency effects rather than with training a fairness-constrained ranker.
