Impression Pacing for Jobs Marketplace at LinkedIn

Geyik et al. · CIKM 2020 · 2020 · 3 citations

link · SP: SP7

P3Track A

한 줄 요약

LinkedIn Jobs 마켓플레이스의 production impression pacing. position 조정된 기대 과금을 impression 시점에 차감하는 — 이론 없이 실무로만 존재하는 — position 가중 전달 ledger의 실증 사례(gap 2의 사금석).

Problem해결하려는 문제

In LinkedIn's promoted-jobs first-price auction, budget pacing was driven by realized click spend. Because clicks are sparse and delayed relative to impressions, spend measurement lags delivery decisions: jobs overshoot or undershoot their daily spend plans, budget is used unevenly across the day, and fewer distinct candidates are reached. Prior pacing work keyed on post-click charges provides no per-impression control signal.

Solution / Contribution해결책 / 기여

An impression-based spend computation system: every time a job is shown, the system assigns a projected charge that accounts for (a) the likelihood the member clicks the job and (b) recommender-system considerations such as the job's rank/position on the page. The pacing controller then paces against this expected-spend stream, making delivery decisions at impression granularity rather than click granularity. Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

Impression-based projected charge (expected spend accounting)

  • What it computes: at the moment a promoted job is shown (impressed), assigns a projected/expected charge amount = f(predicted click probability of the member for that job, recommender-specific factors such as the job's position within the page) → this expected amount is debited against the job's daily budget immediately, rather than waiting for an actual click. [SKIM - verify exact formula in deep read]
  • Why it was created: click-based spend accounting reacts too late — spend is lumpy and pacing controllers over/under-shoot because budget depletion is only observed after clicks arrive; jobs with high click demand exhaust budgets in bursts.
  • Concrete example: a job impressed at slot 3 with predicted CTR 2% and bid $1 would be pre-charged an expected $0.02 (position-adjusted) at impression time. [SKIM - illustrative; paper's exact numbers unverified]
  • How it is used in this paper: it is the core system change — the pacing controller consumes the impression-based expected-spend signal instead of realized click spend, yielding smoother budget delivery.

Budget pacing (spend-plan adherence)

  • What it computes: throttles a campaign's participation in auctions so cumulative spend tracks a preset intraday plan (e.g., even delivery) → input: remaining daily budget + spend rate; operation: adjust bid/eligibility; output: paced impression delivery. Standard computational-advertising concept; used here as the control loop the new spend signal feeds.
  • Why it was created: without pacing, budgets exhaust early in the day, missing later (possibly better-matched) candidates.
  • Concrete example: a $100/day job budget paced to spend ~$4.17/hour over 24 hours instead of exhausting by 9am.
  • How it is used in this paper: the existing LinkedIn pacing scheme is retrofitted from click-based to impression-based spend computation.

Condition Delta — transfer to our setting조건 차이 — 우리 설정으로의 전이

Paper's setting: inputs = member visits to job-recommendations page + promoted jobs with daily budgets and bids; outputs = paced impression allocation of promoted jobs; conditions = first-price auction, monetary budgets, production-scale two-sided jobs marketplace, click-probability model and position effects available. Our setting (SP7 — exposure/impression frequency as a ranking-side control knob): inputs = ranked item lists where exposure frequency itself is the resource to allocate; outputs = per-item impression budgets/pacing without necessarily any monetary auction; conditions = general recommender/ranking systems, exposure fairness or frequency-capping goals rather than advertiser ROI. Differences:

  • Monetary bid/budget in a first-price auction vs. non-monetary impression budgets: missing link — the paper's charge accounting presupposes a price; translating "projected charge" to a pure exposure-unit debit needs adaptation.
  • Advertiser-ROI objective vs. exposure-fairness/frequency objective: reference only — the pacing machinery is objective-agnostic, but the paper does not evaluate fairness outcomes.
  • Position-within-page adjustment of expected charge: resolved — directly reusable; position-weighted expected exposure is exactly the SP's exposure currency (rank-discounted impression counting).
  • Production LinkedIn infrastructure (real-time click-probability model, budget service): missing link — feasibility outside such infrastructure unclear.
Root-cause vs. Symptom근본 원인 대 증상

Mixed — it fixes the root cause of lumpy pacing (the control signal's granularity: click-delayed spend replaced by impression-time expected spend), but reconciling projected vs. realized charges and the deeper problem of allocating exposure optimally across competing jobs are handled by the existing pacing heuristics rather than re-solved.
