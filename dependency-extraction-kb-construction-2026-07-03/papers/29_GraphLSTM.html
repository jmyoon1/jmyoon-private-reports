Cross-Sentence N-ary Relation Extraction with Graph LSTMs

Peng et al. · TACL 2017 · 2017 · 538 citations인용 538회

arXiv:1708.03743 · PDF · SP: SP2

P0Track C

Problem
해결하려는 문제

Prior relation extraction focused on binary relations within a single sentence, tied to the shortest dependency path between two entities. High-value domains (precision medicine) require n-ary relations (e.g., a drug-gene-mutation ternary interaction) whose arguments are frequently spread across multiple sentences and are not expressed by any sentence alone. Feature-based and single-sentence neural methods cannot represent such tuples jointly, and syntactic/discourse structure across sentence boundaries is left unmodeled.

고가치 과학·생의학 관계는 문장 경계를 넘어 서술되어 단일 문장 추출로는 포착되지 않는다.

Solution / Contribution
해결책 / 기여

A general framework: encode the passage with a Graph LSTM over a document graph, extract per-entity contextual vectors, concatenate them, and classify with (logistic-regression) relation classifiers. Key mechanism — generalize the LSTM recurrence to graphs by giving each node one forget gate per predecessor edge:

$$i_t = \sigma\!\left(W_i x_t + \sum_{j\in P(t)} U_i^{m(t,j)} h_j + b_i\right),\quad f_{tj} = \sigma\!\left(W_f x_t + U_f^{m(t,j)} h_j + b_f\right)$$ $$c_t = i_t \odot \tilde{c}_t + \sum_{j\in P(t)} f_{tj}\odot c_j,\quad h_t = o_t \odot \tanh(c_t)$$

The cyclic document graph is partitioned into forward and backward DAGs so backprop runs like bidirectional chain LSTM (asynchronous updates) rather than expensive loopy propagation. Multi-task learning adds a classifier per sub-relation (e.g. drug-mutation) sharing the same Graph LSTM, pooling scarce n-ary supervision with more abundant binary signal.

문서 그래프 위의 Graph LSTM으로 문장 간 n-ary 관계를 추출하여 단일 문장 대비 3–5배 많은 상호작용을 포착함을 보인다.

Key Results
주요 결과
  • Drug-gene-mutation ternary, cross-sentence: Graph LSTM-FULL 80.7% vs. BiLSTM 80.1, CNN 78.1, Feature-Based 77.7 (Table 1).
  • Drug-mutation binary, cross-sentence: Graph LSTM-FULL 76.7%; Tree LSTM 75.9, BiLSTM-Shortest-Path 71.7 (loses 4-5 pts to parse noise) (Table 2).
  • Multi-task learning: ternary 80.7→82.0, drug-mutation 76.7→78.5 (Graph LSTM); also lifts BiLSTM (Table 3).
  • Cross-sentence extraction yields 3–5× more unique interactions than single-sentence at PubMed scale (57,033 vs 10,873 candidates); from only 59 curated triples the model extracts orders of magnitude more (Table 4).
  • GENIA gene-regulation binary: Graph LSTM 34.8 F1 (gold parses 35.8) vs. BiLSTM 33.0, Poon et al. 2015 33.2; gold parses give significant gain → syntax quality matters (Table 7).
  • Manual precision: 75% at p≥0.9, 64% at p≥0.5 (Table 6).
Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

Graph LSTM

  • What it computes: input = word embeddings of a text span + a document graph whose edges encode linear adjacency, syntactic dependencies, and discourse relations → operation = an LSTM recurrent unit generalized so each node may have multiple predecessors (one forget gate per predecessor edge, plus edge-typed weight matrices) → output = one contextual vector $h_t$ per word. Entity vectors are concatenated and fed to a relation classifier.
  • Why it was created: linear-chain LSTMs model only sequential context and ignore syntactic/discourse structure; tree LSTMs are locked to a parse tree. Neither handles the arbitrary graph of intra- and inter-sentential dependencies needed to link entities scattered across multiple sentences.
  • Concrete example: hidden dim = 150, edge-type embedding dim = 3, GloVe 100-d init. On the drug-gene-mutation ternary task, cross-sentence Graph LSTM-FULL reaches 80.7% accuracy vs. BiLSTM 80.1%.
  • How it is used in this paper: it is the core representation learner (Fig. 2) for cross-sentence n-ary relation classification in two biomedical settings (molecular tumor boards; GENIA genetic pathways).

Document graph

  • What it computes: input = a passage of ≤ K consecutive sentences → operation = build a graph with word nodes and typed edges (adjacent-word chain, Stanford syntactic dependencies, coreference, discourse) → output = the backbone over which the Graph LSTM is unrolled. For backprop it is partitioned into two DAGs (forward L→R + backward R→L).
  • Why it was created: unifies prior LSTM variants — restrict edges to adjacency → linear-chain LSTM; restrict to parse tree → tree LSTM — while allowing richer linguistic structure.
  • Concrete example: Fig. 1 links "L858E … EGFR gene" in sentence 1 to "gefitinib" in sentence 2 via a NEXTSENT edge plus dependency arcs, expressing a ternary interaction no single sentence states.
  • How it is used in this paper: adopted from Quirk & Poon (2017); determines what dependencies the Graph LSTM can exploit.

Edge-type parametrization (Full vs. Edge-Type Embedding)

  • What it computes: Full = a distinct weight matrix $U^{m(t,j)}$ per coarse edge type; Embedding = a low-dim edge-type embedding $e_j$ combined via tensor product $h_j \otimes e_j$ with 3-way tensors $U$ to produce a "typed hidden representation".
  • Why it was created: dozens of Stanford dependency labels would explode the parameter count under full parametrization; the embedding shares statistics across fine-grained edge types.
  • Concrete example: FULL slightly beats EMBED (80.7 vs. 80.6 ternary cross-sentence).
  • How it is used in this paper: two variants compared in all experiments; FULL used for the PubMed-scale extraction run.

5Cs Analysis5Cs 분석

Category: new method (neural architecture for relation extraction) Context: builds on Quirk & Poon (2017) (document graph, distant supervision, minimal-span candidates), tree LSTM (Tai et al. 2015), Miwa & Bansal (2016) stacked LSTMs; within this survey it is the foundational cross-sentence / document-level RE mechanism for SP2 (scientific concept & relation extraction). Precedes PURE/PL-Marker/DyGIE++ style span-based RE also in this survey. Correctness: 5-fold CV with document-level fold assignment (no train-test contamination); significance via McNemar's chi-square (p<0.05 ternary, p<0.01 GENIA); manual eval with 88% inter-annotator agreement. Solid. Automatic-eval labels are noisy (distant supervision) — acknowledged. Contributions: (1) first neural architecture for cross-sentence n-ary RE that jointly represents an entity tuple; (2) Graph LSTM generalizing chain/tree LSTMs with edge-typed gating; (3) multi-task learning over sub-relations + PubMed-scale distant-supervision extraction. Clarity: equations and hyperparameters given (Theano, hidden=150, edge-emb=3, GloVe 100d, SGD batch 8, lr 0.02, ≤30 epochs); dataset release promised. Reproducible from description.

Contribution vs. Claim기여 대 주장

Stated claims: general framework for cross-sentence n-ary RE; graph formulation subsumes chain/tree LSTMs; rich linguistic analysis improves extraction; multi-task with sub-relations helps; cross-sentence produces larger KBs. Demonstrated results: all supported with numbers on two biomedical domains. Larger-KB and multi-task claims clearly shown. Gap: — the "rich linguistic analysis helps" claim is only weakly demonstrated on the biomedical ternary task (Graph LSTM beats BiLSTM by <1 pt, and coreference/discourse edges gave no significant gain); the clear syntactic benefit appears only on GENIA where gold parses exist. So the value of graph structure is conditional on parse quality, narrower than the general framing suggests. Only biomedical domains evaluated (generality across domains asserted but not tested).

Implementation Feasibility구현 실현가능성
  • Needs: tokenizer + POS + Stanford dependency parser (CoreNLP/SPLAT), entity taggers, GloVe embeddings, a KB for distant supervision, GPU + Theano (or modern re-impl in PyTorch). PubMed Central full text for the large run.
  • Code: dataset release promised at hanover.azurewebsites.net; .
  • Modifications: for our agent pipeline, reuse the document-graph construction idea as an input representation to an LLM (serialize typed dependency edges into the prompt) rather than training the Graph LSTM; replace fixed biomedical schema with open scientific-concept types.
  • Unknowns: missing link: how well the DAG-partition backprop and edge-typed gating transfer beyond biomedical text; whether the graph structure adds value when parses are noisy (paper suggests limited gain).
Follow-up Papers관련 후속 논문
  • DyGIE++ (Wadden et al., 2019) [17] — span-graph propagation for joint entity+relation, successor to graph-structured document-level RE.
  • PURE (Zhong & Chen, 2021) [19] — reexamines whether shared vs. separate encoders help relation extraction, revisiting Miwa & Bansal-style joint modeling this paper builds on.
  • SciERC / SciIE (Luan et al., 2018) [16] — scientific-domain entity+relation+coref dataset, the schema closest to our open scientific-concept target.
Condition Delta — transfer to our setting
조건 차이 — 우리 환경으로의 전이

Paper's setting: inputs = biomedical passages (≤3 consecutive sentences) with pre-tagged drug/gene/mutation entities + syntactic parses; outputs = accuracy/precision of a fixed relation type (drug-gene-mutation ternary, gene regulation); conditions = distant supervision from GDKD+CIVIC KBs, closed entity-type schema, trained neural model (Theano). Our setting: inputs = a newly-added scientific paper (any CS/ML topic) added to a growing KB; outputs = prerequisite/derivation dependency links to existing KB nodes; conditions = LLM-agent-driven via prompting/retrieval, open/evolving schema, incremental DAG maintenance. Differences:

  • Document-level / cross-sentence relation signal: resolved: reference only — confirms that dependency-bearing relations routinely span sentence boundaries and must be modeled at passage/document scope, a design premise for our extractor.
  • Trained supervised/distant model vs. LLM prompting: missing link — Graph LSTM requires training a custom model; our constraint prioritizes prompt/retrieval methods. The mechanism (build a typed document graph, jointly represent an entity tuple) is transferable as an intermediate representation, not the model itself.
  • Fixed 3-entity biomedical schema vs. open scientific-concept schema: missing link — no entity/relation typology reuse; concept-prerequisite links differ from drug-gene-mutation triples.
  • Reliance on syntactic parse quality: reference only — cautionary signal that dependency-parse-based structure degrades on noisy/OOD text (biomedical parse errors erased the graph advantage).
