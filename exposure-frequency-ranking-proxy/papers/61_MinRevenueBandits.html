Multi-Armed Bandits with Minimum Aggregated Revenue Constraints

Ben Yahmed et al. · arXiv · 2025

arXiv:2510.12523 · PDF · SP: SP6

P4Track B

한 줄 요약

arm별 최소 누적 수익 제약 하의 bandit을 다룬다. 수익 하한은 exposure 하한의 인접 형태다.

Problem해결하려는 문제

Non-contextual bandits with guaranteed revenue per arm (Baudry et al. 2024) admit a closed-form optimal allocation (sample arms proportionally to revenue-guarantee-over-performance ratio) and enjoy free exploration, giving constant regret. With contextual variation and constraints aggregated across contexts, neither property survives: no closed-form allocation exists (the planning problem becomes a general LP), and local per-context reduction is impossible because what matters is how much suboptimal an arm is relative to others globally. Existing general-constraint contextual bandit methods (primal-dual) get $O(\sqrt{T})$ on both metrics and overlook this finer LP structure.

Solution / Contribution해결책 / 기여

Formulate the planning problem as a linear program over per-context allocation matrices $w \in \pi_K^{|\mathcal{C}|}$, then learn online via confidence-bound-parameterized LPs:

  1. Maintain empirical means $\hat\mu_{k,c}(t)$ with Hoeffding radii $\epsilon_{k,c}(t)$.
  2. OLP: play $w(t) = \arg\max \text{LP}(\text{UCB}(t), \text{UCB}(t))$.
  3. OPLP: play $w(t) = \arg\max \text{LP}(\text{UCB}(t), \text{LCB}(t))$ if feasible, else fall back to the doubly-optimistic LP.
  4. Analysis pivots on identifying the optimal active constraint set $\mathcal{I}^\star$ at rate governed by the novel gap $\rho^\star$.
Key Results주요 결과
  • OLP: $\mathbb{E}[\mathcal{R}_T] \leq O(\log(T)^2/\rho^\star)$, $\mathbb{E}[\mathcal{V}_T] \leq O(\log(T)^2/\rho^\star + \sqrt{|\mathcal{K}\cap\mathcal{I}^\star|\log(T)T})$ (Thm 5.1).
  • OPLP: $\mathbb{E}[\mathcal{V}_T] \leq O(\lambda\log(T)^2/\gamma^{\star 2})$ with $O(\sqrt{T})$-order regret (Thm 5.2) — the mirror trade-off.
  • Lower bound: $\min_\pi \max_\nu \mathbb{E}[\mathcal{R}+\mathcal{V}] = \Omega(\sqrt{T})$ and $\Omega(\log T)$ regret for any consistent policy — constant regret (achievable non-contextually) is impossible with contexts (Thm 5.3).
  • When no arm saturates its constraint ($|\mathcal{K}\cap\mathcal{I}^\star|=0$), both regret and violation become poly-logarithmic.
  • Synthetic experiments: OLP/OPLP outperform Optimistic³ (which is $O(\sqrt{T})$ on both metrics) and non-contextual DOC/SPOC baselines.

Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

MAB-ARC (MAB with Aggregated Revenue Constraints)

  • What it computes: at each round, observe context $c_t$, choose arm $k_t$, receive reward $r_t \sim \mathcal{D}_{k_t,c_t}$; objective is $\max \mathbb{E}[\sum_t r_t]$ subject to $\mathbb{E}[\sum_t r_t \mathbb{1}[k_t=k]] \geq \lambda_k T$ for every arm $k$ — a per-arm minimum revenue threshold aggregated across contexts, not per context.
  • Why it was created: prior work (Baudry et al. 2024) handles minimum revenue per arm only without contexts; general constrained contextual bandits (primal-dual) ignore the LP structure of this problem and get worse rates.
  • Concrete example: a recommendation platform with multiple content providers — each provider (arm) must receive minimum engagement $\lambda_k$ summed over all user categories (contexts), even though the platform recommends per-category.
  • How it is used in this paper: the entire problem formulation; the optimal stationary policy is the solution of a linear program LP($\mu$, $\mu$) over per-context allocation matrices $w$.

Sub-optimality gap $\rho(\mathcal{I})$ over active constraint sets

  • What it computes: for a candidate set $\mathcal{I}$ of binding constraints (which arms saturate their revenue threshold, which arm-context pairs get zero probability), $\rho(\mathcal{I}) = \max(s(\mathcal{I}), \mathcal{P}(\mathcal{I}))$ combining a feasibility slack and a normalized performance gap; $\rho^\star = \min_{\mathcal{I} \neq \mathcal{I}^\star} \rho(\mathcal{I})$ plays the role of the classical MAB gap.
  • Why it was created: with aggregated constraints there is no closed-form optimal allocation (unlike the non-contextual case), so the standard per-arm gap analysis breaks; the gap must be defined over LP vertex structures instead. [SKIM - verify full definition mechanics in deep read]
  • Concrete example: in the paper's synthetic instance (Table 8), $\mathcal{I}^\star = \{2, 3, (2,1), (3,1), (3,2), (2,3)\}$ — arms 2 and 3 saturate their thresholds and four arm-context pairs are never played.
  • How it is used in this paper: governs the $\log(T)^2/\rho^\star$ regret bounds of both algorithms; identifying $\mathcal{I}^\star$ is the "find the best arm" analogue.

OLP / OPLP algorithms

  • What it computes: each round, solve the planning LP with confidence-bound parameters. OLP uses UCB estimates for both objective and constraints (optimistic); OPLP uses UCB for the objective and LCB for the constraints (pessimistic on constraints), falling back to doubly-optimistic when the pessimistic LP is infeasible.
  • Why it was created: to trade off performance regret vs. constraint violation at two different Pareto points, without the primal-dual machinery of prior general-constraint work.
  • Concrete example: OLP achieves $O(\log(T)^2/\rho^\star)$ regret with $O(\sqrt{T})$ constraint violation; OPLP inverts this.
  • How it is used in this paper: the two main algorithmic contributions (Section 4, Theorems 5.1–5.2).

Breakdown of free exploration in the contextual setting

  • What it computes: a lower-bound construction (3 arms, 3 contexts) showing that for $K > 2, |\mathcal{C}| > 1$, the optimal allocation always zeroes out at least one arm-context pair (Lemma 5.1), so constraints no longer force all arms to be sampled linearly often.
  • Why it was created: the non-contextual predecessor achieved constant regret by exploiting that revenue constraints force all arms to be played a linear fraction of time ("free exploration"); this paper shows that property is structurally impossible with contexts.
  • Concrete example: nominal instance $\nu^{(0)}$ (Table 1): any consistent policy suffers $\Omega(\log T)$ regret, and $\min_\pi \max_\nu \mathbb{E}[\mathcal{R} + \mathcal{V}] = \Omega(\sqrt{T})$.
  • How it is used in this paper: Theorem 5.3, establishing near-optimality of the upper bounds.
Implementation Feasibility구현 가능성
  • Needs: an LP solver called once per round over a $K \times |\mathcal{C}|$ allocation matrix; empirical-mean statistics per arm-context pair. Trivial compute at the paper's scale; LP-per-round cost grows with $K|\mathcal{C}|$.
  • Code: not available (no repository link found at skim depth).
  • Modifications: for a ranking/exposure setting one would need to replace the arm simplex with a ranking-policy polytope (e.g., doubly-stochastic exposure matrices) and re-derive the gap machinery — non-trivial.
  • Unknowns: missing link: whether the LP-gap analysis survives when constraints are on exposure (allocation-side) rather than realized reward; missing link: sensitivity of OPLP to unknown feasibility margin $\gamma^\star$ in practice (Appendix G.1 shows it silently degenerates to OLP behavior when $\gamma^\star$ is tiny).
Follow-up Papers관련 후속 논문
  • Baudry et al., "Multi-armed bandits with guaranteed revenue per arm" (AISTATS 2024) — the non-contextual predecessor whose free-exploration property this paper refutes in the contextual case; likely the closer SP6 reference for per-provider minimum guarantees.
  • Guo, Zu & Liu, "Triple-optimistic learning for stochastic contextual bandits with general constraints" (ICML 2025) — the general-constraint baseline (Optimistic³) this paper beats by exploiting LP structure.
  • Wang, Bai, Sun & Joachims, "Fairness of exposure in stochastic bandits" (ICML 2021) — the exposure-denominated constraint counterpart, closer to SP6's exposure framing than this paper's revenue framing.
Condition Delta — transfer to our setting조건 차이 — 우리 설정으로의 전이

Paper's setting: inputs = finite arm set $K$, finite context set $\mathcal{C}$ with known probabilities, stochastic scalar rewards (1-sub-Gaussian), known per-arm thresholds $\lambda_k$; outputs = per-context arm-sampling probabilities; conditions = one arm pulled per round (no ranking), strict feasibility of the LP (Assumption 3), synthetic evaluation only. Our setting (SP6 — exposure-frequency as ranking proxy): ranked lists of items shown to users, where provider-side minimum exposure/revenue guarantees must hold under position-bias-weighted exposure, typically with learned relevance and large item/provider spaces. Differences:

  • Single-arm pull vs. ranked slates with position bias: missing link — the paper's LP is over simplex columns, not over rankings/permutations; exposure in ranking is position-weighted, not binary.
  • Reward-denominated constraint ($\lambda_k$ on aggregated revenue) vs. exposure-denominated guarantees common in fairness-of-exposure work: reference only — conceptually adjacent (paper explicitly cites fairness-of-exposure bandits [10] as motivation) but not the same constraint currency.
  • Finite small $K, |\mathcal{C}|$ vs. large-scale item/provider spaces: missing link — paper explicitly flags infinite/large spaces as future work requiring structural assumptions.
  • Known context distribution: resolved — authors argue it can be estimated online with vanishing error since contexts are fully observed and exogenous.
  • Constraints enforced in expectation with violations allowed vs. hard per-window guarantees platforms may need: reference only. [SKIM - verify]
Root-cause vs. Symptom근본 원인 대 증상

Direct — the paper identifies the structural root cause (aggregated constraints destroy both the closed-form allocation and the free-exploration property of the non-contextual problem), builds the analysis machinery (LP-based gap $\rho(\mathcal{I})$) around that structure rather than reusing generic primal-dual bounds, and proves a lower bound showing the limitation is intrinsic to the setting, not an artifact of the algorithm.
