Temporal Knowledge Graph Completion: A Survey

Cai et al. · IJCAI 2023 · 2022

arXiv:2201.08236 · PDF · SP: SP8 SP9

P1Track A

Problem
해결하려는 문제

Standard knowledge graph completion (KGC) methods learn static entity/relation embeddings and cannot represent facts whose validity changes over time. Since real-world KGs (ICEWS, GDELT, Wikidata) carry timestamped events, static KGC produces temporally ambiguous or wrong link predictions. As of this survey there was no published organizing framework for the emerging TKGC literature — no shared taxonomy of how timestamps get integrated, and no consolidated view of benchmarks/metrics.

시계열 KG completion 방법이 파편화되어 있고 증분 유지가 미해결이다.

Solution / Contribution
해결책 / 기여

Survey / taxonomy paper (no new model). Contributions:

  1. A five-category taxonomy of TKGC methods keyed on how the timestamp is integrated into the score function (tensor decomposition / transformation / dynamic embedding / snapshot learning / historical-context reasoning).
  2. Consolidation of benchmark datasets (ICEWS14, ICEWS05-15, ICEWS18, GDELT, YAGO15k, WIKIDATA) with statistics, and the standard evaluation protocol (Hits@k, MR, MRR; entity/relation/time prediction; time-aware filtering).
  3. Identification of open limitations and four future directions: incorporate external knowledge, time-aware negative sampling, larger-scale KGs, and evolutionary (continual/incremental) KGs.

Common loss functions summarized: margin ranking loss, cross-entropy loss, binary cross-entropy loss.

timestamp 통합 5-category taxonomy로 분야를 정리하고, evolutionary/incremental KG(TIE: replay+distillation)를 미해결 미래 방향으로 명시한다.

Key Results
주요 결과
  • No empirical results reported (survey with no comparative evaluation table).
  • Benchmark statistics consolidated: GDELT largest (3,419,607 facts) but smallest entity/relation sets (500 entities, 20 relations) and poorest model performance — attributed to abstract/concept entities (e.g. GOVERNMENT) that fail to align with specific events.
  • Reported observation (from Zhu et al. 2021, cited): >80% of ICEWS events 1995–2019 had occurred previously, motivating copy-generation reasoning.
  • Reported (from West et al. 2014, cited): >70% of Freebase person entities lack place-of-birth — motivating KG completion generally.
Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

Temporal Knowledge Graph Completion (TKGC)

  • What it computes: input = a temporal KG as facts (quadruples) $\{h, r, t, \tau\}$ (head, relation, tail, timestamp) → learn low-dimensional embeddings via a factual score function $q(s)$ that incorporates $\tau$ → output = ranked candidate entities/relations/timestamps for an incomplete query, e.g. $(?, r, t, \tau)$ or $(h, r, t, ?)$.
  • Why it was created: standard KGC assumes facts are static, but many facts are only valid over a time window (e.g. $\{DonaldTrump, presidentOf, USA\}$ valid 2017–2021); ignoring timestamps causes ambiguous/incorrect predictions.
  • Concrete example: query $(?, championOf, NBA, 2010)$ should return $Lakers$; the same query at 2020 should not.
  • How it is used in this paper: the organizing task the entire survey taxonomizes; §2 defines it, §3 categorizes methods by how $\tau$ is integrated.

Interpolation vs. Extrapolation (seen vs. unseen timestamps)

  • What it computes: interpolation = predict missing links at timestamps seen in training; extrapolation = predict at unseen timestamps, split into future timestamps (forecasting) and missing timestamps (imputation).
  • Why it was created: distinguishes the evaluation regime; extrapolation to future is the harder, more realistic setting the community is moving toward.
  • Concrete example: ICEWS18 forecasting predicts events at timestamps after the training cutoff.
  • How it is used in this paper: framing in the evaluation-protocol subsection (§2) and referenced across method categories.

Taxonomy of timestamp integration strategies (5 categories) — the survey's core contribution:

  1. Timestamp-included tensor decomposition — treat KG as a 4-way tensor (entity × relation × entity × timestamp); learn via CP or Tucker decomposition; score $q(s)=\langle e_h, e_r, e_t, e_\tau\rangle$.
  2. Timestamp-based transformation — timestamps act as a transformation on static entity/relation embeddings: synthetic time-dependent relations (e.g. championOf:2010), or linear transformations (hyperplane projection à la HyTE, complex-space rotation à la TeRo).
  3. Dynamic embedding — embeddings are functions of $\tau$ (trend + seasonal decomposition, diachronic embedding) or hidden states of an RNN (Know-Evolve, TeMP).
  4. Learning from KG snapshots — split KG into per-timestamp subgraphs $\{G_1,...,G_{|T|}\}$; model evolution via Markov process or autoregressive GNN (RE-NET, RE-GCN, TANGO).
  5. Reasoning with historical context — predict using pre-query facts as context: attention-based relevance (xERTE, TITer) or heuristic-based relevance (TPmod, CyGNet copy-generation).

5Cs Analysis5Cs 분석

Category: survey (taxonomy). Context: builds on the static KGC embedding literature (TransE, tensor-decomposition KGC, KG-embedding survey by Wang et al. 2017); organizes ~30 TKGC papers spanning 2017–2021 (Know-Evolve, HyTE, TeRo, diachronic embedding, RE-NET, RE-GCN, TANGO, xERTE, TITer, CyGNet, TPmod). For this survey, relevant to SP8/SP9 as the reference framework for temporal dependency modeling and evolving-KG maintenance. Correctness: — no experiments of its own; presents no comparative results table, so no error bars to assess. Taxonomy assignments appear reasonable but the survey does not benchmark methods against each other. Contributions: taxonomy of timestamp integration; benchmark/metric consolidation; future-direction analysis. Clarity: concise (9 pages, IJCAI survey track). Method descriptions are one-paragraph summaries; not reproducible from this paper alone (must consult primary sources).

Contribution vs. Claim기여 대 주장

— As a survey the paper claims completeness ("for the first time we summarize... TKGC") and a general taxonomy. The five categories are demonstrated by assigning specific papers to each, but the survey does not empirically validate that the taxonomy is exhaustive or that categories are mutually exclusive (some methods, e.g. snapshot + attention hybrids, could fit multiple buckets). No comparative benchmark is run, so relative method quality is asserted only via cited numbers, not demonstrated here.

Implementation Feasibility구현 실현가능성
  • Needs: none to reproduce the survey itself. To apply surveyed methods: benchmark datasets (ICEWS/GDELT/YAGO15k/Wikidata, publicly available), standard embedding training (PyTorch), negative sampling.
  • Code: survey has no code; individual methods have separate open-source repos (not linked in this paper).
  • Modifications: for SP8/SP9, would need a dependency-domain temporal KG and an extraction front-end (out of scope here); choose a category (likely snapshot-learning or dynamic-embedding) matching the dependency-evolution structure.
  • Unknowns: missing link — no comparative accuracy numbers here, so method selection needs primary sources; missing link — extraction-to-KB pipeline not covered.
Follow-up Papers관련 후속 논문
  • RE-GCN / "Temporal knowledge graph reasoning based on evolutional representation learning" (Li et al., 2021) — snapshot-based autoregressive GCN, strong forecasting baseline; relevant to SP8 temporal dependency evolution.
  • CyGNet / "Learning from history: sequential copy-generation networks" (Zhu et al., 2021) — copy-generation reasoning exploiting recurring history; relevant to SP9 evolving-KB prediction.
  • TIE / "A framework for embedding-based incremental TKGC" (Wu et al., 2021) — incremental/continual TKGC with experience replay + distillation; directly relevant to SP9 KB maintenance under updates.
  • Diachronic Embedding (Goel et al., 2020) — model-agnostic time-varying embedding; candidate mechanism for SP8.
Condition Delta — transfer to our setting
조건 차이 — 우리 환경으로의 전이

Paper's setting: inputs = temporal KG quadruples $\{h,r,t,\tau\}$ with discrete timestamps (mostly event KGs: ICEWS/GDELT political events, Wikidata/YAGO interval facts); outputs = ranked link/time predictions; conditions = fixed vocabulary of entities/relations/timestamps, embedding-based supervised learning with negative sampling. Our setting (SP8/SP9, dependency extraction & KB construction): inputs = extracted dependency relations among software/knowledge artifacts, likely evolving over versions/time; outputs = a constructed/maintained knowledge base capturing temporal dependency structure; conditions = KB must be extended/updated as new facts arrive. Differences:

  • Domain (political-event KGs vs. dependency/software KB): reference only — methods are domain-agnostic embedding techniques, transferable in principle.
  • SP9 evolving-KB angle: resolved / partially — the survey's §4.4 "Evolutionary Knowledge Graph" (incremental/continual learning, experience replay + knowledge distillation, TIE framework) directly addresses maintaining a KG under fact insertion/deletion over time.
  • SP8 temporal-dependency modeling: resolved / reference — the taxonomy (esp. dynamic embedding and snapshot-learning categories) provides candidate mechanisms for encoding temporal dependency evolution.
  • Extraction step (turning text/artifacts into quadruples): missing link — the survey assumes a completed quadruple set; it covers completion, not extraction, so the front-end extraction pipeline for KB construction is out of scope here.
  • Scale: missing link — survey notes current benchmarks are already slow (hours–days); real-life billion-fact KGs unaddressed.
