SP2: 2024–2026 Frontier: Exposure Control and LLM-era Re-ranking

15 papers · targets O3, O4 · 2024-2026

Dedicated recency probe (O3): what the 2024-2026 literature adds over the pre-2024 exposure-control canon, and whether any frontier paper relaxes SP1's two universal assumptions (known position-bias curve, trusted scores) under the user's black-box, frequency-only constraint.

SP2: 2024–2026 최전선: LLM 시대의 exposure 제어

15편 논문 · O3, O4 목표 대상 · 2024-2026

O3 전용 recency 탐침: 2024–2026 문헌이 2024년 이전 canon에 무엇을 더하는지, 그리고 frontier 논문이 SP1의 두 보편 가정(알려진 position-bias 곡선, 신뢰된 score)을 완화하는지를 검사한다.

Entry Assumption vs. What Was Found

Expected (revised after SP1: check whether each frontier paper relaxes SP1's two universal assumptions — known position-bias curve and trusted scores): Dedicated recency probe for O3: LLM-based re-ranking with exposure objectives, exposure control in feeds, and new 2024–2026 formulations under black-box scorers. Pre-loop search assessed WWW 2026's FUDLR as an 'almost verbatim' match to the user constraint.

Found: The frontier does not relax SP1's assumptions by mechanism design — it empirically demolishes trusted scores for LLM/generative scorers (prompt-position bias, pre-training content priors, gradient starvation amplified by tokenization; scaling 0.6B→8B does not fix it), then mostly abandons black-box feasibility to cure it. The feed/production line delivered TEC: frequency-level eligibility control over an unmodified scorer that raised the platform's true objective.

Assessment: Pivoted — the pre-loop belief that FUDLR was black-box feasible was corrected (it needs gradients and training data); the actual near-verbatim match is TEC. And instead of relaxing SP1's two assumptions, the frontier added a third load-bearing one: homogeneous exposure→utility conversion, contradicted independently by three 2026 papers.

사전 가정과 실제 발견

기대 (SP1 이후 수정됨: 각 frontier 논문이 SP1의 두 보편 가정 — 알려진 position-bias 곡선과 신뢰된 score — 을 완화하는지 검사): O3 전용 recency 탐침으로서 exposure 목표를 갖는 LLM 기반 re-ranking, feed에서의 exposure 제어, black-box scorer 하의 2024–2026 신규 정식화를 기대하였다. 사전 검색에서는 WWW 2026의 FUDLR가 사용자 제약에 '거의 그대로' 부합한다고 판단했었다.

발견: frontier는 SP1의 가정을 메커니즘 설계로 완화하지 않는다. LLM/생성형 scorer의 trusted-scores 가정을 경험적으로 무너뜨린 뒤(prompt 위치 편향, pre-training 내용 prior, tokenization으로 증폭되는 gradient 결핍; 0.6B→8B 스케일링으로도 제거 안 됨), 가장 강한 치료법들은 black-box 실행 가능성을 포기한다. 반면 feed/production 라인은 TEC — 수정되지 않은 scorer 위의 빈도 수준 eligibility 제어로 플랫폼의 진짜 목표를 올린 필드 실험 — 을 내놓았다.

평가: 전환됨 — FUDLR가 black-box에서 실행 가능하다는 사전 판단이 수정되었다(gradient와 학습 데이터 필요). 실제로 사용자 설정에 부합하는 것은 TEC였다. 또한 frontier는 SP1의 두 가정을 완화하는 대신 세 번째 부하 가정(균질한 exposure→효용 변환)을 추가로 드러냈고, 이는 2026년 논문 세 편이 독립적으로 반박한다.

What SP2 Establishes

The 2024-2026 frontier splits into three lines, and none of them relaxes SP1's two assumptions by mechanism design — instead the frontier empirically demolishes the trusted-scores assumption and then mostly abandons black-box feasibility to fix it. (1) The LLM-era line shows that LLM and generative scorers violate trusted scores structurally, through three now-identified channels: prompt-position sensitivity of the ranking output [18] LLMPositionBias, content priors baked in by pre-training/alignment that persist across providers and prompts [19] PolarizationByDefault, and MLE gradient starvation of tail tokens amplified geometrically by shared tokenization [22] EchoesFilterBubble, [23] CRAB — and scaling the backbone 0.6B→8B does not remove it. The strongest cures, however, all require white-box access: influence-function parameter edits [16] FairLLMRec, tokenization redesign with retraining [22] EchoesFilterBubble, codebook splitting plus fine-tuning [23] CRAB. (2) The feed/production line delivers the single most important result for the user's setting: TEC [20] SpotWorkTEC is a field-experimentally validated, purely frequency-level eligibility control over an unmodified engagement scorer that raised the platform's true objective (+9.0 matches/prefecture-day) while the engagement metric stayed flat — the first production successor to FairRec's show/don't-show pattern, directly refining SP1's finding that pure frequency control was nearly absent. (3) The new-formulation line attacks the allocation target itself: [24] BeyondExposure and [25] EquityVsEquality independently argue exposure is a lossy proxy for provider utility when the exposure→utility conversion is context-dependent — which surfaces a third load-bearing assumption (homogeneous conversion) on top of SP1's two, rather than relaxing either. The known-position-bias assumption is untouched everywhere; worse, LLM re-rankers introduce a second, internal position-bias curve (prompt-slot bias) that no paper has measured per-slot.

SP2이 확립한 것

SP2는 2024–2026 frontier가 세 갈래로 갈라짐을 확립한다. (1) LLM 라인: LLM/생성형 scorer는 prompt 위치 민감성, pre-training 내용 prior, tokenization으로 기하급수적으로 증폭되는 tail token gradient 결핍이라는 세 채널을 통해 trusted-scores 가정을 구조적으로 위반하며, 스케일링으로 제거되지 않는다. 가장 강한 치료법(FairLLMRec, EchoesFilterBubble, CRAB)은 모두 white-box 접근이 필요하다. (2) feed/production 라인: TEC는 수정되지 않은 engagement scorer 위에서 exposure 차감식 eligibility 임계값만으로 매칭 +9.0/현day를 달성했다(engagement 불변) — FairRec의 show/don't-show 패턴의 첫 production 후속. (3) 신규 정식화 라인: exposure는 provider 효용의 손실 있는 proxy이며(BeyondExposure, EquityVsEquality), exposure→효용 변환의 균질성이라는 세 번째 부하 가정이 존재한다. 알려진 position-bias 가정은 어디서도 다뤄지지 않았고, LLM re-ranker는 오히려 두 번째 내부 position-bias 곡선(prompt slot)을 쌓는다.

Dominant Approach지배적 접근

The frontier has no single dominant method; it has a dominant finding: the scores are not to be trusted, and the papers divide by where they intervene about it. The LLM-era cluster ([16] FairLLMRec, [18] LLMPositionBias, [19] PolarizationByDefault, [22] EchoesFilterBubble, [23] CRAB, with [26] LLMRecSysSurvey as landscape) traces score contamination to its sources — training samples, prompt geometry, pre-training priors, tokenization — and fixes it upstream, at the parameters: FUDLR's influence-function unlearning edits LoRA weights in one step, Ghost redesigns the tokenizer and retrains, CRAB splits over-popular codebook tokens and fine-tunes. These are "retraining-free" only in the narrow sense of avoiding full retraining; all require gradients, training data, or parameter access. The logic chain matters for the user: [22] EchoesFilterBubble proves the bias is an optimization-level property of MLE on long-tailed data (gradient starvation), which explains why the pre-2024 remedy — item-level re-weighting/re-ranking transplants like IFairLRS — is only marginally effective on generative recommenders, and why the frontier concluded it had to go white-box.

The production/feed cluster reaches the opposite conclusion from the same distrust. [20] SpotWorkTEC does not repair the engagement score at all: it diagnoses that the score optimizes the wrong objective (favoriting probability ≠ realized matches under capacity constraints — "misdirected concentration"), and constrains it orthogonally with a frequency-level eligibility filter fed by supply-side state. This is the 2026 successor of the pre-2024 production pattern [27] LinkedInFairRanking (count-based prefix constraints over a fixed scorer, deployed 2018), upgraded in two ways: the constraint is stateful across rounds (scores debited by realized exposure — literally an impression ledger) and validated against the platform's true objective in a randomized field rollout rather than against representation metrics. [21] ExposureAwareRetrieval sits between the clusters: it accepts the trained model but adds an exposure head so the contamination term can be subtracted at inference with a runtime knob — the deployed γ=0.65 < 1 showing that fully removing the exposure signal from scores hurts, i.e., exposure frequency carries genuine relevance information mixed with the bias.

For the survey objectives this means: O3 is satisfied — every classical line has a named 2024-2026 extension (see Research Questions) — but the frontier's trajectory runs away from the user's constraint set. The methods with the strongest fairness numbers need white-box access; the methods compatible with an immutable black-box scorer are exactly the frequency-level ones (TEC's eligibility thresholds; balanced-word impression scheduling from [28] Expohedron; the efficient group-level Pareto machinery of [10] ParetoRepeatedRankings) plus, potentially, a log-only variant of the γ-knob correction that no paper has tested.

Convergences수렴 지점
  • LLM/generative scorers violate the trusted-scores assumption structurally, not incidentally. Four papers reach this via independent routes: prompt-order shuffling changes rankings ([18] LLMPositionBias); content biases (polarization amplification) persist across 3 providers × 3 platforms × 6 prompts with all metadata stripped ([19] PolarizationByDefault); MLE gradient dynamics starve tail tokens and tokenization amplifies it geometrically ([22] EchoesFilterBubble); token-popularity accumulation biases decoding ([23] CRAB). Mechanism: the contamination enters before any ranking is served, so no amount of downstream trust is warranted — and scaling does not fix it ([22] EchoesFilterBubble: 0.6B→8B).
  • Exposure is a lossy proxy for provider utility. Three independent 2026 papers: [24] BeyondExposure (time-varying unit income), [25] EquityVsEquality (heterogeneous provider preferences over exposure vs sales), and empirically [20] SpotWorkTEC (favorites flat while matches rose — engagement and true objective decoupled). Mechanism: exposure→utility conversion is context-dependent, so equalizing exposure does not equalize outcomes.
  • The frontier's strongest interventions abandon black-box feasibility. [16] FairLLMRec (gradients + training data + parameter edit), [22] EchoesFilterBubble (retrain with new tokenizer), [23] CRAB (codebook surgery + LoRA fine-tune). This repeats SP1's HEA surprise at larger scale: SOTA fairness numbers are bought with access the user does not have.
  • Score-read-only re-ranking remains the default mechanism for the papers that do stay downstream. [24] BeyondExposure, [25] EquityVsEquality, [27] LinkedInFairRanking, [10] ParetoRepeatedRankings, [28] Expohedron all consume fixed scores and reorder/schedule — confirming SP1's access-level finding into 2026.
Divergences분기 지점
  • Where does LLM-recommender bias come from — popularity or position? [18] LLMPositionBias explicitly tests popularity strata and finds popularity is not the main driver of ranking instability in prompted LLM rankers (prompt position is; PC varies only 0.63-0.67 across popularity strata). [22] EchoesFilterBubble and [23] CRAB find popularity (via training-frequency effects on tokens) is the dominant distortion in generative recommenders. Likely cause: different architectures — zero-shot prompting over text titles puts the bias in context attention; SID-based fine-tuned generation puts it in MLE gradient dynamics over tokens. Unresolved for hybrid pipelines (fine-tuned LLM re-ranking a prompted candidate list); no paper adjudicates.
  • **What should exposure be debiased toward?** [23] CRAB debiases generated exposure toward historical interaction frequency (the GU metric's reference); [21] ExposureAwareRetrieval debiases scores away from interaction frequency (conditional-on-exposure relevance); [24] BeyondExposure/[25] EquityVsEquality debias toward realized utility. These are mutually inconsistent reference distributions — historical frequency is itself the contaminated quantity per [21] ExposureAwareRetrieval's own diagnosis. Likely cause: each line inherits its reference from its evaluation tradition (fairness metrics vs retrieval quality vs welfare economics). Unresolved.
Prior Beliefs and Surprises (full)사전 믿음과 놀라움 (전문)

What the entry rationale expected to find:

LLM-based re-ranking with fairness/exposure objectives, exposure control in feeds, and new 2024-2026 formulations of exposure allocation under black-box scorers; for each frontier paper, check whether it relaxes SP1's two universal assumptions (known position-bias curve, trusted scores).

Confirmed:

  • All three expected lines exist and are populated with 2025-2026 publications, including a production field experiment ([20] SpotWorkTEC) — O3's recency requirement is met.
  • The trusted-scores failure mode (SP1's FairPGRank warning) is confirmed and mechanized for LLM-era scorers: the frontier identifies the specific channels (prompt position, pre-training priors, gradient starvation, token popularity).

Surprised:

  • (a) Assumed the frontier would produce new black-box formulations; found instead that the strongest interventions require white-box parameter access ([16] FairLLMRec, [22] EchoesFilterBubble, [23] CRAB). The assumption was wrong because the frontier diagnosed the bias as an optimization-level property of training, which cannot be fully repaired downstream — "retraining-free" in 2026 titles means cheap parameter edits, not output-only access.
  • (b) Assumed pure frequency-level control would remain absent (KB claim 3); found TEC — a field-validated eligibility-threshold control over untouched scores. The pre-loop search's read that WWW 2026's "without costly retraining" paper matched the user constraint "almost verbatim" was wrong (it needs gradients); the actual near-verbatim match is the Timee field experiment.
  • (c) Assumed the frontier would relax SP1's two assumptions; found it instead added a third (homogeneous exposure→utility conversion, attacked by [24] BeyondExposure/[25] EquityVsEquality) and stacked a second unknown position-bias curve (prompt slots) on the first.

KB impact:

  • UUID 2: confirmed — score-read-only re-ranking remains the dominant downstream access level in the 2024-2026 papers that stay downstream.
  • UUID 3: refined — pure frequency-level control is no longer FairRec-only: TEC is a second, production-validated instance; the absence of formal position-weighted exposure guarantees for frequency-only control persists (TEC is empirical).
  • UUID 4: confirmed and strengthened — the trusted-scores assumption now has identified failure mechanisms for LLM/generative scorers; no frontier mechanism paper relaxes the known-position-bias assumption.

Key Concepts핵심 개념

Concepts from SP1 reused here without redefinition: marginal rank-probability matrix, expected exposure / target exposure ε* (see SP1 Key Concepts; formalized in [15] ExpectedExposure), Expohedron and Carathéodory decomposition (SP1; [28] Expohedron).

Eligibility-threshold exposure control (TEC)

  • What it computes: input = per-item supply-side score $p_k$ (posted + unfilled capacity, carried across rounds and debited by realized exposure) and each user's slot positions; operation = convert sorted capped scores into eligibility thresholds $\tau_k$, then each user's slot $s$ may only draw items with $\tau_k \ge z_{is}$; within the eligible set, ranking still uses the untouched engagement score; output = a per-user recommendation list, computable fully in parallel.
  • Why it was created: quota-based exposure control (round-robin serial dictatorship) cannot be parallelized at >1M-user scale; TEC replaces consumed quotas with precomputed thresholds so exposure allocation becomes an embarrassingly parallel filter in front of the scorer.
  • Concrete example: with 16 slots, an item holding 1/16 of total score stays eligible for exactly one slot's worth of selection timings; in the Timee field rollout this lifted matches +9.0/prefecture-day (p<0.05) with daily favorites unchanged.
  • Role in this SP: [20] SpotWorkTEC's deployed treatment — the frontier's only production-validated, score-untouched, frequency-level intervention; the core evidence for O2/O3 that the user's exact knob works at scale.

Inference-time exposure-logit subtraction (γ-knob correction)

  • What it computes: input = an item's retrieval logit for $P(\text{positive} \wedge \text{exposed}\mid \text{item})$ and a separately learned exposure logit for $P(\text{exposed}\mid \text{item})$; operation = $\text{score} = \text{joint logit} - \gamma \cdot \text{exposure logit}$ (subtraction in logit space = division in probability space); output = an approximation of $P(\text{positive}\mid \text{item}, \text{exposed})$, with $\gamma \in [0,1]$ a runtime knob.
  • Why it was created: retrieval models trained on logged interactions rank by the joint probability, which conflates relevance with how often the feedback loop already showed the item; prior fixes (IPS, off-policy correction) bake the correction into training with no post-hoc control.
  • Concrete example: item A exposed 40× with 20 positives beats item B exposed 10× with 7 positives under the joint score, but loses under the conditional score (0.5 vs 0.7 per-exposure rate). Deployed optimum γ=0.65; full correction γ=1.0 drops positive recall 31.7%→28.0%.
  • Role in this SP: [21] ExposureAwareRetrieval's production mechanism — the frontier's cleanest formalization of exposure frequency as a score contaminant, and the closest existing thing to a post-hoc frequency-based score correction (though it still needs a trained auxiliary head).

Prompt-position bias (LLM-internal position bias)

  • What it computes: input = the same candidate set presented in different orders inside an LLM ranking prompt; operation = compare output rankings across shuffles via pairwise Kendall's tau; output = a stability score (low tau = the ranking depends on where candidates sit in the prompt, not on relevance).
  • Why it was created: LLM re-rankers were adopted without checking whether their output depends on input order; it does, strongly — a bias channel entirely distinct from the user-side position-bias curve of classical exposure models.
  • Concrete example: standard list-wise prompting on MovieLens drops from positional consistency 0.67 at K=10 to 0.47 at K=30; iterative one-item-per-call selection (RISE) holds 0.69 at K=30.
  • Role in this SP: [18] LLMPositionBias's central phenomenon; establishes that in LLM-era re-ranking the scorer itself has a position-bias curve — unknown, unmeasured per-slot — stacking a second unknown on top of SP1's known-position-bias assumption.

Gradient starvation + tokenization bias amplification (generative-recommender popularity mechanism)

  • What it computes: (i) under MLE training on a long-tailed interaction distribution, a rarely-targeted semantic-ID token's expected gradient projection onto the user-preference direction is ≤ 0 (it only ever serves as a softmax negative); (ii) because tokenization ignores popularity, a tail item's token sequence must survive $z$ head-dominated branching points during autoregressive decoding, suppressing its generation probability geometrically by $(\gamma_{min})^{-z}$.
  • Why it was created: to explain why generative recommenders amplify popularity bias beyond discriminative models (vs SASRec: popular-item frequency +6.7-7.2%), rather than treating the skew as a generic data artifact.
  • Concrete example: LC-Rec's head/tail HR@10 gap reaches 45× (0.1800 vs 0.0040); >97% of top-10 slots go to head items; the gap persists from 0.6B to 8B parameters.
  • Role in this SP: [22] EchoesFilterBubble's diagnosis (with [23] CRAB's token-popularity variant) — the mechanism-level proof that exposure-frequency distortion is inherited and amplified by the newest scoring paradigm, i.e., the trusted-scores assumption fails by construction for generative recommenders.

Heterogeneous exposure→utility conversion (income fairness)

  • What it computes: item income at timestep $i$ is $v_d^i = \Delta E(d,i)\,\mu(d,q,\xi_i)$ — position-driven exposure times a context-dependent unit-income function μ; fairness is then required of cumulative income relative to relevance, not cumulative exposure.
  • Why it was created: exposure fairness assumes every unit of exposure converts to provider utility identically; when conversion varies with time/context (a news impression at hour 1 vs day 2), exposure-fair rankings still produce unequal realized utility.
  • Concrete example: in [24] BeyondExposure's Criteo-calibrated environment, methods that are exposure-fair remain income-unfair; DIDRF cuts income unfairness ~10% below the best exposure-derived baseline. [25] EquityVsEquality instantiates the same idea with per-provider preference weights over exposure vs sales gains.
  • Role in this SP: the frontier's new formulation of the allocation target — evidence that even a perfectly realized exposure-frequency schedule controls a proxy, not the outcome providers care about.

Open Questions After SP2SP2 이후 미해결 질문

  • Black-box exposure post-processing theory: No Bayes-optimal post-processing result exists for exposure-based ranking fairness over closed scorers. [17] BlackBoxFairPost proves the pattern for classification (elicited output probabilities are sufficient statistics for the optimal fair post-processor) and flags the ranking analogue as missing (missing link: "whether a LinearPost-style Bayes-optimal linear post-processing result exists for exposure-based ranking fairness"); [16] FairLLMRec flags the same from the other side (missing link: "whether a strictly score-level variant is possible"). What would resolve it: extending the Xian & Zhao 2024 optimality theory from label parity to position-weighted exposure constraints.
  • Log-only post-hoc exposure correction: [21] ExposureAwareRetrieval never tests replacing its trained exposure head with an empirical $P(\text{exposed}\mid\text{item})$ estimated from impression logs (missing link), and [20] SpotWorkTEC leaves sensitivity to noisy/delayed exposure signals unknown (missing link). This untested variant is exactly the user's setting: frequency-based correction of a frozen scorer using only logs. What would resolve it: an ablation swapping the learned head for log-frequency estimates and measuring the recall/γ curve.
  • Feedback-loop compounding in the LLM era: exposure distortion is measured one-shot everywhere — [18] LLMPositionBias (missing link: no repeated-interaction loop), [19] PolarizationByDefault (missing link: no longitudinal amplification), [22] EchoesFilterBubble (missing link: single training round despite the filter-bubble title). No paper measures how prompt-position or token-popularity bias compounds over exposure→training-data→exposure cycles. What would resolve it: a multi-round simulation with the LLM retrained/re-prompted on its own exposure-filtered logs.
  • The LLM-internal position-bias curve: [18] LLMPositionBias reports only aggregate stability, never per-slot bias direction (missing link: "does the LLM favor early or late slots?"), and [26] LLMRecSysSurvey does not analyze exposure distributions induced by LLM rankers (missing link). The classical literature's known-position-bias assumption now has an unmeasured second curve stacked on it. What would resolve it: per-slot promotion-probability instrumentation across models.
  • Estimating the conversion function: [24] BeyondExposure assumes μ given and error-free (missing link: no noise sensitivity); [25] EquityVsEquality assumes provider preference vectors known (missing link: elicitation unaddressed). The hard part of replacing the exposure proxy — knowing the conversion — is outside every current method.
