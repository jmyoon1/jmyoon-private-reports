Calibrated Recommendations with Contextual Bandits

Feijer et al. · RecSys 2025 Workshop (CONSEQUENCES) · 2025

arXiv:2509.05460 · PDF · SP: SP8

P2Track B

한 줄 요약

Spotify에서 contextual bandit으로 calibration을 조정한 온라인 A/B. 인기 miscalibrated scorer 위의 exposure 재배분이 정확도를 함께 올리는 double-win의 다섯 근거 중 하나.

Problem해결하려는 문제

Calibration re-ranking (Steck 2018) aligns a slate's content-type mix to a static target distribution — either the user's historical consumption average (over e.g. the last 7 or 90 days) or a fixed business-defined multinomial (Lichtenberg et al. 2024). Both assume user content-type preferences are fixed, so they miss systematic short-term variation: podcast vs. music appetite shifts with time of day, weekday vs. weekend, and device. The static target therefore mis-allocates exposure exactly when context-dependent interest peaks (e.g. podcasts during weekday morning commutes).

Solution / Contribution해결책 / 기여

Keep Steck's greedy KL-calibrated re-ranker (Eq. 1: $I^* = \arg\max_{|I|=N} (1-\lambda) s(I) - \lambda\, C_{KL}(p, I)$) but replace the static target $p(c|u)$ with the output of a neural contextual bandit:

  1. Context observation: feature vector $x_k$ combining user features (demographics, per-content-type consumption over multiple windows), content representations, and contextual signals (device; sine/cosine-encoded time of day and day of week).
  2. Action selection: pick a content-type distribution $p_k$ from a discrete set $\mathcal{P}$; build the slate by solving Eq. 1 with $p = p_k$ (choosing $p_k$ ≡ choosing the slate).
  3. Reward: binary $r_k = 1$ if the user streams any shelf in the slate.
  4. Learning: 2-hidden-layer MLP (256/64) with BCE loss estimates the reward function; policy picks the reward-maximizing $p$ per context.
  5. Exploration: $\epsilon$-greedy at 1.5%, sampling from a truncated Gaussian centered on the user's historical target (uniform exploration produced too many strongly negative slates).

Training uses 3 weeks of logs (2 train / 1 eval); offline comparison uses IPW with ratio capping. Deployed at Spotify since March 2025.

Key Results주요 결과
  • Offline (IPW-estimated Precision@1, Spotify Home first shelf): CB (MLP) vs. Steck-calibration with 7-day history: +35% podcast precision, +10.2% overall accuracy; vs. 90-day history: +25.6% / +7.2%; vs. multinomial blending: +16.6% / +2.8%
  • Online A/B vs. 90-day Steck calibration: +36.6% podcast impression-to-stream ratio, +3.93% overall i2s, +1.28% total consumption (minutes streamed), +1.54% homepage activity
  • Music performance neutral — gains concentrate on the under-represented content type (podcasts)
  • Truncated-Gaussian exploration around the historical target outperformed uniform exploration (more positive training samples); no numbers given
  • Deployed in production at Spotify since March 2025

Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

Calibrated recommendation (Steck 2018 formulation)

  • What it computes: candidate items with relevance scores + a target content-type distribution $p(c|u)$ → greedy maximization of $(1-\lambda)\, s(I) - \lambda\, C_{KL}(p, I)$ over slates $I$ of size $N$ → a re-ranked slate whose empirical content-type mix matches the target
  • Why it was created: pure relevance ranking over-concentrates on the majority content type (music dominates historical logs), starving under-represented types (podcasts)
  • Concrete example: if a user's target is 70% music / 30% podcasts, KL divergence penalizes a 10-shelf slate with 10 music shelves; the greedy builder swaps in podcast shelves until the rank-weighted mix approaches 70/30
  • How it is used in this paper: as the fixed re-ranking machinery (Eq. 1); the paper's novelty is how the target $p(c|u)$ is chosen, not the re-ranker itself

Contextual bandit over content-type distributions

  • What it computes: context vector $x_k$ (user demographics, past consumption per content type over time windows, device, cyclically-encoded time-of-day/day-of-week) → MLP (256→64, ReLU, BCE loss, dropout 0.1) predicting binary engagement reward → selects a distribution $p_k$ from a discrete set $\mathcal{P}$ of content-type distributions; the chosen $p_k$ is then plugged into the calibration re-ranker
  • Why it was created: prior calibration uses a static target — either historical averages over 7/90-day windows (Steck) or fixed business-defined multinomials — which cannot follow preferences that shift by time of day, day of week, or device
  • Concrete example: Figure 1 shows podcast share of consumption fluctuating hourly and between weekday/weekend across user cohorts; a 90-day average target erases this structure
  • How it is used in this paper: the core contribution — learning $p(c|u)$ online with $\epsilon$-greedy exploration (1.5%, truncated Gaussian around the historical target rather than uniform)

Rank-weighted content-type distribution $q(c|I)$

  • What it computes: slate $I$ + per-shelf content-type distributions $p(c|i)$ + rank weights $w_{r(i)}$ (uniform or MRR/nDCG-style) → weighted average → the slate's effective exposure distribution over content types
  • Why it was created: exposure depends on position; a podcast shelf at rank 10 contributes less real exposure than at rank 1
  • Concrete example: with MRR weights $1/r$, a podcast shelf at rank 1 contributes weight 1.0 vs. 0.1 at rank 10 to $q(\text{podcast}|I)$
  • How it is used in this paper: the $q$ term inside the KL calibration penalty of Eq. 1

Off-policy evaluation via Inverse Propensity Weighting (IPW) — standard technique (Horvitz-Thompson 1952; Li et al. 2011), used with probability-ratio capping (truncated importance sampling) to evaluate candidate policies on 7 days of logged data where rewards are observed only for the logged $p_k$. [SKIM - capping thresholds and estimator variants not detailed in paper]

Implementation Feasibility구현 가능성
  • Needs: logged interaction data with recorded logging-policy propensities (or a way to estimate them), per-item content-type/category distributions, a relevance scorer producing $s(i)$, standard MLP tooling; no exotic compute
  • Code: not available (no repository mentioned; production system)
  • Modifications: for an exposure-frequency-proxy study, replace the binary engagement reward with an exposure/frequency-based signal; define $\mathcal{P}$ over the exposure dimension of interest; the greedy KL re-ranker and rank-weighted $q(c|I)$ port as-is
  • Unknowns: missing link: the discrete action set $\mathcal{P}$ (cardinality, granularity of distributions); missing link: $\lambda$ value and how it was tuned; missing link: IPW capping threshold; missing link: A/B test population size and significance
Follow-up Papers관련 후속 논문
  • Steck (2018), "Calibrated Recommendations" — the foundational target-distribution formulation this paper generalizes; core to SP8's calibration line
  • Lichtenberg, Di Benedetto & Ruffini (RecSys 2024), "Ranking across different content types: The robust beauty of multinomial blending" — the fixed-business-target baseline (MB); the static alternative this paper beats
  • Abdollahpouri et al. (WSDM 2023), "Calibrated recommendations as a minimum-cost flow problem" — exact (non-greedy) list construction for the same objective; noted as too slow for 600M-user serving
  • Zhao, Zhu & Caverlee (WWW 2021), "Rabbit holes and taste distortion" — the sequential-model alternative for time-varying calibration targets; the nearest methodological competitor
  • Seymen, Abdollahpouri & Malthouse (RecSys 2021), "A constrained optimization approach for calibrated recommendations" — mixed-integer/total-variation alternative to KL greedy construction
Condition Delta — transfer to our setting조건 차이 — 우리 설정으로의 전이

Paper's setting: inputs = logged bandit feedback (context, chosen content-type distribution, binary stream reward) at 600M-user production scale; outputs = a per-context target content-type distribution feeding a KL-calibrated greedy re-ranker over home-page shelves; conditions = coarse action space (content types: music vs. podcast), position-weighted exposure via $q(c|I)$, short-term engagement reward, 1.5% exploration. Our setting (SP8 / O2): exposure frequency as a ranking proxy — an exposure-shaping layer imposed on top of an arbitrary relevance scorer, where the quantity being controlled is item/category exposure frequency rather than engagement-optimal content mix. Differences:

  • Unit of calibration: content-type (2–3 coarse categories) vs. our finer-grained item/category exposure frequencies — missing link (action space $\mathcal{P}$ over high-cardinality targets is not addressed; discrete-set approach may not scale)
  • Objective: paper maximizes engagement and lets exposure balance emerge as a side effect; our O2 framing treats exposure shaping itself as the target — resolved: the decoupled architecture (bandit chooses target distribution, re-ranker enforces it) transfers directly; only the reward needs replacing with an exposure-based signal
  • Exposure measurement: rank-weighted $q(c|I)$ with MRR/nDCG weights — resolved: directly reusable as an exposure-frequency proxy at the slate level
  • Evidence regime: production logs + A/B testing vs. our likely offline/public-data setting — reference only
  • Reward horizon: short-term binary stream vs. longer-horizon exposure effects — missing link (paper itself defers long-term rewards to future work)
Root-cause vs. Symptom근본 원인 대 증상

Mixed — it removes the root arbitrary restriction of the calibration line (the assumption that the target distribution is static) by making the target a learned, context-conditional decision variable, but it optimizes a short-term binary engagement proxy for what it frames as long-term balanced satisfaction, leaving the deeper reward-specification problem patched rather than solved (the authors say as much in future work).
