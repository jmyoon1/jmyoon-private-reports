Towards Fair Large Language Model-based Recommender Systems without Costly Retraining

Jin Li et al. · WWW 2026 · 2026

arXiv:2601.17492 · PDF · SP: SP2

P0Track B

한 줄 요약

재학습 없이 LLM recommender의 공정성을 교정한다고 하나, influence function 기반 파라미터 편집에 gradient와 학습 데이터 접근이 필요하다. 즉 output-only black-box에서는 실행 불가능하다 — 'retraining-free'가 곧 black-box는 아님을 보여주는 사례.

Problem해결하려는 문제

Existing debiasing methods for LLM-based recommenders (reweighting, data augmentation, adversarial learning) are each designed for one specific bias type — popularity bias OR attribute bias — and cannot adapt to emerging or co-existing biases, and they all require retraining or fine-tuning the LLM, which is computationally prohibitive at LLM parameter scale for dynamic production recommenders.

Solution / Contribution해결책 / 기여

Reformulate debiasing as machine unlearning, two stages:

  1. Bias identification: learn a sigmoid mask over a uniformly-sampled candidate set D_cand ⊂ D_train, minimizing L_mask = λ_fair·L_fair + λ_acc·L_acc + λ_spa·L_spa, where L_fair = −(1/|D_cand|) Σ m_z·I(z, B(θ)) with influence score I(z, B(θ)) = −∇_θ B(θ)ᵀ H_θ^{-1} ∇_θ L_LLM(z; θ). Samples with positive learned logits form D_unlearn.
  2. Debiasing via unlearning: apply the one-step update Δθ ≈ (1/n) Σ_{z∈D_unlearn} H_θ^{-1} ∇_θ L_LLM(z; θ) (Proposition 1), computed with HVP on the last LoRA adapter layer only.

Bias-agnosticism comes from plugging any differentiable fairness metric into B(θ): popularity metric (Eq. 10), demographic parity |μ_0 − μ_1| (Eq. 12), or weighted combination γ·B_pop + (1−γ)·B_attr (Eq. 13) for simultaneous mitigation.

Key Results주요 결과
  • Qualitative (Fig. 1, ML1M): FUDLR flattens BIGRec's over-exposure of popular items toward the ground-truth exposure distribution, and reduces the gender HR disparity of BIGRec.
  • Claims superior fairness–accuracy balance vs. state-of-the-art LLM-RS debiasing baselines on ML1M and Amazon Games. [SKIM - exact metric numbers in §5 tables]
  • Claims negligible gap between the unlearning update and full retraining (Appendix E). [SKIM]
  • Complexity: O((s + c_g)·u·C_f) vs. O(E·n·C_f) for retraining, since |D_unlearn| ≪ n by the sparsity constraint.

Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

FUDLR (Fast Unified Debiasing for LLM-RS)

  • What it computes: a fine-tuned LLM recommender θ + a chosen differentiable fairness metric B(θ) → (stage 1) learns a per-sample mask selecting bias-inducing training samples, (stage 2) computes a one-step influence-function parameter update Δθ → a debiased model θ* = θ + Δθ without retraining.
  • Why it was created: existing LLM-RS debiasing methods are bias-type-specific (no generality across popularity/attribute/co-existing biases) and require full retraining or fine-tuning, computationally infeasible at LLM scale.
  • Concrete example: on ML1M with BIGRec backbone, FUDLR flattens the exposure distribution over item popularity ranks and shrinks the male/female HR gap (Figure 1 a/b).
  • How it is used in this paper: the entire proposed method; instantiated on BIGRec with LoRA adapters.

Bias-agnostic mask learning

  • What it computes: for each candidate training sample z, a learnable logit → sigmoid probability m_z of removal; optimized with L_mask = λ_fair·L_fair + λ_acc·L_acc + λ_spa·L_spa (fairness improvement via influence score on B(θ), accuracy preservation via fine-tuning loss, sparsity via mean-mask penalty, Eqs. 2–5); samples with logit > 0 become D_unlearn.
  • Why it was created: prior debiasing pipelines hard-code one bias type; here swapping only the differentiable metric B(θ) (popularity Eq. 10, demographic parity Eq. 12, or a convex combination Eq. 13) retargets the same machinery to any bias.
  • Concrete example: B_pop(p; θ) = Σ_v p(v|p;θ)·pop(v) — expected popularity of the recommended item under the model's softmax over embedding distances.
  • How it is used in this paper: stage 1 of FUDLR; the selected D_unlearn feeds stage 2.

Influence-function unlearning update (Proposition 1)

  • What it computes: Δθ ≈ (1/n) Σ_{z∈D_unlearn} H_θ^{-1} ∇_θ L_LLM(z; θ) — a closed-form estimate of the parameter change that removing D_unlearn from training would have produced; Hessian handled via Hessian-vector products, restricted to the last LoRA adapter layer.
  • Why it was created: retraining on D_train \ D_unlearn (Eq. 6) is infeasible for LLMs; the update approximates it in one step at O((s + c_g·u)·C_f) vs. O(E·n·C_f) for retraining.
  • Concrete example: claimed negligible performance gap vs. the actually-retrained model (Appendix E) [SKIM - verify numbers in deep read].
  • How it is used in this paper: stage 2; produces the final debiased recommender.
Implementation Feasibility구현 가능성
  • Needs: a LoRA-fine-tuned LLM recommender, access to (a sample of) its training data, gradient + HVP computation on the last adapter layer, a differentiable fairness metric.
  • Code: available at https://github.com/JinLi-i/FUDLR (with data).
  • Modifications: for our fixed-scorer setting, the differentiable popularity metric (Eq. 10) transfers, but the parameter-update stage presumes white-box gradient access — a black-box scorer would need the mask/influence machinery replaced.
  • Unknowns: missing link: exact baseline set and fairness/accuracy trade-off numbers (§5 tables); missing link: sensitivity to λ_fair/λ_acc/λ_spa and to D_cand sample size; missing link: validity of Hessian invertibility assumption on LoRA loss landscape.
Follow-up Papers관련 후속 논문
  • Jiang et al., popularity bias identification + reweighting/reranking in LLM-RS [25] — the direct predecessor for SP2's item-side exposure bias; FUDLR uses it as the retraining-based contrast.
  • Hua et al., Counterfactually-Fair-Prompt via adversarial learning [21] — user-side attribute-bias baseline; useful contrast for the "no retraining" axis.
  • Bao et al., BIGRec [3] — the backbone recommender; needed to understand what scorer FUDLR actually edits.
  • Koh & Liang, influence functions [26] — the estimation machinery underlying both stages.
Condition Delta — transfer to our setting조건 차이 — 우리 설정으로의 전이

Paper's setting: inputs = user interaction sequences verbalized into prompts; model = LoRA-fine-tuned LLaMA-based generative recommender (BIGRec), ranking by embedding distance to the generated item; outputs = next-item recommendation; bias measured by differentiable metrics (expected popularity, demographic parity); intervention = one-step edit of LoRA adapter parameters. Our setting (SP2): a fixed pretrained scorer whose exposure-frequency / popularity bias must be corrected post hoc without retraining, treating ranking scores as the proxy signal. Differences:

  • Intervention point: FUDLR edits model parameters (LoRA last layer) rather than operating purely on output scores/rankings — it avoids retraining but is not a frozen-scorer post-hoc method: missing link — whether a strictly score-level variant is possible is not addressed.
  • Requires access to training data (D_cand sampled from D_train) and gradients/Hessian-vector products: missing link for settings where only the scorer, not training data, is available.
  • Bias target: popularity bias (exposure over item popularity) is one of its two demonstrated instantiations — directly aligned with SP2's exposure-frequency concern: resolved: Eq. 10 gives a differentiable exposure-popularity metric usable as-is.
  • Generative LLM recommender vs. generic ranking scorer: reference only [SKIM - verify claimed portability to T5/TALLRec backbones].
Root-cause vs. Symptom근본 원인 대 증상

Direct — it traces bias back to the specific bias-inducing training samples and removes their estimated influence from the parameters, attacking the training-data cause rather than re-ranking symptomatic outputs; caveat: the influence approximation on a non-convex LLM loss is itself an estimate.
