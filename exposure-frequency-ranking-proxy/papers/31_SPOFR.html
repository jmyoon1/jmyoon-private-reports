End-to-End Learning for Fair Ranking Systems

James Kotary et al. · WWW 2022 · 2022 · 24 citations

arXiv:2111.10723 · PDF · SP: SP3

P0Track A

한 줄 요약

공정 ranking 정책을 end-to-end로 학습하는 SPOFR. LP로 구성한 δ-공정성 보장을 갖지만 그 보장은 score가 참이라는 조건 위에 서 있다.

Problem해결하려는 문제

Existing fair learning-to-rank methods (FULTR, DELTR) enforce exposure fairness by adding a penalty term λ·violation to the loss. Three concrete failures: (1) fairness holds only on average over queries — signed violations favoring opposite groups cancel, so individual queries can be far from fair; (2) the tradeoff cannot be controlled by specifying an allowed violation magnitude, because the λ→violation relationship is unreliable; (3) λ must be grid-searched, and the search grows exponentially harder with more protected groups. No prior LTR method gives a fairness guarantee on predicted rankings.

Solution / Contribution해결책 / 기여

Predict-then-optimize, end-to-end. (1) A shared per-item MLP predicts relevance scores ŷ_q. (2) ŷ_q becomes the cost vector of an LP (Model 1): maximize ŷᵀΠw over doubly stochastic Π subject to per-group linear exposure-fairness constraints |ν(Π,g)| ≤ δ — the LP solution is a fair-by-construction ranking policy for every query. (3) Training minimizes the regret L(y,ŷ) = yᵀΠ*(y)w − yᵀΠ*(ŷ)w via the SPO+ surrogate gradient Π*(2ŷᵀw − yᵀw) − Π*(yᵀw), back-propagating through the LP. Any fairness notion expressible as linear constraints on Π works (multi-group, merit-weighted). Key Results주요 결과
  • SPOFR satisfies δ-fairness with 100% query-level confidence, while FULTR/DELTR satisfy given δ levels for only ~55–85% of queries at comparable DCG (Fig. 4).
  • On German Credit and MSLR (partial-information, simulated clicks, list size 20), SPOFR's DCG converges near the theoretical maximum as δ relaxes and dominates FULTR/DELTR on the fairness–utility frontier (Fig. 3).
  • For baselines, larger λ does not consistently yield smaller fairness violation — empirically demonstrating the unreliable λ↔violation relationship.
  • Multi-group (2–7 groups on MSLR): utility degrades gracefully with group count; no prior method supports this strict per-group guarantee.
  • Cost: SPOFR* (cached/hot-started) is ~7× slower per epoch than FULTR (398s vs 60s at 100k) but converges in 1 epoch vs 18 (Table 2).

Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

SPOFR (Smart Predict and Optimize for Fair Ranking)

  • What it computes: query item features → per-item relevance scores from a shared ReLU MLP → those scores form the cost vector of a linear program whose solution is a doubly stochastic ranking-policy matrix satisfying fairness constraints → regret loss vs. the LP solution under true scores, back-propagated through the LP.
  • Why it was created: penalty-based fair LTR (weighted fairness term in the loss) gives only average-case fairness, needs λ hyperparameter search, and cannot target a specified violation magnitude.
  • Concrete example: for a 20-item list, the LP variable is a 20×20 matrix Π with Π_ij = P(item i at position j), objective ŷᵀΠw with w_i = 1/log2(1+i), plus per-group constraints |ν(Π,g)| ≤ δ.
  • How it is used in this paper: it is the paper's entire contribution; evaluated on German Credit and MSLR against FULTR and DELTR.

δ-fairness (of exposure)

  • What it computes: for each query and each group g, the absolute gap between group-average expected exposure and population-average expected exposure under policy Π: |ν(Π,g)| = |E[E(i,σ)|a_i=g] − E[E(i,σ)]| ≤ δ, where item exposure E(i,σ) = v_{σ_i} with position bias v_j = 1/(1+j)^p.
  • Why it was created: to turn "fairness in expectation over the whole dataset" into a per-query, worst-case-bounded guarantee with a directly settable tolerance δ.
  • Concrete example: δ = 0.05 means for every single query, no group's mean exposure deviates from the population mean by more than 0.05 in position-bias units.
  • How it is used in this paper: as LP constraint (6e)/(8); the x-axis of all tradeoff plots; also a merit-weighted variant (9) where group g's exposure share is scaled by its average relevance μ_g.

SPO+ regret loss (Elmachtoub & Grigas) — defined in prior work; here the regret L(y,ŷ) = yᵀΠ*(y)w − yᵀΠ*(ŷ)w (loss of optimality of the LP solution under predicted vs. true costs), with the convex SPO+ surrogate gradient Π*(2ŷ−y) − Π*(y) enabling back-propagation through the argmax of the LP. [SKIM - verify risk-bound details in deep read]

Doubly stochastic ranking policy / Birkhoff–von Neumann decomposition — from Singh & Joachims 2018: an n×n matrix of marginal item-position probabilities represents a distribution over rankings (convex combination of ≤ (n−1)²+1 permutation matrices); expected DCG becomes the linear function yᵀΠw, so no sampling is needed during training.

Condition Delta — transfer to our setting조건 차이 — 우리 설정으로의 전이

Paper's setting: inputs = per-item feature vectors per query with binary/multi-group protected attributes; outputs = doubly stochastic ranking policy per query; conditions = fixed list size n=20, exposure defined by position bias v_j = 1/(1+j)^p, fairness as linear exposure constraints, partial-information (simulated clicks with IPS debiasing), German Credit + MSLR. Our setting (SP3, exposure-frequency-as-ranking-proxy survey): exposure/impression frequency used as the operative quantity in ranking allocation; interest is in how exposure is defined, constrained, and optimized as a proxy for relevance or merit. Differences:

  • Exposure is position-bias-weighted per-ranking expectation, not cumulative impression frequency over repeated sessions: reference only — SPOFR is single-shot per query, no amortization over time [SKIM - verify].
  • Fairness currency is group-level exposure share, not item-level exposure frequency: missing link for item-level exposure guarantees (though the LP admits any linear constraint, so item-level constraints are plausibly expressible: resolved in principle via constraint swap).
  • Overall pipeline is score-modifying (scores feed an LP): the LP-over-scores inner layer is directly relevant to post-processing/optimization-layer discussions (O2) — resolved: the LP layer is separable from the learned scorer.
  • List size fixed at 20, small-scale LP per query: missing link for web-scale candidate sets.
