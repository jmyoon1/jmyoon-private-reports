Prerequisite Relation Learning: A Survey and Outlook

ACM Computing Surveys (2025) · ACM Computing Surveys 2025 · 2025

SP: SP3

P1Track B

Problem
해결하려는 문제

Prerequisite-relation learning research is fragmented: methods operate at different granularities (concept-level vs. content/object-level) and use heterogeneous feature and enhancement strategies, but no unifying framework connected resource granularity to method design, and evaluation practice (intrinsic classifier metrics vs. downstream educational impact) was inconsistently reported. This survey addresses the lack of an authoritative, structured landscape of the PR-learning task.

선행관계 학습 분야의 방법과 평가가 파편화되어 있다.

Solution / Contribution
해결책 / 기여

Survey / taxonomy paper. Core contribution is a novel two-axis classification: resource granularity (KC vs. LO) × strategy (feature type vs. cross-granularity enhancement), yielding four method families:

  1. Multi-source knowledge features for KC prerequisite learning.
  2. Semantic knowledge features for LO prerequisite learning.
  3. LO-enhanced learning for KC prerequisite learning.
  4. KC-enhanced learning for LO prerequisite learning.

Plus a structured account of evaluation (intrinsic metrics + extrinsic downstream evaluation) and applications (adaptive learning-path generation, curriculum design).

KC/LO × 자질/강화 4-family taxonomy로 분야를 정리하고 intrinsic/extrinsic 평가를 검토하는 권위 있는 서베이를 제공한다.

Key Results
주요 결과
  • No headline numeric result — survey. Organizes PR-learning methods into 4 families across KC and LO granularities.
  • Reviews both intrinsic (PR-prediction metrics) and extrinsic (learning-path / curriculum) evaluation methodologies; specific benchmark numbers require the full PDF.

Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

Prerequisite relation (PR)

  • What it computes: input = pair of learning resources (A, B) → decide dependency direction → output = binary/directed edge "A is a prerequisite of B" (must learn A before B).
  • Why it was created: to formalize dependencies between educational resources so learning paths and curricula can be ordered automatically.
  • Concrete example: "linear algebra" → "machine learning" (learn the former first).
  • How it is used in this paper: the central task the whole survey taxonomizes.

Knowledge concept (KC) vs. Learning object (LO)

  • What it computes: two granularities of "learning resource". KC = an atomic concept/topic (e.g. "eigenvalue"); LO = a container artifact (course, chapter, video, document) that teaches one or more KCs.
  • Why it was created: PR methods differ depending on whether the node is a fine-grained concept or a coarse content object; the survey separates them to structure the field.
  • Concrete example: KC = "backpropagation"; LO = a MOOC lecture video covering backpropagation + gradient descent.
  • How it is used in this paper: the two axes (KC/LO) crossed with feature-type/enhancement generate the 4-family taxonomy.

Four-family classification framework

  • What it computes: classifies PR methods by feature type × enhancement relationship into: (1) multi-source knowledge features for KC PR; (2) semantic knowledge features for LO PR; (3) LO-enhanced learning for KC PR; (4) KC-enhanced learning for LO PR.
  • Why it was created: prior surveys lacked a unifying scheme tying resource granularity to feature/enhancement strategy.
  • Concrete example: family (3) uses signals from LOs (e.g. which video precedes which) to improve concept-level prerequisite prediction.
  • How it is used in this paper: organizing spine of the method review.

Intrinsic vs. extrinsic evaluation

  • What it computes: intrinsic = direct PR-prediction metrics (precision/recall/F1/accuracy on labeled prerequisite pairs); extrinsic = downstream utility (learning-path quality, curriculum design outcomes).
  • Why it was created: to distinguish measuring the relation classifier itself from measuring its educational impact.
  • Concrete example: intrinsic = F1 on a prerequisite-pair test set; extrinsic = improvement in a generated adaptive learning path.
  • How it is used in this paper: dedicated evaluation-methodology section.

Condition Delta — transfer to our setting
조건 차이 — 우리 환경으로의 전이

Paper's setting: inputs = pairs of learning resources (knowledge concepts or learning objects) with features (multi-source knowledge, semantic text, cross-granularity signals); outputs = directed prerequisite edges; conditions = educational-technology domain (MOOCs, curricula, textbooks), supervised PR-pair labels for intrinsic evaluation. Our setting: inputs = scientific/technical concepts extracted from a corpus; outputs = a dependency knowledge base (directed prerequisite/dependency edges between concepts); conditions = KB construction from literature, less education-specific supervision. Differences:

  • Domain (education MOOCs/courses vs. general scientific-concept KB): reference only — the PR task formulation transfers, but resources (LOs) are education-specific.
  • Node granularity (KC/LO dichotomy vs. our concept nodes): resolved/partial — our concepts map to KCs; LO-enhancement families (3,4) may not apply without content-object metadata.
  • Supervision (labeled prerequisite pairs from education datasets vs. our target unsupervised/distant extraction): missing link — survey assumes labeled PR data availability.
  • Evaluation (intrinsic PR-F1 + extrinsic learning-path vs. our KB-quality metrics): reference only — intrinsic metrics adoptable, extrinsic education metrics not.
