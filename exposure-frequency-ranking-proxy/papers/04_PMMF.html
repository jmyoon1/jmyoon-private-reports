P-MMF: Provider Max-min Fairness Re-ranking in Recommender System

Xu et al. · WWW 2023 · 2023 · 48 citations

arXiv:2303.06660 · PDF · SP: SP1

P0Track A

한 줄 요약

provider 간 max-min 공정성을 online dual 공간에서 최적화하는 re-ranking을 제안한다. dual 변수 차원이 catalog 크기가 아니라 provider 수에 묶여 대규모에서도 O(T^{1/2}) regret로 동작하며, 노출 부족분으로 갱신되는 dual price는 사실상 impression pacing 제어기이다.

Problem해결하려는 문제

Existing provider-fairness re-ranking either (a) optimizes proportion fairness (TFROM, CPFair), which maximizes summed provider welfare and does not preferentially protect the worst-off providers, or (b) uses heuristic hard constraints for worst-off providers (FairRec, FairRec+) that lack theoretical guarantees in online settings and lack a tunable trade-off with user utility. MMF-based methods that do exist (Welf) are offline and computationally too costly for real-time serving.

Solution / Contribution해결책 / 기여

Formulate provider-fair recommendation as regularized online resource allocation:

$$\max_{x_t} \frac{1}{T}\sum_{t=1}^{T} g(x_t) + \lambda\, r(e), \quad r(e) = \min_{p}\left[e_p/\gamma_p\right], \quad e \le \gamma,$$

where $g(x_t) = \sum_i x_{ti} s_{u_t,i}$ is user utility and $e_p$ accumulates provider $p$'s exposures over the horizon. Solve the Lagrangian dual (Theorem 1) — variables shrink from $|I|$ integers to $|P|$ reals — with a per-arrival closed-form top-$K$ primal step and a momentum subgradient update of the dual variable projected onto the feasible region $D = \{\mu \mid \sum_{p\in S}\gamma_p \mu_p \ge -\lambda, \forall S \subseteq P\}$ (Theorem 2, Lemma 1). Regret is bounded $O(T^{1/2})$ with learning rate $\eta = O(T^{-1/2})$ (Theorem 3).

Key Results주요 결과
  • $W_1@K$ (trade-off objective, $\lambda=1$): P-MMF beats the best baseline on all 4 datasets and all $K \in \{5,10,20\}$; improvements 0.6%–2.0% typically, up to +10.9% on Steam at $K=5$ (4.443 vs. min-regularizer 4.005).
  • Pareto dominance: P-MMF's (NDCG@K, MMF@K) frontier dominates CPFair, Welf, ROAP, min-regularizer on all 4 datasets.
  • Simulation (5% Yelp): with $\lambda \in \{0.01, 0.05, 0.1\}$, the bottom-60% of providers gain [18.81%, 18.96%, 12.58%] exposure under MMF vs. [10.1%, 16.27%, 9.58%] under PF.
  • Inference: 17–18ms (GPU) / 20–40ms (CPU) per user, nearly flat as items grow to 200k, vs. DMF's ~65ms and linear growth — because dual dimension is $|P| \ll |I|$.
  • Empirical regret decreases with horizon $T$, consistent with the $O(T^{1/2})$ bound (Fig. 5).

Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

Provider Max-min Fairness (MMF) regularizer

  • What it computes: exposure vector $e \in \mathbb{R}^{|P|}$ (per-provider exposure counts) and provider weights $\gamma$ → $r(e) = \min_{p \in P} [e_p / \gamma_p]$ → a scalar equal to the worst-off provider's weighted relative exposure. Contrast: proportion fairness (PF) uses $r(e) = \sum_p \log(1 + e_p/\gamma_p)$.
  • Why it was created: PF-based provider fairness maximizes summed welfare, which can leave the worst-positioned providers under-exposed; MMF (Rawls' principle) preferentially raises the minimum, which the authors argue stabilizes the provider market.
  • Concrete example: with 2 providers, $\gamma = [6, 6]$, $T=2$ users, $K=3$ slots each → 6 total exposure slots; a solution $e = [2, 4]$ gives MMF value $\min(2/6, 4/6) = 1/3$.
  • How it is used in this paper: as the regularizer $\lambda r(e)$ added to the user-utility objective in the resource-allocation LP (Eq. 1–2) and as the evaluation metric MMF@K.

Exposure-as-resource allocation formulation

  • What it computes: user arrival stream + preference scores $s_{u,i}$ → an integer LP where the $T \times K$ ranking slots are resources allocated to providers, with binary decision vector $x_t \in \{0,1\}^{|I|}$ per user and constraint $e \le \gamma$ (max resources per provider) → re-ranked lists $L_K^F(u_t)$.
  • Why it was created: prior heuristic worst-provider guarantees (FairRec's minimum-exposure hard constraints) lack a principled user-utility/fairness trade-off and lack online theoretical guarantees.
  • Concrete example: toy example in Fig. 2: 2 users × 3 slots = 6 resources split across 2 providers.
  • How it is used in this paper: it is the core problem statement that the dual-space online algorithm then solves.

Dual-space online solution with momentum gradient descent

  • What it computes: the integer primal (dimension $|I|$ per step) → Lagrangian dual over $\mu \in \mathbb{R}^{|P|}$ with closed-form conjugates $g^*, r^*$ → per-arrival top-$K$ selection $x_t = \arg\max [g(x_t)/T - (A(\mu_t + m))^\top x_t]$ plus a momentum subgradient update of $\mu_t$ projected onto the dual feasible region $D$.
  • Why it was created: the primal has $|I|$ integer variables per time step, intractable online; the dual has only $|P| \ll |I|$ continuous variables, making inference insensitive to catalog size.
  • Concrete example: intuition given in paper — a high dual value $\mu_p$ suppresses recommendations of provider $p$'s items; inference stays ~17–40ms even as item count grows to 200k.
  • How it is used in this paper: Algorithm 1; underpins the $O(T^{1/2})$ regret bound (Theorem 3, following Balseiro et al.'s regularized online allocation framework).
Implementation Feasibility구현 가능성
  • Needs: any base ranker producing $s_{u,i}$; item→provider mapping; cvxpy (CPU) or its PyTorch differentiable layer (GPU) for the dual projection; per-provider exposure counters. Modest compute — dual dimension is provider count.
  • Code: available at https://github.com/XuChen0427/P-MMF (also a MindSpore version on Gitee).
  • Modifications: for our exposure-frequency-proxy question, would need (a) position-weighted exposure in place of slot counts, (b) possibly item-level rather than provider-level granularity (costly), (c) removing the exogenous $\gamma$ weights or learning them.
  • Unknowns: missing link: sensitivity to horizon length $T$ and to misspecified $\gamma$; missing link: behavior when preference scores are miscalibrated (the allocation trusts $s_{u,i}$ as ground-truth utility).
Follow-up Papers관련 후속 논문
  • Balseiro, Lu & Mirrokni, "Regularized online allocation problems: Fairness and beyond" (ICML 2021) — the online-allocation framework P-MMF's dual algorithm and regret analysis directly extend; foundational for the exposure-as-resource framing.
  • Patro et al., "FairRec: Two-sided fairness for personalized recommendations in two-sided platforms" (WWW 2020) — the hard-constraint worst-provider baseline P-MMF supersedes; relevant as the alternative (constraint-based) exposure-guarantee mechanism.
  • Do et al., "Two-sided fairness in rankings via Lorenz dominance" (NeurIPS 2021, "Welf") — offline welfare-maximization MMF competitor; relevant contrast on offline vs. online exposure optimization.
  • Naghiaei et al., "CPFair" (SIGIR 2022) — proportion-fairness knapsack formulation; the PF-vs-MMF contrast is the paper's central comparison axis.
Condition Delta — transfer to our setting조건 차이 — 우리 설정으로의 전이

Paper's setting: inputs = base-ranker preference scores $s_{u,i}$ (BPR/DMF/LINE/LightGCN) over a fixed item catalog with known item→provider mapping; outputs = re-ranked top-$K$ lists per sequentially arriving user; conditions = online horizon $T=256$, provider counts 23–1024, exposure defined as slot occupancy (position-discounted only inside the NDCG metric, not in the allocation). Our setting (SP1, exposure frequency as ranking proxy): we study exposure frequency — how often an item/provider appears — as a proxy signal for ranking utility or fairness, rather than as a resource to be allocated per se. Differences:

  • Exposure counted as raw slot occupancy $e_p$, position within the list ignored in the allocation objective: reference only — our setting cares whether frequency-of-appearance is a valid proxy; P-MMF shows one concrete system where raw counts are treated as the fungible resource. [SKIM - verify whether position-weighted exposure variant is discussed]
  • Provider-level aggregation (item→provider grouping) vs. our possibly item-level exposure analysis: missing link — item-level MMF would blow up $|P|$ to $|I|$, undermining the dual-space efficiency argument.
  • Requires known $\gamma$ (provider weights set heuristically $\propto |I_p|$): reference only — proxy validity in our setting should not presuppose exogenous fairness weights.
  • Online sequential arrivals with fixed horizon $T$: resolved — the dual-variable machinery transfers to any streaming ranking pipeline where per-entity exposure is tracked.
Root-cause vs. Symptom근본 원인 대 증상

Direct — the paper identifies the structural cause (heuristic/offline methods cannot trade off user utility against worst-provider exposure online with guarantees) and resolves it at that level: a principled objective, an exact dual reformulation, and a regret bound, rather than patching exposure counts post hoc.
