SP7: Impression Capping, Frequency Control, and Pacing in Feeds and Ads

15 papers · targets O1, O3 · 2019-2026

The industrial instantiation of the user's only knob — impression capping, throttling, and budget pacing over an untouched auction/ranker — tested against five accumulated lenses: the pacing=dual-targeting equivalence, the final count→position check, the SP4 discretization gap, the impressions-vs-reach proxy failure, and access-level classification.

SP7: Impression capping·pacing과 feed/광고 전달

15편 논문 · O1, O3 목표 대상 · 2019-2026

사용자의 유일한 knob의 산업적 구현 — 손대지 않은 auction/ranker 위의 impression capping, throttling, budget pacing — 을 다섯 가지 누적 렌즈(pacing=dual-targeting 등가성, count→position 최종 검사, SP4 이산화 gap, impressions-vs-reach proxy 실패, 접근 수준 분류)로 검사한다.

Entry Assumption vs. What Was Found

Expected (revised after SP1–SP6, six accumulated lenses — most decisively the five-leg pacing≡dual-targeting equivalence test and the FINAL count→position check): The literal industrial instantiation of the user's only knob, studied under budget/delivery vocabulary and citation-disconnected from the fairness cluster; test the pacing-dual equivalence formally, run the final count→position check, and look for the discretization mapping and transferable delivery guarantees.

Found: The equivalence test PASSES at algorithm-identity level: RCPacing is explicitly Dual Mirror Descent; parity-ray bid shading, fairness boost-∝-lag re-ranking, P-MMF deficit duals, and drift-plus-penalty virtual queues are the same dual update, differing only in constraint sign, denomination, and granularity. Pacing machinery closes SP4's discretization gap in count units (e*_i is directly a per-item impression budget). Hard caps are deterministic anytime ceilings with tight competitive ratios — the mirror of Fair-Learn's floor.

Assessment: Pivoted — the assumption that frequency-only interventions leave the scorer untouched by definition was inverted by the field experiment: a temporary exposure boost durably retrained the production recommender (+14.75%, persistent), inverting the intervention's goal. Immutability fails dynamically. Also: the count→position gap survives its 5th and final literature; LinkedIn Jobs runs a position-weighted ledger in production with zero theory.

사전 가정과 실제 발견

기대 (SP1–SP6 이후 수정, 여섯 개 누적 렌즈 — 특히 5개 다리의 pacing≡dual-targeting 등가성 검정과 count→position 최종 검사): 사용자의 유일한 knob의 문자 그대로의 산업적 구현으로, budget/delivery 어휘 아래 연구되며 공정성 클러스터와 인용이 단절되어 있을 것으로 기대하였다. pacing-dual 등가성의 형식 검정, count→position 최종 검사, 이산화 사상과 이전 가능한 전달 보장을 찾기로 하였다.

발견: 등가성 검정이 알고리즘 동일성 수준에서 통과한다: RCPacing은 명시적 DMD이고, parity-ray 입찰, 공정성 측 lag 비례 boost, P-MMF의 deficit dual, drift-plus-penalty 가상 큐가 모두 같은 dual 갱신이다 — 다른 것은 제약 부호, 단위, 결정 granularity뿐. pacing 기계는 SP4의 이산화 gap을 count 단위에서 닫는다(e*_i가 곧 item별 impression 예산). hard cap은 tight한 경쟁비를 갖는 결정론적 anytime 상한 — Fair-Learn floor의 거울상이다.

평가: 전환됨 — 빈도 전용 개입은 정의상 scorer를 건드리지 않는다는 가정이 필드 실험으로 반전되었다: 일시적 exposure boost가 production recommender를 지속적으로 재학습시켜(+14.75%, 지속) 개입 목표를 뒤집었다. immutability는 동적으로 실패한다. 또한 count→position gap은 다섯 번째이자 마지막 문헌에서도 생존했고, LinkedIn Jobs는 이론 없이 position 가중 ledger를 production에서 운용한다.

What SP7 Establishes

The ads delivery literature is the literal, production-scale instantiation of frequency-only control over an untouched scorer, and its control mathematics is the same formal object as the fairness side's dual-variable exposure targeting. RCPacing ([71] PercentilePacing) is explicitly Dual Mirror Descent (Balseiro–Lu–Mirrokni 2020): allocate each arriving impression to argmax_j {v_j − α_j}, then update the dual by the stochastic subgradient of the cumulative delivery deficit (−x̃ + ρ), with O(√T) regret. The parity-ray pacer ([83] ParityRayPacing) bids (v_t − ⟨λ_t, c_t⟩)/(1+μ_t) with the same subgradient dual — an additive category-level exposure-debt price on a fixed value, structurally identical to the fairness side's argsort(score + boost ∝ constraint lag) (LongTermConstraints/FairCo, SP5) and P-MMF's deficit-updated dual price (SP1). The equivalence test (uuid 6, five legs) therefore passes at algorithm-identity level: both literatures instantiate the same dual-(mirror-)descent template, differing only in constraint direction (budget ceiling vs exposure floor — a sign flip), denomination (spend/impression counts vs position-weighted exposure), and decision granularity (single-slot accept vs slate). No paper on either side states the reduction — the bridge remains a paper-shaped O4 gap, but it is now a one-page proof, not a hypothesis.

Two of the three remaining lens questions resolve positively and one closes negatively. Positively (1): pacing machinery closes SP4's open policy-weights→per-item-impression-budget mapping in count units — the marginal exposure target e*_i is the per-item budget, and probabilistic throttling + DMD deliver it smoothly over heterogeneous stochastic request streams (the case stride scheduling could not handle), with O(√T) adherence regret and an explicit smoothness metric (unsmoothness index, [71] PercentilePacing). Positively (2): guaranteed-display advertising realizes TaxRank's two-stage exposure-vector→assignment architecture at production scale, and Buchbinder's competitive ratios (3/4 tight in structured cases, 1−1/e general; [80] FreqCapTheory) are the sought POT-style predictable-utility-cost bounds for hard exposure ceilings. Negatively: the count→position transfer gap survives its fifth and final SP. Every formal guarantee in the ads literature — competitive ratios, DMD/OMD regret, GD delivery rates, k-anonymous reach bounds — is denominated in impression counts or spend. The single production system that debits position-adjusted expected charges at impression time ([72] LinkedInJobsPacing) ships with zero theory. The gap reaches O4-final status, sharpened: the position-weighted ledger exists as engineering practice, awaiting a guarantee. Finally, the Meta VRS + FAccT 2025 audit pair ([82] VRSImpressionFairness, [74] MetaVRSEval) supplies the survey's cleanest production demonstration that impression-count-denominated targets are lossy proxies: VRS passes its impression-variance compliance metric (18/18 experiments) while reducing unique reach by 9.82%, raising cost-per-reach 12.02%, and leveling down — and naive budget-splitting (a pure frequency-level, per-group impression-budget intervention) dominates the score-side bid multiplier on both reach and cost.

One finding is new to the whole survey: the immutable-scores assumption fails dynamically. A randomized field experiment ([78] FeedInterventionFieldExp) shows a temporary ranking boost intended to reduce late-night usage instead increased it +14.75%, with effects persisting weeks after the boost ended — the intervention's exposure shock became training data and durably retrained the recommender. The exposure knob is not orthogonal to the black-box scorer over time.

SP7이 확립한 것

SP7은 광고 전달 문헌이 손대지 않은 scorer 위 빈도 전용 제어의 production 규모 구현이며, 그 제어 수학이 공정성 측 dual 변수 exposure targeting과 동일한 형식적 객체임을 확립한다. RCPacing은 명시적 Dual Mirror Descent(도착 impression을 argmax_j{v_j−α_j}에 배분, 누적 전달 결손의 확률적 subgradient로 dual 갱신, O(√T) regret)이고, parity-ray pacer, FairCo류 boost-∝-lag, P-MMF deficit dual, LFG 가상 큐가 모두 같은 템플릿의 인스턴스다 — 차이는 제약 방향(예산 상한 vs exposure 하한, 부호 반전), 단위(지출/count vs position 가중), granularity(단일 슬롯 vs slate)뿐이다. 어느 쪽 문헌도 이 환원을 명시하지 않는다(gap 6). 추가로: e*_i는 곧 item별 impression 예산으로 SP4의 이산화 gap이 count 단위에서 닫히고, guaranteed-display가 TaxRank의 2단계 구조를 production에서 실현하며, Buchbinder 경쟁비(3/4, 1−1/e)가 상한 측의 예측 가능 비용 보장이다. VRS 감사(impression parity 준수, reach −9.82%)와 동적 immutability 실패는 4단계·5단계 권고의 근거가 된다.

Dominant Approach지배적 접근

The dominant method family is dual-priced delivery control over an untouched auction: a target delivery quantity per item (budget, contract, cap) is enforced by a per-item dual price updated from the cumulative delivery deficit, with the per-request decision made by comparing the item's fixed value against its dual price — via bid shading ([83] ParityRayPacing), allocation argmax ([71] PercentilePacing), threshold acceptance ([76] PrimalDualBudget, [80] FreqCapTheory), or probabilistic throttling. It became dominant because it is decentralized (one scalar state per campaign, no catalog-sized optimization), drops into existing serving infrastructure (the double-pacing reduction in [83] ParityRayPacing shows the regularized rule is an additive shading before the standard multiplicative pacer), and carries O(√T) regret under i.i.d. arrivals. The older feedback-heuristic generation ([69] SmoothBudgetDelivery's recursive pacing rate, 2013) is the P-controller ancestor; the DMD generation (2020–2024) is its principled successor, with 2024–2026 refinements handling risk (percentile-space duals, [71] PercentilePacing), forecast uncertainty (decision-calibrated conformal radii, [77] ConformalPacing), non-stationarity (constrained online learning inside a primal-dual safety envelope, [76] PrimalDualBudget), and privacy-aggregated exposure counts (Binomial-latent probabilistic capping, [75] KAnonFreqCap).

Its key limitation is denomination: every guarantee is stated in impression counts or spend. The mechanism never reads a position-bias curve — which makes count-based delivery curve-free (a genuine advantage the fairness cluster lacks, SP4/uuid 16) but also means no result bounds position-weighted realized exposure. [72] LinkedInJobsPacing is the revealing exception: its pacing ledger debits position-adjusted expected charges at impression time (projected charge = f(pCTR, page position)), proving the position-aware ledger is buildable in production — but the paper is an alternate-day engineering test with no formal guarantee. The second limitation is exposed by the VRS pair: when the delivery-layer target itself is denominated in impressions, compliance and benefit decouple — [74] MetaVRSEval shows the intervention satisfies its metric while shrinking unique reach for every group.

For the survey objectives, this SP completes the O1 taxonomy's capping/pacing column with the mechanism-level entries the objectives requested — capping = exposure ceiling (HFC/[80] FreqCapTheory hard; [70] SoftFreqCap soft; [75] KAnonFreqCap probabilistic), boosting = floor (GD contracts, regret-bounded), pacing = temporal smoothing (throttling + duals) — and settles O2's access-level question for the family: eligibility filtering, throttling, and budget debiting are score-free or score-read-only layers over an untouched ranker, making the ads delivery stack the largest deployed member of the show/don't-show family. For O3, five of fifteen papers are 2024–2026, including two live-feed field-experiment lines ([78] FeedInterventionFieldExp, [79] RerankFieldGuide) that operate over completely untouched production rankers.

Convergences수렴 지점
  • Delivery control is a score-free/read-only layer over an untouched ranker. Six independent systems — HFC filters ([70] SoftFreqCap), cap-feasibility allocation ([80] FreqCapTheory), throttling ([71] PercentilePacing), pacing-rate request selection ([69] SmoothBudgetDelivery), impression-discounting plugin over an explicitly black-box engine ([81] ImpressionDiscounting), and client-side feed reranking with no score access at all ([79] RerankFieldGuide) — all intervene at eligibility/participation/ordering level while the value model or auction runs unmodified. Mechanism: exposure control needs only a decision about participation, never the score's value. This is the industrial confirmation of uuid 14 (score-free realization axis).
  • Dual-priced budget debiting is the universal pacing mechanism, and it is the fairness cluster's dual-variable exposure targeting. [71] PercentilePacing, [83] ParityRayPacing, [76] PrimalDualBudget, and [80] FreqCapTheory's primal-dual route all maintain a per-item price updated from cumulative delivery deficit and decide by value-minus-price — the same subgradient-on-deficit template as P-MMF, FairSync, and the Stationary Controller. LFG's drift-plus-penalty virtual queue (SP6) is this same object: a queue update Q ← [Q + demand − service]+ is the projected unit-step Euclidean-mirror-map special case of the DMD dual update. The identification is mechanism-complete; no paper states it.
  • Impression counts are a lossy denomination, now shown in production. [74] MetaVRSEval (impressions parity with −9.82% reach), [81] ImpressionDiscounting and [70] SoftFreqCap (marginal impression value decays steeply with count: −20% CTR after one view, −50% after seven — so equal counts are not equal value), and [75] KAnonFreqCap (count-based reach estimates biased upward under aggregation) independently converge with uuid 8's exposure-as-lossy-proxy limitation from three different angles: parity gaming, fatigue, and measurement.
  • Exposure interventions contaminate the future scorer. [78] FeedInterventionFieldExp (boost durably retrains the recommender), [79] RerankFieldGuide (client-side down-ranking reduces engagement signals, causing the platform ranker to further suppress the content), and [81] ImpressionDiscounting/[70] SoftFreqCap (deployed frequency models reshape the very impression-conversion curves they were fitted on) agree: the exposure knob and the black-box scorer are coupled through the training loop.
Divergences분기 지점
  • Hard vs soft capping. [70] SoftFreqCap shows in-model soft capping strictly beats hard rules (+7.3% CPM; removing the hard rules on top adds +3.6%), while [80] FreqCapTheory's guarantees exist only for hard caps. Likely cause: different objectives and access levels — SFC optimizes learned revenue with full retraining rights; the competitive-ratio line optimizes worst-case value with zero learning. Under the survey's black-box constraint the empirical ordering is untested: no paper evaluates soft capping implemented as a read-only plugin (the [81] ImpressionDiscounting architecture) against hard caps under the same scorer. Unresolved.
  • VRS internal evaluation vs external audit. [82] VRSImpressionFairness reports large fairness-metric wins (76.4% NCAC reduction) with no utility cost quantified; [74] MetaVRSEval finds reduced reach, +12% advertiser cost, and leveling down on the same system. Cause adjudicated by the audit itself: the two evaluations use different denominations (impression variance vs unique people reached) — both results are simultaneously true, which is the proxy failure. The [73] FairAdDelivery simulation predicted exactly this leveling-down outcome a year earlier; the audit confirms it in the wild.
Prior Beliefs and Surprises (full)사전 믿음과 놀라움 (전문)

What the entry rationale expected to find:

The literal industrial instantiation of the user's only knob, studied under budget/delivery vocabulary, citation-disconnected from the fairness cluster; test the pacing-dual equivalence, the final count→position check, discretization mapping, delivery guarantees as transferable finite-T bounds, and guaranteed delivery as the ads analogue of deterministic anytime floors.

Confirmed:

  • Citation disconnection confirmed at note level (zero cross-cluster links, Phase 3.7) while the mechanisms are identical — the bridge-gap-for-O4 reading was correct.
  • The equivalence test passes, and more strongly than hypothesized: not analogy but shared descent from the same named algorithm (DMD), with the drift-plus-penalty virtual-queue identity holding as the Euclidean special case.
  • Smooth delivery / throttling is the production stride-scheduling analogue and closes SP4's discretization gap in count units; the ads convergence guarantees (O(√T) DMD regret, GD delivery rates) are transferable finite-T exposure bounds — count-denominated, as feared.
  • Guaranteed-delivery is the ads analogue of exposure floors, but its floors are regret-bounded, not deterministic-anytime; the deterministic-anytime object on the ads side is the ceiling (HFC/caps), the mirror image of Fair-Learn's floor.

Surprised:

  • (a) Assumed the ads side was purely count-based; (b) found [72] LinkedInJobsPacing debiting position-adjusted expected charges in production; (c) the assumption was wrong because monetary charge accounting naturally imports pCTR-and-position weighting — but it ships with zero theory, so the count→position gap is sharpened (the ledger exists, the guarantee does not) rather than closed.
  • (a) Assumed frequency-only interventions leave the black-box scorer untouched by definition; (b) [78] FeedInterventionFieldExp shows a temporary exposure boost durably retrained the production recommender, inverting the intervention's goal; (c) the assumption treats immutability as static — in deployed systems the exposure knob writes the scorer's future training data. This is a new, survey-level load-bearing consideration.
  • (a) Expected the score-side intervention (VRS bid multiplier) to at least weakly dominate crude frequency-level alternatives; (b) naive per-group budget-splitting beat VRS on reach and cost for every group; (c) because impression-denominated score-side control optimizes the wrong unit, while budget-splitting fixes per-group frequency budgets directly — evidence that the user's knob, correctly denominated, can outperform score adjustment.

Key Concepts핵심 개념

Budget pacing / pacing rate

  • What it computes: per-time-slot spend target b_t (Σb_t = B) + last slot's realized spend, traffic, and win rate → recursive multiplicative update → the fraction of eligible requests the campaign participates in next slot.
  • Why it was created: greedy spending exhausts budgets early and misses better later opportunities; a feedback-controlled participation fraction ties delivery to a per-slot schedule.
  • Concrete example: if slot t spent $2 against a $1 target with unchanged traffic, the pacing rate is halved for slot t+1 ([69] SmoothBudgetDelivery Eq. 4).
  • Role in this SP: the ads-side P-controller — the same reactive tracking-error feedback as FairCo's exposure controller (SP5), operating on spend instead of exposure debt.

Probabilistic throttling / pass-through rate (PTR)

  • What it computes: per (request, campaign) pair, a probability in [0,1] that the campaign participates in the allocation at all; the bid is randomly zeroed otherwise.
  • Why it was created: bid modification controls spend poorly when the bid→win-rate curve shifts; throttling decouples delivery control from value calculation.
  • Concrete example: [71] PercentilePacing Eq. 13 factors PTR into a base budget rate × a dual-position decay × a performance tilt giving higher pass-through to higher-CTR traffic.
  • Role in this SP: randomized show/don't-show eligibility control — the industrial generalization of the fairness cluster's inclusion decisions, and the delivery half of the SP4 discretization mapping.

Dual Mirror Descent (DMD)

  • What it computes: online allocation via Lagrangian duals — assign each arriving impression to argmax_j {v_j − α_j} among non-exhausted campaigns, then mirror-descent-update α with the stochastic subgradient of the cumulative delivery deficit (−x̃ + ρ); O(√T) regret under i.i.d. arrivals.
  • Why it was created: general online allocation under budget constraints without re-solving an LP per request (Balseiro–Lu–Mirrokni 2020).
  • Concrete example: RCPacing = DMD run in the percentile space of each campaign's CTR distribution, with an Itakura-Saito mirror map shrinking steps near percentile 1.0 ([71] PercentilePacing).
  • Role in this SP: the shared ancestor of both literatures' controllers — the algorithm identity behind the pacing=dual-targeting equivalence (uuid 6).

Hard vs soft frequency capping

  • What it computes: hard (HFC): a serving-time filter removing ads whose per-user view count exceeds a fixed threshold — a deterministic, anytime exposure ceiling enforced by feasibility. Soft (SFC): a learned per-frequency-bin weight added to the scorer's logit, pricing fatigue into pCTR instead of excluding by fiat.
  • Why it was created: HFC models the industry rule ("max 5 campaign views/week"); SFC replaces the arbitrary rule because a frequency-blind model deflates first-view scores toward an average of first- and repeat-view CTR ([70] SoftFreqCap).
  • Concrete example: learned campaign-frequency weights fall monotonically from 0 to ≈ −1.4 at ≥25 views; dropping HFC rules once SFC is in-model added +3.6% CPM.
  • Role in this SP: HFC is the deterministic anytime ceiling mirroring Fair-Learn's deterministic anytime count floor (SP6); SFC is the in-model (retraining-required) alternative that wins when the constraint set allows it.

Guaranteed-display (GD) delivery

  • What it computes: publisher sells fixed impression quantities per campaign (a contracted exposure vector), then an online allocator assigns arriving impressions to contracts so each is delivered evenly and completely.
  • Why it was created: brand advertisers buy impression volume, not clicks; delivery shortfalls are contract breaches.
  • Concrete example: RCPacing delivers >99.5% of contracted impressions at billion-request Taobao scale while halving the unsmoothness index vs prior duals.
  • Role in this SP: the production realization of TaxRank's two-stage exposure-vector→assignment architecture (SP3 lens), with delivery floors as regret-bounded (not deterministic) guarantees.

Impression variance (shuffle distance) and impressions vs reach

  • What it computes: shuffle distance = ½·Σ_g |eligible ratio_g − delivery ratio_g| over impressions — the minimum impression fraction that must move between groups to match the eligible distribution. Reach = number of unique users shown the ad; impressions = total display count.
  • Why it was created: the Meta/DoJ settlement's operationalization of fair delivery ([82] VRSImpressionFairness); the impressions/reach distinction is elevated to a metric-design flaw by the external audit.
  • Concrete example: showing an ad 100× to one woman and 1× each to 100 men achieves zero gender impression-variance and a 100:1 reach disparity ([74] MetaVRSEval).
  • Role in this SP: the production case of exposure-count parity as a lossy proxy for people-reached — the SP7 leg of uuid 8.

Impression discounting

  • What it computes: a candidate's (user,item) impression history (count, recency, position, user visit frequency) → fitted decay regression → a multiplier d ∈ (0,1] applied to the black-box engine's score: rank by R·d.
  • Why it was created: high-scored but repeatedly ignored items dominate lists; no-action impressions are implicit negative feedback the base scorer never sees ([81] ImpressionDiscounting).
  • Concrete example: LinkedIn PYMK conversion falls steeply over impressions 1–6; deploying the discount lifted invitation P@10 by ~12–13% in A/B.
  • Role in this SP: the feed-native, score-read-only sibling of frequency capping — a plugin explicitly designed for an unmodifiable engine, and the direct O1 taxonomy anchor for exposure-count-driven demotion.

Forced exploration / inaction zone

  • What it computes: a platform boost exposes content the incumbent policy under-supplies → user responses become training signals → the post-campaign policy durably shifts toward the boosted category. The inaction zone is the state where under-supply prevents the system from ever collecting the data that would correct it.
  • Why it was created: prior intervention evaluations treated boosts as static nudges with short-term metrics ([78] FeedInterventionFieldExp).
  • Concrete example: a 1–5 a.m. "sleep reminder" video boost raised treated users' late-night usage +14.75% and engagement +2.18%, persisting weeks after removal.
  • Role in this SP: field-experimental proof that exposure-frequency interventions retrain the scorer — the dynamic failure of the immutable-scores abstraction.

Open Questions After SP7SP7 이후 미해결 질문

  • Position-weighted delivery guarantees: genuinely unsolved, final status. Missing link flagged in [71] PercentilePacing (single-slot one-hot allocation is load-bearing in the regret proof), [72] LinkedInJobsPacing (position-adjusted debiting deployed with no stated guarantee or controller specification), and [80] FreqCapTheory (caps are unit counts; no exposure-response model). Combined with SP1/SP3/SP5/SP6, no literature in this survey provides a realized-exposure guarantee denominated in position-weighted units for frequency-only control. What would resolve it: a DMD-style pacing theorem where the debit per impression is the position-bias weight γ(k) of the slot occupied, with regret/violation measured in the same units.
  • The pacing↔fairness formal reduction is unstated. Zero citation overlap between the two clusters (confirmed at Phase 3.7); both build on the same dual-descent template ([71] PercentilePacing, [83] ParityRayPacing explicitly on Balseiro–Lu–Mirrokni). No paper states the sign-flip reduction (budget ceiling ↔ exposure floor) or transfers guarantees across it. What would resolve it: a bridge paper proving the two constraint families are duals of one template and porting the ads side's drift-robustness devices (percentile-space duals) to exposure floors and the fairness side's slate/position structure to pacing.
  • No delivery guarantee survives endogenous traffic. [83] ParityRayPacing (regret requires i.i.d. inputs; reactive inputs flagged as missing link), [77] ConformalPacing (calibration exchangeability breaks when the deployed policy changes future exposure distributions), and [78] FeedInterventionFieldExp/[79] RerankFieldGuide (the loop is real and large) jointly leave open: exposure-frequency control with guarantees when the request stream and the scorer respond to the controller. This compounds uuid 23's degenerate-regime gap — SP7's papers assume either known values ([80] FreqCapTheory) or self-estimated pCTR ([69] SmoothBudgetDelivery, [70] SoftFreqCap); none handles fixed exogenous noisy scores.
  • Fatigue vs selection confound. [81] ImpressionDiscounting fits the impression→conversion decay as if causal, but items surviving many impressions without conversion may simply be worse matches (selection); [70] SoftFreqCap measures fatigue curves while its own model shapes the serving distribution (the v=25 anomaly). No paper in the family separates causal fatigue from selection with an identification strategy — yet the discount magnitude applied to the black-box score depends entirely on which it is.
