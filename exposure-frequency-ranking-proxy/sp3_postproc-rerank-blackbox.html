SP3: Post-processing Re-ranking over Black-box Scorers

15 papers · targets O2, O1 · 2019-2026

Directly tests O2's feasibility question: post-processing methods take scores as read-only input and re-rank — the closest relaxation of the score-immutable constraint. This SP annotates every technique on two axes (access level; per-instance slate reordering required?) and checks whether any paper provides a Bayes-optimal exposure-fair post-processor over elicited/black-box scores.

SP3: Black-box scorer 위의 post-processing re-ranking

15편 논문 · O2, O1 목표 대상 · 2019-2026

O2의 실행 가능성 질문을 직접 검증한다: post-processing은 score를 읽기 전용 입력으로 받아 re-rank하는, score 불변 제약의 가장 가까운 완화이다. 모든 기법을 접근 수준과 slate 재배열 필요 여부의 두 축으로 주석하고, 引出된 black-box score 위의 Bayes-optimal exposure-fair post-processor 존재 여부를 검사한다.

Entry Assumption vs. What Was Found

Expected (revised after SP1: add the slate-reordering axis; revised after SP2: check for a Bayes-optimal exposure-fair post-processor over elicited scores): Post-processing as the closest relaxation of the score-immutable constraint; the pre-loop read expected the strict frequency-only subset to come only from SP5/SP6/SP7, with SP3 papers being read-only reorderers.

Found: All genuine post-processors are score-read-only (third confirmation of the KB claim). Feasibility of consuming corrupted scores is decided by the fairness target, not the method: merit-blind targets (FA*IR proportions, prefix counts, parity) never reference scores in the constraint. The taxation line operates at top-K-inclusion level with the first controllability guarantees (Lipschitz knob, POT bound).

Assessment: Surprised twice — (a) SP3 was expected to contain no frequency-only control, but TaxRank's intermediate e* is literally an item-level impression-frequency schedule projected onto users by optimal transport; (b) the Bayes-optimality check came back empty after a dedicated 15-paper pass, upgrading the gap to O4-ready with completed-search evidence.

사전 가정과 실제 발견

기대 (SP1 이후 수정: slate 재배열 축 추가; SP2 이후 수정: 引出 score 위 Bayes-optimal post-processor 검사 추가): score 불변 제약의 가장 가까운 완화로서의 post-processing을 기대하였고, 사전 읽기에서는 엄밀한 빈도 전용 부분집합이 SP5/SP6/SP7에서만 나올 것이며 SP3 논문들은 읽기 전용 재배열기일 것으로 보았다.

발견: 모든 진성 post-processor가 score-read-only임이 세 번째로 확인되었다. 오염된 score를 소비할 수 있는가는 방법이 아니라 공정성 목표가 결정한다: merit-blind 목표(FA*IR의 비율, 접두사 개수, parity)는 제약식에서 score를 참조하지 않는다. taxation 라인은 top-K 포함 수준에서 동작하며 최초의 제어 가능성 보장(Lipschitz knob, POT 상한)을 갖는다.

평가: 두 번 예상과 다름 — (a) SP3에는 빈도 전용 제어가 없을 것으로 보았으나, TaxRank의 중간 객체 e*는 문자 그대로 item 수준 impression-frequency 스케줄이며 optimal transport로 사용자에게 배정된다. (b) Bayes-optimality 검사는 전용 15편 검토 후에도 공백으로 확인되어, 해당 gap이 완료된 탐색 증거를 갖춘 O4 후보로 승격되었다.

What SP3 Establishes

Post-processing re-ranking is uniformly feasible at the score-read-only access level — every genuine post-processor in this SP's 15 papers consumes fixed scores without modifying them — but the SP question decomposes into four parts, and the answers diverge:

  1. Reordering axis: most re-rankers require per-instance slate-reordering rights. The surprise is that the 2024–2025 taxation line ([32] TaxRank, [34] ElasticityTradeoff) operates at the top-K-inclusion level: its decision variable is x_{u,i} ∈ {0,1} (show item i to user u or not), and TaxRank's intermediate object is an item-level exposure vector e* — literally an impression-frequency allocation — projected onto per-user lists by optimal transport. This grows the show/don't-show family (previously FairRec-only among fairness papers) and equips it, for the first time, with controllability guarantees: Lipschitz continuity in the fairness knob and a Price-of-Taxation bound POT ≤ 1 − O(|U|^{−t/(1+t)}). The catch persists from SP1: these guarantees are in count/score-mass exposure units, and no paper transfers them to position-weighted exposure.
  2. Bayes-optimality over elicited scores: absent. After this dedicated pass, no post-processing paper provides a ranking analogue of LinearPost — every optimality statement (LP optimality in [01] FoE, the POT bound in [32] TaxRank, the transfer-ratio theorem in [34] ElasticityTradeoff, LP-constructed δ-fairness in [31] SPOFR) conditions on scores being the truth. [34] ElasticityTradeoff explicitly flags miscalibrated scores as untested; [36] SinkhornFairRank flags score-error sensitivity as unstudied; [14] FairPGRank demonstrates the failure mode. KB claim 10 is confirmed, now with completed-search evidence for O4.
  3. Which re-rankers could consume calibrated elicited scores: the answer is decided by the fairness target, not the method. Merit-blind targets (FA*IR's minimum proportion p, Celis's prefix-count bounds, demographic-parity exposure) never reference scores in the constraint, so constraint satisfaction survives arbitrary score corruption — only utility degrades. Merit-proportional targets (disparate-treatment ratio, equity-of-attention, quality-weighted shares, tax/elasticity objectives over score-mass) import score error directly into the definition of "fair", so calibrated magnitudes are necessary for the target to be meaningful at all. The survey's normative WYSIWYG-vs-WAE axis ([37] FairRankingSurvey) is, operationally, exactly this score-trust axis.
  4. The mechanism explaining why trade-off severity varies (SP1's flatness law, uuid 5) now has a formal account: the fairness→accuracy transfer ratio is governed by inter-group utility elasticity E_{r,p} = (v̄_r/v̄_p)^{−|t|} ([34] ElasticityTradeoff, Thm 3) — flat relevance means low elasticity means cheap fairness.

SP3이 확립한 것

SP3는 post-processing re-ranking이 score-read-only 수준에서 일관되게 실행 가능함을 확립하되, 질문을 네 갈래로 분해한다. (1) 재배열 축: 대부분의 re-ranker는 인스턴스별 slate 재배열 권한을 요구하지만, 2024–2025 taxation 라인은 top-K 포함(x_{u,i}∈{0,1})을 결정 변수로 삼고 item 수준 exposure 벡터 e*를 optimal transport로 사용자 리스트에 투영한다 — show/don't-show 계열이 처음으로 Lipschitz 연속성과 Price-of-Taxation 상한이라는 제어 가능성 보장을 얻었다(단 count 단위). (2) 引出 score 위의 Bayes-optimality: 부재 — 모든 최적성 진술이 score가 참이라는 조건 위에 있다. (3) 오염 score 소비 가능성은 목표가 결정한다: merit-blind 목표는 구조적으로 생존, merit-proportional 목표는 score 오차를 목표 정의에 수입한다. (4) exposure 단위 선택(count/position/impact)은 수혜자를 뒤집는다(43.2% 사례).

Convergences수렴 지점
  • All post-processing re-ranking is score-read-only; none needs more access. [32] TaxRank, [34] ElasticityTradeoff, [36] SinkhornFairRank, [38] FAIRTopK, [39] CelisFairRank, [33] OnlinePostProc, [35] DualSideOnline plus SP1's [01] FoE, [09] FairSort, [11] EquityOfAttention all consume fixed scores. The only SP3 papers requiring more are the deliberate contrast classes ([29] PairwiseFairness in-processing; [31] SPOFR end-to-end training around a read-only LP core). Confirms KB uuid 2 for a third consecutive SP.
  • Naive additive re-scoring is unstable; three independent mechanisms converge. [32] TaxRank proves item-level additive taxation non-Lipschitz with unbounded accuracy cost; [34] ElasticityTradeoff derives the transfer-ratio mechanism governing when the cost is large; [09] FairSort independently observes greedy knapsack re-ranking distributes utility loss unpredictably across users. The field's response is uniform: add a control-theoretic guarantee (POT bound; elasticity-aware taxing; per-user NDCG floor).
  • Exposure units are fragmented into three non-interchangeable currencies. Prefix/inclusion counts ([38] FAIRTopK, [39] CelisFairRank, [32] TaxRank), position-weighted exposure ([01] FoE, [11] EquityOfAttention, [36] SinkhornFairRank's e(k)), and impact/outcomes ([36] SinkhornFairRank's NSW). [37] FairRankingSurvey documents that no consensus exposure definition exists; [36] SinkhornFairRank quantifies the gap — an exposure-fair ranking leaves 43.2% of items worse off in impact. This independently confirms uuid 8 (exposure is a lossy proxy for provider utility; here the conversion factor is per-user relevance).
  • Every optimality guarantee conditions on trusted scores. LP optimality ([01] FoE), POT bound ([32] TaxRank), transfer-ratio theory ([34] ElasticityTradeoff, which names miscalibration untested), Sinkhorn quality parity ([36] SinkhornFairRank, which names score-error sensitivity unstudied), and δ-fairness-plus-regret ([31] SPOFR, whose optimality region assumes reachable ŷ). None elicits or models score uncertainty. Confirms uuid 4 and uuid 10.
Divergences분기 지점
  • Hard constraints vs continuous taxation — each side proves the other's defect. [39] CelisFairRank proves score re-weighting (soft objectives) structurally cannot enforce representation when one group's scores dominate, and [31] SPOFR shows penalty methods satisfy a given δ for only 55–85% of queries vs 100% for constraint-in-the-feasible-set; conversely [32] TaxRank proves hard binary constraint selection is non-Lipschitz with unbounded accuracy cost. Likely cause: different desiderata (worst-case per-instance guarantee vs smooth operator controllability), not contradictory mathematics. Unresolved: no method offers both a per-instance hard floor and a Lipschitz-continuous, cost-bounded knob.
  • Does read-only fairness re-ranking necessarily cost accuracy? Most SP3 papers pay NDCG for fairness (the entire trade-off literature), yet [35] DualSideOnline claims a "double-win" (fairness and accuracy both improve under model-agnostic post-ranking), and [37] FairRankingSurvey synthesizes three prior sightings (DELTR, Sühr, Mehrotra) where optimizing fairness increased utility. Likely cause: when the base scorer is stale or miscalibrated, exposure redistribution can correct scoring error as a side effect — the same mechanism as uuid 9's exposure-signal-carries-relevance finding. Unresolved (35 is abstract-tier; no paper isolates the mechanism).
Prior Beliefs and Surprises (full)사전 믿음과 놀라움 (전문)

What the entry rationale expected to find (revised after SP1/SP2):

Post-processing as the closest relaxation of the score-immutable constraint; annotate the per-instance-reordering axis (read-only necessary but not sufficient); check for a Bayes-optimal exposure-fair post-processor over elicited scores (LinearPost's ranking analogue); label which re-rankers could consume calibrated elicited scores.

Confirmed:

  • Read-only dominance holds across all genuine post-processors (uuid 2, third confirmation).
  • No Bayes-optimal exposure-fair post-processing exists — the dedicated check found nothing; uuid 10 is confirmed with completed-search evidence, upgrading it to an O4-ready gap.

Surprised:

  • (a) Assumed (per the pre-loop landscape read: "most methods are read-only reorderers, not frequency-only — strict subset must come from SP5/SP6/SP7") that SP3 contains no frequency-only control. Found instead that the taxation line's decision variable is top-K inclusion: [32] TaxRank's intermediate e* is literally an item-level impression-frequency schedule, and ElasticRank operates on score-mass inclusion. The assumption was wrong because "re-ranking" vocabulary obscured that these methods never permute within-list positions as their controlled object — they decide who gets shown.
  • (b) Assumed calibrated-elicited-score feasibility would split by method family. Found it splits by fairness target: merit-blind targets survive arbitrary score corruption structurally; merit-proportional ones cannot. The survey's normative worldview axis (WYSIWYG/WAE) turns out to be operationally the score-trust axis — a reading none of the papers states explicitly.
  • (c) The magnitude of the exposure-unit gap: an exposure-fair allocation leaves 43.2% of items worse off in impact terms ([36] SinkhornFairRank) — the count/position/impact unit choice is not a modeling nicety but flips who benefits.

KB impact:

  • UUID 2: confirmed — all SP3 post-processors are score-read-only.
  • UUID 3: refined — the show/don't-show family now includes TaxRank/ElasticRank (inclusion-level, count-based) with the first controllability guarantees; position-weighted absence persists.
  • UUID 5: refined — the flatness law acquires a mechanism: transfer ratio governed by inter-group utility elasticity.
  • UUID 8: confirmed — impact-vs-exposure divergence (43.2% worse off) is the same lossy-proxy phenomenon with conversion factor = per-user relevance.
  • UUID 10: confirmed — dedicated pass found no Bayes-optimal result; every optimality statement conditions on trusted scores.

Key Concepts핵심 개념

(Position-weighted exposure, doubly stochastic ranking policies, and BvN decomposition: see SP1 Key Concepts. Below are concepts new in SP3.)

Ranked group fairness (per-prefix binomial test)

  • What it computes: a top-k ranking + minimum protected proportion p + significance α → one-sided binomial test at every prefix i = 1..k at a corrected level α_c (family-wise Type-I error = α, computed in O(k²)) → accept/reject verdict plus a table m_{α,p}(i) of minimum protected counts per position.
  • Why it was created: averaged parity measures allow a consumer stopping at any depth to see arbitrarily skewed proportions; a per-prefix statistical criterion closes that hole, and naive per-prefix testing inflates Type-I error (Šidák over-corrects under positive dependence).
  • Concrete example: for k = 1000, p = 0.5, α = 0.1, the corrected per-test level is α_c = 0.0096; with p = 0.4, α = 0.1, at least 1 protected candidate is required in the top 5.
  • Role in this SP: the constraint [38] FAIRTopK enforces greedily; the "probability-based" measurement family in [37] FairRankingSurvey's dichotomy; a score-free fairness target relevant to the elicited-scores question.

Constrained ranking maximization (prefix-count constraints in the feasible set)

  • What it computes: value matrix W (item i at position j), property sets, per-prefix lower/upper count bounds → maximize Σ W_ij x_ij subject to the bounds → a single deterministic ranking. Exploits nested constraints + Monge weights for exact DP (constant type count q) and a (Δ+2)-approximation for overlapping groups; feasibility alone is NP-hard in general.
  • Why it was created: score re-weighting structurally cannot enforce representation when one group's raw scores dominate — moving fairness from the objective into the feasible set makes it unconditional on score skew.
  • Concrete example: U_k = ⌈k/2⌉ forces at most half of every top-k prefix to be one gender, whatever the scores say.
  • Role in this SP: [39] CelisFairRank is the theory anchor of the hard-constraint branch; one pole of the hard-constraints-vs-continuous-tax divergence.

Item-level tax policy vs progressive (utility-difference) taxation

  • What it computes: Theorem 1 of [32] TaxRank shows regularization- and constraint-based fair re-ranking both reduce to adding a per-item additive term μ_i to each item's score before top-K selection ("item-level flat tax"). Tax-rank replaces this with maximizing isoelastic welfare Σ γ_i v_i^{1−t}/(1−t) over inclusion variables — taxing the utility gap between item pairs ("progressive tax"), with tax rate t spanning accuracy-first (t=0) → proportional/Nash (t=1) → max-min (t→∞).
  • Why it was created: item-level additive taxation is provably non-Lipschitz in the tax rate (binary selection + non-differentiable fairness terms create breakpoints), so the fairness knob produces erratic jumps and unbounded, unpredictable accuracy loss.
  • Concrete example: two items, K=1: at t=1 utilities become proportional to weights (2:5); at t→∞ they equalize.
  • Role in this SP: the unifying mechanism claim over the whole prior re-ranking family and the core of the SP's strongest new guarantee ([32] TaxRank, generalized by [34] ElasticityTradeoff).

Price of Taxation (POT) and continuity

  • What it computes: POT(t) = (Acc(0) − Acc(t))/Acc(0), the fraction of accuracy sacrificed at tax rate t. Tax-rank proves POT ≤ 1 − O(|U|^{−t/(1+t)}) and Lipschitz continuity of outputs in t.
  • Why it was created: prior fair re-ranking gave no bound predicting how much accuracy a given fairness level costs — operators tuned λ blind.
  • Concrete example: empirically, Tax-rank's POT curve is smooth and below P-MMF's across t ∈ [0,1]; Lorenz curves shift near-uniformly as t grows 10-fold vs erratic shifts for FairRec/P-MMF/Welf.
  • Role in this SP: the first predictable-cost guarantee for a show/don't-show-level exposure controller — the property FairRec's EF1/MMS guarantees lack.

Utility elasticity and transfer ratio

  • What it computes: E_{r,p} = ∂v_r/∂v_p = (v̄_r/v̄_p)^{−|t|} — how much a "rich" group's utility moves when a "poor" group's is nudged; the fairness-tax-to-accuracy-loss transfer ratio is γ = 1 − 1/(1 + k(E_{r,p})). Also proves every standard fairness metric (max-min, Jain, entropy, proportional, α-fair) is an instance of one parametric family f(v;t), so metric choice = elasticity assumption.
  • Why it was created: the accuracy-fairness trade-off magnitude was observed to vary across datasets/metrics with no mechanism; elasticity (from commodity-tax theory) is that mechanism.
  • Concrete example: with Jain's index (t=−1), a poor group at utility 1 vs rich at 3 gives E = 3: promoting a poor-group item is weighted 3× more.
  • Role in this SP: [34] ElasticityTradeoff supplies the mechanism behind SP1's utility-cost flatness law (uuid 5) and the EF-Curve evaluation exposing one-sidedness of P-MMF/CPFair.

Merit-blind vs merit-aware fairness targets (operationalized worldviews)

  • What it computes: a classification of fairness targets by whether the target's definition references the scores. Merit-blind: minimum proportions, prefix counts, exposure parity — constraint satisfaction is checkable without trusting scores. Merit-aware: exposure ∝ merit (DTR/DIR, equity of attention, quality-weighted shares) — the target itself is computed from scores.
  • Why it was created: [37] FairRankingSurvey maps every method to a WYSIWYG (scores trustworthy) or WAE (score gaps = observation bias) worldview; this SP reads that axis operationally as robustness to score corruption.
  • Concrete example: FA*IR with p = 0.4 stays exactly as "fair" if every score is corrupted (only its utility cost changes); a DTR = 1 exposure allocation computed from corrupted merit estimates is fair only nominally — [14] FairPGRank shows it can become less fair in true merit as the fairness weight rises.
  • Role in this SP: the deciding axis for which re-rankers can consume calibrated elicited scores (SP question, part 4).

Impact-based fairness (NSW objective)

  • What it computes: per-item impact Imp_i = Σ_u Σ_k r(u,i)·e(k)·x_{uik} (exposure converted to expected clicks/outcomes via relevance), maximizing Nash Social Welfare Σ_i log Imp_i over doubly stochastic ranking matrices; solved at scale by an exact Sinkhorn reparametrization (~1000× faster than Mosek).
  • Why it was created: exposure-based fairness leaves the exposure→outcome relationship uncontrolled; equalizing exposure does not equalize what items actually receive.
  • Concrete example: the exposure-fair baseline (ExpFair) leaves 43.20% of items worse off in impact terms than uniform, while the NSW solution leaves 0.04% worse off.
  • Role in this SP: [36] SinkhornFairRank adds a third exposure unit (impact) to count-based and position-weighted, and independently confirms exposure is a lossy proxy for outcomes (uuid 8).

Open Questions After SP3SP3 이후 미해결 질문

  • Bayes-optimal exposure-fair post-processing over elicited scores (uuid 10): genuinely open after a dedicated pass. Missing link flagged in [34] ElasticityTradeoff (elasticity on miscalibrated utilities untested), [36] SinkhornFairRank (fair-policy sensitivity to score estimation error unstudied), [31] SPOFR (optimality assumes reachable predicted scores), plus SP2's [17] BlackBoxFairPost/[16] FairLLMRec. What would resolve it: a theorem identifying which statistic of a black-box scorer (calibrated relevance probabilities?) is sufficient for the optimal exposure-constrained ranking policy, with a regret bound under elicitation error. This is now a completed-search-backed O4 gap.
  • Count→position-weighted transfer for inclusion-level control: the show/don't-show family (FairRec → [32] TaxRank → [34] ElasticityTradeoff → TEC) guarantees only count/score-mass exposure; [38] FAIRTopK and [39] CelisFairRank carry the same missing link (prefix counts say nothing about attention within positions). No paper bounds the position-weighted exposure a pure inclusion controller induces. What would resolve it: a bound relating inclusion frequency to expected position-weighted exposure under a known within-list ranking policy.
  • Realized-vs-target exposure concentration: [32] TaxRank samples lists from a continuous relaxation with no bound on the gap between realized exposure and target e* under finite users (its own missing link); [01] FoE's guarantees are expectation-only over BvN sampling (SP1 seed). No SP3 paper gives per-window or concentration guarantees. What would resolve it: anytime/finite-sample exposure floors for stochastic realization — SP4's remit.
  • A mechanism with both hard per-instance floors and continuous controllability (from Divergence 1): appears in no paper; the two guarantee types are currently bought with mutually exclusive machinery (binary feasible-set constraints vs continuous relaxation).
