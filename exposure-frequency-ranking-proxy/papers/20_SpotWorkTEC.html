Designing Recommendation Exposure and Favorite Lists: A Field Experiment in a Spot-Work Platform

Kazuki Sekiya et al. · arXiv · 2026

arXiv:2606.17397 · PDF · SP: SP2

P3Track B

한 줄 요약

스팟워크 플랫폼 Timee의 production 필드 실험으로, 수정하지 않은 engagement scorer 위에서 exposure 차감식 eligibility 임계값(TEC)만으로 플랫폼 진짜 목표(매칭 +9.0/현day)를 올리고 engagement는 유지했다. FairRec의 show/don't-show 패턴의 첫 production 후속으로, 사용자 설정에 가장 근접한 사례다.

Problem해결하려는 문제

Production recommenders on matching platforms optimize intermediate engagement metrics (predicted favoriting/click probability). In spot-work markets where offerings are scarce, short-lived, and filled first-come-first-served, this proxy is misaligned with the platform's real objective (realized matches): exposure concentrates on popular templates with few viable openings while templates with genuine unmet labor demand are under-exposed. Prior exposure-limiting designs (fixed quotas, stable-matching-based recommenders) either require infeasible per-market parameter tuning or are not parallelizable at production scale.

Solution / Contribution해결책 / 기여

A ladder of exposure-control mechanisms replacing pure engagement-proxy ranking:

  1. SQ: uniform per-template exposure quota $q$, allocated by round-robin RSD.
  2. AQ: quotas proportional to a supply-side score $p_k = w^0 x^0_k + w^1 x^1_k$ (posted capacity + unfilled capacity).
  3. TEC (deployed): converts scores into eligibility thresholds so each worker's list is computed independently in parallel; scores carry over rounds and are debited only by realized exposure ($p'_k = p_k + p^+_k - p^-_k$, total score conserved), so low-attractiveness but high-demand templates accumulate eligibility. Within the eligible choice set, ranking still uses predicted favoriting probability — the proxy is constrained, not discarded.

Validated by (a) simulation calibrated to Timee Hokkaido 2024 data and (b) a prefecture-level randomized rollout (treatment: Aomori; control: Iwate; Jan 12–Feb 11, 2026) analyzed with DID-style ITT regressions and distribution-regression DID.

Key Results주요 결과
  • Calibrated simulation per-round job-finding rate: Greedy 57.6% → SQ 64.2% → AQ 69.5% → TEC 70.0%; fill rate 67.4% → 82.2% (Greedy → TEC).
  • Field DID: +9.045 matches per prefecture-day (p<0.05); daily favorites unchanged (−40.5, n.s.) — matches rose without raising the engagement metric.
  • Recommendations per active template +0.571 (p<0.05); distribution-regression DID: share of active template-days with ≤3 recommendations falls ~6.1pp (95% CI [−0.089, −0.030]); zero-subscriber mass also falls.
  • Impression-level: favoriting probability +0.005 (p<0.01) and match-conditional-on-favorite +0.001 (p<0.05) under TEC; the favoriting gain is driven by reduced repeat exposure (novelty), not by open-slot visibility — templates with visible open slots are less likely to be favorited (−0.008).
  • Transition simulation: exposure effects saturate by ~25 rounds, but match/fulfillment gains accrue over 75–200+ rounds — the one-month rollout captured only the early transition.

Contribution vs Claim기여 대 주장
  • Stated claims: engagement-proxy optimization causes misdirected concentration; TEC fixes it scalably; the design principle generalizes to dating, tutoring, appointment markets.
  • Demonstrated: simulation gains (57.6→70.0%) and short-run field effects on matches and exposure distribution in one rural prefecture pair.
  • Gap: (1) subscriber-stock and fulfillment gains are predicted by transition simulation, not observed in the field (nulls attributed to the short window and high 84–85% baseline fill rate); (2) generality beyond spot work is asserted, not tested; (3) single treated cluster means field estimates are evidence from one realized rollout, as the authors themselves state; (4) welfare beyond match counts (job quality, worker surplus, distributional effects) not measured.
Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

Misdirected concentration of exposure

  • What it computes: Not a computation but a diagnosed failure mode — a recommender maximizing predicted favoriting probability (an engagement proxy) concentrates impressions on templates that are popular but post few offerings, while templates with real unmet labor demand get little exposure.
  • Why it was created: names the specific way engagement-proxy optimization fails in capacity-constrained matching markets: the problem is both the degree and the direction of concentration.
  • Concrete example: in the baseline calibrated simulation (Hokkaido 2024 data), the top 10% of templates by recommendation count receive 36.7% of all recommendations under the Greedy recommender.
  • How it is used: motivates the entire recommender redesign; the simulation and field experiment both measure whether reallocation away from this pattern improves realized matches.

Thresholded Eligibility Control (TEC)

  • What it computes: input = per-template score $p_k = w^0 x^0_k + w^1 x^1_k$ (posted capacity $x^0_k$, unfilled capacity $x^1_k$, carried across rounds and debited by realized exposure); operation = sort templates by capped score, convert to eligibility thresholds $\tau_{\pi(j)} = \frac{1}{P}\sum_{h\ge j} p_{\pi(h)}$, then each worker $i$ at slot $s$ may only pick from templates with $\tau_k \ge z_{is}$ where $z_{is} = \frac{1}{c}(s-1+\frac{i-1}{|I|})$; output = a 16-slot recommendation list per worker, computable fully in parallel.
  • Why it was created: the sequential round-robin RSD used by quota methods (AQ) cannot be parallelized across workers and creates memory pressure at production scale (>1M active users); TEC replaces consumed quotas with precomputed thresholds so lists are built independently per worker.
  • Concrete example: with $c=16$, a template holding 1/16 of total score stays eligible for exactly one slot's worth of selection timings; scores above $P/c$ are capped and the excess redistributed proportionally (Appendix B.1).
  • How it is used: the deployed treatment algorithm in both the calibrated simulation (70.0% job-finding rate vs. 57.6% Greedy) and the Aomori field rollout.

Adaptive Quota (AQ)

  • What it computes: per-template quota $q_k = \bar q |K| \cdot p_k / \sum_{k'} p_{k'}$ from the same posting/unfilled-capacity score, then allocates via round-robin random serial dictatorship.
  • Why it was created: static quotas equalize exposure but ignore heterogeneity in labor demand and subscriber stocks; adaptive quotas respond to observed market activity without discretionary per-template judgments.
  • Concrete example: parameters $\bar q = 40$, $w^0 = 50$, $w^1 = 125$ in the baseline simulation; achieves 69.5% job-finding rate.
  • How it is used: the conceptual benchmark TEC approximates; too slow to deploy, so it appears only in simulation.

Favorite list as latent-demand stock

  • What it computes: framing device — the favorite list is a stock variable that mediates between exposure flow (recommendations) and realized transactions (matches); subscribers to a template get push notifications on new offerings.
  • Why it was created: prior conversion literature studies retail settings where items stay purchasable; here capacity is intermittent and perishable, so conversion depends on future supply arrivals, making favorite-list management a dynamic allocation problem under scarcity. Claimed as first paper studying favorite-list management for capacity-constrained goods.
  • Concrete example: in the transition simulation, exposure reallocation saturates by ~round 25 but match gains reach only ~60% of their eventual level by round 25 and ~85% by round 75, because favorite-list stocks adjust slowly.
  • How it is used: explains why the one-month field rollout shows exposure and match effects but null effects on subscriber stocks and fulfillment.
Implementation Feasibility구현 가능성
  • Needs: per-item predicted engagement scores (any existing model), per-item posted-capacity and unfilled-capacity logs per round, and the score-state vector; TEC itself is O(|K| log |K|) threshold construction + O(|K_i|) per worker, embarrassingly parallel.
  • Code: not available (production system; pseudocode complete in Appendix B).
  • Modifications: for non-labor settings, replace $x^0_k$/$x^1_k$ with the domain's supply/underexposure signal; parameters $\bar q, w^0, w^1$ need per-domain tuning (paper's values: 40/50/125).
  • Unknowns: missing link: how sensitive TEC is to noisy or delayed capacity signals; missing link: behavior when favoriting probabilities are endogenous to the policy (simulation holds them fixed — the field's per-impression favoriting response was outside the model).

Condition Delta — transfer to our setting조건 차이 — 우리 설정으로의 전이

Paper's setting: inputs = predicted favoriting probabilities + per-template posting/unfilled-capacity logs; outputs = 16-slot template recommendation lists; conditions = two-sided spot-work matching, FCFS instant matching, perishable capacity, production platform (Timee, >7M registered workers), prefecture-level randomization. Our setting (SP2 — exposure frequency as ranking proxy): analyzing when exposure/impression frequency is a valid or misleading proxy for item relevance or ranking quality in general recommendation/ranking systems. Differences:

  • Matching market with hard capacity (FCFS, perishable slots) vs. general ranking where items are not consumed: reference only — the misalignment mechanism (proxy concentrates exposure where conversion cannot follow) transfers conceptually, but the capacity constraint is what makes the proxy failure sharp here.
  • Supply-side score signal ($x^0_k, x^1_k$) requires observable per-item capacity and fulfillment: missing link for settings without a capacity notion (content feeds, ads).
  • Template/favorite-list two-stage conversion (exposure → favorite stock → match) vs. direct exposure → click: resolved: treat the favorite list as an explicit intermediate-state variable; the paper shows engagement (favorites) can stay flat while the true objective (matches) improves — direct evidence that exposure/engagement frequency is not a sufficient ranking-quality proxy.
  • Prefecture-cluster randomization for interference: reference only — an experimental-design pattern for any market-level exposure intervention.
Root-cause vs. Symptom근본 원인 대 증상

Direct — the paper identifies the structural cause (the objective being optimized, favoriting probability, is misaligned with realized matches under capacity constraints) and redesigns the allocation mechanism to incorporate the missing supply-side state, rather than re-weighting or patching the engagement score; the eligibility-threshold construction is an engineering approximation of the quota mechanism, but the mechanism itself targets the root misalignment.
