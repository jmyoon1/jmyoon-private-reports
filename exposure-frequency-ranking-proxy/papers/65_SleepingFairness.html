Combinatorial Sleeping Bandits with Fairness Constraints

Li et al. · arXiv · 2019 · 195 citations

arXiv:1901.04891 · PDF · SP: SP6

P2Track C

한 줄 요약

가용 arm이 바뀌는 sleeping bandit에서 공정성 제약(최소 선택률)을 만족시키는 조합적 알고리즘을 제안한다.

Problem해결하려는 문제

Prior bandit models handle combinatorial plays (CMAB), sleeping arms (SMAB), and constraints (budget/knapsack, total-reward minimum guarantees) separately, but no unified model integrates all three, and existing constraint types do not model fairness among individual arms: minimum guarantees in prior work apply to total reward of a type/level, not to each arm's selection frequency, and prior learning algorithms for such constraints may violate them (only violation bounds are proven). Standard UCB has no mechanism to honor per-arm long-term selection-fraction requirements.

Solution / Contribution해결책 / 기여

The LFG algorithm integrates two components [SKIM - core rule read; proofs not read]:

  1. Truncated UCB estimate per arm: μ̄_i(t) = min{ μ̂_i(t−1) + √(3 log t / (2 h_i(t−1))), 1 } (exploitation + exploration).
  2. Virtual queue per arm: Q_i(t) = [Q_i(t−1) + r_i − d_i(t−1)]⁺ (fairness debt).
  3. Per-round selection: S(t) ∈ argmax_{S∈S(A(t))} Σ_{i∈S} (Q_i(t) + η w_i μ̄_i(t)), computed greedily as the top-m′ available arms (m′ = min{m, |A(t)|}) by score Q_i(t) + η w_i μ̄_i — efficient because the compound reward is linear.

The approach is a drift-plus-penalty (dual-based) method applied for the first time to a bandit problem with fairness constraints.

Key Results주요 결과
  • LFG is feasibility-optimal: it satisfies any minimum-selection-fraction vector r strictly inside the maximal feasibility region C.
  • Time-average regret ≤ N/(2η) + (β₁√(mNT log T) + β₂N)/T — the √(mNT log T)/T term vanishes as T→∞, leaving a controllable constant gap N/(2η).
  • Simulations reveal a tunable tradeoff (via η) between regret and the speed of convergence to fairness satisfaction: larger η prioritizes reward, smaller η prioritizes fairness. [SKIM - exact simulation numbers not read]

Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

CSMAB-F (Combinatorial Sleeping MAB with Fairness constraints)

  • What it computes: at each round t, given the revealed set of available ("awake") arms A(t) ⊆ {1..N}, select a super arm S(t) ⊆ A(t) with |S(t)| ≤ m; receive compound reward R(t) = Σ_{i∈S(t)} w_i X_i(t); the policy must satisfy lim inf_{T→∞} (1/T) Σ_t E[d_i(t)] ≥ r_i for every arm i (minimum selection fraction).
  • Why it was created: the basic MAB model ignores three real-world factors simultaneously — multiple simultaneous plays (combinatorial), intermittent arm availability (sleeping), and per-arm fairness — no prior unified model had all three.
  • Concrete example: wireless scheduling — N clients, up to m sub-channels per cycle, some clients unreachable due to fading; each client i must be scheduled in at least an r_i fraction of rounds.
  • How it is used in this paper: it is the problem formulation (Section III); the LFG algorithm is designed for it.

Minimum selection fraction fairness (Eq. 1)

  • What it computes: constraint lim inf_{T→∞} (1/T) Σ_{t=0}^{T-1} E[d_i(t)] ≥ r_i, where d_i(t) ∈ {0,1} indicates arm i played in round t and r_i ∈ (0,1) is arm i's required fraction.
  • Why it was created: prior constrained-bandit work (budgets, knapsacks, total-reward minimum guarantees) constrains aggregate quantities, not per-arm treatment — it does not model fairness among arms.
  • Concrete example: r = (0.3, 0.2, 0.1) means arm 1 must be selected in ≥30% of rounds asymptotically.
  • How it is used in this paper: the fairness constraint LFG must satisfy; feasibility-optimality is proven with respect to the maximal feasibility region C of such vectors r.

Virtual queue technique (fairness "debt" counter)

  • What it computes: per-arm counter Q_i(t) = [Q_i(t−1) + r_i − d_i(t−1)]⁺ — grows by r_i each round, drains by 1 when arm i is played; Q_i(t) is the accumulated selection "debt" owed to arm i.
  • Why it was created: imported from Lyapunov/drift-plus-penalty stochastic network optimization (Neely) to turn a long-term time-average constraint into a per-round queue-stability problem; UCB alone was "not born with fairness constraints in mind."
  • Concrete example: with r_i = 0.2 and arm i unplayed for 5 rounds, Q_i grows to 1.0, pushing arm i up in the selection rule.
  • How it is used in this paper: combined with the UCB estimate in the LFG selection rule (Eq. 8).

LFG (Learning with Fairness Guarantee) algorithm

  • What it computes: each round, compute truncated UCB estimate μ̄_i(t) = min{μ̂_i(t−1) + √(3 log t / 2h_i(t−1)), 1}; select S(t) ∈ argmax_{S ⊆ A(t), |S|≤m} Σ_{i∈S} (Q_i(t) + η·w_i·μ̄_i(t)) — solvable greedily by picking the top-m arms by score because the compound reward is linear.
  • Why it was created: neither UCB (no fairness) nor queue-based scheduling (no learning of unknown rewards) alone solves CSMAB-F; the linear combination handles both couplings.
  • Concrete example: η large → reward-greedy behavior; η small → fairness-debt-driven behavior; simulations show a regret-vs-fairness-convergence-speed tradeoff tuned by η.
  • How it is used in this paper: the main algorithmic contribution (Algorithm 1, Section IV), analyzed in Section V.

Feasibility optimality

  • What it computes: a policy is feasibility-optimal if it satisfies constraint (1) for every r strictly inside the maximal feasibility region C (the set of all r achievable by any policy).
  • Why it was created: to certify that LFG's fairness guarantee is as strong as possible — no feasible fairness requirement is sacrificed for learning.
  • Concrete example: [SKIM - verify in deep read] proof is in Section V-A, not read at skim depth.
  • How it is used in this paper: first half of the main theoretical result, alongside the regret bound.

[0,1] rewards, i.i.d. arm-availability distribution, known fixed weights w_i, up to m simultaneous plays; outputs = super arm S(t) per round; conditions = linear compound reward, semi-bandit feedback (rewards of played arms observed), fairness = asymptotic per-arm minimum selection fraction. Root-cause vs. Symptom근본 원인 대 증상

Direct — the paper attacks the root cause of fairness violation in bandit selection (long-term constraints are structurally incompatible with per-round greedy UCB) by converting the constraint into per-round virtual-queue dynamics with a feasibility-optimality proof, rather than post-hoc reweighting or penalizing observed unfairness.
