Can Large Language Models Unlock Novel Scientific Research Ideas?

Sandeep Kumar et al. · EMNLP 2025 · 2024 · 28 citations인용 28회

arXiv:2409.06185 · PDF · SP: SP5

P0Track B

Problem
해결하려는 문제

There is no automated, scalable metric for assessing the quality (novelty, relevance, feasibility) of future research ideas generated by LLMs from a scientific paper. Unlike summarization/translation, idea generation has no reference set or defined structure, so human evaluation — which demands domain expertise, deep paper understanding, and current-literature awareness — has been the only option, and it is costly, slow, and non-scalable as new models appear rapidly.

LLM이 생성한 아이디어의 신규성·다양성을 자동 측정할 척도가 없었다.

Solution / Contribution
해결책 / 기여

Empirical study + two proposed automated metrics (contribution type = benchmark/evaluation methodology):

  1. Dataset: 1,250 post-2022 papers (250 each from CS, Physics, Chemistry, Economics, Medicine) from S2ORC with Future Work sections. Annotate and remove author future-research ideas → AP-FRI corpus. Distinguish Direct FRI (fully removed) vs Mixed FRI (partially edited).
  2. FRI generation: prompt LLMs ("You are a research scientist… brainstorm distinct future research ideas") on the future-work-stripped paper text; chunk-and-combine when text exceeds context.
  3. IAScore (Eqs. 1–2): alignment of generated ideas with author ideas via a GPT-based IdeaMatcher, normalized by author idea count → lower-bound competence signal.
  4. Idea Distinctness Index (Eq. 3–4): mean pairwise $1-\cos$ semantic distinctness of generated ideas.
  5. Human evaluation: 660 CS ideas (66 papers) + 630 Physics ideas (63 papers), authors rate novelty (5-point Best-Worst Scaling), relevance (binary), feasibility (binary). Cohen's kappa 0.83.
  6. RAG variant: retrieve top-20 related papers (190K CS titles/abstracts, BERT embeddings) as background knowledge to reduce generic/non-novel ideas.

저자 미래연구와의 정렬을 재는 IAScore와 아이디어 쌍 의미 거리 평균인 Idea Distinctness Index를 제안하며 5개 도메인 FRI 코퍼스를 구축한다.

Key Results
주요 결과
  • GPT-4o highest IAScore in CS (≈0.41), > GPT-4 ≈ Claude-3 (≈0.39) > Claude-2 ≈ Gemini-2.5 (≈0.37) > Llama-70B (≈0.31) > Gemini-1.0 (≈0.28) (Fig 7).
  • Human novelty (CS): GPT-4 produces 28.70% "very novel" + 6.96% "extremely novel"; Claude-2 produces 20.86% very + 16.52% extremely novel but more non-novel/generic (14.78% non-novel, 16.52% generic) than GPT-4 (7.83% / 13.91%).
  • Relevance/feasibility (CS): GPT-4 93.34% relevant, 96.64% feasible; Claude 76.67% relevant, 83.34% feasible — Claude trades relevance for distinctness.
  • IdeaMatcher selection: GPT prompt 91.8% acc > BERTScore 75.4% > RoBERTa-MNLI 65.5%.
  • Idea length ↔ IAScore: moderate ideas (20–40 words) score highest; <20 and 40–60 words score lower.
  • Claude-2 distinctness ≥ human authors in CS and Physics domains.
  • RAG background knowledge reduces non-novel ideas (GPT-4 −50%, Claude −53.33%) but only marginally raises highly-novel idea rate; hurts Claude relevance.
Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

Idea Alignment Score (IAScore)

  • What it computes: input = set of LLM-generated future research ideas (FRIs) for a paper + the author's own future-work ideas (AP-FRI corpus); operation = for each generated idea $I_{ij}$, an IdeaMatcher (GPT-3.5-turbo prompted to score degree of presence on 0–1) measures alignment with the author's idea set; per-paper average $\text{AvgScore}_j = \frac{1}{N_j}\sum_{i=1}^{N_j} \text{IM}(\text{AP-FRI}_j, I_{ij})$ where $N_j$ = number of author ideas; output = domain-level score $\text{IAScore}_{\text{domain},M} = \frac{1}{P}\sum_{j=1}^{P}\text{AvgScore}_j$.
  • Why it was created: no automated metric existed for evaluating FRI quality; human evaluation of ideas needs domain experts + literature awareness, making it costly and non-scalable. IAScore gives a scalable proxy anchored on expert (author) judgment.
  • Concrete example: GPT-4o reached IAScore ≈ 0.41 in the Computer Science domain (Fig 7), the highest among tested models; GPT-4 and Claude-3 ≈ 0.39, Gemini-1.0 ≈ 0.28.
  • How it is used in this paper: primary automated metric to rank Claude-2, Gemini-1.0, GPT-3.5, GPT-4 (later GPT-4o, Claude-3, Gemini-2.5, Llama-70B) across five domains. Framed explicitly as a lower-bound / precision-driven indicator: normalizing by $N_j$ (author idea count, not generated count) means it measures recall of author-anchored ideas, deliberately NOT rewarding novelty beyond what authors stated.

Idea Distinctness Index

  • What it computes: input = set of generated ideas $I=\{id_1,...,id_n\}$; operation = embed each idea with a GPT embedding (text-embedding-ada-002), compute pairwise distinctness $D_{ij}=1-\text{sim}(v_i,v_j)$ via cosine similarity, then mean over all ordered pairs $D_I=\frac{1}{n(n-1)}\sum_{i}\sum_{j\neq i}D_{ij}$; output = scalar diversity per paper, averaged across papers per domain/model.
  • Why it was created: Distinct-N (Li et al. 2015) counts distinct n-grams (syntactic), missing semantic diversity between ideas that reuse different words for the same concept. This provides a semantic-embedding-based diversity measure.
  • Concrete example: Claude-2's distinctness matches or exceeds human (author) distinctness in Computer and Physics domains (Fig 4); GPT-3.5 lowest.
  • How it is used in this paper: second automated metric; used jointly with IAScore to select the two best models (Claude, GPT-4) for the expensive human evaluation, and to argue Claude generates more distinct-but-sometimes-less-relevant ideas than GPT-4.

AP-FRI Corpus (Author Perspective Future Research Idea Corpus)

  • What it computes: manually annotated set of author-stated future research ideas extracted and removed from each source paper (Direct FRI fully removed; Mixed FRI partially edited) so the LLM never sees them at generation time; serves as ground-truth reference set for IAScore.
  • Why it was created: idea generation lacks a reference set; authors of accepted papers are treated as domain experts whose stated future work is a credible (if incomplete) gold standard.
  • Concrete example: 1,250 papers (250 × 5 domains) from S2ORC filtered to those with a Future Work section; Cohen's kappa 0.94 inter-annotator agreement on FRI extraction.
  • How it is used in this paper: the reference against which every generated idea's alignment is scored.

IdeaMatcher

  • What it computes: a component that decides whether/how much a single generated idea is contained in the author idea set. Compared three implementations: RoBERTa-MNLI entailment (65.5% acc), BERTScore (75.4%), GPT prompted with a containment-degree question (91.8% acc → chosen).
  • Why it was created: needed a reliable semantic "is this idea already among the author's ideas" judge for IAScore.
  • Concrete example: GPT-3.5-turbo-0125 prompt returns a 0–1 degree of presence for each idea.
  • How it is used in this paper: plugs into the IAScore AvgScore computation as IM(·,·).

5Cs Analysis5Cs 분석

Category: benchmark/evaluation methodology (proposes two automated metrics + dataset + human study). Context: builds on hypothesis/idea-generation line (TechNet, PaperRobot, SciMON, Xu et al. concept co-occurrence, Qi et al. LLM zero-shot hypothesis). Metric machinery reuses Distinct-N, BERTScore, RoBERTa-MNLI, RAG (Lewis et al.), Best-Worst Scaling. Directly relevant to SP5 extension-vs-new-contribution scoring (O2): IAScore/Distinctness give quantitative novelty and distinctness measures. Correctness: strong annotation rigor (kappa 0.94 extraction, 0.83 novelty). IdeaMatcher validated on 61 matched + 61 non-matched pairs (small validation set). Metrics acknowledged as lower-bound / cosine-limited. No confidence intervals on IAScore bar charts. IAScore ceiling is author-stated ideas, so genuinely novel ideas are structurally under-credited (authors note this in Limitations). Contributions: (1) IAScore — scalable author-anchored alignment metric; (2) Idea Distinctness Index — semantic diversity metric; (3) annotated five-domain FRI dataset + human novelty/relevance/feasibility study of frontier LLMs. Clarity: reproducible — equations, prompts, model versions, thresholds (BERTScore 0.68), and code/data released.

Implementation Feasibility구현 실현가능성
  • Needs: paper corpus with Future Work sections; annotation labor for AP-FRI extraction; an embedding model (ada-002 / any sentence embedder) for Distinctness; an LLM judge (GPT-3.5) for IdeaMatcher; optional RAG stack (190K abstracts, BERT embeddings, vector DB) for background-knowledge variant.
  • Code: available at https://github.com/sandeep82945/Future-Idea-Generation.git
  • Modifications: for SP5's extension-vs-new-contribution scoring, adopt the Idea Distinctness Index as-is (no reference needed); replace the author-future-work reference of IAScore with a related-work / prior-art reference set to make it a novelty-relative-to-prior-art score rather than alignment-with-author; swap the GPT-3.5 IdeaMatcher for a current model.
  • Unknowns: missing link — IdeaMatcher accuracy on non-scientific-idea artifacts; sensitivity of Distinctness to embedding choice; whether inverting IAScore's normalization yields a meaningful "new contribution" signal.

Condition Delta — transfer to our setting
조건 차이 — 우리 환경으로의 전이

Paper's setting: inputs = full paper text (future work removed); outputs = bulleted future research ideas + IAScore/Distinctness/human scores; conditions = 5 scientific domains, accepted papers 2023–2024, reference = author-stated future work, judge = GPT prompt + human authors. Our setting (SP5, dependency-extraction / KB-construction survey, O2 extension-vs-new-contribution scoring): inputs = research artifacts / claims to be scored on where they sit between incremental extension and genuinely new contribution; outputs = a novelty/distinctness signal usable to classify contributions; conditions = automated, source-agnostic, ideally not requiring hand-annotated author-reference sets. Differences:

  • Reference set requirement: paper anchors novelty on manually-extracted author future-work ideas — missing link for our setting where no per-item author-reference corpus exists; would need a substitute reference (e.g., cited/related-work ideas, which the authors themselves flag as future work).
  • Metric = alignment (recall of expert ideas), not novelty per se: reference only — IAScore explicitly does NOT reward ideas authors did not state, so it is a credibility floor, not a novelty ceiling; for extension-vs-new scoring we would invert its intent.
  • Idea Distinctness Index (pairwise semantic $1-\cos$) is domain-agnostic and directly borrowable: resolved — computes intra-set diversity from embeddings alone, no reference corpus needed.
  • Domain scope (scientific papers, 5 fields) vs our KB/dependency-extraction targets: resolved/how — method is text-domain-agnostic; embeddings + prompt judge transfer.
  • Judge = GPT prompt (91.8% self-validated on 122 pairs): missing link — small validation set; reliability on our artifact type unverified.
