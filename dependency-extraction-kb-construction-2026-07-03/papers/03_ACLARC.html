Measuring the Evolution of a Scientific Field through Citation Frames

Jurgens et al. · TACL 2018 · 2018 · 302 citations인용 302회

SP: SP1

P0Track C

Problem
해결하려는 문제

Bibliometric analysis treats every citation as an equivalent signal, but authors cite for different purposes (background, use, extension, comparison, motivation, future). Understanding of these purposes had been limited to small-scale manual analysis of single papers; there was no field-scale dataset or accurate automatic method to classify citation function, blocking any large-scale study of how framing evolves and affects scientific uptake.

과학 분야의 진화를 추적하려면 인용을 단순 링크가 아니라 기능적으로 유형화해야 하며, use/extend 같은 파생-계보 신호가 필요하다.

Solution / Contribution
해결책 / 기여
  1. Dataset: build the ACL-ARC citation-function corpus (six classes) via double annotation + adjudication, oversampling rare classes, then augment with re-mapped Teufel (2010) data → 3,083 instances.
  2. Classifier: Random Forest (2,500 trees) over four feature families — structural (section #, positions), lexical/morphological/grammatical (Teufel patterns + 132 new patterns, 13 lexical categories, verb tense, connectives), field (venue type, self-citation, reference citation count/PageRank/HITS), usage (direct vs. indirect citation counts per section). Ten novel feature types, including: bootstrapped multi-sentence patterns (citing + preceding + following sentence, avoiding semantic drift), LDA topic-context features, and selectional-preference "prototypical argument" features (single feature per dependency path using distributed word vectors, generalizing over unseen argument words). SMOTE handles class imbalance; paper-stratified cross-validation prevents leakage.
  3. Field-scale application: apply the trained classifier to the 2016 ACL Anthology (21,474 papers, 134,127 inter-ARC citations) to study narrative structure, venue effects, impact prediction, and field evolution.

ACL-ARC citation-frame 데이터셋과 6-way taxonomy(Background/Motivation/Uses/Extends/Comparison/Future)를 도입하며, USES/EXTENDS를 별도 클래스로 유지해 파생-계보 신호를 직접 제공한다.

Key Results
주요 결과
  • Classifier Macro-F1 0.530 vs. Abu-Jbara et al. 2013 0.410, Teufel 2000 0.273, Dong & Schäfer 2011 0.233, Majority-class 0.092, Random 0.138 (all gains significant, McNemar p≤0.01).
  • Ablation: dropping bootstrapped patterns → 0.457, selectional prefs → 0.464, topic features → 0.502; structure/topic/selectional features占 76% of top-100 RF features. Conjunctive RF critical (Naive Bayes 0.286, kNN 0.255, linear SVM 0.393).
  • Citation framing adds significant model fit for predicting 5-year citation count (AIC 35,567 with framing vs. 35,693 baseline); USES (β=0.055) and COMPARISON/CONTRAST (β=0.025) framings most predictive of higher impact.
  • Field trend: COMPARISON/CONTRAST citations decline (r=−0.899, p≤0.01) while USES rise; comparisons concentrate on fewer canonical works → evidence of "rapid discovery science."
Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

Citation function (a.k.a. citation frame)

  • What it computes: input = a citing sentence + surrounding context (from ParsCit-extracted citations) → operation = classify the rhetorical role the citation plays toward the cited paper P → output = one of six discrete labels.
  • Why it was created: prior citation counting treats all citations as equivalent signals; and prior taxonomies were either too fine-grained/rare or too subjective for reliable, field-scale automation. This scheme unifies common aspects of White (2004), Ding et al. (2014), Teufel et al. (2006b), Abu-Jbara et al. (2013) into six reliably-annotatable classes.
  • Concrete example: "The head words can be automatically extracted […] in the manner described by Magerman (1994)" → USES.
  • How it is used in this paper: the label set for the ACL-ARC citation-function dataset and the target of the classifier that is then applied to an entire field.

The six-class taxonomy — BACKGROUND (P provides relevant domain info), MOTIVATION (P illustrates a need/goal), USES (uses data/methods from P), EXTENSION/EXTENDS (extends P's data/methods), COMPARISON OR CONTRAST (similarity/difference to P), FUTURE (P is a potential avenue for future work). COMPARISON and CONTRAST are deliberately collapsed into one class; FUTURE is newly introduced relative to Abu-Jbara et al. (2013).

ACL-ARC citation-function dataset

  • What it computes: ~1,969 citation instances in context, doubly-annotated and adjudicated over 52 fully-labeled ARC papers plus 533 targeted-sampled supplemental contexts; merged with a re-mapped Teufel (2010) set → 3,083-instance combined training corpus (2,600 used after dropping the BACKGROUND-equivalent majority class).
  • Why it was created: no prior dataset showed how citations function at field scale.
  • Concrete example: class distribution — BACKGROUND 1021, USES 365, COMPARES/CONTRASTS 344, MOTIVATION 98, CONTINUATION 73, FUTURE 68.
  • How it is used in this paper: trains the Random Forest citation-function classifier; publicly released with code.

Derivation-lineage frames (USES / EXTENDS) — the two frames that signal a methodological/data dependency between the citing work and P (uses vs. builds-upon). These are the direct "dependency signal" of interest for SP1 (dependency extraction / KB construction).

5Cs Analysis5Cs 분석

Category: benchmark/dataset + new method (classifier) + large-scale empirical study Context: builds on citation-function taxonomies (Teufel 2000/2006b, Abu-Jbara et al. 2013, White 2004, Ding et al. 2014); selectional preferences (Erk 2007); ARC corpus (Bird et al. 2008). For this survey (SP1), it is a canonical benchmark for citation-intent/function classification and the source of the Uses/Extends derivation-lineage frames. Correctness: statistically rigorous — McNemar's tests (p≤0.01) on classifier gains; bootstrapped 95% CIs on trend plots; negative-binomial regression with AIC/likelihood-ratio tests and VIF collinearity checks. Contributions: (1) large field-scale citation-function corpus + SOTA classifier; (2) citations mirror discourse structure but are modulated by venue; (3) framing predicts future citation impact; (4) NLP shows increasing consensus / "rapid discovery science." Clarity: highly reproducible — features tabulated, hyperparameters given, data + code released at github.com/davidjurgens/citation-function.

Contribution vs. Claim기여 대 주장

Stated claims (dataset + SOTA classifier + four field-scale findings) appear well-supported by the reported experiments with significance tests. Potential gap to verify on deep read: generality is claimed for "a scientific field" but all field-scale analysis is on NLP/ACL only; classifier Macro-F1 of 0.530 is SOTA but still modest in absolute terms, so downstream field-scale conclusions inherit ~47% class-level error (esp. on rare USES/EXTENDS — the very classes SP1 cares about).

Implementation Feasibility구현 실현가능성
  • Needs: ParsCit (citation/reference extraction), CoreNLP (syntax), scikit-learn Random Forest, GloVe 840B 300d vectors, LDA (100 topics); the released ACL-ARC dataset + Teufel (2010) re-annotation.
  • Code: available at https://github.com/davidjurgens/citation-function (data + all systems).
  • Modifications: for SP1, retarget to Uses/Extends dependency extraction; likely swap the RF feature pipeline for a modern context-aware encoder; re-validate on any non-NLP domain.
  • Unknowns: missing link: per-class precision/recall of USES and EXTENDS (only macro-F1 headline seen in skim); cross-domain generalization.
Follow-up Papers관련 후속 논문
  • Cohan et al., "Structural Scaffolds for Citation Intent Classification" (NAACL 2019) — introduces the SciCite dataset; the direct neural successor and comparison point to ACL-ARC.
  • Abu-Jbara et al. (2013), "Purpose and polarity of citation" — the closest prior taxonomy/baseline this paper builds on and beats.
  • Teufel et al. (2006b), "Automatic classification of citation function" — the 12-class scheme mapped into this paper's six classes.
Condition Delta — transfer to our setting
조건 차이 — 우리 환경으로의 전이

Paper's setting: inputs = citing sentence + adjacent context from NLP papers (ACL Anthology / ARC); outputs = 6-way citation-function label; conditions = English CL/NLP domain, ParsCit-extracted citation spans, ~3k annotated training instances, Random Forest with hand-engineered features (no neural encoder). Our setting (SP1, inferred): inputs = paper full text / citation contexts across (possibly multiple) domains; outputs = dependency edges for KB construction, especially derivation-lineage (Uses/Extends) relations; conditions = building a structured dependency knowledge base, likely at larger scale and possibly with modern LLM/transformer extractors. Differences:

  • Taxonomy granularity: Uses/Extends frames = our dependency signal — resolved: directly provides the target relation types.
  • Domain scope: NLP-only corpus vs. our (potentially cross-domain) KB — missing link: cross-domain transfer of the classifier untested.
  • Extractor technology: 2018 feature-engineered RF vs. modern neural/LLM extraction — reference only: features and taxonomy transfer, model does not.
  • Label reliability on rare classes: USES/EXTENDS are minority classes with modest F1 — missing link: precision on dependency edges needs its own validation for KB quality.
