Self-Consuming Generative Models with Curated Data Provably Optimize Human Preferences

Ferbach et al. · arXiv · 2024 · 38 citations

arXiv:2407.09499 · PDF · SP: SP10

P4Track A

한 줄 요약

큐레이션된 데이터의 자기 소비 루프가 인간 선호를 최적화함을 증명한다 — exposure 필터링이 t 라운드 후 β=1/t의 암묵적 RLHF와 동치라는 읽기를 뒷받침한다.

Problem해결하려는 문제

Existing theory on self-consuming generative models (iterative retraining on model-generated data) treats the synthetic data as raw model output and asks only whether the loop collapses or stays stable depending on the real-data fraction. In reality, synthetic data on the web has already passed a human selection filter (users pick 1 of K generated candidates before uploading), and no prior analysis characterizes what distribution the retraining loop converges to under that selection — in particular whether selection acts as a hidden optimization pressure rather than mere noise.

Solution / Contribution해결책 / 기여

Model curation as generalized Bradley-Terry choice over K samples under a latent reward $r(x)$ (Eq. 1–2), and analyze the retraining recursion $p_{t+1} = \frac{1}{1+\lambda} p_{\text{ref}} + \frac{\lambda}{1+\lambda} p_t \cdot H^K_{p_t}$ (Eq. 8). Main theoretical claims:

  1. Fully-synthetic loop (λ→∞): expected reward increases each step proportionally to reward variance (Lemma 2.2), and $p_t$ converges in KL to $p_*$, the initial density restricted to the maximum-reward level set (Theorem 2.1) — i.e., reward maximization with diversity collapse.
  2. Mixed loop (λ<∞): expected reward strictly improves over $p_{\text{ref}}$ (Theorem 2.3) while $D_{KL}(p_t \| p_{\text{ref}}) \le -\log(1 - \lambda(K-1))$ stays bounded (Theorem 2.4) — an implicit RLHF objective, with small K acting as regularizer.
  3. Side result: improves Bertrand et al. 2024's no-curation stability guarantee from λ < 1/2 to any λ < α/(2Lε) (Theorem 2.2).

Verified against the full statements in Sections 2.1–2.2; proof details in Appendix A.1 not traced line-by-line [SKIM - deep read for proof mechanics].

Key Results주요 결과
  • Fully synthetic + curated loop: $\mathbb{E}_{p_t}[e^{r(x)}] \to e^{r_*}$ and $\text{Var}_{p_t}[e^{r(x)}] \to 0$; $D_{KL}(p_* \| p_t) \to 0$ (Lemma 2.2, Theorem 2.1) — reward is provably maximized but diversity collapses to the top level set.
  • Mixed loop: reward gain $\ge \frac{\lambda}{(1+\lambda)^3} \frac{(K-1)\text{Var}_{p_{\text{ref}}}[e^{r}]}{K e^{r_*}}$ over $p_{\text{ref}}$ (Thm 2.3) while $D_{KL}(p_t\|p_{\text{ref}}) \le -\log(1-\lambda(K-1))$ (Thm 2.4).
  • Stability without curation extended from synthetic fraction < 1/3 (Bertrand et al. 2024, λ<1/2) to any λ < α/(2Lε) (Thm 2.2).
  • CIFAR10, reward = classifier confidence (class-agnostic on its face): class proportions become strongly skewed over 20 retraining rounds (Automobile grows to >0.5 of samples; several classes near 0) while average confidence rises 0.925 → ~0.98 — bias amplification from a reward only implicitly correlated with class (Fig. 3).
  • CIFAR10, reward = P(airplane): airplane proportion goes ~0.1 → ~1.0 in 10 rounds (Fig. 2). Mixing real data (λ=1/2) keeps proportions near uniform while the reward still increases (Fig. 3 right); FID stays ~4.2–5.3 vs. blowing up to 80–100 in the curated-only settings (Figs. 6–8).

Contribution vs Claim기여 대 주장
  • Stated claims: curation is an implicit preference-optimization mechanism; curated self-consuming loops provably maximize expected reward; real-data injection gives stability; curation amplifies reward-model biases.
  • Demonstrated: all four, at the stated level — proofs for the population (infinite-sample) dynamics, and small-scale experiments (2D toys, CIFAR10 single-run + 3-run check) for the bias-amplification claim.
  • Gap: analysis is infinite-sample (densities, not finite datasets), assumes a single stationary reward shared by all curators, and i.i.d. K-sample choice sets; no finite-sample rates, no heterogeneous-user model, and no web-scale validation. The "web-scale implications" framing is extrapolation. Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

Discrete K-choice curation model (generalized Bradley-Terry selection)

  • What it computes: input = K i.i.d. samples $x_1,\dots,x_K \sim p_t$ from the current generative model; operation = select one sample $\hat{x}$ with probability $P(\hat{x}=x_k) = e^{r(x_k)} / \sum_j e^{r(x_j)}$ under a latent reward $r(x)$; output = the curated sample $\hat{x}$, whose induced density is $p_{t+1}(x) = p_t(x) \cdot H^K_{p_t}(x)$ with $H^K_{p_t}(x) \in (0, K)$.
  • Why it was created: prior self-consuming-loop analyses (Shumailov 2023, Alemohammad 2024, Bertrand 2024) assumed raw synthetic data is fed back; in practice users pick one of K generations (Midjourney upscale, SD 2.1 four-image UI) before the sample enters web datasets — no existing model captured this selection step.
  • Concrete example: Midjourney shows 4 variations per prompt; only the user-upscaled image enters JourneyDB. K=4, and the upscale probability follows the softmax of the reward over the 4 candidates.
  • How it is used in this paper: it is the core object of the whole analysis; every theorem characterizes the retraining dynamics under this selection operator.

Curation-as-implicit-preference-optimization (RLHF equivalence)

  • What it computes: in the limit $K \to \infty$, one curation-retraining step maps $p_t(x) \mapsto p_t(x)\, e^{r(x)} / \mathbb{E}_{p_t}[e^{r(x)}]$; after $t$ iterations from $p_0$ this equals the RLHF solution $\pi(x) \propto p_0(x) e^{r(x)/\beta}$ with $\beta = 1/t$.
  • Why it was created: to show that a purely passive data-side phenomenon (users picking favorites) is mathematically the same operation as explicit reward-model alignment — without the model ever seeing the reward function, negative pairs, or density values.
  • Concrete example: 10 retraining rounds on user-curated data ≙ RLHF with KL-regularization coefficient β = 0.1 against the initial model.
  • How it is used in this paper: Lemma 2.1 states the limit; Theorems 2.3/2.4 extend the reward-increase + bounded-KL pair (the two halves of the RLHF objective) to the mixed real+curated setting.

Self-consuming loop / iterative retraining — established in prior work (Shumailov 2023 "curse of recursion", Bertrand 2024); this paper reuses the framework and adds the curation operator.

  • Needs: for the theory, nothing to run. To replicate experiments: torchcfm (OT conditional flow matching), CIFAR10, a pretrained VGG11 reward classifier; ~22 h per 20-iteration run on one A100 40GB.

Follow-up Papers관련 후속 논문
  • Bertrand et al., "On the stability of iterative retraining of generative models on their own data" (ICLR 2024) — the direct predecessor whose stability bound this paper strengthens; the no-curation baseline for the loop.
  • Wyllie, Shumailov & Papernot, "Fairness feedback loops: training on synthetic data amplifies bias" (2024) — the empirical bias-amplification phenomenon this paper's theory grounds; closest bridge to the survey's exposure-bias framing.
  • Shumailov et al., "The curse of recursion" (2023) — establishes uncurated model collapse, the contrast case.
  • Gerstgrasser et al., "Is model collapse inevitable?" (2024) — accumulating (rather than replacing) data breaks collapse; complements this paper's Lemma A.6 on convex combinations of past iterates.
Condition Delta — transfer to our setting조건 차이 — 우리 설정으로의 전이

Paper's setting: inputs = samples from an image generative model (DDPM / OT-CFM), curated 1-of-K by a Bradley-Terry choice under a known-form latent reward; outputs = the retrained model distribution across iterations; conditions = infinite-sample population dynamics, single global reward, K fixed and small, retraining from scratch each round. Our setting (SP10 — exposure/frequency as a ranking-proxy feedback loop): inputs = items/documents whose exposure is allocated by a ranking system, with user engagement feeding back into the ranker or into LLM training corpora; outputs = long-run item/content distribution and whether frequency becomes a self-fulfilling quality proxy. Differences:

  • Selection operator: this paper's 1-of-K Bradley-Terry pick is structurally the same softmax-selection filter as position/exposure-driven choice in ranking — resolved: the theorems transfer as a formal model of "exposure-filtered retraining," with the reward playing the role of the ranking score.
  • Domain: image generation vs. ranking/recommendation or LLM text corpora — reference only; no ranking experiments here.
  • Reward = fixed latent function vs. a ranker that is itself retrained on the curated outcomes (two-sided loop) — missing link: the paper's reward is static; co-adaptation of the scorer is exactly what SP10 cares about and is not modeled.
  • Population densities vs. finite catalogs with cold-start items — missing link: no finite-sample or item-birth dynamics.
Root-cause vs. Symptom근본 원인 대 증상

Direct — the paper identifies and proves the root mechanism itself (selection between generation and retraining is an implicit reward-ascent operator, with exact convergence targets and an RLHF equivalence), rather than patching an observed collapse metric; it explicitly declines to propose a mitigation, which it flags as future work.
