Polarization by Default: Auditing Recommendation Bias in LLM-Based Content Curation

Nicolò Pagan et al. · arXiv · 2026

arXiv:2604.15937 · PDF · SP: SP2

P4Track B

한 줄 요약

LLM 기반 콘텐츠 큐레이션이 기본값으로 특정 성향 콘텐츠를 증폭하는 현상을 감사한다. pre-training/alignment로 새겨진 내용 prior가 provider와 prompt에 걸쳐 지속됨을 보인다.

Problem해결하려는 문제

Prior fairness audits of LLM-based recommendation (Zhang et al. 2023; Deldjoo & Di Noia 2025 CFairLLM; Jiang et al. 2024; Li et al. 2023) have three gaps the paper names explicitly: (1) domain gap — they cover product/news recommendation, not social media content curation, where exposure biases shape public discourse at platform scale (e.g., Grok now ranking X's feeds); (2) provider gap — single-provider studies cannot distinguish model-specific from structural biases; (3) prompt-sensitivity gap — no study tests whether prompt engineering can mitigate the biases.

Solution / Contribution해결책 / 기여

Controlled simulation audit: 540,000 simulated top-10-of-100 selections across 54 conditions (OpenAI GPT-4o Mini, Anthropic Claude Sonnet 4.5, Google Gemini 2.0 Flash × Twitter/X, Bluesky, Reddit × six prompt styles: neutral, general, popular, engaging, informative, controversial). 100 trials per condition, 100 posts sampled per trial from a 5,000-post per-platform pool, temperature 0.3, non-personalized. Models see raw post text only (no engagement counts, no author metadata), so any recommendation bias reflects pre-training/alignment patterns rather than explicit signals. Each post characterized by 13 features (VADER sentiment, Detoxify toxicity, Cardiff-NLP RoBERTa polarization/topic, style indicators, and LLM-inferred author demographics on Twitter/X). Bias = recommended-vs-pool distribution shift on the unified R² scale.

Key Results주요 결과
  • Polarization is the strongest selection bias: R² = 0.055, significant in 98.1% of the 54 conditions — amplified across all providers and prompt styles, including prompts with no engagement objective ("polarization by default").
  • Toxicity handling inverts with prompt objective: models tolerate/prefer toxic content under engaging prompts and actively avoid it under informative (R² = 0.017, significant in 75.9% of conditions).
  • Political-leaning exposure bias on Twitter/X: left-leaning authors systematically over-represented although the pool is 43.4% right vs. 17.9% left (R² = 0.013, significant in 89% of Twitter/X conditions); persists across providers and prompts.
  • Prompt framing modulates content bias ~4-fold (controversial/informative average R² = 0.015–0.016 vs. popular R² = 0.004) but has constrained effect on demographic attributes.
  • Provider trade-offs: GPT-4o Mini most consistent across prompts; Claude and Gemini highly adaptive on toxicity; Gemini strongest negative-sentiment preference. [SKIM - exact per-provider numbers in Results 4.2 not read]

Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

Structural vs. promptable bias (the audit's organizing distinction)

  • What it computes: for each content/demographic feature, compares bias magnitude across 54 conditions (3 providers × 3 platforms × 6 prompt styles) → classifies the bias as robust-across-all-conditions ("structural") vs. modulated by prompt framing ("promptable")
  • Why it was created: prior LLM-recommendation fairness audits used single providers in isolation, so they could not tell whether an observed bias is model-specific or a shared structural property of LLM curation
  • Concrete example: polarization amplification appears in all 54 configurations (structural); toxicity preference flips sign between engaging and informative prompts (promptable)
  • How it is used in this paper: the entire results section (RQ1–RQ3) is organized around this classification

R²-unified bias measurement

  • What it computes: recommended-set vs. pool feature distributions → Cramér's V (categorical) or Cohen's d (numerical) → converted to a common R² scale ($R^2 = V^2$; $R^2 = d^2/(d^2+4)$); directional bias as $\Delta p_i = p_{rec,i} - p_{pool,i}$ or $\Delta\mu = \bar{x}_{rec} - \bar{x}_{pool}$; significance via chi-square / Welch's t-test at $\alpha = 0.05$
  • Why it was created: to compare bias magnitudes across heterogeneous feature types (13 features spanning categorical demographics and numerical content scores) on one scale
  • Concrete example: polarization score $R^2 = 0.055$ (significant in 98.1% of conditions) vs. gender $R^2 = 0.002$ (67%)
  • How it is used in this paper: the primary metric for every result figure and the feature ranking

Two-LLM agreement protocol for author demographics

  • What it computes: Twitter/X profile bios + sample posts → independent attribute inference by Llama 3.1 8B and Mistral v0.2 → attribute kept only where the two models agree (gender, political leaning, minority status)
  • Why it was created: no ground-truth demographic labels exist for social media authors; single-model inference is noisier
  • Concrete example: pool distribution comes out 43.4% right-leaning vs. 17.9% left-leaning; minority status has a 48.4% unknown rate (treated as exploratory)
  • How it is used in this paper: enables the RQ3 demographic-bias analysis (Twitter/X only — Bluesky/Reddit lack bios) [SKIM - inference validity details in Appendix B not read]

Condition Delta — transfer to our setting조건 차이 — 우리 설정으로의 전이

Paper's setting: inputs = raw social-media post text (100-post pools, no engagement metadata, no author info), outputs = LLM top-10 selections; non-personalized, single-shot ranking; bias measured as recommended-vs-pool distribution shift on content + inferred-demographic features. Our setting (SP2 — exposure biases when LLMs rank/curate content, and whether exposure frequency acts as a ranking proxy): concerned with what drives an LLM's selection when no explicit relevance signal exists — in particular pre-training exposure frequency as a latent proxy. Differences:

  • Paper measures bias on content features (polarization, toxicity, sentiment) rather than on pre-training exposure frequency of the items themselves: missing link — polarization amplification is consistent with an exposure-frequency proxy (polarizing content is over-represented in engagement-filtered pre-training corpora) but the paper does not test frequency directly. [SKIM - verify whether any frequency/familiarity analysis appears later]
  • Metadata-stripped design isolates model-internal priors from explicit popularity signals: resolved — this is exactly the isolation SP2 needs; the design is directly reusable.
  • Social-media curation domain vs. general LLM ranking/retrieval: reference only — findings transfer as evidence of default selection priors, not as a method.
  • Non-personalized single-shot selection vs. feedback-loop/repeated-exposure dynamics: missing link — no longitudinal amplification measured (Chaney-style loops cited but not simulated).
Root-cause vs. Symptom근본 원인 대 증상

Mixed [SKIM] — the multi-provider × multi-prompt factorial design is a genuine step toward root-cause attribution (robustness across all configurations implicates pre-training/alignment rather than task design), but the paper stops at mapping which biases are structural; it does not trace why polarization is amplified (e.g., to corpus exposure frequency), leaving the mechanism as inference.
