Taming Recommendation Bias with Causal Intervention on Evolving Personal Popularity

Tan et al. · arXiv · 2025

arXiv:2505.14310 · PDF · SP: SP8

P4Track B

한 줄 요약

진화하는 개인 인기(personal popularity)에 대한 인과 개입으로 추천 편향을 다스린다(2025). 재학습 계열.

Problem해결하려는 문제

Existing popularity-debiasing methods have two gaps: (i) they debias uniformly across users — recommending unpopular items even to users who genuinely prefer popular ones — ignoring individual, heterogeneous preference for popularity; (ii) they treat item popularity and user popularity-preference as static, or track only item-side evolution (PDA, DecRS), ignoring the consistency between how a user's preference for popular items and an item's popularity co-evolve over time.

Solution / Contribution해결책 / 기여

CausalEPP: (1) define Evolving Personal Popularity $s_u^t$ (windowed fraction of a user's clicks on top-20%-popularity items); (2) build a causal graph with 8 variables where local popularity $P$ confounds item exposure ($P \to I$) and, jointly with EPP $S$, drives a conformity effect ($ (P,S) \to C \to Y$), while item quality $Q$ is disentangled from popularity ($Q \to Y$); (3) deconfound by backdoor adjustment $P(Y|U, do(I=i), Q)$, cutting $P \to I$, with the prediction model $\hat{y}_{ui}^t = \text{Tanh}(q_i + c_{ui}^t)\cdot\text{Softplus}(m_{ui})$ trained with BPR loss + $\lambda \mathcal{L}_Q$ (expectation over $P$ approximated via a Jensen-gap bound); (4) at inference, intervene on both $p^*$ and $s^*$ using moving-average trend extrapolation. Backbone-agnostic ($m_{ui}$ from MF or LightGCN).

Key Results주요 결과
  • LightGCN backbone: Rec@20 on Ciao 0.0293 vs. best baseline PPAC 0.0245 (+14.9%); with intervention (CausalEPP-Inv) 0.0303 (+18.8%). Average rank 2.11 (Inv: 1.00) across 9 dataset-metric cells vs. 10 baselines.
  • MF backbone: Rec@20 on Ciao 0.0313 (+20.4% over best baseline); average improvement 4.17% over TIDE, 4.36% over PPAC.
  • Ablations (LightGCN): removing quality disentanglement −8.84% avg; replacing full popularity elimination ("eliminate p") costs −41.18%; grid-search intervention instead of trend forecast −4.08%.
  • Debiasing effect: popular-item share of recommendations drops from 75.8% → 55.5% (LightGCN, Amazon-Music) and 93.9% → 64.1% (Douban-Movie), against ground-truth shares of 43.3% / 54.9%.

  • Stated claims: user-adaptive, temporally-consistent debiasing outperforms uniform/static debiasing; forecasting-based intervention beats grid search.
  • Demonstrated: consistent but mostly small gains (2–6% typical, up to 20% on the smallest dataset Ciao) on three rating datasets with MF/LightGCN backbones; ablations support each component.
  • Gap: debiasing quality is shown against a static ground-truth popular-item share; the "quality proportional to global popularity" assumption in $\mathcal{L}_Q$ is asserted (following TIDE) rather than validated — it partially re-injects the popularity signal the method claims to remove. Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

Evolving Personal Popularity (EPP)

  • What it computes: for user $u$ at time $t$, the ratio of the user's clicks on high-popularity items to the user's total interactions within a sliding window $[t-w_1, t]$: $s_u^t = |\{(u,i): i \in \mathcal{D}_u^{t-w_1,t} \text{ and } p_i^t > \hat{p}^t\}| / |\mathcal{D}_u^{t-w_1,t}|$, where the high-popularity threshold $\hat{p}^t$ is the top-20% percentile of local popularity in that window.
  • Why it was created: prior debiasing methods (TIDE, PDA, DecRS) apply a uniform correction across all users, ignoring that users differ in how much they prefer popular items and that this preference drifts over time.
  • Concrete example: on Douban-Movie, 14,975 users click popular items in fewer than 50% of interactions while 32,823 click them frequently; over time 14,709 users shift from frequent to infrequent popular-item interaction and 18,459 shift the other way.
  • How it is used in this paper: enters the conformity effect via a consistency score $c_{ui}^t = e^{-\alpha|s_u^t - p_i^t|} \cdot p_i^t \cdot \text{MLP}(i)$ during training, and is forecast forward via moving-average gradients for the inference-time intervention.

Local popularity (from PDA, Zhang et al. 2021) — item $i$'s share of all interactions in a recent window: $p_i^t = |\mathcal{D}_i^{t-w_1,t}| / |\mathcal{D}^{t-w_1,t}|$. Reused, not new.

Temporal evolution-guided intervention

  • What it computes: instead of grid-searching the intervened popularity value $p^*$, it extrapolates: $p_i^* = p_i^T + \frac{\partial MA}{\partial t}(p_i^T)\cdot\Delta_i^T$ (and analogously $s_u^*$), i.e., last observed value plus moving-average slope times a step size.
  • Why it was created: setting $p^*$ to the mean fully removes popularity signal and hurts accuracy (−41% in their ablation); grid search over an unbounded value space ignores the direction popularity is actually evolving.
  • Concrete example: optimal steps found were $\Delta_i^T = 5$ (item) and $\Delta_u^T = 10$ (user) on Amazon-Music.
  • How it is used: applied only at inference (CausalEPP-Inv variant), adjusting the conformity effect via do-calculus on $P$ and $S$.

Quality loss — a pairwise loss $\mathcal{L}_Q = \sum -\log(\sigma(\text{Sign}(d_i - d_j)(q_i - q_j)))$ forcing a learnable per-item quality scalar $q_i$ to be ordered consistently with global popularity $d_i$, separating a "benign" quality component from the harmful popularity confounder (extends TIDE's quality/popularity split to a confounder graph).

Implementation Feasibility구현 가능성
  • Needs: timestamped interaction data, PyTorch, single RTX 3090Ti-class GPU; windowed popularity statistics per 100 time steps.
  • Code: available at https://github.com/ShiyinTan/CausalEPP
  • Modifications: for our setting, would need explicit exposure logs (or propensity estimates) to break the click≈exposure conflation; swap MF/LightGCN matching score for the target ranker.
  • Unknowns: missing link: sensitivity to the top-20% popularity-threshold choice outside these three datasets; behavior when exposure is heavily platform-controlled (feedback loops) rather than organic.
Follow-up Papers관련 후속 논문
  • PPAC (Ning et al., WWW 2024) — the closest predecessor: group-level personal popularity debiasing; CausalEPP claims to subsume it with per-user, evolving signal.
  • TIDE (Zhao et al., TKDE 2023) — source of the quality/popularity disentanglement that CausalEPP ports from a collider to a confounder structure.
  • PDA (Zhang et al., SIGIR 2021) — temporal item-popularity intervention baseline; CausalEPP's inference intervention is a direct refinement.
Condition Delta — transfer to our setting조건 차이 — 우리 설정으로의 전이

Paper's setting: inputs = timestamped explicit-rating interaction logs (Ciao, Amazon-Music, Douban-Movie), 5/10-core filtered, chronological split into 100 time steps; outputs = top-K item ranking; conditions = click frequency observable per item and per user, MF/LightGCN backbones, offline evaluation with Recall/Precision/NDCG@20. Our setting: exposure frequency used as a proxy for ranking/relevance quality (SP8) — we care about when observed interaction/exposure frequency misleads a ranker about true preference, and what corrections are principled. Differences:

  • Paper corrects the conformity/popularity channel of frequency bias with an explicit causal graph — directly relevant as a recent per-user, per-time refinement of the popularity-as-confounder view: reference only
  • Paper assumes item exposure ≈ item interaction counts (no explicit exposure logs / propensities); our question distinguishes exposure from interaction: missing link
  • Personal popularity is computed from observed clicks, which are themselves exposure-biased — circularity between the debiasing signal and the bias: missing link [SKIM - verify]
  • Offline top-K evaluation on organic logs, no unbiased/held-out-exposure test set: reference only
Root-cause vs. Symptom근본 원인 대 증상

Mixed — the causal-graph + backdoor-adjustment treatment targets the confounding structure of popularity bias (root-cause direction), but the EPP signal is itself a click-frequency heuristic computed from biased logs, and the quality loss hard-wires quality to global popularity, so part of the frequency-as-proxy problem is patched rather than resolved.
