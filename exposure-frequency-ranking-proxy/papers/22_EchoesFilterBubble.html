Echoes in Filter Bubble: Diagnosing and Curing Popularity Bias in Generative Recommenders

Jun Yin et al. · arXiv · 2026

arXiv:2605.16825 · PDF · SP: SP2

P4Track B

한 줄 요약

생성형 recommender의 인기 편향을 MLE gradient 결핍과 공유 tokenization의 기하급수적 증폭으로 진단하고, tokenization 재설계와 재학습으로 교정한다. 백본을 0.6B에서 8B로 키워도 편향이 제거되지 않음을 보인다.

Problem해결하려는 문제

SID-based generative recommenders (LETTER, LC-Rec, ED2) severely over-recommend popular head items — head/tail HR@10 gaps up to 45x, >97% of recommendation-list slots taken by head items — and scaling the backbone from 0.6B to 8B does not fix it. Traditional debiasing transplants (re-weighting, re-ranking, e.g. IFairLRS) are marginally effective, and the fundamental mechanism by which the generative paradigm produces popularity bias (as opposed to the discriminative paradigm) was unexplained.

Solution / Contribution해결책 / 기여

Two-part diagnosis + matched cure ("Ghost"):

  1. Diagnosis (theoretical): (i) under MLE/NLL training on a long-tailed interaction distribution, tail-item SID tokens receive expected gradient $\leq 0$ projected onto user preference (gradient starvation, Lemma 1); (ii) undifferentiated tokenization forces tail items through $z$ head-dominated branching points, geometrically suppressing tail probability by $(\gamma_{min})^{-z}$ (Lemma 2).
  2. Cure: SKT (tail items inherit head-item SID prefixes, deferring competition to one predictable step) + AUO (unlikelihood penalty on a per-tail-item collection of textually-similar / SID-divergent head items), combined as $\mathcal{L} = \mathcal{L}_{NLL} + \alpha\mathcal{L}_{AUO}$.
Key Results주요 결과
  • Popularity-bias magnitude in SOTA GRs: head/tail HR@10 gap up to 45x (LC-Rec: 0.1800 vs 0.0040); head items fill >97% of top-10 lists (LC-Rec: 24,212 head vs 560 tail items retrieved); bias persists as backbone scales 0.6B→8B.
  • Ghost improves tail HR/NDCG over standard GRs by +63.9%/+70.7% avg across three Amazon datasets (Instruments, Arts, Games); over existing debiasing baselines by +28.4%/+15.0% avg.
  • Fairness: MGU reduced 55.8% avg vs standard GRs, and a further 16.7% avg vs the strongest debiasing baseline IFairLRS-RR.
  • Cost: overall HR/NDCG drops only 7.5%/6.3% vs the strongest utility baseline (LC-Rec); composite CNS score gains 16.8% avg (max 22.1%).
  • Ablation: without SKT, tail HR@10 falls 0.0173→0.0095 and MGU@10 rises 0.096→0.306 — tokenization structure is the dominant factor; AUO alone is insufficient.

Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

Generative Recommender (GR) with Semantic IDs (SIDs)

  • What it computes: item text features → vector quantization (RQ-VAE / RQ-KMeans) → a discrete token sequence (SID) per item; an LLM backbone then autoregressively generates the next item's SID token-by-token from the user's interaction history.
  • Why it was created: replaces discrete item-ID embedding tables of discriminative recommenders with a unified end-to-end sequence-generation formulation that leverages LLM capabilities.
  • Concrete example: an item is encoded as a 4-token SID $(c^{(1)},c^{(2)},c^{(3)},c^{(4)})$, each token chosen from a 256-entry codebook per residual-quantization level.
  • How it is used in this paper: the paradigm whose popularity bias is diagnosed; LETTER, LC-Rec, ED2 are the studied SID-based GRs.

Gradient starvation of tail tokens (LEMMA 1)

  • What it computes: expected MLE gradient update for a token embedding, $\mathbb{E}_\mathcal{D}[\Delta e_c] \propto \mathbb{E}[\sum_i (\mathbb{I}\{c=c_v^{(i)}\} - P_\theta(c|h_u,c^{<i}))X_{h_u}]$; for tokens that almost never appear as targets, the indicator term vanishes and the expected projection onto the user-preference vector is $\leq 0$.
  • Why it was created: explains why GRs are popularity-biased at the token level — prior debiasing work extended item-level heuristics without an optimization-level diagnosis.
  • Concrete example: a tail-specific SID token acts only as a softmax negative sample, so its embedding is consistently pushed away from $X_{h_u}$; empirically, LC-Rec's head/tail HR@10 gap reaches 45x (0.1800 vs 0.0040).
  • How it is used in this paper: motivates the AUO loss that supplies corrective gradients.

Bias amplification via undifferentiated tokenization (LEMMA 2)

  • What it computes: since standard tokenization ignores popularity, a tail item's SID shares prefixes with many head items and must survive $z$ competitive "branching points"; its generation probability is geometrically suppressed by $\leq (\gamma_{min})^{-z}$ relative to the true data distribution, with per-step amplification factor $\gamma_i > 1$.
  • Why it was created: shows how the token-level flaw compounds into item-level exposure distortion during autoregressive decoding.
  • Concrete example: LC-Rec's top-10 lists contain 24,212 head vs 560 tail items (>97% head) on the Instruments dataset.
  • How it is used in this paper: motivates skeleton-founded tokenization (SKT), which fixes the branching point at a single predictable step.

Skeleton-founded tokenization (SKT)

  • What it computes: head items get $L^h$-token SIDs via RQ-KMeans (the "skeleton"); each tail item inherits the full $L^h$-token prefix of its semantically closest head item, then appends $L^t$ residual-quantized tokens of its own — variable-length SIDs with the head-vs-tail competition deferred to the single step $L^h{+}1$ (where the head item emits EOS).
  • Why it was created: eliminates the unpredictable multi-step head/tail token competition of undifferentiated tokenization (reduces suppression from $O(\gamma_{min}^z)$ to a single-step $O(\gamma_{EOS})$, LEMMA 3).
  • Concrete example: with $L^h{=}4$, $L^t{=}2$, a tail violin item inherits the 4-token prefix of the closest head instrument and adds 2 distinguishing tokens.
  • How it is used in this paper: one of Ghost's two components; ablation shows removing SKT collapses tail HR@10 from 0.0173 to 0.0095.

Asymmetric unlikelihood optimization (AUO)

  • What it computes: for each tail target item, builds an "undesired collection" $\bar\Omega$ = head items with high textual similarity but low SID similarity (top-$K_a$=200 by cosine, then top-$K_b$=5 by shortest common prefix), and adds $\mathcal{L}_{AUO} = -\sum_{\Omega\in\bar\Omega}\sum_i \log(1 - P_\theta(c^{(i)}|h_u,c^{<i}))$; total loss $\mathcal{L}_{NLL} + \alpha\,\mathcal{L}_{AUO}$ with $\alpha{=}0.1$.
  • Why it was created: MLE never penalizes over-confident head tokens, so tail tokens stay gradient-starved; unlikelihood penalties on "hijacking" head tokens redistribute probability mass back to tail tokens (LEMMA 4 gradient-rescue analysis).
  • Concrete example: for a tail violin, the undesired set is 5 popular instruments that are textually similar but SID-divergent.
  • How it is used in this paper: the second Ghost component; applied only to tail-item training instances (hence "asymmetric").

Condition Delta — transfer to our setting조건 차이 — 우리 설정으로의 전이

Paper's setting: inputs = user interaction sequences + item text (3 Amazon categories, 10k–21k items, 99.9% sparsity); outputs = next-item SID generation from an LLM (Qwen 0.6B–8B); condition = offline sequential recommendation with head/tail split at top-20% popularity. Our setting (SP2 — exposure frequency as a distorted ranking proxy): analyzing how interaction/exposure frequency distorts what ranking systems surface, and whether the newest paradigms escape or inherit that distortion. Differences:

  • Paradigm: generative (SID-generation) vs. our general ranking-proxy framing — resolved: the paper shows the exposure distortion transfers into and is amplified by the generative paradigm, i.e., limitation-type evidence that changing the scoring machinery does not remove frequency bias.
  • Mechanism granularity: token-level gradient dynamics vs. item-level exposure statistics — resolved: paper explicitly bridges the two (Lemma 2 propagation from token to item exposure).
  • Feedback loop: paper treats a single training round; long-run exposure→data→exposure feedback (filter-bubble dynamics over time) is invoked in the title but not simulated — missing link.
  • Training regime: SFT/MLE only; RL-aligned GRs — reference only (paper's own stated future work).
Root-cause vs. Symptom근본 원인 대 증상

Direct — the paper traces the observable exposure skew back to two identified structural causes (expected-gradient sign for rarely-targeted tokens under MLE, and popularity-agnostic token-space sharing) and designs one intervention per cause, rather than re-ranking or re-weighting the symptom; the ablation confirms the causal ordering (tokenization structure dominant, AUO corrective).
