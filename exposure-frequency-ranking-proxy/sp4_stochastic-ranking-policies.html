SP4: Stochastic Ranking Policies and Randomized Exposure Allocation

15 papers · targets O1, O2 · 2018-2026

How a target exposure distribution becomes actual served impressions under an immutable black-box scorer — the realization axis of the survey (BvN decomposition, expohedron/Carathéodory, Plackett-Luce sampling, balanced-word delivery), and whether any paper bounds the gap between realized and target exposure.

SP4: 확률적 ranking 정책과 무작위 exposure 배분

15편 논문 · O1, O2 목표 대상 · 2018-2026

목표 exposure 분포가 불변 black-box scorer 아래에서 실제 서빙 impression이 되는 과정 — 조사의 실현(realization) 축 — 을 다루며, 실현된 exposure와 목표 사이의 편차를 묶는 논문이 있는지 검사한다.

Entry Assumption vs. What Was Found

Expected (revised after SP1: how are policy weights discretized into serving-frequency budgets?; revised after SP3: bound the realized-vs-target gap): The canonical mechanism for realizing a target exposure distribution over repeated impressions without changing scores — the user's exposure-frequency knob formalized — plus two injected questions: policy-weight discretization (SP1) and realized-vs-target concentration (SP3).

Found: Three realization families with a strict cost/expressiveness ordering; under fixed scores PL sampling degenerates to a one-parameter temperature family (Pareto-dominated). Headline: decomposition and delivery never touch scores — scores enter exactly once, at target selection. Exactly one per-realization mechanism exists (balanced-word/stride delivery, deterministic m=N−1 discrepancy) but no exposure-unit theorem.

Assessment: Surprised — (a) the realized-vs-target mechanism was assumed absent, but stride delivery exists buried as 'step 3' of a geometry paper (only the theorem is missing); (b) the pipeline is stronger than score-read-only: it is score-free given the target, relocating O2's entire feasibility question to target selection; (c) realization machinery froze in 2022 — 2024–2026 papers change targets, never mechanisms.

사전 가정과 실제 발견

기대 (SP1 이후 수정: 정책 가중치의 서빙 빈도 예산 이산화 질문 추가; SP3 이후 수정: 실현 대 목표 편차 한계 검사 추가): score를 바꾸지 않고 반복 impression에 걸쳐 목표 exposure 분포를 실현하는 정준 메커니즘 — 사용자의 빈도 knob을 정식화한 것 — 을 기대했고, 두 가지 주입된 질문(정책 가중치 이산화, 실현 대 목표 집중 한계)을 함께 검사하였다.

발견: 엄격한 비용/표현력 순서를 갖는 세 실현 계열을 확인하였다. 고정 score 아래에서 PL 표집은 온도 1-파라미터 곡선족으로 퇴화한다(Pareto 열등). 핵심 발견: 분해와 전달은 score를 전혀 건드리지 않는다 — score는 목표 선택에서 정확히 한 번만 개입한다. per-realization 메커니즘은 balanced-word(stride) 전달 단 하나뿐이며(결정론적, 창당 m=N−1), exposure 단위의 정리는 없다.

평가: 예상과 다름 — (a) 실현 대 목표 메커니즘이 없을 것으로 가정했으나 stride 전달이 기하 논문의 '3단계'로 숨어 있었다(정리만 부재). (b) 파이프라인은 read-only보다 강한 score-free(목표 조건부)로, O2의 실행 가능성 질문 전체가 목표 선택으로 이동한다. (c) 실현 기계는 2022년 이후 동결 — 2024–2026 논문은 목표만 바꾼다.

What SP4 Establishes

SP4's question splits into a machinery question (how is a target exposure vector turned into impressions?) and a guarantee question (how far can realized exposure drift from the target?). The literature answers the first completely and the second only partially.

Machinery. Three realization families exist, with a strict cost/expressiveness ordering. (1) The LP + Birkhoff-von Neumann pipeline [01] FoE attains any feasible target exactly in expectation, at O(n⁵) total cost with a support of up to (n−1)²+1 rankings; [05] IncompleteExposure generalizes it to top-k display at O(k³n²). (2) The expohedron + Carathéodory pipeline [28] Expohedron, [10] ParetoRepeatedRankings attains the same targets exactly at O(n² log n), with support ≤ n rankings, and recovers the entire utility-fairness Pareto front rather than one point. (3) Plackett-Luce/Gumbel sampling [40] PLRank costs only O(n log n) per impression, but under immutable scores it spans only the one-parameter temperature family of exposure vectors — hitting an arbitrary target requires training the scores, which violates the survey's constraint — and its achievable trade-off curve is Pareto-dominated by decomposition-based realization except near nDCG = 1 [28] Expohedron.

The key structural finding: the entire decompose-and-deliver pipeline is score-free given the target. Scores are read exactly once, to orient the utility direction during target selection; the decomposition and delivery steps never touch them. The immutable-scores constraint therefore does not bind on the realization axis at all — it binds only on which targets are meaningful (merit-proportional targets need trusted scores; see SP3's score-trust dichotomy).

Per-realization guarantees. Exactly one mechanism controls realized-vs-target deviation: balanced-word (stride-scheduling) delivery of the decomposed support [28] Expohedron. It is deterministic — any ranking's frequency in equal-length serving windows deviates from its target proportion by at most m = N−1, where N is the support size — and is empirically ~10× tighter than i.i.d. sampling (normalized unfairness ~10⁻⁴ vs ~10⁻³ at T = 1000). This is precisely why support size matters: Carathéodory's ≤ n support permits m = n−1 balance where BvN's (n−1)²+1 support cannot. However, no paper states a concentration or deviation theorem in position-weighted exposure units, none handles heterogeneous (non-identical) query streams, and the general policy-weights → discrete-impression-budget mapping remains an explicitly flagged missing link [05] IncompleteExposure. The SP3 seed (realized-vs-target concentration) is thus narrowed — a mechanism exists — but not closed: the theorem is absent.

Position-bias curve dependence is architecturally confined: the curve enters at target computation (the LP objective/constraints; the expohedron's very geometry is defined by γ) and at exposure accounting (crediting the amortized ledger), while all delivery machinery — BvN sampling, Carathéodory decomposition, Gumbel top-K, stride scheduling — is curve-free. The only paper addressing where the curve comes from is [43] UnbiasedLTR (low-impact swap intervention; conservative overestimation of small propensities is safe, underestimation is harmful).

SP4이 확립한 것

SP4는 실현 축을 완결한다. 세 계열의 엄격한 순서: LP+BvN(기대값 기준 정확, O(n⁵), support ≤(n−1)²+1), expohedron+Caratheodory(동일 목표를 O(n² log n)에, support ≤ n, Pareto front 전체), PL/Gumbel 표집(impression당 O(n log n)이지만 고정 score 하에서는 온도 곡선족만 도달 가능 — 분해 기반 실현에 Pareto 지배됨). 구조적 핵심: 분해-전달 파이프라인은 목표가 주어지면 score-free이므로 score 불변 제약은 실현 축에 전혀 구속되지 않는다. 실현 대 목표 편차를 다루는 유일한 메커니즘은 balanced-word 전달(결정론적, 창당 m=N−1, i.i.d. 표집보다 ~10배 조밀)이나 position 가중 단위의 집중 정리는 존재하지 않는다 — gap 7의 근거.

Dominant Approach지배적 접근

The dominant approach is open-loop two-stage realization: (stage 1) solve a convex program for the target in marginal or exposure space — feasible because utility and exposure are both linear in the marginal rank-probability matrix (SP1, KB uuid 1) — then (stage 2) decompose the solution into a small distribution over concrete rankings and serve them across repeated impressions. It originates in [01] FoE (LP over doubly stochastic matrices + BvN decomposition + i.i.d. sampling) and was successively cheapened along the same architecture: [05] IncompleteExposure generalizes BvN to top-k slates at O(k³n²) and shows the decomposition's non-uniqueness is itself a design degree of freedom (FELIX re-selects decompositions to avoid rankings whose exposure cannot be estimated, cutting their probability mass from 0.39 to 0.20 at literally identical NDCG and expected-exposure loss); [28] Expohedron moves the whole problem into n-dimensional exposure space (Carathéodory decomposition, support ≤ n, O(n² log n), whole Pareto front, balanced-word delivery); [10] ParetoRepeatedRankings extends the geometry to group-fairness targets. The approach became dominant because it is exact: the served distribution attains the optimized target exactly in expectation, with utility optimality by construction — a property neither sampling heuristics nor score-proxy regularizers offer.

Its limitations are equally clear. Guarantees are expectation-level; the delivery step was an afterthought until [28] Expohedron's balanced words, and even there no exposure-unit deviation theorem is stated. The geometric machinery numerically caps at n ≤ 100 items per query (both [28] Expohedron and [10] ParetoRepeatedRankings discard larger queries). And the expohedron's feasible set is constitutively defined by the position-bias curve — a wrong curve silently deforms what "achievable exposure" even means. The alternatives each trade away the exactness: PL sampling ([40] PLRank, [14] FairPGRank, [15] ExpectedExposure) is cheap per impression but score-parameterized — under immutable scores it degenerates to a one-knob (temperature) family and is Pareto-dominated by decomposition; closed-loop controllers ([11] EquityOfAttention's ILP ledger; the Ctrl baseline in [28] Expohedron/[10] ParetoRepeatedRankings) skip target decomposition entirely and greedily repay accumulated exposure deficit, with divergent evidence on whether that suffices (see Divergences); and the in-processing contrast class ([41] ScalableExposureControl, [42] TopKFairLTR, [44] DELTR) shapes exposure by retraining scores — infeasible under the survey's constraint, and notably yielding no guarantee on realized ranked exposure at all (exADMM's "provably fair" means provable ADMM convergence on a score-second-moment proxy, with the proxy-to-Gini gap formally unbounded).

For the survey's objectives this settles the realization column of O1 and sharpens O2 substantially: given any feasible target exposure vector, realization is a solved, score-free, black-box-compatible problem for repeated queries up to ~100 candidates — and the binding open problems are exactly the three the entry rationale predicted: the finite-T deviation bound (partially solved by balanced words, theorem absent), the policy→impression-budget discretization for heterogeneous production traffic (open), and curve dependence (confined but unquantified under misspecification). A separate O3-relevant finding: the realization machinery itself has been frozen since 2022 — every 2024–2026 paper in this SP ([41] ScalableExposureControl, [10] ParetoRepeatedRankings, [42] TopKFairLTR, [06] HEA, [24] BeyondExposure) changes the target (income, hierarchical KL, top-k disparity) or the scale, never the realization mechanism.

Convergences수렴 지점
  • Realization is score-free given the target. [01] FoE, [05] IncompleteExposure, [28] Expohedron, and [10] ParetoRepeatedRankings all architect the same separation: scores are consumed once as the merit/utility direction in the convex program; decomposition and delivery operate on the target and the position-bias vector only. Mechanism: utility-linearity in the marginal matrix pushes all score dependence into the objective, leaving the feasible set and its decomposition purely combinatorial.
  • BvN's cost and support size are the recognized bottleneck, independently attacked. [05] IncompleteExposure (top-k generalization, O(k³n²)) and [28] Expohedron (exposure-space geometry, O(n² log n), support n) reach the same conclusion by different routes: realization cost drops by orders of magnitude (15–56× end-to-end in [28] Expohedron Table 1) with the achieved target unchanged.
  • Randomization-policy dominance ordering. [15] ExpectedExposure shows score-aware PL dominates score-free rank-transposition shuffling at every disparity level; [28] Expohedron shows decomposition-based realization Pareto-dominates PL except near nDCG = 1. Combined ordering: score-free shuffle < PL sampling < decomposition. [40] PLRank improves PL's training cost, not its expressiveness, so the ordering stands through 2021+.
  • Fair exposure is achievable only in expectation or amortized — never within a single ranking. Stated independently as a framework premise by [01] FoE (constraints hold in expectation), [45] FairInfoAccess (§6.2, as the field's consensus), and [15] ExpectedExposure (by metric construction). This is the formal reason the survey's exposure-frequency knob must be an over-time schedule.
Divergences분기 지점
  • Does a closed-loop controller adequately realize exposure targets? [28] Expohedron finds the heuristic controller (Ctrl) near-Pareto-optimal and fastest for horizons under a few thousand impressions; [10] ParetoRepeatedRankings finds Ctrl converging to a single point with catastrophic normalized unfairness 0.93 on MSLR at T = 1000. Likely cause: different fairness targets (individual merit-proportional vs group-exposure) and MSLR's steeper relevance distribution — under flat relevance a reactive controller can rotate within ties cheaply, under steep relevance it gets pinned (consistent with the utility-cost flatness law, KB uuid 5). Unadjudicated; flagged for SP5.
  • What "provable exposure control" means. The decomposition line proves exact expectation-level target attainment with utility optimality ([01] FoE, [28] Expohedron); the scalable in-processing line proves only optimization convergence on a score-level proxy — [41] ScalableExposureControl's "provably fair" title means ADMM convergence to KKT points of a score-second-moment objective, with no bound relating the proxy to Gini@K of actual rankings (the paper asserts monotonicity, does not bound the gap). The same word covers an exact-attainment guarantee and a no-realized-guarantee regime. Cause: different optimization substrates (marginal matrix vs embedding parameters), not different rigor.
Prior Beliefs and Surprises (full)사전 믿음과 놀라움 (전문)

What the entry rationale expected to find (revised after SP1 and SP3):

The canonical mechanism for realizing a target exposure distribution over repeated impressions without changing scores; plus two injected questions — how are policy weights discretized into serving-frequency budgets (SP1's IncompleteExposure flag), and can the realized-vs-target gap be bounded (SP3's seed, which taxation and BvN both leave unbounded)?

Confirmed:

  • The realization mechanism exists and is mature: three families with a clean cost/support/expressiveness ordering, all consuming a fixed merit vector — the "exposure-frequency knob formalized" exactly as the rationale predicted.
  • The discretization question is genuinely open in general: [05] IncompleteExposure's missing link stands after reading all 15 papers.

Surprised:

  • (a) Assumed: the realized-vs-target gap is left entirely unbounded. Found: [28] Expohedron's balanced-word delivery is a deterministic per-realization mechanism with an implicit discrepancy guarantee (m = N−1 per window) and ~10× empirical improvement — the mechanism exists; only the exposure-unit theorem is missing. The assumption was wrong because the delivery step is buried as "step 3" of a geometry paper and never advertised as a concentration result.
  • (b) Assumed (from SP1's framing): the whole pipeline is score-read-only. Found: it is stronger — decomposition and delivery are score-free; scores enter only at target selection. The immutable-scores constraint does not bind on realization at all, which relocates the entire feasibility question of O2 to target selection (where SP3's score-trust dichotomy governs).
  • (c) Assumed: 2024–2026 would advance realization machinery. Found: the machinery froze in 2022 (expohedron); the recent papers change targets and scale only. The assumption was wrong because the field treats realization as solved and moved to target semantics (income, hierarchy, top-k).
  • (d) PL sampling, the family this SP is named after, turns out to be the weakest realization mechanism under the survey's constraint: score-parameterized, one-knob under immutability, Pareto-dominated.

KB impact:

  • UUID 1: confirmed — realization-as-frequency-schedule now has the full machinery inventory behind it.
  • UUID 2: refined — dominance chain extended (shuffle < PL < decomposition); PL-based exposure control reclassified as effectively score-modifying when targets are arbitrary.
  • UUID 6: refined — stride scheduling/balanced words is the fairness literature's own pacing algorithm, strengthening the SP7 formal-equivalence hypothesis.

Key Concepts핵심 개념

(Exposure, the doubly stochastic marginal rank-probability matrix, and the LP formulation were defined in SP1 Key Concepts; expected exposure / EE-D / EE-R in SP1 via [15] ExpectedExposure. New in SP4:)

Generalized top-k Birkhoff-von Neumann decomposition

  • What it computes: an n×k marginal rank-probability matrix (columns sum to 1, row sums ≤ 1) → extend with one aggregate column and run iterated bipartite perfect matching (adjusted Hopcroft-Karp) → a convex combination of top-k permutation matrices, i.e. an executable stochastic top-k policy. Complexity O(k³n²) versus O(n⁴√n) for square BvN.
  • Why it was created: the classical BvN theorem covers only square doubly stochastic matrices (full-length rankings); real systems serve top-k slates, and square BvN is infeasible for large n.
  • Concrete example: for n = 200 candidates and k = 10 slots, decomposition cost drops from order 200^4.5 to order 10³ · 200².
  • Role in this SP: [05] IncompleteExposure Theorem 4.1/Algorithm 1; makes the convex-optimization realization pipeline applicable to the top-k serving that production systems actually do (O1, O2).

Expohedron + Carathéodory (GLS) decomposition

  • What it computes: the expohedron is the convex hull of all n! permutations of the position-bias vector γ — exactly the set of expected item-exposure vectors achievable by any distribution over rankings. The GLS Carathéodory decomposition takes any interior point (target exposure vector) and expresses it as a convex combination of ≤ n vertices (rankings), using the majorization test x ≺ γ for membership, in O(n² log n).
  • Why it was created: the Birkhoff polytope has n² dimensions but utility and exposure depend only on n exposure marginals; optimizing and decomposing in exposure space cuts cost from O(n⁵) to O(n² log n) and support from (n−1)²+1 to n.
  • Concrete example: at n = 100 items, GLS decomposition takes ≈0.5–0.8 s versus ≈29–70 s for BvN [28] Expohedron Table 1.
  • Role in this SP: the 2022-frontier realization machinery [28] Expohedron, extended to group fairness by [10] ParetoRepeatedRankings; its small support is what enables tight balanced-word delivery (O1, O2).

Balanced-word (stride-scheduling) delivery

  • What it computes: a distribution over N rankings → a deterministic serving sequence (argmin of accumulated inverse-density counters) in which any ranking's frequency within equal-length substrings deviates from its target proportion by at most m = N−1.
  • Why it was created: i.i.d. sampling converges slowly to target proportions at finite horizons (10 fair coin flips land exactly 5/5 only ~25% of the time); a deterministic schedule removes sampling variance entirely.
  • Concrete example: [28] Expohedron Fig. 6 — balanced-word delivery reaches normalized unfairness ~10⁻⁴ by T = 1000 while sampled delivery stalls near 10⁻³.
  • Role in this SP: the only per-realization mechanism in the surveyed literature; the formal bridge from policy weights to an actual impression-frequency schedule for a repeated query (O2).

Plackett-Luce policy with Gumbel top-K sampling

  • What it computes: per-item log-scores m(d) → sequential softmax without replacement defines a distribution over rankings; sampling is done by adding i.i.d. Gumbel noise to log-scores and sorting, an exact PL sample in O(n log n) with no renormalization.
  • Why it was created: deterministic sort-by-score can never give two items equal probability of the top slot; PL is the standard differentiable stochastic-ranking family, and Gumbel top-K makes serving cheap.
  • Concrete example: with scores m = (2, 2, 0), items 1 and 2 each get probability ≈0.47 of rank 1 — a deterministic sorter must pick one of them 100% of the time [40] PLRank.
  • Role in this SP: the third realization family; [40] PLRank makes optimizing PL policies sample-efficient (PL-Rank-2 converges at N = 10–100 sampled rankings where REINFORCE needs 1000), but the exposure vector realized by a PL policy is a function of the scores — under immutable scores only the temperature can move it (O2 feasibility boundary).

Swap-intervention propensity estimation

  • What it computes: randomly swap the result at landmark rank k with the result at rank r before serving; the CTR ratio of the swapped document at position r versus k estimates the position-bias ratio p_r/p_k (propensities are needed only up to a constant).
  • Why it was created: uniform randomization of rankings degrades user experience; a single swap is a low-impact intervention sufficient to calibrate the position-bias curve that every exposure target and accounting step depends on.
  • Concrete example: [43] UnbiasedLTR's production deployment swapped the top document with a uniformly random rank j ∈ {1..21} for 7 days, estimating p_r decaying to ≈0.12 at rank 21.
  • Role in this SP: the only surveyed answer to "where does the position-bias curve come from"; its misspecification analysis (conservative overestimation of small propensities is safe; underestimation is harmful; clipping is principled conservatism) is the risk-management result for every curve-dependent realization step (O2).

Open Questions After SP4SP4 이후 미해결 질문

  • Realized-exposure concentration theorem: no paper bounds the deviation between realized cumulative exposure and the target vector in position-weighted exposure units. Missing link in [01] FoE (expectation-only, per-realization explicitly deferred), [05] IncompleteExposure ("how to certify realized exposure when only impression frequency is controlled"), and SP3's [32] TaxRank (sampling variance of realized exposure vs e*). Balanced words [28] Expohedron give a deterministic ranking-frequency discrepancy for a single repeated query, but the exposure-unit translation, i.i.d.-sampling concentration rates, and heterogeneous query streams are all unaddressed. What would resolve it: a Hoeffding/discrepancy-style theorem "after T impressions, realized exposure deviates from target by ≤ f(n, support size, γ, T)" for both sampled and stride-scheduled delivery.
  • Policy weights → discrete impression budgets in production: explicit missing link in [05] IncompleteExposure; [28] Expohedron's stride scheduling solves only the single-query fixed-support case. No paper maps a distribution over rankings onto per-item impression caps/budgets across heterogeneous traffic with finite, unknown horizons. What would resolve it: a reduction from the decomposition-support schedule to per-item pacing budgets, or a direct budget-space formulation (SP7's native vocabulary).
  • Curve-misspecification sensitivity of realization: the expohedron's feasible set and every exposure ledger are defined by γ, yet no paper propagates curve error to realized-fairness error. Missing links in [15] ExpectedExposure (γ-sensitivity unstudied), [06] HEA (curve-choice sensitivity), [11] EquityOfAttention (assumed generic click models vs real attention); [43] UnbiasedLTR gives the only directional result (conservative overestimation safe) but for IPS training, not realization. What would resolve it: a perturbation bound ‖γ − γ̂‖ → realized-unfairness error, or an empirical audit with eye-tracking-calibrated curves.
  • Exact realization at catalog scale: [28] Expohedron and [10] ParetoRepeatedRankings both cap at n ≤ 100 for numerical reasons; BvN is O(n⁵); the only method demonstrated at 10⁵+ users ([41] ScalableExposureControl) abandons realized-exposure guarantees and score immutability. No surveyed method provides exact realization guarantees at production candidate-set sizes.
