Towards Fairness in Personalized Ads Using Impression Variance Aware Reinforcement Learning

Timmaraju et al. · KDD 2023 · 2023 · 14 citations

arXiv:2306.03293 · PDF · SP: SP7

P0Track C

한 줄 요약

impression 분산 인지 강화학습으로 개인화 광고의 공정성을 개선하는 Meta의 VRS(KDD 2023). 74번 외부 감사의 대상 시스템.

Problem해결하려는 문제

Impression delivery in Meta's personalized ads skews demographically relative to the ad's eligible (targeted) audience — e.g., housing ads shown disproportionately by gender or estimated race — which regulators treat as potentially discriminatory for housing/employment/credit. Prior fairness methods (pre-/in-processing classifiers, re-ranking rules like Geyik et al. 2019) were not designed for a live multi-stage auction where (a) individual PC attributes may not be used at inference, and (b) feedback about group-level delivery is only available aggregated, delayed, and DP-noised.

Solution / Contribution해결책 / 기여

Post-auction-input bid modulation, leaving the ranker and auction mechanics untouched:

  1. Define per-ad fairness as shuffle distance between the eligible ratio (from advertiser targeting) and the delivery ratio (from realized impressions), measured per PC attribute with BISG + DP aggregation.
  2. Train one offline, off-policy RL controller per PC attribute: state = (PC-free user embedding from a two-tower click model, sign of current delivery variance); action = {adjust-up, no-adjustment}; terminal-only reward = Σᵢ (countᵢ¹ − countᵢ⁰)·sign(vᵢ⁰)·(−1) over PC buckets, decomposed to per-step rewards via the regression in Eq. 11.
  3. At inference, a hand-engineered hierarchical meta-controller takes an equal vote across the per-PC controllers (tie → conservative no-adjustment).
  4. The bid-adjustment module maps the winning action to a fixed multiplier on advertiser_bid (P40-to-top for adjust-up; P15-to-bottom when no-adjustment is converted to adjust-down), then the auction proceeds normally.
Key Results주요 결과
  • Offline simulation, 10% variance threshold: NCAC reduction 84.08% (gender) / 50.79% (race) with Adjust-up Only; 92.25% / 67.68% with Adjust-up and Down.
  • Online A/B (two weeks, Meta ads system): NCAC reduction 53.77% (gender) / 30.45% (race) Adjust-up Only; 76.36% / 53.95% Adjust-up and Down.
  • Online consistently underperforms offline simulation (infrastructure overhead + imperfect auction emulation); NCAC reduction takes ~2 weeks to plateau.
  • Deployed to all US users for housing ads; expansion to credit and employment ads stated as in progress.
5Cs Analysis5Cs 분석

Category: system/engineering (deployed production framework) + new method (return-decomposition RL under DP-aggregated feedback) Context: builds on equal opportunity (Hardt et al. 2016), demographic parity (Dwork et al. 2012), fair re-ranking (Geyik et al. 2019), RL-based exposure fairness in recsys (Ge et al. 2021/2022), return decomposition (RUDDER; Efroni et al. 2021), BISG (Elliott et al. 2008). Motivated by the Ali et al. 2019 discrimination-through-optimization finding and Meta's civil-rights settlement context. Within this survey: this is the mechanism whose external evaluation is MetaVRSEval (Track C link). Correctness: [SKIM] — no confidence intervals or significance tests reported in Tables 1–2; A/B test is two weeks with ad-segment randomization; simulation admittedly does not perfectly emulate online auction dynamics (acknowledged offline→online degradation). Contributions: (1) first large-scale deployed multi-PC-attribute fairness framework in online advertising; (2) privacy-preserving variance measurement (BISG + batched DP) feeding an RL controller that never touches individual PC attributes; (3) offline simulation + online A/B evidence of large NCAC reductions. Clarity: high-level design reproducible; exact hyperparameters, aggregation level k, DP (ε, δ), embedding dimensions, and simulator details are not disclosed — not reproducible outside Meta.

Contribution vs Claim기여 대 주장
  • Stated claims: "first large-scale deployed framework for pursuing fairness for multiple PC attributes in online advertising"; significant improvement in the fairness metric; privacy-preserving.
  • Demonstrated: large NCAC reductions offline and online for gender and estimated race on US housing ads; DP mechanism described but its (ε, δ) parameters and utility cost are not reported.
  • Gap: no measurement of advertiser/platform utility cost (revenue, click-through, ad relevance) from bid distortion — the fairness-utility trade-off is asserted qualitatively ("ads will still be reasonably relevant") but never quantified. No external validation of BISG accuracy for this population. Effect on non-housing ads sharing auctions with boosted housing ads is unexamined. Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

Variance Reduction System (VRS)

  • What it computes: (user embedding, per-ad delivery-variance sign vector) → RL controller emits a categorical action {adjust-up, no-adjustment/adjust-down} → bid-adjustment module converts the action to a fixed multiplier on advertiser_bid, so total_bid = advertiser_bid * vrs_multiplier + quality_bid before the auction runs.
  • Why it was created: prior fairness work is mostly pre-/in-processing on classifiers; no deployed mechanism existed to equalize impression delivery across protected-class (PC) groups in a live auction system without using individual PC attributes at inference.
  • Concrete example: the adjust-up multiplier is the P40 of historical multipliers needed to bring a housing ad to the top of its auction (~40% of adjustments reach the top); adjust-down uses the P15 to push toward the bottom.
  • How it is used in this paper: the entire system contribution — deployed for all US users on housing ads at Meta.

Impression/delivery variance (Shuffle Distance between eligible and delivery ratios)

  • What it computes: per ad and PC attribute, the eligible ratio (demographic distribution of the advertiser-targeted eligible audience) and delivery ratio (distribution of actual impressions) → Shuffle Distance = ||p − π||₁ / 2, the minimum fraction of impressions that would need to move between groups to match the eligible distribution.
  • Why it was created: equal opportunity / demographic parity are classification-task notions; auction delivery needed an outcome-level fairness metric anchored to a policy-constrained baseline (targeting criteria).
  • Concrete example: shuffle distance 10% means moving 10% of impressions from males to females would zero out the variance.
  • How it is used in this paper: it is both the fairness metric (Eq. 5) and the RL state/reward signal.

NCAC / NCAC reduction

  • What it computes: Non-conforming Ad Coverage = fraction of ads whose shuffle-distance variance exceeds a threshold (10%); NCAC_reduction = (1 − NCAC_test/NCAC_control) × 100%, on ads with ≥300 impressions.
  • Why it was created: aggregates the per-ad variance into a single reportable system-level metric for A/B tests.
  • Concrete example: online Test 2 achieves 76.36% NCAC reduction for gender.
  • How it is used in this paper: the sole reported evaluation metric, offline and online.

BISG + differential privacy race estimation

  • What it computes: (surname, zipcode) batches → Bayesian Improved Surname Geocoding lookup against US Census statistics → aggregated per-ad race-bucket impression counts, with Gaussian/Laplace additive DP noise per batch (sensitivity 1).
  • Why it was created: self-reported race is unavailable; VRS must measure race-level delivery variance without accessing individual PC attributes at training or inference.
  • Concrete example: batching means noise adds up across N batches, so relative uncertainty vanishes as 1/√N instead of 1/N.
  • How it is used in this paper: supplies the aggregated, noisy variance measurements that form the RL reward and state.

Return decomposition for aggregated episode rewards

  • What it computes: an episode of k adjust-up actions with a single terminal reward R_T (count-difference weighted by variance signs, DP-noised) → a shared MLP f(S|θ) trained so that Σ_S f(S|θ) ≈ R_T, recovering per-step rewards in {−1, +1}; the policy then greedily argmaxes the learned reward.
  • Why it was created: DP aggregation makes feedback delayed, batched (every k impressions), and noisy — per-step rewards are unobservable, and full Q-learning is unnecessarily complex since actions do not affect state transitions (contextual-bandit-like structure).
  • Concrete example: with episode reward R = 2x − k, the expected number of correct adjust-ups is x = (R + k)/2 (Appendix A.3).
  • How it is used in this paper: the training algorithm for each single-objective PC controller; controllers are combined by an equal-voting hierarchical meta-controller (tie → no-adjustment).
Implementation Feasibility구현 가능성
  • Needs: production-scale impression logs with candidate-ad and auction-slot data, a PC-free user-embedding model, BISG tooling with census data, DP noise infrastructure, offline auction re-simulation environment.
  • Code: not available (proprietary Meta system; no released code or data).
  • Modifications: for a research setting, the auction layer would need replacement with a public ranking simulator; BISG is US-census-specific; the eligible-audience baseline must be redefined for non-advertising ranking.
  • Unknowns: missing link: aggregation level k, DP (ε, δ) values, VRS multiplier magnitudes, utility/revenue impact — all undisclosed. [SKIM - some may be in the companion external evaluation]
Follow-up Papers관련 후속 논문
  • MetaVRSEval (external evaluation of VRS) — the independent audit of whether VRS's impression-variance metric actually delivers the claimed equity; direct Track-C successor within this survey.
  • Ali et al., "Discrimination through optimization" (CSCW 2019) — the predecessor audit showing Facebook ad delivery skew; the problem VRS responds to.
  • Geyik, Ambler & Kenthapadi (KDD 2019) — deterministic fair re-ranking at LinkedIn; the main prior deployed re-ranking alternative VRS contrasts itself with.
  • Ge et al. (WSDM 2021, 2022) — RL-based item-exposure fairness in recommendation; the in-processing counterpart to VRS's post-processing approach.
Condition Delta — transfer to our setting조건 차이 — 우리 설정으로의 전이

Paper's setting: inputs = live user requests + housing-ad candidates in Meta's production auction; outputs = per-ad bid multipliers equalizing impression distribution vs. eligible audience; conditions = US-only, housing vertical, PC attributes {gender, estimated race (4 buckets)}, aggregated DP-noised group feedback, individual PC attributes forbidden at inference. Our setting (SP7 — exposure frequency as a ranking proxy): analyzing whether impression/exposure counts are a valid proxy for ranking quality/fairness, and how deployed exposure-intervention systems reshape the exposure–relevance relationship. Differences:

  • VRS optimizes impression distribution alignment (shuffle distance), an exposure-count-based proxy for equitable outcomes — exactly the proxy pattern SP7 examines: resolved — the paper is a primary instance of exposure frequency used as the fairness objective itself.
  • VRS is evaluated only by its own internal metric (NCAC); independent external verification is delegated to the companion evaluation work (MetaVRSEval): missing link — this paper alone cannot establish that the proxy metric equals the intended fairness outcome.
  • Meta-scale proprietary infrastructure (user embeddings, auction logs, BISG tooling) vs. any reproducible research setting: reference only.
  • Housing-ads vertical with a policy-constrained eligible baseline vs. general recommendation/ranking where no legally-mandated baseline distribution exists: missing link — the baseline-choice question is open outside regulated verticals.
Root-cause vs. Symptom근본 원인 대 증상

Symptom — VRS corrects the observable delivery skew by re-weighting auction bids downstream, while the ranking and bid models that generate the skew (the actual bias source identified by Ali et al. 2019) are left entirely untouched; the RL controller is a perpetual compensator, not a fix.
