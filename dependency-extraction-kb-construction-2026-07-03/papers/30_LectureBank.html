What Should I Learn First: Introducing LectureBank for NLP Education and Prerequisite Chain Learning

Irene Li et al. · AAAI 2019 · 2019 · 67 citations인용 67회

arXiv:1811.12181 · PDF · SP: SP3

P0Track A

Problem
해결하려는 문제

Prior prerequisite-chain corpora are narrow (MOOC video captions with up to 244 topics in 3 domains; ACL Anthology scientific papers with only a subset annotated) and depend on external resources like Wikipedia/Baidu to obtain concept entity representations, which fail when a concept has no Wikipedia page. There is no large, well-organized, in-domain (NLP) lecture-based dataset with high-agreement prerequisite annotations on which to learn concept prerequisite chains.

학습 자료에는 학습 순서가 없어, 어떤 개념을 먼저 배워야 하는지의 선행관계 학습이 필요하였다.

Solution / Contribution
해결책 / 기여

Empirical/benchmark contribution. (1) Build LectureBank: manually collect 1,352 lecture files from 60 courses (5 domains), classify each into the 305-topic TutorialBank taxonomy, extract a 1,221-term vocabulary automatically from slide headers. (2) Annotate binary prerequisite relations over 208 concepts (two PhD annotators, Cohen's $\kappa = 0.7$, "substantial"), intersect to get a 208-vertex / 921-edge directed concept graph. (3) Cast prerequisite chain learning as binary classification / link prediction: learn 300-d Doc2Vec concept embeddings, then compare traditional classifiers (NB, SVM, LR, RF; concatenated source+target embeddings) against GAE/VGAE, using oversampling to handle the 921-positive vs 41,829-negative imbalance.

NLP 도메인 선행관계 주석을 담은 LectureBank 데이터셋을 도입하고 임베딩·신경망 기법을 적용하며, 소규모에서는 oversampled SVM이 GAE/VGAE를 F1 0.76으로 능가함을 보고한다.

Key Results
주요 결과
  • Best F1 = 0.761 (SVM, Doc2Vec trained on TutorialBank); on LectureBank SVM F1 = 0.740; combined TutorialBank+LectureBank SVM F1 = 0.748.
  • Traditional classifiers → high precision / low recall (e.g. NB on LectureBank: P 0.853, R 0.611); GAE/VGAE → high recall / low precision (VGAE on LectureBank: P 0.545, R 0.921, F1 0.684).
  • SVM beats GAE/VGAE on F1 across all three corpora; oversampling is essential (imbalanced training gave "very poor results").
  • Inter-annotator agreement $\kappa = 0.7$ vs TutorialBank's 0.3 on the same pairs.
  • Corpus scale: 1,352 lectures, 51,939 slides, 3.44M tokens; concept graph 208 vertices / 921 edges.
Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

Concept prerequisite chain / prerequisite relation

  • What it computes: input = a pair of concepts (A, B); operation = binary judgment "is A a prerequisite of B, i.e. does learning A help learn B"; output = directed edge A → B (or none). Aggregated over all pairs this yields a directed concept graph $G=(V,E)$ with concepts as vertices and prerequisite dependencies as edges.
  • Why it was created: online learning material is unstructured and gives no learning order; a student who wants to learn a concept (e.g. POS tagging) does not know which concepts must be learned first (Viterbi, Markov Models, …).
  • Concrete example: In LectureBank's annotated graph, Hidden Markov Models → POS Tagging; the longest recovered chain has 14 concepts (Matrix Multiplication → Differential Calculus → Backpropagation → … → Scientific Article Summarization).
  • How it is used in this paper: the entire dataset and task are built on this relation — 208 concepts, 921 positive prerequisite edges annotated, then learned as a link-prediction / binary classification problem.

LectureBank dataset

  • What it computes: not a method — a corpus of 1,352 English lecture files (51,939 slides, ~3.44M tokens) from 60 university courses across 5 domains (NLP, ML, AI, DL, IR), each lecture classified into the 305-topic TutorialBank taxonomy, plus binary prerequisite annotations over 208 concepts and a 1,221-term in-domain vocabulary.
  • Why it was created: prior prerequisite corpora draw from scientific papers (ACL Anthology) or MOOC video captions and rely on Wikipedia for entity representations; the authors wanted well-organized lecture files and concept representations learned solely from the in-domain corpus.
  • Concrete example: labeled concept graph = 208 vertices, 921 edges, 41,829 negative pairs, 12 cyclic pairs, 7 isolated topics.
  • How it is used in this paper: it is the benchmark on which Doc2Vec + classifiers/GAE/VGAE are evaluated; becomes the standard evaluation dataset for the prerequisite-relation task (SP3).

Doc2Vec concept feature learning — uses Distributed Memory Paragraph Vectors (Le & Mikolov 2014) to produce 300-d document vectors, then infers a dense vector per concept, used as the vertex feature $X$. Reuses an established method as feature extractor.

Graph Autoencoder (GAE) / Variational Graph Autoencoder (VGAE)

  • What it computes: GCN encoder $Z = \text{GCN}(X,A)$ over adjacency $A$ and features $X$, decoder $\hat A = \sigma(ZZ^T)$ reconstructs edges (link prediction). VGAE adds Gaussian latent variables with a KL-regularized variational lower bound.
  • Why it was created: originally for link prediction in citation networks (Kipf & Welling 2016b); applied here to predict prerequisite edges.
  • Concrete example: 2-layer GCN, 32 then 16 hidden units, 200 epochs, Adam lr 0.01.
  • How it is used in this paper: neural baseline compared against traditional classifiers; yields high recall, low precision.

5Cs Analysis5Cs 분석

Category: benchmark/dataset (+ empirical comparison of methods) Context: builds directly on TutorialBank (Fabbri et al. 2018 — source of the 208 concepts and 305-topic taxonomy); contrasts with Gordon et al. 2016 (ACL Anthology, unsupervised), Pan et al. 2017a/b (MOOC/Coursera, Wikipedia-dependent), Liu et al. 2016. Uses GAE/VGAE (Kipf & Welling 2016b) and Doc2Vec (Le & Mikolov 2014). Correctness: Cohen's $\kappa=0.7$ reported (vs $\kappa=0.3$ for TutorialBank's ternary scheme) — a genuine improvement. 5-fold CV with precision/recall/F1. No confidence intervals on F1; single Doc2Vec configuration. Binary (vs ternary) annotation is a deliberate simplification. Contributions: (1) LectureBank dataset + taxonomy classification + vocabulary; (2) higher-agreement binary prerequisite annotations (921 edges); (3) empirical finding that oversampled SVM beats GAE/VGAE for this task. Clarity: Reproducible — hyperparameters, preprocessing (PDFMiner, python-pptx, Gensim), splits, and equations all given. Data released as links (copyright).

Contribution vs. Claim기여 대 주장

  • Stated claims: LectureBank is a useful in-domain dataset for prerequisite chain learning and NLP education; neural graph methods are compared with traditional classifiers.
  • Demonstrated: dataset built and annotated with substantial agreement; SVM (not the "novel" graph methods) gives best F1. The paper honestly reports that GAE/VGAE underperform SVM here (attributed to parallel-edge/oversampling mismatch with citation-network design).
  • Gap: F1 ~0.76 is modest; concept-graph recovery is shown only qualitatively on one random fold (14 vertices / 12 edges), not quantified as full-graph reconstruction. Downstream applications (search engine, reading-list generation) are described as future work, not evaluated.
Implementation Feasibility구현 실현가능성
  • Needs: LectureBank data (links released on GitHub), Gensim (Doc2Vec), scikit-learn classifiers, GAE/VGAE implementation (Kipf & Welling), PDFMiner / python-pptx for preprocessing. Modest compute (200 epochs, small graph).
  • Code: dataset at https://github.com/Yale-LILY/LectureBank (files as links for copyright reasons).
  • Modifications: for a general KB-construction setting, add automatic concept extraction, extend beyond binary prerequisite to typed dependencies, and swap Doc2Vec for modern embeddings.
  • Unknowns: missing link: how full concept-graph reconstruction (not just pairwise F1) would score; how the approach transfers outside the NLP/ML education domain.
Follow-up Papers관련 후속 논문
  • TutorialBank (Fabbri et al., 2018) — source of the 208 concepts and 305-topic taxonomy; the complementary training corpus.
  • Modeling Concept Dependencies in a Scientific Corpus (Gordon et al., 2016) — unsupervised prerequisite discovery on ACL Anthology; key prior baseline.
  • Prerequisite Relation Learning for Concepts in MOOCs (Pan et al., 2017a) — feature-based binary classification baseline the paper positions against.
  • LectureBank 2.0 / RefD and later prerequisite-relation work (Li et al.) — extend this benchmark; relevant as SP3 SOTA lineage.
Condition Delta — transfer to our setting
조건 차이 — 우리 환경으로의 전이

Paper's setting: inputs = 208 predefined NLP/ML concepts + Doc2Vec embeddings from lecture-file corpus; outputs = binary prerequisite label per concept pair; conditions = closed fixed concept vocabulary, manually annotated gold edges, NLP-education domain, supervised binary classification / link prediction. Our setting (dependency extraction for KB construction, SP3): inputs = arbitrary domain corpus; outputs = extracted dependency/prerequisite relations forming a knowledge base; conditions = potentially open concept set, automatic concept extraction, cross-domain. Differences:

  • Fixed 208-concept list vs open concept extraction: reference only — concepts here are given by TutorialBank, not discovered; our KB task needs concept extraction upstream.
  • Binary prerequisite vs richer dependency typology: missing link — only "is-prerequisite" is modeled; other dependency relation types are out of scope.
  • In-domain Doc2Vec (no Wikipedia) vs modern LLM/graph embeddings: resolved / partial — demonstrates corpus-only representation is viable, but embeddings are dated.
  • Evaluation is the standard reference benchmark for prerequisite relations: resolved — this is the widely-used evaluation dataset (SP3's stated role).
