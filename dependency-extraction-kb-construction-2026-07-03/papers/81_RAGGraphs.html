Retrieval-Augmented Generation with Graphs (GraphRAG)

Han et al. · arXiv · 2024 · 225 citations인용 225회

arXiv:2501.00309 · PDF · SP: SP6

P1Track B

Problem
해결하려는 문제

Existing RAG surveys focus on i.i.d. text/image data and uniform neural-embedding retrieval; only one prior survey [319] covers graphs, and it forces graphs into the conventional RAG architecture without addressing that graph-structured data is diverse-formatted, interdependent, and domain-specific. The GraphRAG research landscape is fragmented with inconsistent concepts, techniques, and datasets, and over-concentrated on knowledge/document graphs while overlooking other domains (a "bubble effect").

그래프 기반 RAG의 구성·검색 설계 공간이 정리되지 않았다.

Solution / Contribution
해결책 / 기여

A survey (Category: survey). Two-level contribution: (1) a holistic 5-component GraphRAG framework (query processor, retriever, organizer, generator, graph data source) unifying the design space globally; (2) domain specialization across 10 graph domains — knowledge, document, scientific, social, planning & reasoning, tabular, infrastructure, biological, scene, and random graphs — each reviewed by task applications (when to retrieve), graph construction + relational rationale (what to retrieve), and component-level techniques (how to retrieve). Plus challenges/future-directions. Formalized as: $\hat{Q}=\text{Processor}(Q)$; $C=\text{Retriever}(\hat{Q},G)$; $\hat{C}=\text{Organizer}(\hat{Q},C)$; $A=\text{Generator}(\hat{Q},\hat{C})$.

5-요소 × 10-도메인 GraphRAG taxonomy로 구성 대 검색 설계 공간을 조망하는 서베이를 제공한다.

Key Results
주요 결과
  • Survey — no empirical metrics on first 5 pages. Headline organizational result: taxonomy of GraphRAG into 5 components × 10 domains.
  • Public survey repository maintained at https://github.com/Graph-RAG/GraphRAG/ .
  • 225 citations (as of metadata) indicating strong field influence.
Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

Holistic GraphRAG framework (5 components)

  • What it computes: query $Q$ → Processor → preprocessed $\hat{Q}$ → Retriever(\hat{Q}, G) → content $C$ → Organizer(\hat{Q}, C) → refined $\hat{C}$ → Generator(\hat{Q}, \hat{C}) → answer $A$; the five components are query processor, retriever, organizer, generator, and graph data source $G$.
  • Why it was created: prior RAG surveys treat data as i.i.d. text/image chunks and cannot express relational/structural information; the field of graph-based RAG was fragmented with inconsistent concepts.
  • Concrete example: for KGQA "What drugs treat epithelioid sarcoma and affect the EZH2 gene product?", the retriever traverses the relational path Disease→[indication]→Drug→[target]→Gene to intersect candidate drugs, rather than BM25/embedding similarity.
  • How it is used in this paper: organizing spine of the survey; each of 10 domains is reviewed by how it instantiates these five components.

Graph construction from unstructured data

  • What it computes: unstructured/domain data → nodes + typed edges → domain-specific graph (document graph = sentence chunks as nodes; knowledge graph = triplets/paths; molecule graph = higher-order cellular complexes).
  • Why it was created: to make heterogeneous, diverse-format sources retrievable under a graph abstraction — directly relevant to SP6 (dependency extraction / KB construction).
  • Concrete example: document graph embeds entity sentence-chunks as nodes with relation edges enabling multi-hop traversal.
  • How it is used in this paper: each domain section summarizes its "what to retrieve" via graph construction methods and relational rationales.

Three differences graph data poses vs. conventional RAG

  • Diff 1 Unified vs. Diverse-Formatted: text/image are uniform 1D/2D grids; graphs span triplets, paths, cellular complexes, multimodal text-attributed graphs — defeats one-size-fits-all embedding retrieval.
  • Diff 2 Independent vs. Interdependent: RAG chunks are indexed independently (blocks multi-hop); GraphRAG edges enable multi-hop traversal, structural organization (graph pruning), and structure-aware generation (positional encoding).
  • Diff 3 Domain Invariance vs. Domain-specific: images/text share transferable units (textures, tokenizer vocab) enabling scaling laws; graph generative processes vary by domain (homophily for citation topic vs. structural role for airport hubs), so no unified GraphRAG is feasible.

Condition Delta — transfer to our setting
조건 차이 — 우리 환경으로의 전이

Paper's setting: inputs = user query + graph-structured data source (10 domains); outputs = generated answer via 5-component GraphRAG pipeline; conditions = survey of published GraphRAG systems, emphasis on retrieval over graphs. Our setting (SP6, dependency extraction / KB construction): inputs = unstructured source (code/docs) → extract dependencies → construct a knowledge base/graph; outputs = structured dependency graph / KB; conditions = building the graph, not (only) retrieving over it. Differences:

  • Scope emphasis: paper centers on retrieval + generation over an existing graph; SP6 centers on graph/KB construction. — resolved: the paper's per-domain "graph construction methods (what to retrieve)" subsections directly inform KB construction.
  • Framing: survey/taxonomy vs. our need for a concrete pipeline. — reference only (situates design space, provides no runnable method).
  • Domain match: SP6 likely maps to document graph + knowledge graph domains; paper covers these most heavily. — resolved: relevant domains are the best-covered.
