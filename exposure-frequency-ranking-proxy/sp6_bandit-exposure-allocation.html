SP6: Bandit and Online Learning for Exposure Allocation

15 papers · targets O1, O2, O4 · 2019-2026

Do bandit methods fill O2's score-free column — exposure allocation with regret guarantees under an immutable black-box scorer — and do their bounds transfer to position-weighted exposure units and non-stationary arrivals?

SP6: Exposure 배분을 위한 bandit·online learning

15편 논문 · O1, O2, O4 목표 대상 · 2019-2026

bandit 방법이 O2의 score-free 열 — 불변 black-box scorer 하에서 regret 보장을 갖는 exposure 배분 — 을 채우는지, 그 한계가 position 가중 단위와 비정상(non-stationary) 도착으로 이전되는지를 검사한다.

Entry Assumption vs. What Was Found

Expected (revised after SP2/SP4/SP5: separate 'learn the exposure estimate' from 'learn the scores'; check whether regret bounds double as the missing concentration bounds; check ledger-state regret under non-stationarity and position-weighted denomination): Bandit/exploration methods allocate impressions online without access to the underlying scorer, making them score-free candidates for O2's feasibility map; fairness-constrained bandits give regret guarantees under minimum-exposure constraints.

Found: The split is structural: FairX-style merit-proportional methods define merit as a function of their own estimated means — they replace the scorer, and under trusted immutable scores the learning problem degenerates to a closed-form target plus SP4 realization. The genuine score-free column is the quota-floor family: Fair-Learn wraps any black-box decision-maker with an O(1)-per-round debt-priority override and a deterministic anytime count floor.

Assessment: Pivoted — FairX was expected to be the flagship applicable method, but under the user's constraint its learning problem vanishes; the user's actual regime (fixed noisy scores, exposure-only control) is addressed by nobody — this became O4 gap 1 (the degenerate regime). Also negative: bandit regret bounds cannot close SP4's open-loop concentration gap even in principle (fairness regret is a policy-space distance).

사전 가정과 실제 발견

기대 (SP2/SP4/SP5 이후 수정: exposure 추정 학습과 score 학습의 분리, regret 한계가 결핍된 집중 한계를 겸하는지, 비정상 도착과 position 가중 단위 검사): bandit/탐색 방법은 기저 scorer에 접근하지 않고 온라인으로 impression을 배분하므로 O2 실행 가능성 지도의 score-free 후보이며, 공정성 제약 bandit이 최소 exposure 제약 하의 regret 보장을 줄 것으로 기대하였다.

발견: 분리는 구조적이다: FairX류 merit 비례 방법은 merit를 자신이 추정한 평균의 함수로 정의하므로 scorer를 대체하며, 신뢰된 불변 score 아래에서는 학습 문제가 폐형 목표 + SP4 실현으로 퇴화한다. 진짜 score-free 열은 quota-floor 계열이다: Fair-Learn은 임의의 black-box 의사결정자를 라운드당 O(1)의 debt-priority override로 감싸 결정론적 anytime count floor를 준다.

평가: 전환됨 — FairX가 대표 적용 방법일 것으로 기대했으나 사용자 제약 하에서는 학습 문제 자체가 소멸한다. 사용자의 실제 regime(고정된 잡음 score, exposure 전용 제어)은 아무도 다루지 않는다 — 이것이 O4 gap 1(퇴화 regime)이 되었다. 또한 bandit regret 한계는 원리적으로도 SP4의 open-loop 집중 gap을 닫을 수 없다(공정성 regret는 정책 공간 거리이다).

What SP6 Establishes

The bandit exposure-allocation literature splits cleanly along the survey's respect-vs-replace axis, and the split is structural, not incidental. The dominant merit-proportional line (FairX [55] FairExposureBandits and its restless-bandit descendant [64] OnlineRestlessExposure) REPLACES the scorer: merit is defined as a function $f(\mu^*)$ of the bandit's own estimated mean reward, so its fairness-regret bounds are bounds on learning the scores — under the user's constraint (scores given, immutable, trusted) the entire learning problem degenerates, because the optimal fair policy $\pi^* \propto f(\text{score})$ is computable in closed form and only a realization mechanism (SP4) is needed. The constraint-based line is the genuine score-free column: minimum-exposure floors enforced by debt counters or virtual queues ([66] FairSMAB, [65] SleepingFairness, [67] FairContextualMAB, [58] RestlessFairMAB) hold irrespective of the learner — Fair-Learn [66] FairSMAB explicitly wraps any black-box decision-maker with an O(1)-per-round debt-priority override and delivers the strongest realized-exposure guarantee found anywhere in this survey: a deterministic, anytime, uniform-in-time count floor ($\lfloor r_i t\rfloor - N_{i,t} \le \alpha$ at every round), with the utility price exactly characterized (regret becomes $O(T)$ precisely when quotas bind on truly suboptimal arms).

Three qualifications bound this answer. (1) Every constraint-violation guarantee is count/pull-denominated; exactly one paper ([56] ConcaveRewards) proves regret in position-weighted exposure units, and that bound is on the amortized concave objective (target side), not on realized-exposure concentration — the count→position transfer gap survives a fourth SP. (2) No bandit bound closes SP4's open-loop concentration gap: fairness regrets ($\sum_t \|\pi^* - \pi_t\|_1$) live in policy space, and the results that do bound realized counts (Fair-Learn's debt rule, LFG's virtual queues) are ledger-feedback mechanisms — closed-loop by construction, confirming SP5's pattern (uuid 18) across a fourth literature. (3) Non-stationarity and estimated relevance are handled at most one at a time: [67] FairContextualMAB takes adversarial losses but requires a known stationary context distribution and single pulls; the restless line ([58] RestlessFairMAB, [64] OnlineRestlessExposure) handles Markov item-state drift but not arrival drift; nobody combines either with position-weighted exposure. On the production question, [62] ItemCentricExploration (RecSys 2025 Industry, YouTube Shorts) documents deliberate cold-start over-exposure as a managed impression budget, gated by a score-read-only show/don't-show filter — a new member of the strict frequency-only family, and evidence that match quality dominates exposure quantity (−20% exploration impressions with better cold-start outcomes).

SP6이 확립한 것

SP6는 bandit 문헌이 조사의 respect-vs-replace 축을 따라 깔끔히 갈라짐을 확립한다. merit 비례 라인(FairX와 그 restless 후속)은 merit = f(자체 추정 평균)으로 scorer를 대체한다 — 신뢰된 불변 score 아래에서는 최적 공정 정책 π* ∝ f(score)가 폐형으로 계산되어 학습 문제 전체가 퇴화하고 SP4의 실현 메커니즘만 남는다. 진짜 score-free 열은 제약 기반 quota-floor 계열이다: Fair-Learn은 임의의 black-box 학습기를 감싸 매 라운드 ⌊r_i t⌋−N_{i,t}≤α의 결정론적·anytime·학습기 독립 count floor를 O(1)/라운드에 보장하며, 효용 대가도 정확히 특성화된다(quota가 진짜 열등 arm에 구속될 때만 regret O(T)). 한정 사항: 제약 위반 보장은 전부 count 단위이고(position 가중 regret는 ConcaveRewards의 목표 측 하나뿐), 실현 count 보장은 다시 폐루프 ledger 메커니즘이며, 비정상 도착과 추정 relevance는 한 번에 하나씩만 다뤄진다.

Convergences수렴 지점
  • Score-free exposure floors with learner-independent guarantees are a solved problem — in count units. [66] FairSMAB (deterministic anytime), [65] SleepingFairness (asymptotic, feasibility-optimal), [67] FairContextualMAB (adversarial losses, no rate loss), and [58] RestlessFairMAB (restless, sublinear violation regret) independently establish that minimum-exposure constraints can be enforced without any score access, and converge on the same cost characterization: the floor is free until the quota exceeds the exposure an arm would naturally earn, after which the price is linear in the excess. The mechanism is shared: a debt/queue dual variable converted into per-round forced allocation.
  • Exploration is not an exposure-balancing mechanism. [63] CascadingExposure (larger UCB α does not improve fairness; bias stops falling once exploration decays), [55] FairExposureBandits (UCB/TS concentrate all exposure winner-takes-all even between near-equal arms), and [68] EARS (greedy selection converts ε relevance gaps into maximal exposure gaps) agree via different models: fair exposure requires an explicit allocation target or constraint, not exploration noise.
  • Realized-exposure guarantees come only from closed-loop ledger mechanisms. Fairness regrets ([55] FairExposureBandits, [64] OnlineRestlessExposure) are policy-space quantities ($\ell^1$ to the target policy), leaving the sampling deviation of realized counts unbounded; the results that do bound realized counts — [66] FairSMAB's debt invariant, [65] SleepingFairness's queues — feed realized counts back into the decision. This is the same pattern SP5 found (uuid 18), now confirmed in a fourth literature: no open-loop realized-vs-target concentration bound exists here either.
  • Merit-proportional learnability requires merit-function regularity. [55] FairExposureBandits proves necessity (linear fairness regret without min-merit $\gamma$ or Lipschitz $L$); [64] OnlineRestlessExposure inherits $\gamma, L$ multiplicatively in its restless-bandit bound — two settings, same structural conditions.
Divergences분기 지점
  • Hard floors vs proportional control, round two. [59] FairOLTR finds FairCo's proportional exposure control "overreacts" (worse online NDCG and unfairness than hard placement templates) in OL2R, while SP5's adjudication found FairCo reaches the LP unfairness floor in the known-relevance amortized regime. Likely cause: regime difference again — under a learning ranker with uncertain relevance, proportional control amplifies estimation error (it promotes all under-exposed-group items uniformly), whereas with converged relevance the same controller is target-adequate. Consistent with, and extends, uuid 15's regime-dependence: relevance-estimate maturity is a second moderator alongside steepness. Unresolved formally (FairOLTR is arXiv-only, no cumulative-regret bound).
  • Equality-Gini vs merit-proportional metrics rank methods oppositely. [63] CascadingExposure's EALinUCB beats FRMLinUCB (=[55] FairExposureBandits) on 1−Gini equality metrics — but FairX optimizes merit-proportional exposure, which a Gini-uniformity metric penalizes by design. Cause: different fairness targets, not method quality; a comparison is only meaningful within a target family (same lesson as SP3's target dichotomy).
  • "Under-exposed items need more exposure" vs "wrong-audience exposure harms them." The fairness-bandit line treats exposure as the terminal currency to be topped up; [62] ItemCentricExploration shows in production that removing 20% of exploration impressions (the badly-matched ones) improves cold-item outcomes and corpus growth. Cause: exposure→utility conversion is heterogeneous across (item, audience) pairs (uuid 8) — a floor denominated in raw impressions can be satisfied with worthless impressions.
Prior Beliefs and Surprises (full)사전 믿음과 놀라움 (전문)

What the entry rationale expected to find (revised rationale after SP1/SP2/SP4/SP5):

Bandit methods fill O2's score-free column with regret guarantees; separate "respect black-box scores" from "replace them"; check whether any exposure-allocation regret bound doubles as SP4's missing open-loop concentration bound; check ledger-state regret bounds under non-stationary arrivals and estimated relevance, and position-weighted denomination.

Confirmed:

  • The respect-vs-replace separation is real and structural: the merit-proportional line replaces the scorer (merit = function of learned means); the quota-floor line is genuinely score-free ([66] FairSMAB learner-independent by proof).
  • Bandits do fill the score-free column with regret guarantees — but only for count-denominated floors, exactly as the pre-loop landscape read anticipated ("must separate respect from replace").
  • Google production cold-start over-exposure documented as expected ([62] ItemCentricExploration).

Surprised:

  • (a) Assumed: bandit fairness regret bounds might double as SP4's open-loop concentration bounds. Found: they cannot even in principle — fairness regret is a policy-space distance, leaving realized-draw deviation unbounded; every realized-count guarantee is again a closed-loop ledger mechanism. Why wrong: conflated "bound involving exposure" with "bound on realized exposure"; the literature's regret currency is target-side.
  • (b) Assumed: under the user's constraint, FairX would be the flagship applicable method. Found: with trusted immutable scores the FairX learning problem degenerates entirely (closed-form target, SP4 realization); its machinery is relevant only in the score-distrust regime it uses to justify replacing the scorer. The user's actual regime — fixed noisy scores, exposure-only control — is addressed by nobody. Why wrong: the pre-loop read centered Wang & Joachims 2021 without checking what remains of the problem once scores are exogenous.
  • (c) Assumed: cold-start exploration = over-exposure boosting. Found: the production system's win came from reducing exploration impressions via better matching — impression quantity is not the binding resource. Why wrong: carried the fairness literature's exposure-as-terminal-currency premise into the production setting where exposure→utility heterogeneity (uuid 8) dominates.

Key Concepts핵심 개념

Merit-proportional exposure target and fairness regret (FairX)

  • What it computes: given per-arm mean rewards $\mu^*$ and a designer-chosen increasing merit function $f$ with $f \ge \gamma > 0$ and Lipschitz constant $L$, the target policy is $\pi^*(a) = f(\mu^*_a)/\sum_{a'} f(\mu^*_{a'})$; fairness regret is $FR_T = \sum_{t=1}^T \sum_a |\pi^*(a) - \pi_t(a)|$, the cumulative $\ell^1$ distance between deployed and target policies.
  • Why it was created: conventional bandits converge to winner-takes-all — two arms with nearly identical merit receive drastically different exposure; a regret notion against the fair policy (not the argmax arm) was needed to measure how fast an algorithm learns to allocate exposure proportionally.
  • Concrete example: two arms with means $(\theta, 2\theta)$ and identity merit give target $[1/3, 2/3]$ instead of $[0,1]$; UCB accumulates $FR_T$ linear in $T$ (~12,000 at $T$=200k on Yahoo! data) vs sub-linear $\tilde{O}(L\sqrt{KT}/\gamma)$ for FairX.
  • Role in this SP: the shared normative target of [55] FairExposureBandits, [64] OnlineRestlessExposure, and the FRM baseline in [63] CascadingExposure; its necessity conditions ($\gamma$, $L$) are exactly conditions on the score→exposure map when the user adopts merit-proportional targets over black-box scores.

Minimum-selection-fraction floor with debt/virtual-queue enforcement

  • What it computes: an exogenous quota vector $r$ ($r_i$ = required long-run selection fraction of arm $i$); enforcement maintains a per-arm debt counter — Fair-Learn's $r_i(t-1) - N_{i,t-1}$ or LFG's virtual queue $Q_i(t) = [Q_i(t-1) + r_i - d_i(t-1)]^+$ — and forces a pull of the most-indebted arm when debt exceeds a tolerance, otherwise delegates to the base learner.
  • Why it was created: long-term average constraints are structurally incompatible with per-round greedy selection; the debt/queue device (imported from Lyapunov drift-plus-penalty network optimization) converts the time-average constraint into per-round dynamics without touching the learner's scores.
  • Concrete example: $r = (0.2, 0.3, 0.25)$, tolerance $\alpha = 0$: at $t=100$, arm 2 must have been pulled ≥ 30 times, deterministically, regardless of its rewards or of which learner is plugged in.
  • Role in this SP: the score-free mechanism of [66] FairSMAB, [65] SleepingFairness, [67] FairContextualMAB, [58] RestlessFairMAB, [61] MinRevenueBandits; the bandit-theory anchor for O2's frequency-only floors and the formal ancestor of SP5/SP7-style dual-variable pacing (a virtual queue is a dual variable).

α-fairness (anytime deterministic floor)

  • What it computes: the certificate $\lfloor r_i t \rfloor - N_{i,t} \le \alpha$ for all arms $i$ and all rounds $t$ — a uniform-in-time, deterministic (not in-expectation, not asymptotic) bound on realized count shortfall, with slack knob $\alpha \ge 0$.
  • Why it was created: prior floors held only asymptotically (LFG's lim-inf) or in expectation (Celis et al.), so any finite prefix could violate the quota; production exposure contracts need every-round guarantees with unknown horizon.
  • Concrete example: $\alpha = 2$, $r_i = 0.1$: the arm is never more than 2 pulls behind $\lfloor 0.1 t\rfloor$ at any time, no matter what the wrapped learner does.
  • Role in this SP: proven learner-independently in [66] FairSMAB (Theorem 1); the strongest realized-exposure guarantee in the survey to date — but count-based and floor-only.

Frank-Wolfe reduction for contextual bandits with concave rewards (CBCR)

  • What it computes: for a known concave welfare $f$ over the vector of average rewards (e.g., user utility minus a Gini penalty on per-item cumulative exposures), run Frank-Wolfe in reward space: the per-round FW gradient $\nabla f(\hat{s}_{t-1})$ (computed from the realized exposure ledger $\hat{s}_{t-1}$) defines a scalar-reward bandit; any scalar bandit with regret $R^{scal}_T$ yields welfare regret $R_T \le (R^{scal}_T + O(\sqrt{T}))/T$.
  • Why it was created: policy-space optimization over continuous contexts is intractable; the reduction bypasses policy space and lets any exploration principle plug in.
  • Concrete example: FW-LinUCBRank serves a ranking per round by top-$\bar{k}$ sorting UCB scores plus an adaptive $\nabla f$ bonus that boosts under-exposed items; welfare regret $O(d\bar{k}\ln(T/\delta)/\sqrt{T})$ with position weights $b_k$ inside the exposure coordinates.
  • Role in this SP: [56] ConcaveRewards — the only regret guarantee in the entire SP denominated in position-weighted exposure units, and the regret-theoretic foundation for ledger-boosted sorting (its full-information counterpart is SP5's OFFR).

Position-based exposure, amortized fairness ledgers, and dual-variable pacing: see SP1/SP5 Key Concepts.

Open Questions After SP6SP6 이후 미해결 질문

  • Position-weighted floor guarantees: every constraint-violation bound in this SP is count/pull-denominated; [65] SleepingFairness and [66] FairSMAB both flag the slate/position-weighted extension as an explicit unknown (missing link in both notes: whether the queue/debt invariants survive unequal per-slot exposure weights), and [56] ConcaveRewards's position-weighted bound is objective-side only. The count→position transfer gap has now survived SP1, SP3, SP5, and SP6 — SP7 (ads pacing, where impressions are the native currency) is the last candidate before it is O4-final. What would resolve it: a concentration or violation bound for a debt-style controller where the "pull" is a position-weighted exposure increment.
  • The intermediate score-trust regime: no paper treats scores as given, immutable, partially informative inputs to exposure allocation. [55] FairExposureBandits assumes merit is a function of the true mean (learned by the bandit itself — missing link: sensitivity to misspecified/estimated merit); [64] OnlineRestlessExposure assumes merit identifiable independently of the exposure policy (missing link: no exposure→merit feedback). Under trusted scores the problem degenerates to realization; under untrusted scores the literature replaces the scorer. The user's actual regime — fixed noisy scores, exposure-frequency-only control, guarantees on realized exposure vs a score-derived target — is unaddressed by any of the 15 papers. What would resolve it: a regret/violation analysis where the merit input is an exogenous score with bounded (or adversarial) error and the only decision variable is impression allocation.
  • Online tuning of correction strength: [63] CascadingExposure's γ is dataset-sensitive (γ≈0.005 helps, γ=0.2 collapses clicks) and offline-tuned; [68] EARS's ε is a fixed designer knob. Despite SP2's seed (correction-strength tuning as an online-learning problem) and both quantities being one-dimensional online decisions with observable feedback, no paper formulates the tuning itself as a bandit/online-learning problem. What would resolve it: regret analysis for adapting γ/ε online against a drift or utility-floor criterion.
  • Non-stationary arrivals + estimated relevance + slates, jointly: [67] FairContextualMAB achieves adversarial losses only with known stationary context distribution and single pulls; [56] ConcaveRewards requires iid contexts and defers feedback loops; the restless line covers item-state drift only. No paper delivers a ledger-state guarantee under arrival drift with estimated relevance in a ranking action space — the question SP5 handed to SP6 comes back negative.
  • Multi-pull merit-proportional feasibility: [64] OnlineRestlessExposure concedes merit-proportional exposure can be structurally infeasible for $K>1$ (one arm's merit exceeding $K\times$ others') and offers no formal fairness notion for the multi-pull case — precisely the slate regime rankings need.
