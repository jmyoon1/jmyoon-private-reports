SP8: Popularity Debiasing and Calibrated Exposure versus Relevance

15 papers · targets O1, O2 · 2020-2026

Completes the controlled-objective axis (O1): when does observed exposure deviate from merited exposure, and how does the debiasing/calibration literature reallocate impressions — organized by what the exposure target SHOULD be proportional to, and by post-hoc vs retraining feasibility (O2).

SP8: Popularity debiasing과 calibrated exposure

15편 논문 · O1, O2 목표 대상 · 2020-2026

O1의 제어 목표 축을 완성한다: 관측된 exposure가 응당한(merited) exposure에서 언제 벗어나며, debiasing/calibration 문헌은 impression을 어떻게 재배분하는가 — exposure 목표가 무엇에 비례해야 하는지(기준 분포)와 post-hoc 대 재학습 실행 가능성(O2)으로 조직한다.

Entry Assumption vs. What Was Found

Expected (revised after SP1–SP7, seven accumulated lenses — most decisively the FINAL check on the degenerate-regime gap and the dynamic-immutability lens from SP7): Organize debiasing papers by reference distribution; test the double-win lens (fairness interventions that improve accuracy); run the final check for exposure-only correction guarantees over fixed noisy exogenous scores; check whether any method models the scorer's post-intervention update.

Found: The 'when does exposure deviate' half is answered with a mechanism: θ^t>0 compounds monotonically through the exposure→clicks→retraining loop, exhibited by every standard algorithm tested. The 'toward what' half yields only a taxonomy of four defective reference classes (historical-frequency circular; merit-proportional imports score error; engagement-learned myopic; uniform arbitrary) — no paper adjudicates or estimates a contamination-free reference.

Assessment: Surprised — (a) the closed-loop intervention evaluation assumed absent actually exists (DynamicExposure's 600-round loop: memoryless per-round constraints fail cumulatively; cumulative-ledger normalization fixes it empirically); (b) the exposure→training-data coupling is not only a hazard: InvR's kick-start exploits it beneficially in production. The degenerate-regime gap survived its last candidate literature — O4-FINAL.

사전 가정과 실제 발견

기대 (SP1–SP7 이후 수정, 일곱 개 누적 렌즈 — 특히 퇴화 regime gap의 최종 검사와 SP7의 동적 immutability 렌즈): debiasing 논문을 기준 분포로 조직하고, double-win 렌즈(정확도를 올리는 공정성 개입)를 검정하며, 고정된 잡음 외생 score 위 exposure 전용 교정 보장의 최종 검사와 개입 후 scorer 갱신을 모델링하는 방법의 존재 여부를 확인하기로 하였다.

발견: '언제 벗어나는가'의 절반은 메커니즘으로 답해졌다: θ^t>0이면 exposure→클릭→재학습 루프를 통해 단조 증폭되며, 검증된 모든 표준 알고리즘이 이를 보인다. '무엇을 향해'의 절반은 네 가지 결함 있는 기준 분포 계열의 taxonomy만 남았다(역사적 빈도는 순환적, merit 비례는 score 오차 수입, engagement 학습은 근시안적, 균등은 규범적으로 자의적) — 어느 논문도 계열 간 판정이나 오염 없는 기준 추정을 하지 않는다.

평가: 예상과 다름 — (a) 없다고 가정했던 폐루프 개입 평가가 실재한다(DynamicExposure의 600 라운드 루프: 라운드별 memoryless 제약은 누적으로 실패, 누적 ledger 정규화가 경험적으로 교정). (b) exposure→학습 데이터 결합은 위협만이 아니다: InvR의 kick-start는 이를 production에서 유익하게 활용한다. 퇴화 regime gap은 마지막 후보 문헌에서도 생존 — O4 최종 확정.

What SP8 Establishes

The literature answers the "when does observed exposure deviate from merited exposure" half of this SP's question decisively, with a mechanism: any recommender whose recommendations are on average more popular than its training data (per-iteration uplift $\theta^t > 0$) monotonically compounds that deviation through the exposure→clicks→retraining loop ([86] FeedbackLoop Eq. 4), and every standard algorithm tested exhibits $\theta^t > 0$ — ΔGAP is positive for the mainstream artist group and negative for the 97%+ of providers in the middle/tail under all five classic CF algorithms except Random ([93] ArtistExposure), and cumulative exposure Gini worsens over 600 simulated retrain rounds under an uncorrected scorer ([95] DynamicExposure).

The "how to reallocate" half splits cleanly into a retraining column (causal debiasing, regularizers, architectures — infeasible under our constraints) and a post-hoc distribution-matching column (Steck-style calibrated re-ranking and its descendants — score-read-only, directly feasible). But the deepest question — reallocate toward what? — is answered only as a taxonomy of four defective reference classes: historical-frequency references are themselves exposure-contaminated (circularity), merit-proportional references import score error, engagement-learned references are myopic, and uniform/floor references are normatively arbitrary. No paper adjudicates between reference classes or estimates a contamination-free reference. And the survey-level FINAL check completes here: no SP8 paper provides exposure-only correction guarantees when scores are fixed noisy exogenous inputs — the degenerate-regime gap (uuid 23) survives its last candidate literature and is O4-FINAL.

SP8이 확립한 것

SP8은 제어 목표 축을 완성하며 두 가지 절반으로 답한다. (1) 이탈의 시점과 메커니즘: 추천이 학습 데이터보다 평균적으로 더 인기 편향되면(θ^t>0) exposure→클릭→재학습 루프가 그 편차를 단조 증폭하고(FeedbackLoop Eq. 4), 5개 고전 CF 알고리즘 전부에서 주류 집단만 ΔGAP 양수, 나머지 97% provider는 음수이며, 무교정 scorer 하에서 누적 exposure Gini는 600 라운드에 걸쳐 악화된다. (2) 재배분 방법: 재학습 열(인과 debiasing, 정규화기, 아키텍처 — 제약상 실행 불가)과 post-hoc 분포 정합 열(Steck류 calibrated re-ranking — score-read-only, 직접 실행 가능)로 갈린다. 남는 최심층 질문 '무엇을 향해 재배분하는가'는 네 결함 계열의 taxonomy로만 답해져 있다. 또한 double-win이 다섯 개의 독립적 online/필드 근거로 판정되었다: 정확도-exposure trade-off는 상당 부분 exposure 편향 로그 위 offline 평가의 인공물이다 — 조건부이지 보편은 아니다.

Dominant Approach지배적 접근

The dominant post-hoc family is distribution-matching greedy re-ranking founded by [84] CalibratedRec: define a target distribution, measure the list's divergence from it, and construct the list greedily under a $(1-\lambda)$·relevance − $\lambda$·divergence objective. It became dominant because it is score-read-only, model-agnostic, cheap ($O(nkm)$), and target-agnostic — [89] CalibSurvey catalogs 53 papers, all sharing this pipeline while varying target definitions, divergences, and solvers. The 2024–2026 frontier innovates entirely on the target side, leaving the realization machinery frozen exactly as SP4 predicted: [90] CalibBandits (Spotify, deployed 2025) makes the target a contextual-bandit action while reusing Steck's 2018 greedy re-ranker verbatim; [91] CaliRec (LeapRec) makes the target recency-weighted and the trade-off rank-dependent ($\lambda^{1/k}$: relevance wins at top ranks, calibration at bottom ranks); [88] CausalEPP forecasts intervention values from popularity trends. A structurally telling regularity: the entire calibration line has used whole-list greedy/flow/MIP construction — never score adjustment — since 2018, because the list-level KL objective is composition-dependent (non-decomposable over items). This is an eight-years-early instance of [50] ScoringNotEnough's planner-vs-sorter theorem; even LeapRec's training-phase attempt to push calibration robustness into the scores (CD-BPR) still requires the greedy list constructor at inference.

The dominant family's key limitation is the reference-distribution problem. Sorting SP8's papers by what exposure should be proportional to yields four classes, each defective: (i) historical-frequency references (user's own history: Steck, LeapRec; popularity tiers: Calibrated Popularity; data popularity share: ΔGAP) are computed from logs that are themselves exposure-contaminated — [93] ArtistExposure's "merit" is data popularity and [88] CausalEPP's per-user popularity-preference signal is computed from exposure-biased clicks, so the reference re-imports the loop bias the intervention is meant to remove; (ii) merit-proportional references ([01] FoE DTR, [11] EquityOfAttention) import score error into the target (SP3's score-trust dichotomy, uuid 12); (iii) realized-utility references ([90] CalibBandits: target chosen to maximize engagement) sidestep both defects but optimize a short-term binary reward the authors themselves flag as the unsolved piece; (iv) uniform/floor references ([95] DynamicExposure uniform, [92] RepurposeLongTail minimum-impression floor) are merit-blind and score-robust but normatively arbitrary (threshold/target choice is flagged open in [89] CalibSurvey). The retraining alternative ([87] ControllingPopBias regularizers, [88] CausalEPP causal intervention, [94] CDN frequency-gated architecture) achieves stronger per-model debiasing but is off the O2 frontier entirely.

For the survey objectives this closes the O1 taxonomy's last column (debiasing/calibration/long-tail lift as controlled objectives) and sharpens O2 and O4. On O2: the feasible subset is the distribution-matching re-rankers (score-read-only, slate-level) plus InvR (frequency-level impression floors — the show/don't-show family's SP8 member). On O4, two survey-level checks conclude here. First, the degenerate-regime gap is O4-FINAL: the debiasing literature either retrains the scorer or re-ranks trusting it — no paper gives exposure-only correction guarantees over fixed noisy exogenous scores. Second, the double-win divergence held open since SP3 (uuid 13) is adjudicated: online and field evidence ([89] CalibSurvey's Klimashevskaia field test — tail promotion with no CTR loss; [90] CalibBandits +3.93% overall impression-to-stream online; [91] CaliRec accuracy rising with calibration weight; [94] CDN head and tail improving simultaneously; [88] CausalEPP accuracy gains from debiasing) shows the accuracy-vs-exposure trade-off is substantially an artifact of offline evaluation on exposure-biased logs over a popularity-miscalibrated scorer — exactly the hypothesized mechanism. The counterweight ([92] RepurposeLongTail: −1.04% overall CTR for +33% tail exposure share) shows the double-win is conditional on the base scorer being miscalibrated in the direction the intervention corrects, not universal.

Convergences수렴 지점
  • Amplification is algorithm-generic, not an artifact of one model. [86] FeedbackLoop (formal $\theta^t$ argument + 3 algorithms over 20 retrain rounds), [93] ArtistExposure (ΔGAP positive for the head group under all 5 non-random algorithms), and [95] DynamicExposure (uncorrected MF's cumulative exposure Gini worsening over 600 rounds) independently establish that recommenders amplify — not merely reproduce — the data's popularity skew. Mechanism: any positive recommendation-over-data popularity uplift, once logged and retrained on, compounds.
  • Popularity carries genuine relevance signal; full removal hurts. [88] CausalEPP's ablation (full removal −41.18% vs partial trend-forecast intervention), [94] CDN (item frequency as a gating feature improves both head and tail; full-rebalance baselines collapse overall accuracy), and [85] BiasDebiasSurvey (names the "double-edged nature of popularity bias" as an open problem) converge with SP2's ExposureAwareRetrieval (γ=0.65 optimum): exposure/popularity is a mixture of bias and signal, so correction strength is non-monotone in value.
  • 2024–2026 innovation is entirely target-side; realization machinery is frozen. [90] CalibBandits (bandit-learned target, verbatim 2018 greedy re-ranker), [91] CaliRec (recency-weighted target, rank-dependent λ, same greedy scaffold), [88] CausalEPP (forecast intervention values) — independently confirming SP4's frozen-machinery finding in a fourth literature.
  • The double-win is real online. Klimashevskaia field test (via [89] CalibSurvey: tail promotion, no CTR loss), [90] CalibBandits (+3.93% i2s, +1.28% consumption in production A/B), [91] CaliRec (accuracy rises with calibration weight to λ=0.7 on Goodreads), [94] CDN (+1.43% online KPI with tail slice +3.75%), [88] CausalEPP (accuracy gains from debiasing) — five independent legs that reallocating exposure over a popularity-miscalibrated scorer frequently improves accuracy.
  • Distribution-matching is all whole-list construction. [84] CalibratedRec, [89] CalibSurvey (all 53 papers), [90] CalibBandits, [91] CaliRec, [95] DynamicExposure (flow-based) — zero score-adjustment realizations of composition-dependent targets, consistent with [50] ScoringNotEnough's theorem and uuid 19's planner column.
Divergences분기 지점
  • Offline trade-off vs online double-win. Offline evaluations show a (small) accuracy cost of calibration/fairness re-ranking ([84] CalibratedRec "small accuracy loss"; [95] DynamicExposure MF slightly above DM variants on nDCG); online/field evaluations show zero cost or gains (Klimashevskaia, [90] CalibBandits, [94] CDN). Likely cause — adjudicated: offline evaluation on exposure-biased logs treats the logged (miscalibrated) policy as ground truth, so any exposure deviation is scored as error; when the base scorer over-states head-item merit, reallocation toward the tail recovers true matches that offline logs cannot credit. [92] RepurposeLongTail's −1.04% overall CTR shows the win is conditional, not universal — the residual moderator (how miscalibrated the base scorer is) is unmeasured.
  • Uniform vs merit-proportional reference. [95] DynamicExposure equalizes exposure over all items (uniform is the goal); [01] FoE/[11] EquityOfAttention require exposure proportional to merit. This is a normative divergence no SP8 paper adjudicates — and the two sides fail differently under corrupted scores (uniform survives, merit imports error; uuid 12).
  • Score-ordering vs user-rank ordering inside InvR. Absolute embedding scores (popularity-biased) lost to user-rank ordering on CTR in [92] RepurposeLongTail's A/B — the authors explicitly cannot explain the magnitude. Minor, but it is production evidence that even within a debiasing overlay, score trust degrades the intervention.

Key Concepts핵심 개념

Calibration (of a recommendation list, Steck 2018)

  • What it computes: user interaction history + recommended list → compare the category distribution of the history $p(c|u)$ against the (rank-weighted, smoothed) distribution of the list $\tilde{q}(c|u)$ via a divergence (usually KL) → a scalar miscalibration score; minimized by greedy list construction maximizing $(1-\lambda)\,\mathrm{REL}(L) - \lambda\,C_{KL}(p, q(L))$.
  • Why it was created: accuracy-optimized rankers crowd out a user's lesser interests — a 70% romance / 30% action history yields a ~100% romance list.
  • Concrete example: [90] CalibBandits uses MRR rank weights, so a podcast shelf at rank 1 contributes weight 1.0 vs 0.1 at rank 10 to the list's effective exposure distribution — calibration is measured in position-weighted exposure units.
  • Role in this SP: the founding formalism of the distribution-matching column ([84] CalibratedRec, [89] CalibSurvey, [91] CaliRec, [90] CalibBandits); the divergence-plus-greedy machinery is target-agnostic, so it is the O2-relevant realization mechanism for any exposure reference distribution.

Reference distribution (the exposure target's normative anchor)

  • What it computes: a distribution over items/categories/providers that observed exposure is compared against or steered toward; the choice defines what "merited exposure" means.
  • Why it was created: implicitly, by every debiasing paper — each must pick a baseline against which exposure counts as "biased."
  • Concrete example: Steck uses the user's own history proportions; Calibrated Popularity uses the user's historical Head/Mid/Tail popularity mix; [95] DynamicExposure uses uniform-over-items; [01] FoE uses group merit (relevance) proportionality; [90] CalibBandits learns the target as a contextual-bandit action rewarded by engagement.
  • Role in this SP: the organizing axis of the synthesis (see Dominant Approach); each of the four reference classes carries a named, unresolved defect.

ΔGAP (change in Group Average Popularity)

  • What it computes: for a provider group $G$, the relative change $\Delta GAP(G) = (GAP(G)_r - GAP(G)_d)/GAP(G)_d$ between the group's average popularity in recommendations vs in training data → signed percentage isolating algorithmic amplification from pre-existing data skew.
  • Why it was created: raw long-tail plots cannot distinguish "users genuinely prefer popular items" from "the algorithm amplifies the existing skew."
  • Concrete example: Most-pop yields ΔGAP ≈ +760% for mainstream artists on LastFM; UserKNN/NMF sit between Random (the only algorithm favoring the niche group) and Most-pop.
  • Role in this SP: [93] ArtistExposure's diagnosis metric; its known circularity (the "merited" baseline is data popularity, itself exposure-biased) exemplifies the historical-reference defect.

Feedback-loop popularity amplification ($\theta^t$ compounding)

  • What it computes: with $\overline{P}_{R^t} \propto \overline{P}_{D^t} + \theta^t$ (recommendations exceed data popularity by uplift $\theta^t$) and $K$ accepted interactions logged per round, data popularity rises by $K\theta^t/(|D^t|+K)$ each round — so as long as $\theta^t > 0$ and $K > 0$, recommendation popularity strictly increases every retrain iteration.
  • Why it was created: to show formally that amplification is baked into the loop, not an artifact of one algorithm.
  • Concrete example: 20 retrain iterations on MovieLens 1M drive monotone popularity growth, catalog-coverage decline, and group homogenization for UserKNN, BPR, and MostPopular; the minority (female) group's represented taste drifts hardest toward the majority distribution.
  • Role in this SP: [86] FeedbackLoop's core result — the theoretical skeleton under the survey's dynamic-immutability finding (uuid 25): the exposure knob writes the scorer's future training data.

Cumulative-exposure-normalized constraint ($DM^{dynamic}$)

  • What it computes: replaces a static per-round per-item exposure target $C^t(i)$ with $C^{\le t}(i) = \lfloor C^t(i)/E^{\le t}(i) \rfloor$, where $E^{\le t}(i)$ is the item's position-discounted ($1/\log_2(1+k)$) cumulative exposure to date; fed into an unchanged min-cost-flow re-ranker.
  • Why it was created: a memoryless per-round-fair constraint, repeatedly applied inside a retrain loop, still concentrates cumulative exposure — a system can be fair every round yet starve half the catalog long-run.
  • Concrete example: over 600 simulated rounds, cumulative equality of exposure (1−Gini): $DM^{dynamic}$ ≈ 0.125 > $DM^{static}$ ≈ 0.11 > uncorrected MF ≈ 0.04 (declining).
  • Role in this SP: [95] DynamicExposure's sole methodological move — structurally the same ledger-feedback mechanism as SP5's amortized controllers, but evaluated for the first time inside a scorer-retraining loop.

Inverse Retrieval (InvR) and the kick-start effect

  • What it computes: inverts a trained two-tower model — query an ANN index of user embeddings with an under-exposed item's embedding → the item's top-N best users → insert up to 3 such items per 20-slot slate after ranking, guaranteeing each treated item a minimum count of visible impressions, then switch off.
  • Why it was created: user→items retrieval structurally starves the long tail; single-stage randomization fails in multi-stage industrial pipelines (Bower et al.); InvR bypasses the biased pipeline with components that already exist, no retraining.
  • Concrete example: production A/B at Seznam.cz: bottom-50% exposure share +33.3%; CTR on inserted slots +300% vs random insertion (which maximizes raw exposure, +181% PSEI, with ~0% CTR gain). Kick-start: InvR supplies only ~40% of an item's minimum exposure before the main recommender organically takes over, and InvR interactions feeding back into training grow the main pipeline's unique recommended items +18%.
  • Role in this SP: [92] RepurposeLongTail — the SP8 member of the show/don't-show family (per-item impression floor via slot insertion), and the first production evidence that the exposure→training-data coupling can be exploited deliberately rather than only feared.

Concepts from prior SPs used here: exposure / doubly-stochastic LP and merit-proportional targets (SP1: [01] FoE); amortized attention∝relevance (SP1/SP5: [11] EquityOfAttention); scorability ceiling for composition-dependent objectives (SP5: [50] ScoringNotEnough).

Open Questions After SP8SP8 이후 미해결 질문

  • Contamination-free merit reference: no paper estimates an exposure-decontaminated reference distribution for merited exposure. [93] ArtistExposure's merit baseline is data popularity (missing link: never defines merit independently of observed data), [88] CausalEPP's EPP signal is computed from exposure-biased clicks (missing link: circularity between debiasing signal and bias), and [89] CalibSurvey flags popularity-bucket threshold choice as unresolved. What would resolve it: importing deconvolution machinery (Sinha et al. 2016, cited by [86] FeedbackLoop and [95] DynamicExposure but absent from the corpus) to recover pre-loop preferences as the reference.
  • Exposure-only correction with guarantees under fixed noisy exogenous scores (uuid 23, O4-FINAL): the debiasing literature either retrains the scorer ([88] CausalEPP, [94] CDN, [87] ControllingPopBias) or re-ranks trusting it ([84] CalibratedRec, [91] CaliRec); no paper in any of the 8 candidate SPs analyzes exposure-frequency-only correction with regret/violation guarantees when merit is a fixed noisy exogenous score. Completed-search evidence now spans SP6, SP7, SP8.
  • Guarantee-bearing control of an exposure-endogenous scorer (uuid 25): [86] FeedbackLoop models the amplification but tests no intervention (its own future-work item); [95] DynamicExposure evaluates an intervention inside the retrain loop but purely empirically (one method, one dataset, no theory); [92] RepurposeLongTail demonstrates deliberate exploitation without any model. No paper designs or guarantees an exposure controller modeling the scorer's response to intervention-shaped training data.
  • Cross-user aggregation of the calibration machinery: calibration targets are per-user list-level distributions; exposure targets are cross-user aggregates. Missing link in [84] CalibratedRec (aggregation across rankings not treated) and [91] CaliRec (whether trade-off gains survive cross-user targets untested). The bridge from per-user KL matching to aggregate impression-frequency schedules is unbuilt.
  • Reach denomination: no SP8 paper distinguishes impressions from unique-user reach — the impression-vs-reach failure mode documented at SP7 (VRS: impression parity with −9.82% reach) is invisible to the entire debiasing/calibration literature, whose metrics (B50PS, PSEI, exposure Gini, ΔGAP) are all impression- or interaction-denominated.
  • LLM-era feedback loops: absent from SP8. [85] BiasDebiasSurvey explicitly predates LLM recommenders (missing link: whether the seven-bias taxonomy transfers is unaddressed); no SP8 paper touches generative amplification. Watch-item trigger: if SP9 also misses, a new SP is warranted.
