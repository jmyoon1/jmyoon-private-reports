Deep Automated Mechanism Design for Integrating Ad Auction and Allocation in Feed

Li et al. · SIGIR 2024 · 2024 · 18 citations

arXiv:2401.01656 · PDF · SP: SP9

P0Track B

한 줄 요약

피드에서 광고 경매와 유기 배분을 통합하는 심층 자동 mechanism design(SIGIR 2024).

Problem해결하려는 문제

E-commerce feeds decide "which ad shows" (auction) and "where it shows among organic items" (allocation) in two separate stages. This causes two concrete failures: (1) the auction scores ads with point-wise pCTR that ignores externalities — the actual position and surrounding organic items change the ad's CTR; (2) the allocation stage places the ad using the auction winner's payment, which under GSP does not change when the winner raises its bid, so the winner can never improve its slot by bidding more — incentive compatibility is broken across the pipeline. Prior work optimized each stage separately (externality-aware auctions: DNA, DPIN, EXTR; allocation: Cross DQN, HCA2E) or integrated them only under the separated-CTR assumption (IAS, Li et al. 2023).

Solution / Contribution해결책 / 기여

MIAA, a three-module end-to-end pipeline that jointly decides ranking, payment, and display position:

  1. Candidate generation: for each of n candidate ads, insert it at each of m positions in the fixed-order organic list → m×n candidate allocations (organic relative order never changes).
  2. EPM: list-wise self-attention model predicts pCTR/pGMV per item conditioned on the whole allocation (global externalities).
  3. AAM: learned affine-maximizer auction — SW(a) = Σ_j f_j^μ(x_j|z^u|z^r) · b_j · q_j(a) + f^λ(a); winner = argmax SW; payment per click p_i(a*) = [SW(a*_{−i}) − SW(a*)_{−i}] / (f^μ_{σ(i)} · q_{σ(i)}(a*)) (AMA/VCG-style critical payment), preserving IC and IR.
  4. DSM: softmax relaxation of the argmax so the objective E[Rev + αGMV] is differentiable; μ/λ-networks trained end-to-end against pCTR/pGMV-simulated feedback.
Key Results주요 결과
  • Meituan offline: MIAA reaches Revenue+0.5·GMV = 1.1105 relative to GSP+Fixed Position = 1.0 (+11.05%), vs. GSP+Cross DQN +5.47%, IAS +3.30%, Score-Weighted VCG +1.23%.
  • Meituan offline: MIAA pGPM +10.47% while Score-Weighted VCG loses GMV (−1.13%) — the learned α-weighted mechanism trades revenue for combined objective.
  • Avito offline: MIAA Revenue+αGMV = 91,069.80 (+5.35% over GSP+Fixed Position), best of five mechanisms.
  • EPM list-wise pCTR: Meituan all-position AUC 0.7077 vs. 0.6485 point-wise (DIN); PCOC 0.9989 vs. 0.9075; largest fix at position 1 (PCOC 0.9921 vs. 0.7246).
  • Online A/B (Meituan, 5% traffic, 2023-10-15 → 12-15, α=0.3): Revenue+αGMV +8.17% / RPM +11.25% / GPM +7.37% vs. GSP+Fixed Position; +4.87% / +7.63% / +4.15% vs. GSP+Cross DQN — with ad-display counts equalized across arms.
5Cs Analysis5Cs 분석

Category: new method (system/engineering with mechanism-design guarantees), production-deployed. Context: builds on AMA (Sandholm & Likhodedov 2015), GSP/VCG position auctions, externality-aware CTR models (EXTR, DNA, DPIN), ad-allocation RL (Cross DQN), and integrated auction work (IAS — Li et al. 2023, Score-Weighted VCG — Li et al. KDD 2023). Within this survey it is the Track-B evidence that slot/exposure allocation over a fixed organic scorer is itself a designable, learnable mechanism. Correctness: offline results reported as mean ± std over 5 seeds; IC/IR argued by inheritance from the AMA family rather than empirically tested (the online A/B explicitly could not measure advertiser bidding incentives because traffic was user-split, not advertiser-split). Offline mechanism comparison is simulated from pCTR/pGMV, not real user behavior — the paper itself flags this offline/online consistency risk. [SKIM - deeper scrutiny of the IC claim under learned, feature-dependent μ pending] Contributions: (1) first mechanism jointly deciding ad ranking, payment, and display position under global externalities with IC+IR; (2) list-wise EPM demonstrating large pCTR accuracy gains over production point-wise models; (3) deployed at Meituan with 2-month A/B evidence. Clarity: architecture and equations are stated concretely (Eqs. 9–19); reproducibility is limited by the proprietary Meituan dataset and unreleased code, though the Avito setup is described (with simulated clicks for unlogged positions and simulated bids/GMV).

Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

MIAA (Mechanism for Integrating ad Auction and Allocation)

  • What it computes: input = n candidate ads + an ordered organic-item list (m slots); operation = enumerate all m×n candidate allocations (ad inserted at each position), score each with a learned affine-maximizer social-welfare function, select the argmax; output = winning ad, its display position, and its pay-per-click price, decided jointly.
  • Why it was created: the standard two-stage pipeline (GSP auction first, then dynamic slot allocation using the winner's payment) ignores externalities in the auction and breaks incentive compatibility in the allocation — a winning ad that raises its bid pays the same and thus can never buy a better position.
  • Concrete example: with m=4 slots and n candidate ads, m×n candidate lists are generated per page-view request; m ≤ 5 in production, so the O(m×n) enumeration is feasible online.
  • How it is used in this paper: the entire proposed system; deployed on Meituan's retail feed and evaluated offline (Avito, Meituan datasets) and in a 2-month online A/B test.

Externality-aware Prediction Module (EPM)

  • What it computes: input = one full candidate allocation (all m items' features) + request/user features; operation = embeddings → self-attention over the m items → MLP → per-item sigmoid heads; output = pCTR (and pGMV) for every item conditioned on the entire list layout.
  • Why it was created: point-wise CTR models rely on the "separated CTR assumption" (final CTR = content CTR × position CTR), ignoring how surrounding items and the actual position change an item's click probability.
  • Concrete example: on the Meituan dataset, EPM lifts all-position AUC from 0.6485 (production point-wise DIN) to 0.7077 and moves PCOC from 0.9075 to 0.9989.
  • How it is used in this paper: trained separately on logged display lists; its list-wise pCTR/pGMV feed both the auction module's welfare scores and the training reward.

Automated Auction Module (AAM) — deep AMA

  • What it computes: input = a candidate allocation with per-item value-distribution features x_j, pCTR q_j(a), pGMV g_j(a); operation = a μ-network outputs per-item positive weights f_j^μ and a λ-network outputs an allocation-level boost f^λ(a); output = social welfare SW(a) = Σ_j f_j^μ · b_j · q_j(a) + f^λ(a), plus the AMA payment rule (Eq. 16).
  • Why it was created: plain VCG is IC/IR under externalities but yields lower revenue than GSP; affine-maximizer auctions (AMA, Sandholm & Likhodedov 2015) keep IC/IR while adding revenue-tunable parameters, but classical AMA solves μ, λ by inefficient search — here they are neural networks trained end-to-end.
  • Concrete example: VCG is the special case f_j^μ = 1, f^λ = 0.
  • How it is used in this paper: selects the winning allocation online (Step 3 of serving) and computes the ad's payment; Roberts/Lavi characterization results are invoked to argue only AMAs are IC+IR for combinatorial-auction settings, so the learned mechanism inherits IC/IR by construction. [SKIM - verify: IC holds exactly only if μ, λ are bid-independent; the paper asserts this but the argument deserves a deep-read check]

Differentiable Sorting Module (DSM)

  • What it computes: input = the vector of m×n social-welfare scores; operation = softmax(SW/τ) with temperature τ; output = a winning-probability vector over allocations, making the argmax selection differentiable so Loss = −Σ_k Pr(a_k)(Rev(a_k) + αGMV(a_k)) can train the μ/λ-networks end-to-end.
  • Why it was created: the argmax/sorting step in auction winner selection is non-differentiable, and no real user feedback exists for non-displayed allocations (training relies on pCTR/pGMV as simulated feedback).
  • Concrete example: with α = 0.5 offline, the trained mechanism trades some revenue (pRPM +72% vs. Score-Weighted VCG's +93%) for GMV (+2.2% vs. −2.5%).
  • How it is used in this paper: training only; removed at serving time (hard argmax used online).
Implementation Feasibility구현 가능성
  • Needs: request-level logs with full candidate sets + displayed lists (Meituan-scale: 232M requests), embedding + self-attention + MLP stacks (modest compute), an offline simulator built from pCTR/pGMV.
  • Code: not available (no repository mentioned in the paper).
  • Modifications: for a bid-free exposure-allocation setting, drop the payment rule and bids; keep the pattern {enumerate candidate placements → list-wise externality-aware scorer → learned welfare function → differentiable-softmax selection} and replace Rev+αGMV with the exposure-side objective. The DSM softmax relaxation is directly reusable.
  • Unknowns: missing link: whether IC strictly holds when μ is a learned function of item features (bid-independence needs verification); missing link: sensitivity to EPM miscalibration, since training feedback is entirely model-simulated; missing link: multi-slot (multiple ads / general exposure vectors) extension.

Condition Delta — transfer to our setting조건 차이 — 우리 설정으로의 전이

Paper's setting: inputs = n candidate ads with bids + a fixed-order organic list scored by a separate GMV-based scorer, m ≤ 5 slots, one ad per page; outputs = winning ad, its position, pay-per-click price; conditions = production e-commerce feed (Meituan), monetary bids, IC/IR constraints, pCTR/pGMV-simulated offline evaluation. Our setting: exposure/frequency treated as a ranking proxy — asking whether how often/where an item is shown can be designed as an explicit allocation layer on top of a fixed relevance/content scorer, rather than emerging as a side effect of score-sorted ranking. Differences:

  • Monetary auction with bids vs. our bid-free exposure allocation: reference only — the payment/IC machinery has no direct analogue without advertiser bids, but the decomposition (fixed content scorer underneath, learned allocation mechanism on top) transfers.
  • One ad inserted into a fixed organic order vs. general exposure distribution over many items: missing link — MIAA never re-ranks organic items, so it designs exposure for exactly one slot-occupant; extending to full-list exposure design is their own stated future work (multi-slot).
  • Externality-conditioned pCTR (position + context changes click probability) vs. our exposure→engagement feedback concern: resolved: EPM shows position/context effects are large and learnable (AUC 0.6485→0.7077), directly supporting the premise that observed engagement is a function of allocated exposure, not item quality alone.
  • Offline evaluation via model-simulated feedback vs. real user behavior: missing link — the paper acknowledges offline/online inconsistency risk; any exposure-mechanism study inherits this counterfactual-evaluation gap.
Root-cause vs. Symptom근본 원인 대 증상

Direct — the paper identifies the structural cause (two-stage separation makes the auction externality-blind and the allocation non-IC) and removes it by redesigning the mechanism to decide ranking, payment, and position in one IC/IR-preserving optimization, rather than patching either stage's metrics.
