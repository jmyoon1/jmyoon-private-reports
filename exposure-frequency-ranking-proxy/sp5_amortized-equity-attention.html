SP5: Amortized Fairness and Equity of Attention over Time

15 papers · targets O1, O2 · 2018-2026

The temporal form of the survey's knob: per-instance rankings stay (approximately) score-driven while a cumulative impression ledger is equalized across time — plus four adjudication tasks inherited from SP1–SP4 (inclusion vs position ledgers, exposure vs realized-utility ledgers, finite-T realized-exposure bounds, and the controller-adequacy divergence).

SP5: 시간 축 amortized 공정성과 equity of attention

15편 논문 · O1, O2 목표 대상 · 2018-2026

조사 knob의 시간적 형태: 인스턴스별 ranking은 (근사적으로) score 주도로 두고 누적 impression ledger를 시간에 걸쳐 균등화한다. SP1–SP4에서 상속된 네 가지 판정 과제(포함 vs 위치 ledger, exposure vs 실현 효용 ledger, 유한 T 한계, 제어기 적정성 분기)를 수행한다.

Entry Assumption vs. What Was Found

Expected (revised after SP1–SP4: four inherited adjudication lenses — inclusion vs position ledgers, exposure vs utility ledgers, finite-T realized-exposure bounds, controller-adequacy divergence): The temporal form of exposure-frequency control: per-instance rankings stay score-driven while cumulative impression counts are equalized; adjudicate the controller-adequacy divergence with relevance steepness as candidate moderator; check for finite-T realized-exposure bounds.

Found: A mature three-generation architecture (reactive controller → Lagrangian-dual unification → future-aware planning) with a 2026 structural ceiling: ScoringNotEnough proves ledger-boosted sorting cannot reach the frontier; planners escape by construction and are the empirical winners. FairSync is the sole inclusion-controlled member — retrieval-stage count floors at billion scale, proving stage-1 inclusion is necessary for any downstream guarantee.

Assessment: Surprised — SP4's concentration gap was answered by construction, not concentration: four finite-T realized-exposure bounds exist (FairCo O(1/τ); FARA deterministic; VerFair < one position weight; OFFR anytime O(1/√t)), all closed-loop ledger mechanisms, so the gap localizes to open-loop realization only. The controller-adequacy divergence was adjudicated as regime-dependence with relevance steepness as the confirmed moderator.

사전 가정과 실제 발견

기대 (SP1–SP4 이후 수정: 네 가지 상속 판정 렌즈 — 포함 vs 위치 ledger, exposure vs 효용 ledger, 유한 T 실현 한계, 제어기 적정성 분기): exposure-frequency 제어의 시간적 형태로서, 인스턴스별 ranking은 score 주도로 유지하며 누적 impression 횟수를 균등화하는 계열을 기대하였다. relevance 가파름을 후보 조절 변수로 제어기 적정성 분기를 판정하고 유한 T 실현 한계의 존재를 검사하기로 하였다.

발견: 성숙한 3세대 구조(반응형 제어기 → Lagrangian dual 통일 → 미래 인지 계획)와 2026년의 구조적 천장을 확인하였다: ScoringNotEnough는 ledger 부스트 정렬이 frontier에 도달할 수 없음을 증명하고, planner는 구성상 이를 벗어나며 경험적 승자다. 포함 수준 제어는 FairSync가 유일하다 — 10억 규모 retrieval 단계 count floor이며, 1단계 포함이 모든 하류 보장의 필요조건임을 증명한다.

평가: 예상과 다름 — SP4의 집중 한계 gap은 집중이 아니라 '구성에 의해' 답해졌다: 유한 T 실현 exposure 한계가 넷 존재하며(FairCo O(1/τ), FARA 결정론적, VerFair 위치 가중치 1 미만, OFFR anytime O(1/√t)) 모두 폐루프 ledger 메커니즘이다. 따라서 gap은 open-loop 실현에만 국한된다. 제어기 적정성 분기는 relevance 가파름을 조절 변수로 하는 regime 의존성으로 판정되었다.

What SP5 Establishes

The amortized branch is mature and answers SP5's question with a clear architecture and a 2026 structural ceiling. The architecture: keep a running ledger of what each item/group has cumulatively received, and make each served ranking a function of (scores, ledger state). Three generations exist. Generation 1 is the reactive controller — [11] EquityOfAttention's per-query ILP repaying attention deficit, and [46] FairCo's proportional controller sorting by score + λ·(τ−1)·accumulated-disparity, which provably drives amortized disparity to zero at O(1/τ) for any λ>0 and any bounded fixed scorer. Generation 2 unifies the controllers as Lagrangian dual methods: [53] LongTermConstraints proves FairCo's "heuristic" P-controller is a special case of a Stationary Controller whose multiplier is exactly the tracking error against a scaled cumulative target, and [54] FastOnlineFair (OFFR) reformulates the whole problem as online Frank-Wolfe whose iterate is the realized average exposure, with an anytime O(1/√t) regret bound. Generation 3 is future-aware planning: [52] FARA plans exposure jointly over the next ΔT lists via an exact convex QP and fills slots vertically, and [51] VerFair does the batch analogue — both dominate controllers at top ranks at equal unfairness (MSLR-10k cNDCG@1: FARA 129.0 vs FairCo 85.5).

Four lens verdicts. (1) Inclusion vs position: the branch is overwhelmingly position-ledger + position-assignment (all methods above reorder slates); the sole inclusion-controlled member is [48] FairSync, which enforces cumulative count-based group floors at the retrieval stage via a synced dual price — the production-grade successor to FairRec/TEC's show/don't-show pattern, and it proves stage-1 inclusion is a necessary condition for any downstream exposure guarantee. (2) Exposure vs realized utility: ledgers are almost all exposure-denominated; the realized-utility variants exist but their theory lags — FairCo(Imp)'s convergence is explicitly a conjecture, and [24] BeyondExposure's income ledger requires an oracle conversion function μ. The two ledger types genuinely diverge: optimizing impact fairness can increase exposure unfairness ([46] FairCo MovieLens). (3) Finite-T realized-exposure bounds — the SP4 concentration gap: they exist here, by construction rather than by concentration. FairCo's O(1/τ) disparity rate, FARA's deterministic allocation error ≤ P_{k_s} for all but k_s items, VerFair's shortfall < one position weight, and OFFR's anytime O(1/√t) regret on the realized average-exposure matrix are all finite-horizon statements about served exposure. The mechanism: closed-loop methods never sample from a target distribution — each ranking is chosen deterministically from the realized ledger, so realized-vs-target deviation is controlled by feedback, not by chance. The SP4 gap is thereby localized to open-loop sample-based realization. Caveat: every bound is in model-credited (position-bias-curve) exposure units. (4) Controller adequacy (KB uuid 15) — adjudicated: see Divergences; the short version is that the two SP4 results answer different questions (convergence vs Pareto-efficiency), with relevance-distribution steepness and controller-gain scaling as the moderators, and [11] EquityOfAttention's own experiments supply the steepness evidence directly.

Finally, the ceiling: [50] ScoringNotEnough (2026) proves that no per-document scoring function — deterministic, randomized, or amortized across queries (its Appendix C) — can reach the optimal utility-fairness trade-off when the exposure objective is non-decomposable over documents. This splits the amortized family in two: ledger-boosted sorting (FairCo, Stationary Controller with u=e, OFFR) is per-item score-plus-sort at every instant and inherits the ceiling; whole-list construction (FARA's and VerFair's slot allocation, [53] LongTermConstraints's per-request LP, beam search) sees list composition and escapes it. The methods that escape the theorem are exactly the ones that win empirically — the 2026 theory retroactively explains the 2023 empirical succession.

SP5이 확립한 것

SP5는 amortized 분기의 구조를 확립한다. 아키텍처는 '각 item/집단이 누적으로 받은 것의 ledger를 유지하고, 서빙되는 각 ranking을 (score, ledger 상태)의 함수로 만든다'이다. 1세대는 반응형 제어기(EquityOfAttention의 per-query ILP, FairCo의 비례 제어기 — 임의 λ>0에서 amortized disparity O(1/τ) 수렴 증명), 2세대는 제어기의 Lagrangian dual 통일(LongTermConstraints: FairCo는 Stationary Controller의 특수case; OFFR: online Frank-Wolfe iterate가 곧 실현 평균 exposure, anytime O(1/√t)), 3세대는 미래 인지 계획(FARA의 볼록 QP 공동 계획, VerFair의 배치 대응) — 동일 불공정성에서 상위 랭크 효용이 planner 우세(cNDCG@1 129.0 vs 85.5). 2026년 천장: score-then-sort 계열은 비분해성 목표의 frontier에 구조적으로 도달 불가(ScoringNotEnough). 조사 전체를 관통하는 발견: 유한 T 실현 exposure 보장은 전부 폐루프 ledger feedback의 산물이다.

Dominant Approach지배적 접근

The dominant approach is closed-loop ledger control over a fixed scorer: maintain cumulative exposure state, and serve each ranking as score-sort perturbed by a function of the ledger. It became dominant because it is cheap (counters + one sort per request, [46] FairCo; O(n+m) memory and a top-k sort, [54] FastOnlineFair), it is the only architecture that can even express horizon-level goals ([53] LongTermConstraints), and it carries the branch's distinctive property: guarantees are about realized served exposure, not expectations over a sampling distribution. The lineage is now theoretically unified — [53] LongTermConstraints shows the P-controller is a clipped dual-gradient method, [54] FastOnlineFair shows the same dynamics arise as online Frank-Wolfe with the iterate equal to the empirical average exposure, and [48] FairSync ports the identical dual machinery to distributed retrieval. Reactive amortized fairness is, mechanically, impression pacing with a dual price.

Its key limitation is the myopia/expressiveness pair. Myopia: greedy repayment of exposure debt collides with relevance at top ranks, and the collision grows with relevance-distribution steepness — the succession [51] VerFair → [52] FARA demonstrates that planning exposure jointly over future lists removes the collision (+51% to +114% cNDCG@1 at the same unfairness floor). Expressiveness: [50] ScoringNotEnough proves any per-item score-plus-sort instant — which is what every controller serves — cannot reach the Pareto frontier for non-decomposable exposure objectives, even amortized across queries; only whole-list construction escapes. The alternatives inside the SP either abandon the fixed scorer entirely (constrained-RL policies [47] LongTermFairRec, [49] ItemLifecycle — model-retraining, infeasible under the survey's constraint, and [47] LongTermFairRec's "cumulative" constraint is actually enforced per-step) or abandon position control for count-based inclusion ([48] FairSync).

For the objectives this settles most of SP5's O1 column (amortized = temporal branch, three generations, unified) and sharpens O2: under the strict frequency-only reading, the amortized branch contributes exactly one member (FairSync, count-based, retrieval-stage) — every position-ledger method assumes slate-reordering rights, replicating SP1's pattern. The count→position-weighted transfer gap survives its third SP: no surveyed method controls only inclusion and guarantees position-weighted exposure. For O3, the branch is active through 2024–2026 (FairSync WWW 2024, LongTermConstraints WSDM 2024, ElasticityTradeoff SIGIR 2025, ScoringNotEnough and BeyondExposure 2026), with the 2026 work moving to structural limits and target redefinition rather than new controllers — consistent with SP4's "machinery frozen, targets evolving" reading.

Convergences수렴 지점
  • The reactive amortized branch is one algorithm family: dual-variable pacing. [53] LongTermConstraints proves FairCo is a special case of its Stationary Controller (multiplier = clipped tracking error); [54] FastOnlineFair's pacing heuristic β_t = min(β, γt/n) replicates FairCo's dynamics inside a Frank-Wolfe scheme; [48] FairSync runs the same sub-gradient dual update as a synced price in retrieval. Mechanism: cumulative linear constraints dualize into per-request additive score offsets whose magnitude tracks constraint lag. This is the third independent leg of the pacing-equivalence hypothesis (KB uuid 6).
  • Greedy/myopic ledger repayment is dominated by whole-list or multi-list construction. Four papers reach this by different routes: [52] FARA (planning over ΔT lists beats per-session repayment, +51–114% cNDCG@1 at equal unfairness), [51] VerFair (vertical beats horizontal allocation at top ranks), [47] LongTermFairRec (policy-level optimization Pareto-dominates FOE re-ranking), and [50] ScoringNotEnough (ex-post construction dominates any trained scorer — with a proof of why). The mechanism is shared: per-instant score-sorting cannot see list composition; construction can.
  • Finite-T guarantees on served exposure come from closed-loop construction, not concentration. [46] FairCo (O(1/τ) disparity, from arbitrary initial debt), [52] FARA (deterministic allocation error ≤ P_{k_s}), [51] VerFair (shortfall < p_{c*,k}), [54] FastOnlineFair (anytime O(1/√t) regret where the iterate is the realized average exposure). All four choose each ranking from the realized ledger rather than sampling from a target — the SP4 concentration gap is a property of open-loop realization only.
  • Known position-bias curve + trusted/converging relevance remain universal load-bearing assumptions (KB uuid 4 confirmed a fourth time): [46] FairCo (propensities given, misspecification untested), [51] VerFair (PBM with known η, scoped out), [52] FARA (exactness breaks if the ledger is mis-credited), [53] LongTermConstraints (full-information, learned-model interaction deferred), [54] FastOnlineFair (oracle μ_ij, PBM required for FW linearity). Every finite-T bound above is denominated in curve-credited units.
Divergences분기 지점
  • Controller adequacy (KB uuid 15) — adjudicated as regime-dependence, not contradiction. SP4's conflict: [28] Expohedron found the heuristic controller near-Pareto at short horizons; [10] ParetoRepeatedRankings found it converging to normalized unfairness 0.93 on MSLR. SP5 evidence resolves it in three parts. (a) The two results answer different questions: convergence of the fairness target vs Pareto-efficiency of the trajectory. Properly scaled controllers do reach the unfairness floor — [52] FARA shows FairCo attaining the same unfairness lower bound as LP on MSLR-10k itself — but pay a top-rank utility price (cNDCG@1 85.5 vs 129.0). (b) Relevance-distribution steepness is confirmed as the moderator, with direct evidence from [11] EquityOfAttention's own experiments: on near-uniform relevance (Airbnb, many ties) amortization is nearly free even at zero allowed quality loss, while on synthetic exponential relevance the ILP cannot satisfy any quality constraint θ ≥ 0.5 and collapses to relevance-only ranking. Steep relevance (MSLR's graded labels) leaves no tie-room, so reactive rotation must either violate relevance or stall — consistent with the utility-cost flatness law (KB uuid 5). (c) Controller-gain scaling is the likely implementation-level cause of the 0.93: FairCo's convergence proof requires the error term to grow as (τ−1)·disparity so it eventually dominates any bounded score; a constant-gain boost stalls against accumulated debt, and [54] FastOnlineFair adds that FairCo-style controllers optimize no objective and cannot target intermediate trade-offs. Verdict: controllers are adequate for reaching an exposure target cheaply; they are inadequate for Pareto-efficient trajectories under steep relevance, where planners take over.
  • What should the ledger count — exposure or realized utility? The exposure-ledger camp ([48] FairSync, [51] VerFair, [52] FARA, [54] FastOnlineFair) treats position-credited exposure as the end; the impact/income camp shows the two diverge: optimizing FairCo's impact fairness can increase exposure unfairness ([46] FairCo MovieLens, Figs. 8–10), and exposure-fair rankings can produce unequal realized income when the exposure→income conversion is context-dependent ([24] BeyondExposure; also [34] ElasticityTradeoff's utility-elasticity mechanism). Likely cause: heterogeneous exposure→utility conversion (KB uuid 8) — the ledgers measure different quantities, so the disagreement is substantive, not methodological. Unresolved asymmetry: the utility-ledger side has no convergence theory (FairCo(Imp) is a conjecture; μ is oracle in BeyondExposure).
Prior Beliefs and Surprises (full)사전 믿음과 놀라움 (전문)

What the entry rationale expected to find (final revised form after SP1–SP4):

Per-instance rankings stay score-driven while cumulative impression counts are equalized; record inclusion-vs-position for O2; check exposure-vs-realized-utility ledgers and income extensibility; test whether any amortized ledger gives position-weighted guarantees under inclusion-only control; adjudicate the controller-adequacy divergence with relevance steepness as candidate moderator; check for finite-T realized-exposure bounds.

Confirmed:

  • The amortization branch is exactly the temporal form of the frequency knob, with an internal succession (reactive → planned) matching the pre-loop landscape read (VerFair suboptimality of the original mechanism; FARA future-aware planning beats reactive repayment; FairSync production-scale).
  • Relevance-distribution steepness is the controller-adequacy moderator, as hypothesized — and the cleanest evidence was already in [11] EquityOfAttention (2018): flat relevance → amortization free; exponential relevance → collapse.
  • No position-weighted guarantee under inclusion-only control exists in the branch; the count→position gap survives its third SP.

Surprised:

  • (a) Assumed (from SP4): finite-T realized-exposure bounds would be absent everywhere. Found: four of them, hiding in plain sight — because closed-loop construction replaces sampling, the bounds are deterministic or regret-style rather than concentration theorems, so SP4's search vocabulary ("concentration") missed them. The SP4 gap is real only for open-loop realization.
  • (b) Assumed: controllers vs planners was an empirical performance ordering. Found: a 2026 impossibility theorem ([50] ScoringNotEnough) that retroactively explains the ordering structurally — ledger-boosted sorting is per-item scoring and provably cannot reach the frontier; planners escape by construction. The assumption missed that the field would produce a why, not just a which.
  • (c) Assumed: FairCo-style controllers are heuristics. Found: [53] LongTermConstraints proves the P-controller is a clipped dual-gradient method — the reactive branch is formally Lagrangian pacing, which upgrades the SP7 pacing-equivalence hypothesis from analogy toward theorem.
  • (d) Assumed: the amortized branch would contribute several strict frequency-only members to O2. Found: exactly one ([48] FairSync); the branch overwhelmingly assumes slate-reordering rights, replicating SP1's pattern in the temporal dimension.

Key Concepts핵심 개념

(Exposure, the marginal rank-probability matrix, and LP+BvN were defined in SP1; balanced-word delivery and the expohedron in SP4; equity of amortized attention originates in [11] EquityOfAttention, covered in SP1/SP4. New in SP5:)

Amortized merit-based exposure disparity (the controller's error signal)

  • What it computes: per-timestep group exposures from the position-bias model and group merits → difference of time-averaged exposure-to-merit ratios D^E_τ(G_i,G_j); zero means cumulative exposure has been allocated proportional to relevance over the whole history.
  • Why it was created: static fairness-of-exposure assumes merit is known and each ranking is judged alone; in a served system fairness must be defined over the accumulated stream, tolerating per-instance deviation as long as the ledger balances.
  • Concrete example: two news groups differing 2% in merit; relevance sorting yields a vastly larger exposure gap; D^E_τ quantifies exactly the accumulated gap, and [46] FairCo drives its pairwise average to 0 at O(1/τ).
  • Role in this SP: the shared state variable of the entire branch — FairCo's error term, [53] LongTermConstraints's constraint-progress accumulator s_t, [52] FARA's exposure ledger E^t(d), OFFR's average exposure matrix π^(t) are all instances (O1, O2).

Proportional (P-) controller ranking policy

  • What it computes: sort by score(d) + λ·err_τ(d), where err_τ(d) = (τ−1)·max-group-disparity for d's group — an additive, time-scaled boost that grows with accumulated debt until it outweighs any bounded score difference.
  • Why it was created: per-step LP solutions cost O(n²+|G|²) variables per request; a controller needs only counters and a sort, and corrects arbitrary unfairness accumulated before merit estimates converge.
  • Concrete example: λ=0.01 works across all of [46] FairCo's experiments; any λ>0 suffices asymptotically because the (τ−1) factor does the scaling — a constant-gain boost would stall against growing debt.
  • Role in this SP: generation 1 of the branch; subsumed by [53] LongTermConstraints's Stationary Controller and replicated by [54] FastOnlineFair's pacing heuristic — evidence for the controller-unification convergence (O1).

Stationary Controller / Lagrangian multiplier as tracking error

  • What it computes: per request, argmax of utility − μᵀ·(constraint contribution), with multiplier update μ_t = clip(η·(scaled target − cumulative state)) — online gradient descent on the dual variable; when utility and exposure position weights coincide it reduces to argsort(score + boost ∝ constraint lag).
  • Why it was created: to make "long-term goals a per-request ranker cannot even express" (cumulative exposure floors over a month) solvable by a principled per-request rule rather than an ad-hoc boost.
  • Concrete example: constraint "1M exposures to artist a this week", state 400K mid-week → multiplier grows with the lag, boosting a proportionally [53] LongTermConstraints.
  • Role in this SP: the unification result — reactive amortized fairness IS dual-variable pacing; connects to [48] FairSync's dual price and SP7's ads pacing (strengthens KB uuid 6) (O1, O2).

Future-aware exposure planning + vertical allocation

  • What it computes: Phase 1 — an exact convex QP over per-item planned marginal exposure ΔE for the next ΔT lists (exact because the squared-disparity fairness metric is degree-2 in exposure, so the second-order Taylor expansion is an equality, with PSD Hessian and O(n) variables); Phase 2 — fill the ΔT lists rank-major (all rank-1 slots first), each slot taking the most relevant item with planned exposure remaining.
  • Why it was created: greedy ledger repayment is myopic — repaying a starved item forces it to the top of a single list (relevance collision); planning boosts it smoothly at rank 2 across many lists instead.
  • Concrete example: [52] FARA at maximum fairness pressure reaches the same unfairness floor as FairCo with cNDCG@1 129.0 vs 85.5 (MSLR-10k); increasing horizon ΔT 1→100 monotonically raises cNDCG@3 for vertical fill and not for horizontal — the gain is specifically planning + slot construction.
  • Role in this SP: generation 3; also the escape route from the scorability ceiling, since slot construction sees list composition (O1, O2).

Dual-space retrieval (inclusion-ledger enforcement at stage 1)

  • What it computes: cumulative count-based group exposure floors e_g ≥ m_g are dualized (strong duality); the dual price μ_g is appended to item embeddings (h_i = e_i ‖ A_i, query q = e_u ‖ −μ) so unmodified distributed KNN in the augmented space solves the per-request constrained problem; μ is sub-gradient-updated every B requests.
  • Why it was created: stage-2 re-rankers traverse the full item set and aggregate exposure centrally — infeasible for millions of sharded items at millisecond latency; and a group absent from stage-1 candidates cannot be exposed by any downstream ranker.
  • Concrete example: Amazon-Book has 313,966 items but 165 groups → the synced state is a 165-dim vector; overhead < 0.25 ms/user at batch B > 8; ESP = 100% (all groups meet m_g = 200) across 5 base retrieval models [48] FairSync.
  • Role in this SP: the only strict inclusion-controlled (show/don't-show family) member of the amortized branch; the production feasibility datum for O2/O3.

Scorability (the non-decomposability ceiling)

  • What it computes: for a (utility, unfairness) pair and trade-off level α, asks whether any scoring function score(rel(d), group(d)) — even knowing true relevance and the query distribution — sort-attains the optimal expected trade-off over all instances; Theorems 1–2 of [50] ScoringNotEnough answer no for deterministic and randomized scoring, Appendix C for across-query amortized fairness.
  • Why it was created: fair LTR implicitly assumed "enough data → a learned scorer reaches the Pareto frontier"; nobody had tested the score-then-sort architecture itself.
  • Concrete example: DCG weights, m = 9: every scoring function leaves a trade-off gap ≥ 0.05·(1−α); Monte-Carlo puts the real gap ≈ 6× larger. Root cause: exposure disparity depends on the group composition of the whole query, which a per-document score cannot see; an adversarial mixture of two compositions defeats any fixed ordering.
  • Role in this SP: the structural limit on the ledger-boosted-sorting half of the amortized family; the theoretical explanation of why planners/slot-constructors dominate (O2, O4).

Open Questions After SP5SP5 이후 미해결 질문

  • Position-weighted guarantees under inclusion-only control — third SP, still absent. [48] FairSync counts retrieval-list membership with no analysis of how stage-1 counts translate to position-weighted impressions (explicit missing link); FairRec's EF1/MMS and the taxation line's POT bound (SP1/SP3) are likewise count-based. Completed-search evidence now spans SP1, SP3, and SP5: no method controls only show/don't-show and bounds position-weighted exposure. O4-ready. What would resolve it: a transfer theorem "inclusion frequency f + any score-driven within-slate ordering ⇒ position-weighted exposure ∈ [l(f), u(f)]" under a known bias curve.
  • Ledger correctness under position-bias misspecification. Every finite-T bound in the branch is denominated in curve-credited units, and no paper propagates curve error into ledger/bound error: missing links in [46] FairCo (propensity misspecification untested), [51] VerFair (known η scoped out), [52] FARA (QP plans against a wrong ledger if exposure is misestimated), [53] LongTermConstraints (modeled exposure). Extends SP4's curve-misspecification question to the closed-loop branch. What would resolve it: a perturbation bound ‖γ−γ̂‖ → amortized-disparity error, or count-based (curve-free) fallback guarantees.
  • Amortized control under heavy-tailed, non-stationary traffic. [52] FARA's planning requires the same query to recur ΔT times (missing link: heavy-tailed streams); [54] FastOnlineFair assumes a stationary user-activity distribution; [48] FairSync inherits i.i.d. arrivals for dual validity. No paper gives an amortized guarantee under adversarial or drifting arrivals — precisely production feed conditions. What would resolve it: adversarial-arrival regret bounds for the dual controllers (SP6's vocabulary) or robust-MPC analysis of [53] LongTermConstraints's Predictive controller.
  • Realized-utility ledger theory. FairCo(Imp) convergence is conjectured only [46] FairCo; income ledgers assume the conversion function is known and error-free [24] BeyondExposure. No convergence or robustness result exists for any ledger denominated in realized utility with an estimated conversion. What would resolve it: FairCo-style convergence proof for impact disparity, plus μ-noise sensitivity analysis.
  • Ledger-driven feedback contamination. The ledger boosts exposure, exposure generates clicks, clicks move relevance estimates — the circularity is bracketed out by [54] FastOnlineFair (§7, explicit limitation) and [11] EquityOfAttention (relevance assumed worthiness), and only log-replay-simulated in [47] LongTermFairRec. Feeds SP8 (feedback loops / popularity bias).
