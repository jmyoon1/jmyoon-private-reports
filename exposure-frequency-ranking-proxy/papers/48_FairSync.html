FairSync: Ensuring Amortized Group Exposure in Distributed Recommendation Retrieval

Xu et al. · WWW 2024 · 2024 · 20 citations

arXiv:2402.10628 · PDF · SP: SP5

P0Track B

한 줄 요약

분산 retrieval 단계에서 동기화된 dual price로 누적 집단 exposure 하한을 보장하는 FairSync(WWW 2024). 10억 규모 candidate에서 <0.25ms 오버헤드로 동작하며, 1단계 포함(retrieval floor)이 모든 하류 exposure 보장의 필요조건임을 증명한다.

Problem해결하려는 문제

Amortized minimum-exposure guarantees in recommendation have been enforced only in stage-2 (ranking) — FairRec, TFROM, CP-Fair, P-MMF, LTP-MMF all traverse the full item set and centrally aggregate exposure state. In production two-stage pipelines, stage-1 (retrieval) selects hundreds of candidates from millions of items sharded across servers within milliseconds; if stage-1 fails to retrieve items of a group, no stage-2 method can restore that group's exposure (the paper shows a strong positive correlation between stage-1 and stage-2 minimum exposures in a simulation, Fig. 1b). Existing stage-2 methods cannot run in stage-1 because full-item traversal is infeasible in distributed dense retrieval.

Solution / Contribution해결책 / 기여

Formulate retrieval-with-exposure-floors as constrained resource allocation (Eq. 2: maximize $\sum_t \sum_i x_{u_t,i} r_{u_t,i}$ s.t. $|L|=K$ per request, $e_g \ge m_g$ per group). Theorem 1 gives the strong dual

$$\min_{\mu} \Big[ \sum_{t=1}^{T}\sum_{k=1}^{K}(r_{u_t,i} - A\mu)_{[k]} + \sum_g m_g \mu_g + \max_g\{\mu_g\}(TK - \sum_g m_g) \Big]$$

with dual variable $\mu \in \mathbb{R}^{|\mathcal{G}|}$ and item-group adjacency $A$. Inference: augment embeddings ($q_{u_t} = e_{u_t}\|-\mu_t$, $h_i = e_i\|A_i$) so plain distributed KNN in the dual space solves the per-request problem. Learning: buffer per-request sub-gradients (Eq. 6) and Adam-update $\mu$ every $B$ requests. [SKIM - proof of Theorem 1 (knapsack + minimax argument, Appendix A) read only at statement level.]

Key Results주요 결과
  • ESP = 100% (all groups meet $m_g = 200$) on both Amazon-Book and Taobao across all 5 base models (YoutubeDNN, GRU4Rec, MIND, ComiRec-SA/DR), while regularized-fair and IPW reach only ~34–83% ESP.
  • Among ESP-100% methods, FairSync dominates: e.g. ComiRec-DR top-20 Amazon-Book Recall 4.92 vs. Uncalibrated 4.76 and K-neighbor 0.09; accuracy is statistically significantly higher (p < 0.05) and essentially matches the unconstrained-fairness baselines (IPW/regularized-fair) that violate the floors.
  • Latency: with dual-update batch $B > 8$, per-user inference overhead < 0.25 ms (industrially viable); constraint satisfaction breaks for $B > 64$ — $B$ is the efficiency/effectiveness dial.
  • Extreme case (all users dislike group $g_2$, $m_2$=2000): recall drops exactly by $m_2/(KT) = 4\%$ — the accuracy cost equals the forced exposure fraction.

Contribution vs Claim기여 대 주장
  • Stated claims: FairSync guarantees minimum amortized group exposure in distributed retrieval while maintaining accuracy and production latency; adaptable to any distributed retrieval architecture (ANN, tree-based like TDM).
  • Demonstrated: ESP=100% + accuracy parity on two public datasets with faiss-based KNN; latency measured on a single RTX 3090.
  • Gap: "any distributed retrieval architecture" (Sec. 4.3.3) is argued, not tested — only faiss KNN is evaluated; no experiment on tree-based indexes. Actual multi-server distributed deployment is simulated rather than run on a real sharded production cluster. [SKIM - verify appendix C for any further deployment evidence in deep read.]
Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

Amortized group max-min fairness (MMF) with minimum-exposure thresholds

  • What it computes: for each item group $g$ (provider, category), the cumulative exposure count $e_g = \sum_{t=1}^{T}\sum_{i \in \mathcal{I}_g} x_{u_t,i}$ over a time horizon $t=1..T$ → constraint check $e_g \ge m_g$ where $m_g$ is a platform-set minimum ("minimum wage policy").
  • Why it was created: per-list fairness is too strict for production; amortizing over a period is the realistic industrial requirement (line of Biega 2018, FairRec, P-MMF).
  • Concrete example: main experiments set $m_g = 200$ exposures per group over the test horizon; the ESP metric = fraction of groups whose cumulative exposure exceeds $m_g$.
  • How it is used in this paper: it is the constraint FairSync enforces in the retrieval (stage-1) rather than the ranking (stage-2) stage. Amortized-exposure concept is inherited from prior work (P-MMF etc.), the stage-1/distributed enforcement is new here.

Dual-space retrieval (dual-vector-augmented KNN)

  • What it computes: the constrained allocation problem is dualized (Theorem 1, strong duality); the dual distance becomes $d^{Dual} = d(e_u, e_i) + \mu_g$ for $i \in \mathcal{I}_g$. Implemented by concatenation: query $q_{u_t} = e_{u_t} \| -\mu_t$ and item embedding $h_i = e_i \| A_i$ (one-hot group column), so $d^{Dual} = -q^\top h$ — standard dot-product KNN in an augmented space.
  • Why it was created: stage-2 fairness methods traverse all items and aggregate exposure info centrally; infeasible when millions of items are sharded across servers under millisecond latency. The dual variable $\mu \in \mathbb{R}^{|\mathcal{G}|}$ ($|\mathcal{G}| \ll T \times |\mathcal{I}|$) is the only state that must be synced to servers.
  • Concrete example: Amazon-Book has 313,966 items but only 165 groups, so the synced dual vector is 165-dimensional.
  • How it is used in this paper: core mechanism — a central node aggregates historical exposure into $\mu$, distributes it; each server runs unmodified faiss KNN on the augmented embeddings.

Periodic sub-gradient dual update (batch size $B$)

  • What it computes: sub-gradient $s \in \partial W^{Dual}/\partial \mu$ per request (Eq. 6, driven by which groups got exposed vs. their $m_g$), buffered and applied with Adam every $B$ users.
  • Why it was created: per-request dual updates cause asynchronous update storms across servers and inflate recall latency; $B$ trades efficiency vs. constraint tightness.
  • Concrete example: with $B \le 8$ FairSync adds ~0.2–1 ms per user vs. base model; with $B > 8$ overhead < 0.25 ms; constraint (ESP=100%) breaks for $B > 64$.
  • How it is used in this paper: the online learning phase of Algorithm 1; ablated in Figs. 5–6.

ESP (Enough Satisfaction groups Percentage)

  • What it computes: $\frac{1}{|\mathcal{G}|}\sum_g \mathbb{I}(e_g > m_g)$ — fraction of groups meeting their minimum-exposure requirement.
  • Why it was created: needed a satisfaction metric for threshold-type (not equality-type) exposure constraints; adapted from the "enough relevant items" metric of Wang & Joachims 2023.
  • Concrete example: FairSync achieves ESP = 100% in all Table 2 settings; regularized-fair only 37–83%.
  • How it is used in this paper: primary fairness metric in all experiments.
Implementation Feasibility구현 가능성
  • Needs: trained two-tower embedding model, item-group mapping, faiss (or any ANN index), PyTorch for Adam dual updates; experiments ran on one RTX 3090. Public data: Amazon-Book, Taobao user-behavior.
  • Code: available at https://github.com/XuChen0427/FairSync
  • Modifications: for a general exposure-proxy study, replace the hard floor $m_g$ with the exposure schedule of interest; the $\mu$-augmentation trick ports to any dot-product retrieval index.
  • Unknowns: missing link: behavior under non-stationary user arrival; missing link: interaction with stage-2 re-ranking when both stages run exposure control simultaneously (double-counting of exposure credit).
Follow-up Papers관련 후속 논문
  • P-MMF (Xu et al., WWW 2023) — direct predecessor: dual mirror descent for provider max-min fairness at stage-2; FairSync moves the same dual machinery to distributed stage-1.
  • LTP-MMF (Xu et al., 2023) — extends the line to long-term exposure under recommendation feedback loops; relevant to SP5's exposure-as-proxy feedback concerns.
  • Wang & Joachims (WSDM 2023), "Uncertainty Quantification for Fairness in Two-Stage Recommender Systems" — source of the two-stage fairness framing and of the IPW/Uncalibrated baselines and ER metric.
  • FairRec (Patro et al., WWW 2020) / FairRec+ — origin of the minimum-exposure ("minimum wage") guarantee formulation at stage-2.
  • Balseiro et al. (ICML 2021), "Regularized online allocation problems" — the online dual/mirror-descent resource-allocation machinery FairSync's Theorem 1 rests on.
Condition Delta — transfer to our setting조건 차이 — 우리 설정으로의 전이

Paper's setting: inputs = user/item embeddings from a trained two-tower retrieval model + item-group adjacency + platform-set exposure floors $m_g$; outputs = per-request top-$K$ candidate lists satisfying cumulative floors; conditions = single unique group per item, groups known and fixed, users arrive randomly (i.i.d. assumption inherited from P-MMF), offline replay of Amazon-Book/Taobao logs, floors enforced over a fixed horizon $T$. Our setting (SP5 — exposure frequency as ranking proxy in production feeds): concerned with how cumulative exposure counts function as a control variable / proxy in ranking pipelines, including feedback between exposure control and learned relevance. Differences:

  • Enforcement point: FairSync operates at retrieval (stage-1) candidate generation, whereas most exposure-control literature (and much of SP5) is at ranking — resolved: the paper itself bridges this, showing stage-1 floors are necessary for stage-2 exposure targets (Fig. 1b).
  • Exposure semantics: retrieval-list membership is counted as "exposure," which is a proxy for actual user-facing impressions after stage-2 — missing link: no analysis of how stage-1 candidate exposure translates to final impression counts beyond the correlation simulation.
  • Feedback loop: dual updates react to realized exposure but embeddings are frozen; long-term feedback (retraining on exposure-biased logs) is out of scope here — reference only (handled in the authors' LTP-MMF).
  • Single-group-per-item and known static groups: our setting may need overlapping/dynamic groups — missing link.
  • i.i.d. user-arrival assumption for sub-gradient validity — missing link for adversarial or bursty traffic. [SKIM - verify whether appendix discusses non-stationary arrivals.]
Root-cause vs. Symptom근본 원인 대 증상

Direct — the paper identifies that exposure guarantees fail structurally upstream (a group absent from stage-1 candidates cannot be exposed downstream, regardless of the ranker) and fixes exactly that root cause with a dual decomposition compatible with distributed KNN, rather than further patching stage-2 re-ranking.
