User Welfare Optimization in Recommender Systems with Competing Content Creators

Yao et al. · KDD 2024 · 2024 · 29 citations

arXiv:2404.18319 · PDF · SP: SP9

P0Track B

한 줄 요약

경쟁하는 콘텐츠 creator가 있는 추천에서 사용자 후생을 최적화하는 HMT(KDD 2024). 수정되지 않은 relevance 모델 위에서 사용자군별 retrieval pool 크기 조정만으로 3주 A/B에서 LTR +1.13%, 신선 콘텐츠 impression +3.7%를 달성한 순수 빈도 수준 creator 조향의 production 사례.

Problem해결하려는 문제

Prior work on creator-side game dynamics (Ben-Porat & Tennenholtz 2017/2018; Yao et al. 2023a,b; Hu et al. 2023) guarantees welfare only under strong behavioral assumptions — creators run no-regret learning or have oracle access to utilities — so that Nash equilibria are reachable. Real creators do local, black-box trial-and-error updates; under such dynamics the paper shows creators pile onto "popular" user regions and converge to local equilibria with sub-optimal user welfare, even when a unique PNE exists. The root cause identified: information asymmetry — creators cannot see the global user-preference distribution, only the reward/exposure signal the platform sends.

Solution / Contribution해결책 / 기여

The platform steers the content-creation game by reshaping the signal creators receive. Concretely:

  1. Model creators as LBR updaters in the C³ext game (Algorithm 2): random exploration direction, accept if utility improves.
  2. Choose an intervention channel: UIR (reweight post-matching rewards by $w(x)$), SMT (per-user temperature $\beta(w(x))$), or HMT (per-user retrieval pool $K(w(x))$) — Eqs. (8)–(10).
  3. Adaptively compute $w$ by multiplicative weight update (Algorithm 1): $w^{(i+1)}(x) \propto w^{(i)}(x)\exp(-\alpha\bar\pi(x))$, i.e. lower the weight of users already enjoying high utility, raise it for under-served users. Theorem 2 proves this is an ascent direction on welfare $W$ in a stylized setting (large $n$, orthogonal user basis, $\sigma = s^\top x$), via implicit-function-theorem differentiation through the equilibrium.

Theorem 1 additionally shows a unique PNE exists when $K=n$ with smooth concave $\sigma$ (via strict monotone game / Rosen 1965), but the paper deliberately pivots to LNE as the practical solution concept.

Key Results주요 결과
  • Synthetic (10 clustered user groups, 200 creators): all three mechanisms beat no-intervention with a "double-ascent" welfare curve; HMT gives the largest gain with least variance and a Pareto improvement across all group sizes; SMT gains but with high variance and increased utility skew (Fig. 2).
  • MovieLens-1m (1578 users, 20 creators, MF embeddings, RMSE 0.739): same ordering; HMT lifts welfare from ~0.56 to ~0.61 vs. flat baseline (Fig. 3).
  • Online 3-week A/B (3% users / 3% creators per arm, closed-loop symmetric design, HMT on cold-start retrieval): LTR +1.13%, cold-start impressions +0.76%, fresh (<2h) content impressions +3.7%, topics consumed per user +0.71% — all reported statistically significant.
  • Per-group online breakdown: smaller user groups gain more LTR (e.g. groups 6–20: +1.40%; 75+: +1.36% vs. top-5 groups +0.43%) — echoing the offline niche-group prioritization (Table 2).
  • Creator DAU for popular creators (>1000 followers) trends up over weeks: −0.2% → +0.24% → +0.48%, suggesting creator response is slower than the 3-week window.
5Cs Analysis5Cs 분석

Category: new method (mechanism design + adaptive algorithm) with theoretical support and industrial online validation. Context: extends the C³ game of Yao et al. 2023a,b; relaxes behavioral assumptions vs. Ben-Porat & Tennenholtz 2017/2018, Hu et al. 2023, Zhu et al. 2023; complements empirical lines (Mladenov et al. 2020 constrained matching; Prasad et al. 2023 content prompting). Directly instantiates exposure allocation as the platform's incentive instrument (survey objective O3). Correctness: Theorem 2's improving-direction guarantee holds only under strong simplifications (orthogonal user basis, $n$ large, ignoring higher-order terms, $\beta^{-1} \gg 1$); real deployment is heuristic. Offline results averaged over 10 runs with error bars (note: MovieLens plot uses 0.2-sigma bars, which visually understates variance). Online A/B claims statistical significance but reports no CIs/p-values in the main text. Contributions: (1) formalizes welfare sub-optimality under realistic local creator dynamics and pins it on platform–creator information asymmetry; (2) three deployable intervention mechanisms (UIR/SMT/HMT) + adaptive reweighting with a stylized-setting improvement guarantee; (3) a 3-week live A/B test on a leading short-video platform showing significant welfare/diversity gains. Clarity: algorithms are fully specified (Algs. 1–5, incl. the online weight→percentile mapping); offline setup reproducible; online environment necessarily anonymized and unreproducible externally.

Contribution vs Claim기여 대 주장
  • Stated claims: platform can steer creators toward welfare-optimal outcomes via reward/matching-signal design, with theoretical justification.
  • Demonstrated: welfare improvement under LBR creator simulator (offline) and significant engagement/diversity gains in one 3-week online test; Theorem 2's ascent guarantee only in a stylized orthogonal-basis setting.
  • Gap: no guarantee the adaptive reweighting improves welfare under general user distributions or general (non-LBR) creator behavior; the online test validates HMT only, on cold-start retrieval only, for 3 weeks (the paper itself notes creator response was likely not yet saturated). Relative merits of UIR/SMT/HMT left explicitly as future work. [SKIM - deep read could verify appendix approximation steps in Thm 2 more carefully]
Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

C³ext game (extended Content Creator Competition game)

  • What it computes: a tuple $(\mathcal{X}, \{S_i\}_{i=1}^n, \sigma, \beta, K, R(\cdot))$ — user distribution, creator strategy sets (content embeddings $s_i \in \mathbb{R}^d$), relevance function $\sigma(s,x) \in [0,1]$, softmax matching temperature $\beta$, top-$K$ truncation, and post-matching reward $R$. Creator $i$'s utility: $u_i(s) = \mathbb{E}_{x}[R(s_i,x)\cdot P_i(s,x)]$ where $P_i(s,x) \propto \exp[\beta^{-1}\sigma_{l(i),x}]$ over the top-$K$ ranked items.
  • Why it was created: prior C³ analyses (Yao et al. 2023a,b) required strong behavioral assumptions (no-regret learning, oracle utility access) for Nash equilibrium to be reachable; this extension models realistic creators with only black-box, local trial-and-error updates.
  • Concrete example: 5 users at $\{(0,0),(1,0),(0,1),(-1,0),(0,-1)\}$, 5 creators, $R=\sigma=\max\{2-\|s_i-x\|_2,0\}$, $\beta=10$, $K=3$ — the PNE is each creator on one user, but local dynamics never reach it.
  • How it is used in this paper: the environment for all mechanism design and the offline simulators (synthetic + MovieLens-1m).

Local Nash Equilibrium (LNE) + Local Better Response (LBR) dynamics

  • What it computes: LBR — at each step a creator samples a random direction $g_i$, moves $s_i \leftarrow s_i + \eta g_i$ only if utility increases (point-estimate comparison); converged LBR profiles are LNEs (best response within an open neighborhood only).
  • Why it was created: PNE existence fails for $K < n$ (top-$K$ ranking makes utilities discontinuous) and even a unique PNE is not reached by natural local dynamics; creators exhibit path dependency and lack global utility knowledge.
  • Concrete example: in the 5-user example above, LBR traps 2 creators on the central "popular" user and leaves 2 peripheral users unserved (Fig. 1 left) — sub-optimal welfare despite a unique PNE existing.
  • How it is used in this paper: the creator response model in offline simulations; motivates why the platform must intervene.

UIR / SMT / HMT intervention mechanisms

  • What it computes: three ways to inject a per-user weight $w(x)$ into creator utility. UIR (User Importance Reweighting): scale post-matching reward, $u_i = \mathbb{E}_x[w(x)R(s_i,x)P_i(s,x)]$. SMT (Soft Matching Truncation): scale the softmax temperature, $\beta(x)=\beta\cdot w(x)$. HMT (Hard Matching Truncation): scale the retrieval pool size, $K(x)=\lceil K\cdot w(x)\rceil$.
  • Why it was created: platforms differ in what levers they control — payments (UIR) vs. only matching-policy knobs (SMT/HMT under budget constraints).
  • Concrete example: online HMT deployment maps group weight to cold-start relevance percentile: weight $<1.0 \to$ top-1% (percentile 0.99), weight $\geq 2.68 \to$ top-90% (percentile 0.1) (Table 1).
  • How it is used in this paper: the three deployed mechanisms compared against no-intervention in all experiments; HMT chosen for the online A/B test.

Adaptive Reweighting (multiplicative weight update on user-group weights)

  • What it computes: per epoch, measure each user group's average utility $\bar\pi_l$; update $w_l \leftarrow w_l \cdot \exp(-\alpha\bar\pi_l)$, normalize, clip to $[w_{min}, w_{max}]$. Down-weights well-served (over-exposed) groups, up-weights under-served ones.
  • Why it was created: the bi-level problem $\max_w W(s^*(w))$ has an intractable $dW/dw$ in general; Theorem 2 shows $w_j' = w_j e^{-\eta\bar\pi(x_j)}$ is a guaranteed-improving direction in a stylized setting (many creators, orthogonal user basis, dot-product relevance).
  • Concrete example: offline config — 20 K-means user groups (synthetic), $\alpha = 0.5 \to 0.1$, clip $(0.2, 5.0)$, epoch length $M=5$, horizon $T=3000$.
  • How it is used in this paper: Algorithm 1, the core platform-side algorithm; online, weights were recomputed daily from relative like-through-rate (LTR) changes.
Implementation Feasibility구현 가능성
  • Needs: user/item embeddings + relevance model (MF sufficed offline), user clustering (K-means or demographic features), a per-group satisfaction signal (LTR), and control over either creator payments (UIR) or retrieval parameters $\beta, K$ (SMT/HMT). Compute is trivial (weight vector of dimension = #groups, daily update).
  • Code: not available (no repository link found in the paper).
  • Modifications: for a general research setting, need a creator-response simulator (Alg. 2 is specified and easy to reimplement); the online HMT weight→percentile mapping (Table 1) is platform-specific and must be re-tuned.
  • Unknowns: missing link — how sensitive results are to the user clustering granularity (20 vs. 15 vs. hundreds of groups online); missing link — behavior when creators are strategic about the intervention itself (gaming the reweighting).

Condition Delta — transfer to our setting조건 차이 — 우리 설정으로의 전이

Paper's setting: inputs = user/content embeddings with a learned relevance score $\sigma$, a finite creator population doing local strategy updates; outputs = per-user-group weights driving reward payments or retrieval-pool sizes; conditions = platform observes per-group satisfaction (utility/LTR), creators respond to exposure/monetary incentives over weeks; welfare = expected matched relevance (or LTR online). Our setting (SP9 — exposure allocation as the platform's incentive instrument, within the exposure-frequency-as-ranking-proxy survey): we study when exposure frequency functions as a proxy/currency in ranking systems and how allocating it steers ecosystem participants. Differences:

  • Exposure treated as the incentive currency paid to creators (via matching probability $P_i$ and reward $R$) rather than as a ranking-quality proxy per se: resolved — the paper is the direct instantiation of O3; HMT literally allocates exposure (retrieval-pool size) per user group as the steering instrument.
  • Creator behavior model (LBR, embedding-space moves) vs. real creators choosing discrete topics/formats: missing link — online results show only aggregate correlation (Pearson 0.2 between delivery volume and creation volume per topic); individual creator response is not modeled from data.
  • Welfare defined as matched relevance / LTR vs. broader notions (long-term retention, fairness across creators): reference only — paper flags creator-side fairness and platform cost as open questions.
  • Uniqueness/stability of the steered outcome: missing link — LNE is non-unique; the bi-level problem (Eq. 11–12) is acknowledged ill-defined in general.
Root-cause vs. Symptom근본 원인 대 증상

Mixed — the paper correctly diagnoses the root cause (information asymmetry: creators can't see global demand, so the platform's exposure/reward signal is their information channel) and designs the fix at that level, but the deployed remedy is a heuristic multiplicative-weight loop whose optimality guarantee (Thm 2) holds only in a heavily stylized setting; in general environments it patches the observable symptom (low group utility → raise weight) without a convergence or welfare guarantee.
