Controlling Popularity Bias in Learning-to-Rank Recommendation

Abdollahpouri et al. · RecSys 2017 · 2017

link · SP: SP8

P0Track A

한 줄 요약

learning-to-rank 추천의 인기 편향을 정규화로 제어하는 초기 대표작(RecSys 2017).

Problem해결하려는 문제

Learning-to-rank recommenders trained on interaction data inherit that data's popularity skew: popular items are recommended frequently while long-tail items — often the more valuable, novel recommendations for users and the more profitable ones for niche suppliers — rarely appear in top-k lists at all. Prior work offered no tunable, in-training mechanism to control this trade-off; accuracy-optimal training simply reproduces the bias.

Solution / Contribution해결책 / 기여

[SKIM - verify] A regularization-based framework: augment a learning-to-rank objective with a long-tail-coverage penalty term weighted by a coefficient λ, giving a single tunable knob between ranking accuracy and long-tail exposure. Training (not post-hoc re-ranking) is modified — the ranker itself is re-fit with the penalized objective. The core claim: moderate λ values substantially increase long-tail item presence in recommendation lists at small cost in ranking accuracy.

Key Results주요 결과
  • [SKIM] Headline finding (from abstract-level summary): adding the long-tail regularizer increases long-tail coverage of top-k lists with only a small loss in ranking accuracy; λ traces a controllable trade-off curve.
  • [SKIM] Exact datasets (likely MovieLens [SKIM - verify]), metrics, and numbers require the PDF.
  • Adoption signal: 447 citations (S2, 2026) — a standard reference point for popularity-bias control.

Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

Long-tail coverage regularizer (in-objective popularity penalty) [SKIM - verify in deep read]

  • What it computes: takes the learning-to-rank model's predicted scores/rankings → adds a penalty term to the LTR training objective that measures how concentrated the recommended items are in the popular (short-head) segment → outputs a modified loss L = L_rank + λ · LT(·) whose minimization trades ranking accuracy against long-tail item coverage.
  • Why it was created: prior LTR recommenders optimize accuracy only, and because interaction data is skewed toward popular items, their top-k lists over-represent the short head; no prior mechanism let the system designer dial coverage of less popular items directly inside training.
  • Concrete example: [SKIM] — exact regularizer form (reported to be built on a short-head/long-tail item partition, with a scalar λ controlling the trade-off) needs the methods section.
  • How it is used in this paper: plugged into a factorization-based pairwise LTR algorithm (RankALS [SKIM - verify base ranker]); sweeping λ produces the accuracy-vs-coverage curve that is the paper's main result.

Short-head / long-tail item partition [SKIM - verify in deep read]

  • What it computes: item interaction counts → threshold split (Pareto-style) → binary label per item (popular "head" vs unpopular "tail") used inside the regularizer and in the coverage metric.
  • Why it was created: gives an operational, measurable definition of "the items popularity bias suppresses," so coverage can be both penalized in training and reported in evaluation.
  • Concrete example: [SKIM] — exact cutoff (e.g., head = items generating the top ~20% of ratings) needs the paper.
  • How it is used in this paper: defines both the regularization target and the long-tail coverage evaluation metric.
Implementation Feasibility구현 가능성
  • Needs: interaction dataset with popularity statistics; an LTR implementation whose objective can be augmented (RankALS-style ALS solver [SKIM - verify]); modest compute (2017-era MF scale).
  • Code: not available (no repository listed; closed-access paper) [SKIM - verify].
  • Modifications: for the survey's setting, the penalty term would need to be re-derived for the target ranker's loss and for exposure-frequency (not binary head/tail) definitions of popularity.
  • Unknowns: missing link: exact regularizer equation, λ sweep results, datasets, and evaluation metrics — all require the PDF.

Condition Delta — transfer to our setting조건 차이 — 우리 설정으로의 전이

Paper's setting: inputs = explicit/implicit user-item interactions; model = factorization-based pairwise LTR retrained with a popularity regularizer; output = top-k lists with tunable long-tail coverage; intervention point = training time. Our setting (SP8, exposure-frequency-as-ranking-proxy survey): controlling popularity/exposure bias in ranking outputs, with the SP frontier focused on post-hoc methods that do not retrain the ranker. Differences:

  • Intervention point: retraining the ranker vs post-hoc re-ranking — reference only (searcher flag: record for the O2 feasibility map, but this paper sits on the retraining line, off the post-hoc frontier).
  • Bias definition: static head/tail item partition vs exposure-frequency-based measures — missing link (whether the regularizer generalizes to continuous exposure-frequency targets is not addressed here) [SKIM - verify].
  • Base ranker: 2017-era matrix-factorization LTR vs modern neural rankers — missing link (regularizer is objective-level so may transfer, but no evidence in this paper).
Root-cause vs. Symptom근본 원인 대 증상

Symptom — the regularizer counteracts the popularity skew in the model's outputs by penalizing head-item concentration, without addressing why the training signal over-scores popular items in the first place (data imbalance / exposure feedback loop); it is an explicit, tunable counterweight, not a de-biasing of the underlying estimator.
