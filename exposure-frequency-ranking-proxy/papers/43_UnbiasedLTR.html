Unbiased Learning-to-Rank with Biased Feedback

Joachims et al. · arXiv · 2016 · 640 citations

arXiv:1608.04468 · PDF · SP: SP4

P2Track C

한 줄 요약

click 데이터의 position bias를 counterfactual 추정으로 제거하는 unbiased learning-to-rank의 기초 논문. position-bias 곡선을 낮은 비용의 개입(swap)으로 추정하는 실무 경로를 제공한다.

Problem해결하려는 문제

Implicit feedback (clicks) is the only affordable training signal in most ranking applications, but it is corrupted by position/presentation bias: how many clicks a result gets depends heavily on where it was shown, so naive click-as-relevance training is asymptotically biased. Prior fixes each had a blocking limitation — click-preference heuristics only learn preferences opposing the presented order; generative click models need the same query to repeat many times (impossible for tail queries and personal search); online/bandit randomization degrades live ranking quality during data collection.

Solution / Contribution해결책 / 기여

A counterfactual inference framework: treat each result's relevance as revealed with a propensity $Q(o_i(y)=1 \mid x_i, \bar{y}_i, r_i)$, then train by propensity-weighted ERM on the IPS estimator

$$\hat{R}_{IPS}(S) = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n} \sum_{y: c_i(y)=1} \frac{\text{rank}(y \mid S(x_i))}{p_{\text{rank}(y|\bar{y}_i)}}$$

which is provably unbiased for the true risk when propensities are positive, and whose minimizer is provably unchanged under click noise ($\epsilon_+ > \epsilon_-$; the noise-induced bias is order-preserving). Instantiated as Propensity SVM-Rank; propensities come from a position-based examination model calibrated by a small swap-to-top randomization pilot.

Key Results주요 결과
  • Synthetic (Yahoo LTR Challenge set 1, binarized; $\eta=1$, $\epsilon_-=0.1$): Propensity SVM-Rank's avg-rank-of-relevant approaches the noise-free full-information skyline as click data grows (1.7E3 → 1.7E6 clicks), while Naive SVM-Rank's learning curve is essentially flat — asymptotic bias does not shrink with more data.
  • Noise robustness: at $\epsilon_- = 0.3$ (59.8% of clicks on irrelevant documents) Propensity SVM-Rank still substantially outperforms Naive SVM-Rank and continues to benefit from 5x more data.
  • Misspecification: propensities misspecified conservatively (overestimating small $p_r$, assumed $\eta < 1$ vs. true $\eta = 1$) remain beneficial; underestimating small propensities ($\eta > 1$) is harmful. Clipping is a principled instance of conservative overestimation.
  • Live search engine (21 days of logs, 5437 train clicks): interleaving vs. hand-crafted production ranker 87 wins / 48 losses / 83 ties (p = 0.001); vs. Naive SVM-Rank 95 / 60 / 102 (p = 0.006).

Contribution vs Claim기여 대 주장
  • Stated claims: unbiased LTR framework general across feedback settings and click models; robustness to bias severity, noise, misspecification; real-world applicability.
  • Demonstrated: unbiasedness proven for the sum-of-ranks loss with binary relevance; robustness shown on one synthetic corpus (Yahoo set 1) under the same position-based model used for both generation and correction (misspecification tested only within the $\eta$ family); real-world gain shown on one small-scale engine (5437 clicks) with a linear ranker.
  • Gap: generality to other loss functions (DCG-style), listwise/pointwise methods, and richer click models (trust bias, cascade) is conjectured (Sections 5.4, 9), not demonstrated. [SKIM - flagged from body; deep read would not change this]
Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

Counterfactual model of partial-information LTR (observation vector o_i)

  • What it computes: for each query instance $(x_i, r_i)$ with presented ranking $\bar{y}_i$, a 0/1 vector $o_i \sim P(o \mid x_i, \bar{y}_i, r_i)$ indicating which relevance values were revealed to the system; the marginal $Q(o_i(y)=1 \mid x_i, \bar{y}_i, r_i)$ is the propensity of observing result $y$'s relevance.
  • Why it was created: naive click-as-label training is biased because implicit feedback is missing-not-at-random — top-ranked results are far more likely to be observed. Prior click-model approaches required repeated queries to infer relevance; this model de-biases individual observations instead.
  • Concrete example: in the paper's synthetic setup, examination probability at rank $r$ is $p_r = (1/r)^\eta$; with $\eta = 1$ the result at rank 10 has a 10% chance of being examined at all.
  • How it is used in this paper: it is the theoretical foundation for the IPS risk estimator (Eq. 4) and everything downstream.

IPS (inverse-propensity-scored) risk estimator for rankings

  • What it computes: for a new ranking $y$, $\hat{\Delta}_{IPS} = \sum_{y: o_i(y)=1 \wedge r_i(y)=1} \frac{\text{rank}(y \mid \boldsymbol{y})}{Q(o_i(y)=1 \mid x_i, \bar{y}_i, r_i)}$ — each clicked (observed-and-relevant) result's rank, weighted by the inverse of its observation propensity. Provably unbiased for the sum-of-ranks-of-relevant-results loss whenever propensities of relevant results are $> 0$.
  • Why it was created: enables Empirical Risk Minimization over biased click logs without needing negative labels or repeated queries.
  • Concrete example: a click at presented rank 10 under $p_{10} = 0.1$ contributes with weight $1/0.1 = 10$, exactly compensating its low exposure.
  • How it is used in this paper: it is the training objective (Eq. 5) that Propensity SVM-Rank optimizes an upper bound of.

Position-based propensity model (examination model as propensity estimator)

  • What it computes: $P(\text{click}) = P(e_i(y)=1 \mid \text{rank}(y|\bar{y})) \cdot P(c_i(y)=1 \mid r_i(y), e_i(y)=1)$; under noise-free clicking, examination = observation, so $Q \equiv p_{\text{rank}}$ — a per-rank examination probability vector.
  • Why it was created: gives a concrete, estimable form for the propensities. Key conceptual shift: the click model assigns propensities to clicks in hindsight rather than aggregating relevance across repeated queries.
  • Concrete example: the real-world deployment estimated $p_r$ decaying with rank down to $p_r \simeq 0.12$ at rank 21.
  • How it is used in this paper: supplies the $q_j$ weights in Propensity SVM-Rank; estimated via the swap intervention below.

Swap intervention for propensity estimation

  • What it computes: randomly swap the result at landmark rank $k$ with the result at rank $r$ before presenting; the ratio of the swapped document's CTR at position $r$ vs. position $k$ estimates $p_r / p_k$ (propensities only need to be known up to a multiplicative constant).
  • Why it was created: uniform ranking randomization (prior work) severely degrades user experience; a single swap is a far lower-impact intervention.
  • Concrete example: real-world experiment swapped the top document with a uniformly random rank $j \in \{1,\dots,21\}$ for 7 days.
  • How it is used in this paper: calibrates the position-based propensity model on the operational search engine.

Propensity SVM-Rank (and propensity clipping)

  • What it computes: SVM-Rank with each clicked example's slack term weighted by $1/q_j$; clipping variant replaces $Q$ by $\max\{\tau, Q\}$ to trade bias for variance on small datasets.
  • Why it was created: concrete ERM instantiation of the counterfactual framework.
  • Concrete example: objective $\min_w \frac{1}{2}\|w\|^2 + \frac{C}{n}\sum_j \frac{1}{q_j}\sum_{y} \xi_{jy}$.
  • How it is used in this paper: the evaluated learning method in all experiments.
Implementation Feasibility구현 가능성
  • Needs: click logs with presented rankings; a propensity estimate per rank (requires either a small swap-randomization pilot or an assumed decay model); SVM-Rank-style QP solver.
  • Code: SVM-Rank base available at https://www.joachims.org/svm_light/svm_rank.html; paper says modified IPS-weighted version "will be available online" [SKIM - check joachims.org for release].
  • Modifications: for our survey's purpose the reusable pieces are the exposure definition ($p_r$ examination model) and the IPS unbiasedness argument; adapting to frequency-based exposure proxies means replacing rank-indexed propensities with impression-count-based ones.
  • Unknowns: missing link — how propensities are obtained without any randomization (fully observational logs); missing link — behavior when propensities are near zero for never-exposed items (the $Q > 0$ support condition fails exactly for items with zero exposure, which is the frequency-proxy failure mode SP4 cares about).
Follow-up Papers관련 후속 논문
  • Wang et al., "Learning to Rank with Selection Bias in Personal Search" (SIGIR 2016) — the direct predecessor whose per-query propensity idea this paper generalizes to per-click propensities.
  • Wang et al., "Position Bias Estimation for Unbiased Learning to Rank in Personal Search" (WSDM 2018) — removes the randomization requirement via regression-EM propensity estimation (addresses the missing link above).
  • Ai et al., "Unbiased Learning to Rank with Unbiased Propensity Estimation" (SIGIR 2018) — joint learning of ranker and propensity model, extending this framework.
  • Schnabel et al., "Recommendations as Treatments" (ICML 2016) — sibling IPS framework for recommender exposure bias, closer to frequency-proxy settings.
Condition Delta — transfer to our setting조건 차이 — 우리 설정으로의 전이

Paper's setting: inputs = search click logs (query, presented ranking, clicks) + a small randomized swap pilot; outputs = a linear ranking function; conditions = position bias as the sole exposure mechanism, binary relevance, propensities known/estimable and bounded away from 0. Our setting (SP4 — exposure frequency as a ranking proxy): we need a formal definition of exposure an item receives under a ranking and how observation frequency relates to rank, to justify or critique using exposure/click frequency as a relevance or quality proxy. Differences:

  • Paper defines exposure implicitly as examination probability $p_r$ (a per-rank propensity), exactly the primitive SP4 needs to define exposure formally: resolved — the position-based propensity model is the canonical pre-2018 exposure definition (this is why the source was flagged foundational).
  • Paper's goal is training an unbiased ranker; our interest is exposure as a measurement/proxy question: reference only — we borrow the exposure model and the unbiasedness argument, not the SVM learner.
  • Paper assumes propensities estimable via a live intervention; a purely observational setting (no ability to randomize) breaks the estimation recipe: missing link — later work on intervention-free propensity estimation would be needed. [SKIM - verify what SP4's other papers assume]
  • Single-slot examination model (rank alone determines observation) vs. richer exposure notions (attractiveness, trust bias): acknowledged by the paper as extensions, not solved: reference only.
Root-cause vs. Symptom근본 원인 대 증상

Direct — the paper identifies the root cause of biased click training (relevance observations are missing-not-at-random with rank-dependent propensity) and removes it at the estimator level with a proven-unbiased IPS correction, rather than patching symptoms with heuristic preference rules or reweighting tricks.
