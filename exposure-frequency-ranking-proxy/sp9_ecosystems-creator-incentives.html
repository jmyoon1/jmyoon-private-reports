SP9: Content Ecosystems, Creator Incentives, and Platform Exposure Mechanisms

15 papers · targets O3, O4 · 2022-2026

The 2018-2026 mechanism-design line that treats exposure allocation as the platform's policy lever over creator behavior and content supply — the final ranked SP, carrying five decisive cross-survey lenses (exposure=payoff denomination, ads-pacing bridge, exposure-endogenous scorer, LLM feedback loops, gaming of frequency interventions).

SP9: 콘텐츠 생태계와 creator 인센티브

15편 논문 · O3, O4 목표 대상 · 2022-2026

exposure 배분을 creator 행동과 콘텐츠 공급에 대한 플랫폼의 정책 레버로 다루는 2018–2026 mechanism-design 라인 — 마지막 ranked SP로서 다섯 개의 결정적 교차 렌즈(exposure=보수 단위, ads-pacing 인용 다리, exposure 내생 scorer, LLM feedback loop, 빈도 개입 gaming)를 수행한다.

Entry Assumption vs. What Was Found

Expected (revised after SP2/SP5/SP6/SP7/SP8 — five decisive lenses including the exposure-endogenous-scorer last chance and the decisive LLM-feedback-loop spawn check): The mechanism-design / ecosystem view treats exposure allocation as the platform's policy lever over creator behavior and content supply — the likeliest source of unaddressed constraint combinations for O4's open questions; test exposure=payoff against heterogeneous conversion and the VRS proxy failure.

Found: Established with theorems and a production field experiment: the allocation rule alone determines equilibrium content; relevance-argmax is both strategically unstable (no pure Nash under any complete fair mediator) and ecosystem-suboptimal; purely frequency-level creator steering works in production (HMT: +1.13% LTR over an unmodified relevance model). But every model is single-impression-per-user — the impressions-vs-reach distinction is inexpressible.

Assessment: Pivoted — show/don't-show was assumed to be an access-level compromise; StrategicProviders proves incomplete display is axiomatically required for fairness + strategic stability, giving the survey's core object a normative foundation. All four decisive negative checks landed: no pacing citation bridge, no exposure-endogenous scorer model, no LLM-loop work (spawn condition met → SP10), and gaming of frequency interventions unmodeled (→ SP11).

사전 가정과 실제 발견

기대 (SP2/SP5/SP6/SP7/SP8 이후 수정 — exposure 내생 scorer의 마지막 기회와 결정적 LLM feedback loop 스폰 검사를 포함한 다섯 렌즈): mechanism-design/생태계 관점은 exposure 배분을 creator 행동과 콘텐츠 공급에 대한 플랫폼의 정책 레버로 다루며, O4 open question의 가장 유력한 원천으로 기대하였다. exposure=보수 가정을 이질적 변환과 VRS proxy 실패에 비추어 검정하기로 하였다.

발견: 정리와 production 필드 실험으로 확립되었다: 배분 규칙만으로 균형 콘텐츠가 결정되고, relevance-argmax는 전략적으로 불안정하며(어떤 완전 공정 mediator로도 순수 Nash 부재) 생태계 관점에서도 열등하다. 순수 빈도 수준 creator 조향은 production에서 작동한다(HMT: 무수정 relevance 모델 위 LTR +1.13%). 다만 모든 모델이 사용자당 단일 impression이어서 impressions-vs-reach 구분은 표현조차 불가능하다.

평가: 전환됨 — show/don't-show를 접근 수준의 타협으로 가정했으나, StrategicProviders는 불완전 표시가 공정성+전략적 안정성에 공리적으로 필요함을 증명한다 — 조사의 핵심 대상이 규범적 기초를 얻었다. 네 가지 결정적 부정 검사가 모두 확정되었다: pacing 인용 다리 부재, exposure 내생 scorer 모델 부재, LLM 루프 연구 부재(스폰 조건 충족 → SP10), 빈도 개입 gaming 미모델링(→ SP11).

What SP9 Establishes

The ecosystem literature establishes, with both theorems and a production field experiment, that exposure allocation IS the platform's incentive instrument: the allocation rule alone determines what content gets created at equilibrium (temperature and cost-geometry phase transitions in [105] CreatorIncentives, [106] SupplySideEq), relevance-argmax allocation is both strategically unstable (no pure Nash equilibrium under any complete fair mediator, [104] StrategicProviders) and ecosystem-suboptimal (provider attrition collapses long-run welfare, [96] LongTermWelfareMatching), and purely frequency-level steering of creators works in production ([99] WelfareCompetingCreators's HMT: per-user-group retrieval-pool sizing over an unmodified relevance model, 3-week A/B, LTR +1.13%, fresh-content impressions +3.7%).

But the entire line rests on the exposure=payoff assumption in its rawest form: creator utility is linear in single-slot display counts or display probabilities, with exactly one impression per user per round — so position weighting, repeat impressions, and the impressions-vs-reach distinction are not merely unmodeled, they are inexpressible in every model ([104] StrategicProviders, [105] CreatorIncentives, [106] SupplySideEq, [100] PPAGame). Against the survey's accumulated evidence (heterogeneous exposure-to-utility conversion in four production systems; Meta VRS satisfying impression parity while reach fell 9.82%), the line's welfare guarantees are conditional on an assumption already falsified in production. The four decisive negative checks all land: no ecosystem paper cites the ads-pacing/DMD cluster (the pacing↔fairness-dual reduction is now a CONFIRMED O4 gap after all 9 SPs); no paper models the scorer as endogenous to exposure-shaped training logs (uuid 25's last chance fails — [98] PerformativeRec supplies the nearest formal skeleton, performative prediction, but only for the creator-content channel and only as a white-box training-time intervention); no paper studies LLM-era exposure feedback loops ([101] LoRe uses LLM agents only as behavioral simulators) — the spawn condition set after SP8 is met; and no paper models creators strategically gaming a frequency-level intervention (caps/floors/boosts) — [99] WelfareCompetingCreators explicitly flags gaming-the-reweighting as open.

One genuine surprise strengthens the survey's core object: [104] StrategicProviders proves fairness + strategic stability jointly force incomplete display — the only fair, stable, efficient mediator (Shapley) must sometimes show nothing. Show/don't-show frequency control is not just an access-level convenience under the black-box constraint; it is axiomatically necessary for a stable creator ecosystem.

SP9이 확립한 것

SP9은 생태계 문헌이 정리와 production 필드 실험 양쪽으로 'exposure 배분이 곧 플랫폼의 인센티브 수단'임을 확립함을 보인다: 배분 규칙만으로 균형에서 어떤 콘텐츠가 생성되는지가 결정되고(CreatorIncentives의 온도 상전이, SupplySideEq의 전문화 상전이), relevance-argmax 배분은 전략적으로 불안정하며(StrategicProviders — 완전 mediator 하 순수 Nash 부재; 공정성+안정성+효율에는 불완전 표시가 공리적으로 필요) 장기 후생 관점에서도 열등하다(LongTermWelfareMatching). 순수 빈도 수준 creator 조향의 production 검증(HMT)과 Shapley mediator라는 두 명의 엄밀 빈도 전용 구성원을 추가했다. 결정적 한계: 이론 라인 전체가 creator 효용을 단일 슬롯 표시 횟수에 선형으로 가정한다 — 조사가 축적한 반증(4개 production 시스템의 이질적 exposure→효용 변환, VRS의 reach −9.82%)에 비추면 이 라인의 후생 보장은 production에서 이미 반증된 가정 위에 서 있다.

Dominant Approach지배적 접근

The dominant method family is the strategic-creator game over exposure payoffs, in three generations. The 2018-2020 foundation ([104] StrategicProviders, [96] LongTermWelfareMatching) formalized exposure as the creator's payoff and derived two structural results: relevance-argmax display admits no stable creator equilibrium, and myopic matching starves providers below viability, collapsing long-run welfare — in both, the corrective is deliberate exposure withholding/reallocation. The 2022-2023 generation ([105] CreatorIncentives, [106] SupplySideEq, [98] PerformativeRec) moved creators into continuous content spaces and showed the allocation rule's shape alone — softmax temperature, cost-norm geometry — flips the equilibrium content supply between homogenization and specialization, and that under retraining the content pool itself (not just its presentation) drifts. The 2024-2026 generation ([99] WelfareCompetingCreators, [100] PPAGame, [101] LoRe, [102] AdAuctionAllocation, [103] Uniboost) turned platform-side: intervention mechanisms with realistic (local, black-box) creator dynamics, decentralized learning to equilibrium under proportional exposure splitting, and industrial exposure-allocation machinery (learned auction-allocation integration; attributable additive boosts with per-plan ROI ledgers).

The family became dominant because exposure is the one quantity the platform unambiguously controls and the creator unambiguously observes — it closes the game without income or conversion data. That is also its key limitation: every equilibrium result is denominated in raw single-impression counts (no positions, no repeats, no conversion heterogeneity), and every model either knows the scorer exactly (known $\sigma_i$, owned embeddings) or replaces it with game primitives (exogenous weights $w_{j,k}$). The alternatives that exist inside the SP — information revelation ([101] LoRe), creator payments (UIR in [99] WelfareCompetingCreators), content prompting ([97] RecEcosystems §7) — abandon the exposure lever rather than fixing its denomination.

For the survey objectives: O3's ecosystem recency requirement is satisfied (KDD 2024/2025, SIGIR 2024/2026 entries with production deployments), and the SP delivers two additions to the O2 feasibility map's strict frequency-only subset (HMT; the Shapley mediator as a design pattern). For O4, all four decisive negative checks completed here (pacing bridge, exposure-endogenous scorer, LLM feedback loops, gaming of frequency interventions) convert accumulated watch items into confirmed, completed-search gaps.

Convergences수렴 지점
  1. Myopic relevance-argmax allocation is ecosystem-suboptimal, and deliberate exposure reallocation improves long-run welfare. Established via MILP/equilibrium theory ([96] LongTermWelfareMatching: welfare $8+2\varepsilon \to 10-2\varepsilon$ by subsidizing a near-threshold provider), game simulation plus production A/B ([99] WelfareCompetingCreators: HMT lifts welfare offline ~0.56→0.61 and LTR +1.13% online), and retraining dynamics ([98] PerformativeRec: accuracy-only training loses ~40% content diversity in 10 rounds). Mechanism: exposure allocation determines both which providers survive (viability) and what surviving providers make (incentive gradients); per-query argmax optimizes neither.
  1. Exposure=payoff, linear in raw single-impression counts, is the universal creator-utility assumption of the theory line. [104] StrategicProviders (displays), [105] CreatorIncentives (softmax probability, explicitly "linear utility in exposure" in its own limitations), [106] SupplySideEq (user-win counts), [100] PPAGame (proportional shares) — four independent models, same denomination; [97] RecEcosystems independently names it a defect (creator utility plausibly sigmoidal/saturating in cumulative engagement) without repairing it. The lone non-linear conversion is [96] LongTermWelfareMatching's step-function viability threshold — exogenously known, never estimated.
  1. The allocation rule's shape flips the equilibrium content-supply regime. Three independent formalisms: softmax temperature ([105] CreatorIncentives: $\tau$ above threshold → all creators at the mean consumer, an $\epsilon$-PNE with $\epsilon_\tau \le \epsilon/\tau$), cost geometry ([106] SupplySideEq: single genre iff $\beta \le \beta^* = 2/(1-\cos\theta^*)$), and rule smoothness ([100] PPAGame: proportional splitting restores PNE existence that winner-take-all destroys; [104] StrategicProviders is the winner-take-all impossibility anchor). The shared mechanism: exposure spread flattens the payoff gradient that rewards niche positioning.
  1. Steering is persistent-policy work, not a one-shot intervention. [98] PerformativeRec (regularizer off at round 5 → immediate diversity collapse) and [99] WelfareCompetingCreators (creator response still growing at week 3; weights recomputed daily) agree from opposite methodologies.
Divergences분기 지점
  1. Is the exposure lever structurally adequate for creator steering? [101] LoRe argues no — exposure-based steering competes for the same ranking slots as accuracy, degrading both, and moves steering to an information-revelation channel (genre suggestions), claiming superiority over exposure-fair re-ranking. [99] WelfareCompetingCreators and [103] Uniboost demonstrate the exposure lever working in production with positive engagement deltas. Likely cause: LoRe's critique targets within-slate slot competition (re-ranking-level steering), while HMT operates at retrieval eligibility (pool sizing) and Uniboost at calibrated additive blending — levers that do not contest individual slots the same way; additionally LoRe's evidence is LLM-agent simulation against re-ranking baselines, versus field A/Bs on the other side. Unresolved formally — no paper compares the levers on a common substrate.
  1. Does spreading exposure increase or decrease diversity? The consumer-side literature (SP1-SP8) treats stochastic exposure spreading as the diversity/fairness instrument; [105] CreatorIncentives proves the producer-side inverse — more exploration (higher $\tau$) incentivizes homogeneous mainstream content, less exploration incentivizes niche specialization. Cause (identified within the paper, but never adjudicated jointly): spreading diversifies what is shown from a fixed pool while blunting the payoff gradient that diversifies what is made. No paper optimizes both sides of this inversion simultaneously.
  1. Do intervention effects persist after removal? The creator-channel model predicts reversion ([98] PerformativeRec: diversity drops immediately when the incentive is removed); SP7's log-channel field evidence (ref 78) shows durable persistence (+14.75% weeks after a boost ended, via scorer retraining). Cause: two different feedback channels — creators revert when the incentive disappears, but the scorer keeps recommending what the intervention wrote into its training logs. No paper models both channels in one system, so the net removal behavior of a real frequency intervention is unpredicted by theory.
Prior Beliefs and Surprises (full)사전 믿음과 놀라움 (전문)

What the entry rationale expected to find:

Exposure allocation as the platform's policy lever affecting creator behavior and content supply; test exposure=payoff against heterogeneous conversion and the VRS proxy failure; run the pacing-citation bridge check, the exposure-endogenous-scorer last chance (uuid 25), and the decisive LLM feedback-loop check; classify creator-payoff models against the four-class reference taxonomy.

Confirmed:

  • Exposure=payoff assumed in raw impression counts across the entire theory line, exactly as suspected — and worse: the models are single-impression-per-user, so the impressions-vs-reach failure is inexpressible, not merely unexamined.
  • Pacing bridge check: negative — no ecosystem paper cites the DMD/pacing theory cluster; the pacing↔fairness reduction (uuid 6) is a confirmed O4 gap after its final check.
  • LLM feedback-loop check: negative — SP9 misses too; spawn condition met.
  • uuid 25 last chance: the log-contamination channel is modeled nowhere; gap final.
  • Pre-loop landscape read confirmed: the ecosystem line assumes exposure as the platform's sole instrument while abstracting away the black-box scorer — that mismatch held up as the SP's central gap.

Surprised:

  • (a) Assumed the ecosystem line would offer no direct member of the survey's strict frequency-only subset; found two — HMT (production-validated eligibility scaling for creator steering) and the Shapley mediator (score-read-only randomized display). The assumption was wrong because the line's interventions concentrate at the matching/eligibility layer, which is precisely the frequency layer.
  • (b) Assumed show/don't-show was an access-level compromise; [104] StrategicProviders proves incomplete display is axiomatically required for fairness + strategic stability — the survey's core object acquires a normative foundation from an unexpected direction.
  • (c) Assumed exploration/stochastic spreading is diversity-positive (the consumer-side consensus of SP1-SP8); [105] CreatorIncentives proves the producer-side inverse (exploration → homogeneous content incentives). Key Concepts핵심 개념

Exposure=payoff assumption

  • What it computes: the modeling convention that creator/provider utility equals expected exposure — display count ([104] StrategicProviders: payoff = expected number of displays), softmax display probability summed over users ([105] CreatorIncentives: $u_i(s) = \mathbb{E}_{c\sim P_c}[\mathrm{softmax}(\tau^{-1}\langle c, s\rangle)_i]$), hardmax user-win count minus production cost ([106] SupplySideEq), or a proportional share of topic exposure mass ([100] PPAGame).
  • Why it was created: it makes creator competition a well-posed game — exposure is the one quantity the platform directly allocates, so treating it as the payoff closes the model without needing income, conversion, or engagement data.
  • Concrete example: in [104] StrategicProviders, a provider deviates from the socially better item (user welfare 2.6) to a worse one (welfare 2.0) because it doubles her display count from 1 to 2 — the deviation is fully determined by raw display counts.
  • Role in this SP: the load-bearing assumption of the whole theory line and the SP2/SP5/SP7 lens target. Critically, every model serves one impression per user per round, so impressions ≡ reach by construction — the VRS failure mode (impression parity via repeat exposure to the same users) cannot even be stated inside these models. [97] RecEcosystems names the defect ("user engagement may serve as, at best, an indirect proxy for creator utility"; creator utility may be sigmoidal/saturating in cumulative engagement) but no paper repairs it.

Provider viability threshold ($\nu_c$)

  • What it computes: provider $c$'s cumulative engagement in an epoch compared against a fixed threshold; below it, the provider abandons the platform and its content is permanently lost ([96] LongTermWelfareMatching).
  • Why it was created: static-catalog RS models make provider attrition — the mechanism by which myopic ranking degrades long-run welfare — invisible.
  • Concrete example: 3 providers each needing 2 impressions/period; the myopic policy gives $c_3$ only 1, so $c_3$ exits and its ideal user gets reward 0 forever; constrained matching "subsidizes" $c_3$ and lifts per-period welfare from $8+2\varepsilon$ to $10-2\varepsilon$.
  • Role in this SP: the one non-linear exposure→utility conversion in the theory line (a step function — but exogenously known, not estimated), and the ecosystem-side justification for cumulative exposure floors. Notably it is formally the same object as SP5/SP6's amortized count floors (FairSync, Fair-Learn), with zero citation contact in either direction.

Exposure game / pre-deployment incentive audit

  • What it computes: $n$ producers each choose a unit-norm content embedding to maximize expected softmax exposure against a fixed demand distribution of user embeddings; the game's (local) Nash equilibria describe what content the ranking algorithm implicitly incentivizes. The audit fits a real recommender, takes its user embeddings as demand, and finds equilibria by simultaneous gradient ascent before deployment ([105] CreatorIncentives).
  • Why it was created: prior creator-game models restricted creators to finite item catalogs, excluding factorization/two-tower rankers with continuous embedding spaces; and A/B tests cannot randomize producers, so incentive harms surface only post-hoc.
  • Concrete example: on MovieLens and LastFM, at temperature $\tau=1$ all producers collapse into one cluster near the mean consumer embedding; at $\tau=0.01$ they scatter into distinct niches.
  • Role in this SP: the formal machinery behind the exploration→homogenization inversion (Convergence 3 / Divergence 2) and the only ex-ante tool for checking what a frequency intervention does to creator incentives.

Performative recommendation

  • What it computes: recommendation as performative prediction — deployed scorer $f^t$ → creators best-respond by modifying item features → shifted data → retrained $f^{t+1}$; the intervention is a training-time regularizer that penalizes homogeneity of the anticipated post-response content ([98] PerformativeRec).
  • Why it was created: re-ranking diversifies the presentation of a fixed pool; under retraining dynamics with strategic creators, the pool itself homogenizes (~40% diversity loss over 10 rounds), which no presentation-layer fix can reverse.
  • Concrete example: on semi-synthetic Yelp, the strategically-aware regularizer roughly doubles initial diversity and sustains it over 10 retraining rounds at the same NDCG cost where the non-strategic variant only preserves ~80%.
  • Role in this SP: the only paper in 9 SPs that models the scorer as endogenous to intervention-shaped data in its design — but through the creator-content channel (features shift), not uuid 25's log-contamination channel (exposure-biased interaction logs), and via a white-box training objective that violates the survey's immutable-scores constraint.

Local Better Response (LBR) creator dynamics

  • What it computes: at each step a creator samples a random direction, moves its content embedding a small step, and keeps the move only if utility increases; converged profiles are Local Nash Equilibria ([99] WelfareCompetingCreators).
  • Why it was created: prior equilibrium analyses required no-regret learning or oracle utility access; real creators do local black-box trial and error, and under LBR they pile onto "popular" user regions and converge to welfare-suboptimal local equilibria even when a unique PNE exists.
  • Concrete example: 5 users on a cross pattern, 5 creators — the PNE is one creator per user, but LBR traps 2 creators on the central popular user and leaves 2 peripheral users unserved.
  • Role in this SP: the behavioral model that justifies platform intervention (the platform's exposure signal is the creators' only information channel), and the environment in which the frequency-level HMT intervention is validated.

HMT (Hard Matching Truncation)

  • What it computes: per user group, scales the retrieval pool size $K(x) = \lceil K \cdot w(x)\rceil$, with group weights updated multiplicatively from realized group utility ($w \leftarrow w\,e^{-\alpha\bar\pi}$, down-weighting over-served groups) ([99] WelfareCompetingCreators).
  • Why it was created: platforms that cannot pay creators (UIR) still control matching-policy knobs; changing who is eligible to be matched reshapes the exposure payoff creators chase, without touching the relevance function $\sigma$.
  • Concrete example: online deployment maps group weight to a cold-start relevance percentile (weight <1.0 → top-1%; weight ≥2.68 → top-90%); 3-week A/B on a leading short-video platform: LTR +1.13%, cold-start impressions +0.76%, fresh (<2h) content impressions +3.7%, topics per user +0.71%.
  • Role in this SP: the ecosystem line's member of the show/don't-show family — a production-validated, score-read-only, eligibility-level exposure intervention whose target is creator-side steering. The ecosystem analogue of SP2's TEC.

Shapley mediator and the completeness impossibility

  • What it computes: display probabilities equal to each provider's Shapley value in the per-user cooperative game $v_i(\mathcal{C}) = \max_{j\in\mathcal{C}}\sigma_i(X_j)$, computable in $O(N)$: draw $Y\sim U(0,1)$, show nothing if $Y > \max_j \sigma_i(X_j)$, else uniform among items with $\sigma_i \ge Y$ ([104] StrategicProviders).
  • Why it was created: Theorem 1 proves no mediator that always displays something can satisfy the fairness axioms and equilibrium existence simultaneously (a cyclic satisfaction matrix forces any fair complete mediator into a Rock-Paper-Scissors game with no PNE); the Shapley mediator escapes by making total display probability $\max_j\sigma_i < 1$.
  • Concrete example: with satisfaction levels (0.8, 0.9), provider 1 is shown with probability $0.8/2 = 0.4$; with some probability nothing is shown.
  • Role in this SP: an axiomatic derivation of show/don't-show control — withholding exposure is necessary for a fair, stable creator ecosystem — and a score-read-only randomized exposure mechanism (it reads $\sigma_i$, never modifies it) with provable strategic stability (exact potential game) and provider-welfare PoA < 2.

(For expected exposure, position-bias curves, dual-variable pacing, and the four-class reference-distribution taxonomy, see SP1/SP4/SP7/SP8 Key Concepts.)

Open Questions After SP9SP9 이후 미해결 질문

  • Exposure-endogenous scorer, log-contamination channel (uuid 25 — final status after 9 SPs): no paper in any SP models the scorer retraining on exposure-intervention-shaped interaction logs in its design or guarantees. [105] CreatorIncentives flags "the exposure→training-signal loop is outside the model" (missing link); [100] PPAGame flags feedback-coupled quality weights (missing link); [98] PerformativeRec supplies the nearest skeleton (performative prediction with anticipatory design) but for the creator-content channel only, as a white-box training intervention. What would resolve it: a performative-prediction formulation where the distribution map is exposure-frequency → interaction logs → retrained scores, with a frequency-only (score-untouched) intervention and a stability/steering guarantee.
  • Strategic gaming of frequency-level interventions: creators in every model respond to scores or allocation rules; none respond to a cap/floor/boost as such. [99] WelfareCompetingCreators names gaming-the-reweighting an explicit unknown; [105] CreatorIncentives audits a fixed recommender with no intervention layer (missing link). Industrially familiar behaviors (re-uploading content to reset cold-start boosts, splitting content to farm floors) have no model. What would resolve it: an exposure game whose payoff includes the intervention layer (eligibility caps, debited floors), characterizing which interventions are strategy-proof.
  • Ecosystem theory under position-weighted, repeated exposure: all four game models serve one impression per user per round ([104] StrategicProviders, [105] CreatorIncentives, [106] SupplySideEq single-slot/top-1 missing links; [100] PPAGame topic-level mass), so position-bias curves, repeat impressions, and the impressions-vs-reach split are inexpressible. What would resolve it: re-deriving equilibrium existence and the phase transitions under slate exposure with a position-bias curve and per-user repeat impressions — checking whether $\beta^*$/τ-threshold structure survives.
  • LLM-era exposure feedback loops — SPAWN CONDITION MET: SP8 missed (survey predates LLM recommenders); SP9 also misses — [101] LoRe uses LLM agents as creator/user simulators, not as scorers in a feedback loop, and no SP9 paper models LLM-generated content supply responding to exposure incentives or an LLM scorer retraining on exposure-shaped logs. Per the SP8 decision rule, a new SP is warranted.
  • Two unwritten bridges into the ecosystem line: (a) no ecosystem paper cites the ads-pacing/DMD cluster — [103] Uniboost subsumes PID ad-delivery control as a special case of its additive boost form ($w=0$, $b=$ PID), an engineering-level unification with no theory, and cites none of Balseiro-Lu-Mirrokni/RCPacing — so the pacing↔fairness-dual reduction (uuid 6) completes its final check as a confirmed unwritten O4 gap; (b) provider-viability floors ([96] LongTermWelfareMatching) are formally amortized cumulative count floors, yet neither the ecosystem line nor the amortized-fairness line (FairSync, Fair-Learn) cites the other — the ecosystem side proves why floors matter (equilibrium selection), the amortized side proves how to enforce them (deterministic anytime floors), and the composition theorem is unwritten.
