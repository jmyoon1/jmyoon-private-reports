Finding Interest Needle in Popularity Haystack: Improving Retrieval by Modeling Item Exposure

Rahul Agarwal et al. · UMAP 2025 · 2025

arXiv:2503.23630 · PDF · SP: SP2

P3Track B

한 줄 요약

retrieval 단계에서 item의 과거 노출량을 명시적으로 모델링하여 인기 편향 속에서 관심 item을 되찾는 방법을 제안한다.

Problem해결하려는 문제

Retrieval models trained on logged interactions rank items by the joint probability P(positive label & exposed | item), so the closed feedback loop's exposure history directly inflates the scores of already-popular items. Existing bias-mitigation methods — IPS variants at the ranking stage (which fail at retrieval due to severe data sparsity) and Off-Policy Correction at training time — bake the correction in and give operators no real-time control over the exposure-vs-engagement trade-off at inference.

Solution / Contribution해결책 / 기여

Reframe candidate selection as P(positive label | item, exposed) instead of P(positive label & exposed | item), via the factorization:

P(positive | item, exposed) = P(positive & exposed | item) / P(exposed | item)

Implementation: (1) add an exposure-prediction task (random-negative BCE) to the multi-objective retrieval model, loss Σ BCE(P) + w · BCE(Exposed); (2) at inference, rank by score = exposure_and_positive_logit − γ · exposure_logit, where γ ∈ [0, 1] is a tunable knob (γ = 0 recovers the biased baseline, γ = 1 is full exposure normalization). γ is grid-searched offline and re-tuned online against business metrics.

Key Results주요 결과
  • Online A/B in a production video recommender: +25% uniquely retrieved items and −40% dominance of over-popular content, with overall engagement maintained.
  • Offline, exposure-aware scoring at γ = 0.65: positive recall@1000 = 31.7% vs 29.8% (γ = 0 baseline) and vs 26.0% (single-task OPC baseline); negative recall@1000 = 9.9% vs 11.0% (γ = 0) and 8.7% (OPC).
  • Full correction γ = 1.0 drops positive recall to 28.0% (negative recall 7.9%) — the fairness-engagement trade-off is non-monotone in γ, motivating the tunable-knob design.

Contribution vs Claim기여 대 주장
  • Stated claims: real-time control over exposure dynamics; decoupling exposure from engagement; "domain-agnostic and applicable to music, news, and e-commerce"; "a new paradigm for bias-aware personalization."
  • Demonstrated results: one production video platform, one offline table (4 configurations, 2 metrics, no variance), two online headline percentages.
  • Gap: domain-agnosticism is asserted, evaluated only on video. The claim that the method "decouples" exposure from engagement is only partially exercised — the deployed γ = 0.65 keeps 35% of the exposure signal in the score, so what ships is a damped joint score, not the clean conditional probability. No comparison against ranking-stage IPS in their own system.
Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

Note: the paper is a 3-page industry short paper; the skim covered the full text, so concept definitions below are complete rather than [SKIM]-flagged.

Exposure-aware retrieval score (logit-space exposure subtraction)

  • What it computes: input = an item's retrieval logit for P(positive label & exposed | item) and a separately learned exposure logit for P(exposed | item); operation = score = exposure_and_positive_logit − γ · exposure_logit at inference time (subtraction in logit space = division in probability space); output = a retrieval ranking score approximating P(positive label | item, exposed).
  • Why it was created: standard retrieval models rank by the joint P(positive & exposed | item), which conflates relevance with how often the closed feedback loop has already shown the item — so popular items dominate regardless of per-exposure quality. Prior fixes (IPS at ranking, OPC at training) offer no inference-time control knob.
  • Concrete example: item v1 exposed 40 times with 20 positives, item v2 exposed 10 times with 7 positives. Joint-probability ranking favors v1; conditional-on-exposure ranking favors v2 (0.7 vs 0.5 positive rate per exposure).
  • How it is used in this paper: it is the core contribution — applied at retrieval-stage inference in a production video recommender, with γ grid-searched offline (best 0.65) and re-tuned online.

Exposure modeling task (auxiliary training head)

  • What it computes: input = (user, content, context) features and randomly sampled negatives; operation = an additional binary task trained jointly with the engagement objective, total loss Σ BCE(P) + w · BCE(Exposed) where P = positive engagement signals (views, likes, shares); output = the exposure logit P(exposed | item) consumed by the inference-time score above.
  • Why it was created: to obtain an explicit, separable estimate of exposure probability inside a modern multi-objective retrieval model, rather than folding the correction into training (as OPC does) where it cannot be adjusted afterward.
  • Concrete example: the multi-objective retrieval model in Figure 1 has heads Pctr, Pview, Plike, ..., Pexposure combined by weighted multiplication.
  • How it is used in this paper: trained as one head of the production multi-objective retrieval model; its logit is the subtracted term at inference.
Implementation Feasibility구현 가능성
  • Needs: impression (exposure) logs per item; ability to add a BCE head to an existing multi-objective retrieval model; a grid-search loop for γ. Compute cost is marginal over the base model (one extra logit; the correction is a subtraction at inference).
  • Code: not available (industry paper, no repository).
  • Modifications: for a fixed-model post-hoc setting, replace the learned exposure head with an empirical exposure-frequency estimate from logs and apply the same logit subtraction — untested by the paper. [SKIM — no appendix exists to check]
  • Unknowns: missing link: how the exposure task's random-negative sampling interacts with the engagement task's negatives; missing link: online A/B duration, traffic fraction, and significance; missing link: sensitivity of γ across domains.

Condition Delta — transfer to our setting조건 차이 — 우리 설정으로의 전이

Paper's setting: inputs = logged user interactions (views/likes/shares) plus impression logs from a closed-loop production video recommender at Meta scale (billions of items); outputs = retrieval-stage candidate ranking; conditions = access to per-item exposure logs, ability to add a training head to the retrieval model, and online A/B infrastructure. Our setting (SP2 — post-hoc exposure control over a fixed model, exposure frequency as a ranking proxy): inputs = a fixed already-trained ranking/retrieval model plus exposure-frequency statistics; outputs = an adjusted ranking that controls for exposure at inference; conditions = no retraining assumed, no live A/B loop necessarily available. Differences:

  • Requires an auxiliary exposure head trained jointly with the model — our SP2 setting assumes a fixed model with no retraining: missing link (the paper's exposure logit could in principle be replaced by an external exposure-frequency estimate P(exposed | item) computed from logs alone, but the paper never tests a fully post-hoc variant).
  • γ tuned via online A/B against business metrics — our setting may lack a live loop: missing link (offline γ selection via the recall grid is shown, so partially resolved: grid-search offline as in Table 1).
  • Retrieval stage (recall-oriented, random negatives) vs ranking-stage proxy use in parts of our SP: reference only (the paper explicitly argues IPS-style ranking corrections fail at retrieval due to sparsity — useful as a boundary claim).
  • Production scale with exposure logs available — resolved: exposure counts per item are the only extra data the inference-time correction needs.
Root-cause vs. Symptom근본 원인 대 증상

Mixed — the factorization P(positive | item, exposed) = P(positive & exposed | item) / P(exposed | item) targets the root cause (the joint objective conflates exposure with relevance), but the deployed system retreats to a business-metric-tuned partial correction (γ = 0.65 < 1) and never resolves why full correction hurts positive recall, so the residual bias is managed as a tunable symptom rather than explained.
