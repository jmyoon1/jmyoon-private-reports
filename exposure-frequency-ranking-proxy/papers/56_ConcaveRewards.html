Contextual bandits with concave rewards, and an application to fair ranking

Do et al. · ICLR 2023 · 2023 · 5 citations

arXiv:2210.09957 · PDF · SP: SP6

P0Track A

한 줄 요약

오목 보상 contextual bandit으로 공정 ranking을 다루며, 조사 전체에서 유일하게 position 가중 exposure 단위의 regret 상한을 제공한다(목표 측에 한함).

Problem해결하려는 문제

Multi-objective contextual bandits where the trade-off is a known concave function $f$ of the vector of average rewards had no algorithm with vanishing regret in the general (unrestricted-policy) setting: policies are mappings from a continuous context space to action distributions, so the direct policy-space optimization used in non-contextual BCR (Agrawal & Devanur 2014) or tabular RL (Cheung 2019) is inapplicable, and the only prior contextual result (Agrawal et al. 2016) requires a finite policy set. In particular, for fairness of exposure in contextual ranking bandits, only heuristics without regret guarantees existed.

Solution / Contribution해결책 / 기여

A reduction: run Frank-Wolfe in the space of feasible expected rewards $\mathcal{S}$ (unknown, but never explicitly needed). At each step, the FW gradient $g_t = \nabla f(\hat{s}_{t-1})$ defines a scalar-reward contextual bandit with reward $\tilde{r}_t = \langle g_t | r_t\rangle$; any scalar contextual bandit algorithm with sublinear scalar regret $R^{scal}_T$ yields CBCR regret

$$R_T = f^* - f(\hat{s}_T) \le \frac{R^{scal}_T + LD_\mathcal{K}\sqrt{2T\ln(1/\delta)} + \tilde{C}\ln(eT)}{T}$$

(Theorem 2, smooth $f$; Theorem 3 extends to nonsmooth $f$ via Moreau-Yosida smoothing with time-decreasing factor $\beta_0/\sqrt{t+1}$). Instantiations: FW-LinUCB (linear rewards), FW-SquareCB (general rewards via online regression oracles — the first FW-based bandit approach not tied to optimism), and FW-LinUCBRank for fair ranking, whose per-step cost is dominated by a top-$\bar{k}$ sort of UCB scores plus an adaptive $\nabla f$ bonus that boosts under-exposed items.

Key Results주요 결과
  • Theorem 2: $R_T \le (R^{scal}_T + LD_\mathcal{K}\sqrt{2T\ln(1/\delta)} + \tilde{C}\ln(eT))/T$ — any sublinear scalar-regret bandit gives vanishing CBCR regret; near minimax-optimal since worst-case contextual bandit regret is $\Omega(\sqrt{T})$.
  • FW-LinUCBRank (fair ranking, Theorem 5): $R_T \le O(d\bar{k}\ln(T/\delta)/\sqrt{T})$ w.h.p. — the first regret guarantee for contextual combinatorial bandits with fairness of exposure.
  • Last.fm music recommendation ($m=50$ items, $\bar{k}=10$ slots): FW-LinUCBRank reaches the best objective values vs. LinUCBRank, Unbiased-LinUCBRank, and FairLearn; Unbiased-LinUCBRank degrades after ~$10^4$ rounds because it does not converge to an optimal utility/fairness trade-off.
  • At convergence ($5\times10^6$ rounds): FW-LinUCBRank achieves higher user utility than FairLearn at all inequality levels, including near-zero unfairness at little utility cost.
  • Multi-armed CBCR (synthetic, Mehrotra et al. environments): FW-SquareCB comparable to MOLinCB, converging in ~100 rounds — with theoretical guarantees MOLinCB lacks.
5Cs Analysis5Cs 분석

Category: new method (theoretical reduction + algorithms with regret guarantees). Context: extends BCR (Agrawal & Devanur 2014) and FW-bandit analyses (Berthet & Perchet 2017) to contexts; builds on LinUCB (Abbasi-Yadkori et al. 2011), SquareCB (Foster & Rakhlin 2020), position-based ranking bandits (Lagrée et al. 2016; Li et al. 2016); fairness objectives from Do et al. 2021, Morik et al. 2020. Correctness: [SKIM] — proofs are in appendices C–J; regret bounds stated with explicit high-probability confidence terms; experiments repeated over 100 (synthetic) / 10 (Last.fm) seeds. Contributions: (1) first vanishing-regret algorithm for CBCR without policy-space restriction, via the FW-to-scalar-bandit reduction; (2) reduction works with any exploration principle, not just UCB; (3) first regret-guaranteed algorithm for contextual combinatorial bandits with fairness of exposure (FW-LinUCBRank), computationally efficient (top-k sort) and working without knowing context-dependent position weights $b(x_t)$. Clarity: main-paper algorithm boxes (Alg. 1) plus appendix pseudocode; reproducible in principle — experiments "fully implemented in Python 3.9" but no public code link visible in the skimmed pages [SKIM - check for code release].

Contribution vs Claim기여 대 주장

[SKIM - verify in deep read] Headline claims (first algorithm for unrestricted-policy CBCR; first fairness-of-exposure guarantee for contextual ranking bandits) are theorem-backed in the skimmed pages. Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

Contextual Bandits with Concave Rewards (CBCR)

  • What it computes: at each step t, observe context $x_t \sim P$, choose action $a_t \in \mathcal{A}$, receive a noisy D-dimensional reward vector $r_t$ with $\mathbb{E}[r_t|x_t,a_t]=\mu(x_t)a_t$; the goal is to minimize $R_T = f^* - f(\hat{s}_T)$ where $\hat{s}_T = \frac{1}{T}\sum_t r_t$ and $f$ is a known concave trade-off function over the D cumulative rewards.
  • Why it was created: prior Bandits-with-Concave-Rewards (BCR, Agrawal & Devanur 2014) has no contexts, so it cannot model per-user personalization; the only prior contextual work (Agrawal et al. 2016) is restricted to a finite policy set.
  • Concrete example: fair ranking with $m$ items gives $D = m+1$ objectives — each item's cumulative exposure plus user utility — combined as $f(z) = z_{m+1} - \beta\frac{1}{2m}\sum_{i,j}|z_i - z_j|$ (utility minus Gini-style exposure inequality).
  • How it is used in this paper: it is the paper's problem definition; everything else is an algorithm/reduction for it.

Frank-Wolfe reduction to scalar-reward bandits

  • What it computes: reparameterizes CBCR as concave maximization over the convex set $\mathcal{S}$ of feasible expected rewards, then shows (Lemma 1) that the Frank-Wolfe linear subproblem $\max_{s\in\mathcal{S}}\langle\nabla f(\hat{s}_{t-1})|s\rangle$ equals in conditional expectation the per-step action problem $\max_{a}\langle\nabla f(\hat{s}_{t-1})|\mu(x_t)a\rangle$. This defines a scalar-reward bandit with extended context $\tilde{x}_t=(\nabla f(\hat{s}_{t-1}), x_t)$ and scalar reward $\tilde{r}_t=\langle\nabla f(\hat{s}_{t-1})|r_t\rangle$; Theorem 2 bounds $R_T \le (R^{scal}_T + LD_\mathcal{K}\sqrt{2T\ln(1/\delta)} + \tilde{C}\ln(eT))/T$.
  • Why it was created: direct optimization in policy space is intractable when policies map a continuous context space to action distributions; the reduction bypasses policy space entirely.
  • Concrete example: plugging in LinUCB gives FW-LinUCB with $R^{scal}_T = O(d\sqrt{T})$, hence $R_T = O(1/\sqrt{T})$.
  • How it is used in this paper: the central theoretical tool; instantiated as FW-LinUCB, FW-SquareCB, and FW-LinUCBRank.

Fairness of exposure (amortized, in the position-based model) [SKIM - the position-based observation model details are in Sec. 4; verify feedback assumptions in deep read]

  • What it computes: each item i's reward-component is its exposure probability $\sum_k a_{i,k} b_k(x)$ under ranking $a$ with position weights $b(x)$; fairness objectives penalize inequality of these exposures accumulated over time.
  • Why it was created: prior fair-exposure bandit work either ignored rankings, ignored contexts, or (Mansoury et al. 2021; Jeunen & Goethals 2021) was heuristic without guarantees.
  • Concrete example: DCG-style weights $b_k = 1/\log_2(1+k)$ for the top $\bar{k}=10$ slots, 0 below.
  • How it is used in this paper: defines the combinatorial CBCR instance solved by FW-LinUCBRank (Algorithm 1).
Implementation Feasibility구현 가능성
  • Needs: any scalar contextual bandit (LinUCB / SquareCB / ε-greedy) plus a running average $\hat{s}_t$ and gradient of the chosen $f$; per-step cost of FW-LinUCBRank is dominated by a top-$\bar{k}$ sort — computationally light. Ridge regression with $\lambda\ge D_\mathcal{X}^2\bar{k}$. For nonsmooth $f$ (Gini), gradients of the Moreau envelope via Do & Usunier (2022).
  • Code: not linked in the skimmed pages [SKIM - check appendix/OpenReview for a repository]; experiments in Python 3.9 with the Implicit library on public Last.fm data.
  • Modifications: for an exposure-frequency-proxy study, exposure indicators would have to be simulated or position-inferred; the fairness objective $f$ would need to be replaced or elicited for the target trade-off.
  • Unknowns: missing link: robustness when position weights b(x) are misspecified or exposure feedback e_{t,i} is unobserved; missing link: behavior under exposure→preference feedback loops (explicitly out of scope).
Follow-up Papers관련 후속 논문
  • Usunier, Do & Dohmatob, "Fast online ranking with fairness of exposure" (FAccT 2022) — same group's amortized-exposure FW machinery in the full-information setting; direct precursor.
  • Jeunen & Goethals, "Top-k contextual bandits with equity of exposure" (RecSys 2021) — the heuristic contextual-ranking-fairness baseline this paper supersedes with guarantees.
  • Mansoury et al., "Unbiased cascade bandits: mitigating exposure bias in online learning to rank" (2021) — exposure-bias mitigation module used here as the Unbiased-LinUCBRank baseline; directly relevant to SP6's exposure-as-proxy framing.
  • Zhu & Mineiro (ICML 2022) / Zhu et al. (ICML 2022) — SquareCB extensions to large/continuous action spaces the authors note plug into their reduction.
Condition Delta — transfer to our setting조건 차이 — 우리 설정으로의 전이

Paper's setting: inputs = per-round user/item feature contexts (linear reward model $v_i(x)=\theta^\top x_i$), position-based exposure model with binary exposure ($e_{t,i}$) and click ($c_{t,i}$) feedback; outputs = a ranking (permutation matrix) per round; conditions = known concave trade-off $f$ chosen by the designer, stochastic i.i.d. contexts, online interaction over $T$ up to $5\times10^6$ rounds. Our setting (SP6 — exposure frequency as a ranking proxy): analyzing how accumulated exposure frequency acts as (or distorts) a relevance/ranking signal, and how exposure should be allocated when the ranking system itself controls what gets observed. Differences:

  • The paper controls amortized exposure directly as reward components with a fairness objective — it treats exposure as an allocation target, whereas SP6 treats exposure frequency as a measurement proxy: reference only (conceptual anchor for principled exposure allocation, not a drop-in method).
  • Requires explicit per-item exposure observation $e_{t,i}$: in typical logged-ranking settings exposure is inferred from position, not observed: missing link.
  • Requires a designer-specified concave $f$ (trade-off parameter $\beta$); our setting has no canonical $f$: missing link (the paper itself flags preference elicitation for $f$ as open).
  • Linear reward assumption for item values: resolved: FW-SquareCB variant handles general reward functions via any online regression oracle.
  • i.i.d. stochastic contexts, no feedback loops between exposure and future user behavior — the paper explicitly defers feedback-loop effects (citing Jiang et al. 2019): missing link for SP6, where exposure→popularity feedback is central.
Root-cause vs. Symptom근본 원인 대 증상

Direct — the paper identifies the exact structural obstacle (policy space over a continuous context set is not amenable to optimization) and removes it by reparameterizing into reward space, with a proved reduction theorem; the fair-ranking guarantee follows from the same mechanism rather than from patching a fairness metric onto an existing ranker.
