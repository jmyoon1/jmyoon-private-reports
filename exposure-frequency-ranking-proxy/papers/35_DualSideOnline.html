Model-Agnostic Dual-Side Online Fairness Learning for Dynamic Recommendation

Haoran Tang et al. · IEEE TKDE 2025 · 2025 · 8 citations

link · SP: SP3

P2Track B

한 줄 요약

모델에 구애받지 않는 dual-side 온라인 공정성 학습으로 동적 추천에서 양측 공정성을 조정한다. 공정성 개입이 정확도를 올릴 수 있다는 double-win 주장의 한 출처.

Problem해결하려는 문제

Fairness-aware recommendation methods are predominantly offline: they consume the entire stationary interaction dataset, correct a global unfairness measure, and are evaluated in a one-time paradigm. In deployed systems users interact continuously, so unfairness evolves online and a one-shot offline correction goes stale. Additionally, existing fairness interventions typically trade away recommendation accuracy rather than improving it.

Solution / Contribution해결책 / 기여

[SKIM - verify in deep read] From the abstract, the method has three components:

  1. Dynamic dual-side fairness learning — traces the evolution of unfairness from both the user side and the item side as interactions stream in.
  2. Unified framework — couples the fairness task and the recommendation task in one objective, targeting simultaneous improvement of both ("double-win").
  3. Model-agnostic post-ranking — an efficient re-ranking stage attachable to any existing dynamic recommendation model, operating on that model's outputs without modifying the base scorer.

Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

MDOFair (Model-agnostic Dual-side Online Fairness Learning)

  • What it computes: scores from any existing dynamic recommendation model (read-only input) → an online-learned post-ranking adjustment that traces evolving unfairness on both the user side and the item side → a re-ranked recommendation list per time step. [SKIM - verify in deep read: exact update rule and re-ranking objective require the methods section]
  • Why it was created: prior fairness-aware recommenders are trained offline on the full stationary interaction log and evaluated once, so they cannot track unfairness that evolves as users interact continuously; and prior mitigation methods rarely improve (often hurt) recommendation accuracy.
  • Concrete example: [SKIM - no numerical instance available from abstract; closed-access PDF]
  • How it is used in this paper: incorporated into existing dynamic recommendation models as a post-ranking stage; evaluated for both unfairness mitigation and recommendation-accuracy improvement.

Dual-side (user + item) dynamic fairness

  • What it computes: interaction stream over time → tracking of two unfairness signals simultaneously — how information access is distributed across users (user side) and how exposure is distributed across items (item side) → time-varying fairness state fed to the post-ranker. [SKIM - verify: exact fairness metrics on each side unknown from abstract]
  • Why it was created: most prior work handles one side only (item exposure fairness or user fairness), and treats unfairness as a global, static quantity rather than one that evolves online.
  • Concrete example: [SKIM]
  • How it is used in this paper: the core learning signal that the online post-ranking stage optimizes jointly with recommendation utility ("double-win").

Condition Delta — transfer to our setting조건 차이 — 우리 설정으로의 전이

Paper's setting: inputs = score lists / outputs of an arbitrary dynamic (time-evolving) recommendation model plus the streaming interaction log; outputs = re-ranked top-k lists per time step; conditions = base scorer treated as a black box (scores read-only), fairness tracked online on both user and item sides. Our setting (SP3, O2/O1): interventions that treat ranking scores as read-only inputs and control item exposure frequency purely via re-ranking, with explicit labeling of scorer-access assumptions (score-free / score-read-only / score-modifying). Differences:

  • Scorer access: paper is explicitly model-agnostic post-ranking over a black-box scorer → resolved: directly instantiates the score-read-only access label for the O2 taxonomy.
  • Objective: paper optimizes dual-side fairness (user + item), while our focus is item exposure frequency specifically → resolved: item-side exposure fairness is one of its two tracked sides [SKIM - verify the item-side metric is exposure-based].
  • Temporal regime: paper is online/dynamic (per-step re-ranking as unfairness evolves); much of our SP3 corpus is static one-shot re-ranking → reference only: adds the online axis to the taxonomy.
  • Claimed accuracy improvement (not just constrained loss) under read-only access → missing link: mechanism by which post-ranking improves accuracy is not explained in the abstract.
Root-cause vs. Symptom근본 원인 대 증상

Mixed [SKIM - verify] — it directly addresses the root cause of staleness in fairness interventions (offline one-shot correction vs. online evolving unfairness) by learning online, but as a post-ranking layer it patches the base scorer's biased outputs downstream rather than resolving why the scorer produces unfair exposure in the first place — which, for SP3's purposes, is exactly the intended intervention point rather than a defect.
