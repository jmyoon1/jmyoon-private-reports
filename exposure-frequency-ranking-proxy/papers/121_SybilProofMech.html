On Sybil-proof Mechanisms

Pan et al. · arXiv · 2024 · 2 citations

arXiv:2407.14485 · PDF · SP: SP11

P4Track B

한 줄 요약

ex-post Sybil-proofness가 대칭 단조 배분 규칙 전체를 second-price 경매로 붕괴시킨다는 2024년 유일성 정리. 최상위 신호 보유자 밖으로 배분을 퍼뜨리는 모든 규칙(복권, 그리고 구조적으로 exposure floor)이 Sybil 증폭을 부른다 — 단 모든 정리가 지불(payment) 의존적이고 one-shot이라 exposure 설정으로 그대로 이전되지는 않는다.

Problem해결하려는 문제

Mechanism designers often want allocation rules other than pure highest-bidder auctions — lotteries, capped-quantity underpriced sales, randomized proposer selection — to serve fairness or decentralization goals (e.g., underpriced concert tickets, blockchain block-proposal rights). In permissionless / cheap-identity environments these mechanisms are targets for Sybil attacks (middlemen with many fake accounts in ticket sales; identity-splitting in blockchain allocation). Prior work (Yokoo et al. 2004) proved no Pareto-efficient false-name-proof combinatorial auction exists, but left open whether non-efficient distributional rules (which drop Pareto efficiency) could be simultaneously incentive compatible and Sybil-proof in the basic single-parameter setting.

Solution / Contribution해결책 / 기여

Theoretical characterization (Theorem 1): in the Myersonian single-parameter environment (quasi-linear utility $U_i = v_i x_i - p_i$, Myerson payments $p_j(v) = v_j x_j(v_j, v_{-j}) - \int_0^{v_j} x_j(z, v_{-j})\,dz$), the only allocation rule satisfying payment normalization + non-wastefulness + symmetry + monotonicity (⇔ IC) + Sybil-proofness is: allocate everything to the highest-value bidder with uniform tie-breaking (equal sharing among ties if the good is divisible) — i.e., the second price auction. Sybil-proofness collapses the entire space of monotone symmetric rules to this single rule. [SKIM - proof mechanics not read]

Extensions: holds beyond linear valuations (Prop. 4); fails for multi-unit allocation with unit-demand bidders (Prop. 3 — a possibility result there); a revelation principle with Sybils rules out indirect-mechanism escapes (Section 5); and the impossibility does NOT extend to Bayesian Sybil-proofness — Theorem 2 exhibits a threshold-lottery-with-auction-fallback mechanism that is Bayesian Sybil-proof with higher interim bidder payoff, breaking the classical DSIC≡BIC equivalence under Sybil constraints.

Key Results주요 결과
  • Theorem 1: second price auction with symmetric tie-breaking is the unique non-wasteful, symmetric, IC, Sybil-proof direct mechanism in the single-parameter setting — lotteries and any rule not always allocating to a highest-value bidder are ruled out. [SKIM - exact statement of tie-breaking/divisible case per pp. 2]
  • Theorem 2: a threshold-lottery mechanism (lottery among bidders above a threshold, auction fallback) satisfies all axioms with Bayesian Sybil-proofness and yields strictly higher interim bidder payoff than a second price auction — DSIC≡BIC equivalence fails under Sybil constraints. [SKIM]
  • Proposition 3: the impossibility does not extend to multi-unit allocation with unit-demand bidders (a possibility result). [SKIM]
  • No empirical results — pure theory paper, no benchmarks.

Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

Sybil-proofness (this paper's weak ex-post version)

  • What it computes: an axiom on direct mechanisms — for every bidder set N, value profile v, bidder i, and one extra fake identity j with arbitrary bid u: bidder i's utility from truthful solo participation must be ≥ the combined utility from participating truthfully plus operating one Sybil j (allocation shares of i and j summed, payments of both subtracted).
  • Why it was created: prior literature used "false-name-proofness" (Yokoo et al. 2004), which bundles incentive compatibility with Sybil-immunity against arbitrary misreports and arbitrarily many Sybils. The authors want the weakest possible ex-post axiom (truthful original report, one Sybil) so their impossibility theorem is as strong as possible — it then holds a fortiori for all stronger notions.
  • Concrete example: in a uniform lottery over participants, a bidder with value 10 among 2 participants gets expected share 1/2; creating one free Sybil raises the combined share to 2/3 — the lottery violates the axiom.
  • How it is used in this paper: it is the fifth axiom (with payment normalization, non-wastefulness, symmetry, incentive compatibility) whose joint satisfaction is shown to force the second price auction.

Bayesian Sybil-proofness [SKIM - verify in deep read]

  • What it computes: a weakening — no bidder gains in expectation (at equilibrium, others truthful and Sybil-free) from creating additional Sybils, rather than for every ex-post value profile.
  • Why it was created: to escape the main impossibility result and re-open the design space for distributional goals (lotteries).
  • Concrete example: Theorem 2's mechanism — a lottery among bidders above a value threshold, falling back to an auction if none exceed the threshold — attains higher interim bidder payoff than a second price auction while remaining Bayesian Sybil-proof.
  • How it is used in this paper: the existence example proving that dominant-strategy and Bayesian implementation are no longer equivalent once Sybil constraints are added.

Revelation principle with Sybils [SKIM - verify in deep read]

  • What it computes: for every symmetric indirect mechanism implementing an objective in dominant strategies in the presence of Sybils, the induced direct mechanism is Sybil-proof and incentive compatible — so restricting attention to direct mechanisms is without loss.
  • Why it was created: to close the loophole that indirect mechanisms might evade the direct-mechanism impossibility.
  • Concrete example: [SKIM - see Section 5]
  • How it is used in this paper: Section 5, extending Theorem 1 from direct to all mechanisms.

Follow-up Papers관련 후속 논문

Condition Delta — transfer to our setting조건 차이 — 우리 설정으로의 전이

Paper's setting: single-parameter auction — inputs = reported scalar values from a variable, unverified-identity bidder population; outputs = allocation shares + Myerson payments for one unit of a private good; conditions = quasi-linear utility, free Sybil creation, dominant-strategy (then Bayesian) solution concepts. Our setting (SP11, exposure-frequency-as-ranking-proxy survey): exposure/impression frequency used as a proxy signal for ranking or reward allocation — an environment where cheap fake identities (Sybil accounts) can inflate frequency counts to game exposure-based allocation, with no explicit payments and typically repeated/online interaction. Differences:

  • Money/payments: the uniqueness result is driven by Myerson payments; exposure-ranking systems are usually payment-free — the theorem does not directly transfer. missing link
  • One-shot allocation of a single unit vs. repeated exposure slots over time: missing link
  • Conceptual import — "any allocation rule that deviates from rewarding the top signal-holder invites Sybil amplification" is the transferable insight for why frequency-proxy rewards (which spread allocation across many identities) are structurally Sybil-gameable: resolved: use as theoretical grounding for SP11's Sybil-vulnerability argument, cite as impossibility anchor
  • Bayesian relaxation (Theorem 2) suggests population-level/statistical Sybil-resistance may be achievable where per-profile resistance is not — relevant to statistical anti-abuse defenses in ranking: reference only [SKIM - verify]
Root-cause vs. Symptom근본 원인 대 증상

Direct — the paper attacks the root cause head-on: rather than patching Sybil exploits case by case, it characterizes exactly which allocation rules are structurally immune (only the second price auction, ex-post) and proves where the boundary of possibility lies (Bayesian relaxation, multi-unit case).
