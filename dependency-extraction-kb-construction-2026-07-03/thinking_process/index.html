PHASE 0b — DECOMPOSITION Dependency extraction & linking for incremental KB construction 2026-07-03 14:11 KST
Objectives → SP mapping

  • O1 (extract dependency signals from a new paper's text/citations) → SP1 (citation intent/function classification), SP2 (scientific concept/relation extraction), SP3 (concept prerequisite learning, secondary), SP5 (related-work generation/extension detection), SP6 (agent pipelines, cross-cutting)
  • O2 (link signals to existing KB nodes or spawn new ones) → SP3 (concept prerequisite learning, primary), SP4 (scientific entity/concept linking), SP5 (novelty/extension detection), SP6 (agent pipelines), SP7 (ontology/taxonomy alignment)
  • O3 (maintain global DAG consistency as it grows) → SP6 (agent pipelines, cross-cutting), SP8 (incremental KG construction/maintenance), SP9 (link prediction/completion for hierarchical graphs)

SP set (9 sub-problems)

  • SP1 — Citation intent and function classification: why a paper cites another (background/method-use/extension/comparison) is the primary derivation-lineage signal.
  • SP2 — Scientific concept and relation extraction: pulls candidate prerequisite-concept signals out of body text before any linking step.
  • SP3 — Concept prerequisite learning and prediction: defines the prerequisite-relation task itself, with benchmarks and matching criteria.
  • SP4 — Scientific entity and concept linking: core mechanism for matching extracted signals to existing KB nodes vs. new nodes.
  • SP5 — Related work generation and extension/novelty detection: generates derivation-lineage edges and informs new-node-vs-extension calls.
  • SP6 — LLM-agent and RAG pipelines for KG construction: cross-cutting, demonstrates end-to-end agent pipelines matching this workspace's architecture.
  • SP7 — Ontology and taxonomy alignment: structural-level matching/confidence criteria above single-entity linking.
  • SP8 — Incremental knowledge graph construction and maintenance: how to update a KG as new nodes/edges arrive while preserving consistency.
  • SP9 — Link prediction and completion for hierarchical/DAG graphs: detecting missing/redundant edges and validating DAG structure without full retraining.

Ranking rationale

SP1 is ranked first because citation-intent classification is the most direct, longest-established signal source for O1 and anchors the vocabulary (background/method/extends/compares) that later SPs (SP5, SP6) reuse. The five P1-objective SPs (SP1–SP5) are front-loaded ahead of the cross-cutting agent-pipeline SP6, so that by the time SP6 is surveyed, the loop already has concrete extraction and linking techniques to check against modern LLM-agent pipelines rather than the reverse. The two P2-only SPs (SP8, SP9, DAG maintenance) are ranked last since O3 is priority 2 and the convergence loop may exit before reaching them if O1/O2 saturate early — SP6 was deliberately placed at rank 6, before SP7/8/9, because it targets all three objectives and its findings are expected to reshape queries for the remaining graph-maintenance SPs.

Field assumptions

The ranker assumed: (1) citation intent/function classification and concept-prerequisite learning are mature enough sub-fields to have standard benchmarks (SciCite, ACL-ARC, LectureBank/LectureBankCD) that will return non-trivial hit counts; (2) LLM-agent/RAG pipelines for KG construction is a fast-moving but real category with dedicated papers (not just blog posts) by 2022–2026; (3) DAG-aware link prediction/completion is a genuine sub-topic distinct from generic KG completion, worth its own SP rather than folding into SP8. Phase 1 search results will confirm or contradict these before synthesis begins.
