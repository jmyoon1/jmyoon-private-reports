Ranking with Long-Term Constraints

Brantley et al. · arXiv · 2023

arXiv:2307.04923 · PDF · SP: SP5

P4Track C

한 줄 요약

FairCo류 '휴리스틱' P-제어기가 누적 목표 대비 추적 오차를 승수로 갖는 Stationary Controller(Lagrangian dual)의 특수case임을 증명한다. 반응형 amortized 공정성 = dual 변수 pacing이라는 통일의 핵심 고리.

Problem해결하려는 문제

Ranking systems trained myopically on choice data (clicks, purchases) optimize per-request engagement, and per-request optimization cannot even express long-term goals — cumulative exposure guarantees for content providers, demographic parity over an ad's lifetime, per-user caps on push messages. Prior long-term work is either fairness-specific (constraints within a single ranking, or cumulative-fairness algorithms like Morik et al.) or full RL, which is hard to apply at IR scale and obscures decision points (reward hacking, user tampering).

Solution / Contribution해결책 / 기여

[SKIM - verified through Section 3.2; Section 3.3+ and experiments unread] A macro/micro separation: decision-makers specify $C$ long-term constraints as cumulative exposure/impact targets $\sum_{t=1}^T V_c(y_t|x_t) \geq \tau_c$ over a horizon $T$; a closed-loop controller translates them into per-request rankings that maximize cumulative micro utility minus a terminal hinge-loss violation cost $\lambda^\top(\tau - s_T)_+$. Three controllers are derived: Myopic (per-step LP against a linearly scaled target), Stationary (Lagrangian/online-gradient multiplier = tracking error; generalizes the Morik et al. P-controller), and a Predictive controller using model-predictive-control ideas for non-stationary context streams. Utilities and constraints are linear in a permutation-matrix representation ($U = r^\top P u$, $V_c = a_c^\top P e$), so each per-step argmax is a linear program over doubly stochastic matrices.

Key Results주요 결과
  • All three controllers achieve stated long-term goals; they trade off efficiency, robustness, and planning ability (abstract-level claim). [SKIM]
  • MC satisfies macro goals at high micro-utility cost (overly conservative by construction — charges full violation each step).
  • SC with $u = e$ reduces to relevance-score boosting proportional to constraint tracking error, recovering Morik et al.'s P-controller as a special case.
  • Specific numbers from synthetic + real-world experiments: [SKIM - experiments section unread]

Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

Macro/micro control problem for ranking

  • What it computes: a sequence of contexts $x_1,\dots,x_T$ (queries/requests) → controller $\pi(x_t, s_{t-1}, t)$ selects a ranking $y_t$ per request → cumulative objective $\sum_t U(y_t|x_t) - \lambda^\top(\tau - \sum_t V(y_t|x_t))_+$, i.e. total micro-level utility (e.g. DCG) minus a hinge-loss penalty for missing cumulative macro targets $\tau$ at horizon $T$.
  • Why it was created: per-request ranking optimization cannot even express goals that span days/weeks/months (cumulative exposure guarantees, demographic-parity-over-lifetime); prior fairness work either enforced constraints within a single ranking or handled cumulative fairness only for specific criteria.
  • Concrete example: "give local artists at least $x\%$ of overall exposure over the next month" — a single scalar constraint over thousands of individual ranking decisions.
  • How it is used in this paper: it is the paper's central formalization (Section 2); all three controllers are derived to solve it.

State $s_t$ (constraint-progress accumulator)

  • What it computes: $s_t = \sum_{i=1}^{t} V(y_i|x_i)$ — running sum of per-ranking contributions toward each of the $C$ macro constraints.
  • Why it was created: future contexts are unknown at decision time, so the controller must act on current progress rather than the full-horizon constraint.
  • Concrete example: if constraint 1 is "1M exposures to new artist $a$ over a week" and $s_{t-1,1}=400{,}000$ mid-week, the controller sees a lag and boosts $a$.
  • How it is used in this paper: every controller is a function of $(x_t, s_{t-1}, t)$; the macro goal is met iff terminal state $s_T \geq \tau$.

Myopic Controller (MC)

  • What it computes: per step, $\arg\max_y U(y|x_t) - \lambda^\top(\frac{t}{T}\tau - s_{t-1} - V(y|x_t))_+$ — charges the full violation cost against a linearly scaled target at every step. Implemented as a linear program.
  • Why it was created: baseline that makes the sequential problem well-defined by treating each step as if it were final.
  • Concrete example: at $t = T/2$ it demands $s_t \geq \tau/2$ or pays the penalty immediately.
  • How it is used in this paper: baseline controller; shown to satisfy constraints at high cost to micro-level utility (overly conservative).

Stationary Controller (SC)

  • What it computes: alternates Lagrangian steps — $y_t = \arg\max_y U(y|x_t) - \mu_{t-1}^\top V(y|x_t)$ with multiplier update $\mu_t = (\eta(\frac{t}{T}\tau - s_t))_{[0,\lambda]}$ (online gradient descent on the multiplier, clipped to $[0,\lambda]$). The multiplier is exactly the step-size-scaled tracking error between scaled target and current state.
  • Why it was created: MC's per-step full-violation charge is too strict; online convex programming lets the violation cost be learned dynamically, appropriate for i.i.d./stationary context distributions.
  • Concrete example: when $u = e$ (utility and exposure position weights coincide), SC reduces to $\text{argsort}(r_t + \eta A_t^\top(\frac{t-1}{T}\tau - s_{t-1})_{[0,\lambda]})$ — sorting by relevance scores boosted proportionally to constraint lag.
  • How it is used in this paper: main controller for stationary settings; the paper shows the previously heuristic P-controller of Morik et al. (fairness-constrained ranking) is a special case.

Predictive Controller [SKIM - verify in deep read]

  • One-line: a model-predictive-control-style controller that plans ahead for non-stationary context distributions with temporal patterns (Section 3.3, beyond skimmed pages). Details of its planning model and update rule need the deep read.

Standard background relied on (not new here): position-based model (PBM) of exposure, DCG position weights, doubly stochastic matrices with Birkhoff–von Neumann decomposition for sampling rankings from a relaxed policy.

Condition Delta — transfer to our setting조건 차이 — 우리 설정으로의 전이

Paper's setting: inputs = a stream of ranking requests (contexts with known relevances $r_t$ and constraint-membership $A_t$; full-information setting with modeled metrics like PBM exposure); outputs = a ranking per request; conditions = horizon $T$ known, targets $\tau$ and violation costs $\lambda$ exogenously specified by a decision-maker, position weights known, constraints linear in the permutation matrix. Our setting: exposure/impression frequency treated as a proxy signal in ranking systems — asking when cumulative exposure counts can stand in for (or must be corrected toward) the quantity a ranking system actually intends to control. Differences:

  • Paper assumes full information (true relevances, known group membership); production rankers have learned, biased relevance estimates — the paper explicitly defers learned-model interaction to future work: missing link
  • Exposure is modeled (PBM position weights assumed known) rather than measured from logged impressions; our proxy question concerns logged/observed frequency: [SKIM - verify] missing link
  • Targets $\tau$ are given exogenously; how to choose exposure targets that actually produce long-term platform health is out of scope: reference only
  • Amortized-over-horizon constraint accounting (state $s_t$ as cumulative exposure ledger) is directly the mechanism our SP needs for reasoning about frequency-based control: resolved — the framework gives the formal template
Root-cause vs. Symptom근본 원인 대 증상

Mixed — the paper directly solves the mechanism problem it poses (translating horizon-level exposure constraints into per-request rankings, with an exact Lagrangian treatment rather than heuristic boosting), but it takes the long-term targets themselves as exogenous and assumes modeled exposure with known position weights, leaving the causal link between exposure targets and actual platform health — the underlying reason the constraints exist — explicitly unaddressed.
