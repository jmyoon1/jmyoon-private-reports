Recommender Systems in the Era of Large Language Models (LLMs)

Zihuai Zhao et al. · IEEE TKDE 2024 · 2024 · 537 citations

arXiv:2307.02046 · PDF · SP: SP2

P1Track C

한 줄 요약

LLM 시대 recommender 연구 전반을 정리한 IEEE TKDE survey. LLM-era 프론티어의 landscape anchor로 사용된다.

Problem해결하려는 문제

DNN-based recommenders (CNN/RNN/GNN, BERT-scale PLMs) have three stated limits: (1) insufficient natural-language understanding of textual side information about users and items; (2) task-specific architectures that do not generalize to unseen recommendation tasks/scenarios; (3) no multi-step reasoning for complex decisions. Meanwhile, the LLM-for-RecSys literature exploded post-ChatGPT with no systematic review of the adaptation techniques (as opposed to application catalogs) — prior surveys covered only PLM-era methods or orthogonal splits.

Solution / Contribution해결책 / 기여

A comprehensive survey organized as: §2 milestones timeline for RecSys and LMs; §3 two representation routes (ID-based, e.g. P5 special-token indexing and RQ-VAE Semantic IDs, vs. textual side-information encoding); §4 pre-training & fine-tuning paradigms; §5 prompting paradigms (ICL, CoT, prompt tuning); §6 challenges and future directions. Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

This is a survey; it organizes rather than invents. The organizing taxonomy itself is the conceptual contribution, plus a few terms it canonicalizes for the field:

Pre-training / fine-tuning / prompting taxonomy for LLM-based RecSys

  • What it computes: takes the space of LLM-for-recommendation methods → partitions it by how the LLM's parameters or inputs are adapted → outputs three method families (pre-training on recommendation-format corpora; fine-tuning, incl. parameter-efficient; prompting/ICL/CoT with frozen LLMs)
  • Why it was created: prior surveys (Liu et al. on PLM-era methods; Wu et al.'s discriminative-vs-generative split; Lin et al.'s where/how split) reviewed applications but not the LLM-domain adaptation techniques systematically
  • Concrete example: P5 (T5 backbone, pre-trained on user-item interactions verbalized as text) sits in pre-training; TALLRec in fine-tuning; Chat-Rec (ChatGPT re-ranks a traditional RecSys's candidate set via conversation) in prompting
  • How it is used in this paper: it is the survey's section structure (§4 pre-training & fine-tuning, §5 prompting)

ID-based vs. textual-side-information-enhanced representation (§3)

  • What it computes: two routes for feeding users/items to an LLM — discrete ID tokens (e.g. P5's <item_6637> special tokens, or Semantic IDs: tuples of RQ-VAE codewords carrying content meaning) vs. encoding item/user text (titles, reviews, profiles) with the LM as feature encoder
  • Why it was created: pure ID indexing is semantically empty, fails under sparse interactions, and forces vocabulary/parameter changes in the LLM
  • Concrete example: Hua et al.'s four indexing schemes over P5 — sequential, collaborative, semantic (content-based), hybrid
  • How it is used in this paper: §3's two-way split of representation-learning approaches, feeding into the later paradigm sections

LLM-as-ranker/recommender task framings (Fig. 1): top-K recommendation, rating prediction, conversational recommendation, explanation generation — all cast as task-specific prompts to a frozen or tuned LLM. [SKIM - the detailed per-paradigm technique tables (Tables 1/2/3) need the deep read]

Condition Delta — transfer to our setting조건 차이 — 우리 설정으로의 전이

Paper's setting: inputs = the 2023–24 LLM-RecSys literature (methods using ChatGPT, LLaMA, T5, etc. for top-K, rating, conversational, explainable recommendation); outputs = taxonomy + challenges; conditions = recommendation domains (movies, e-commerce), user-item interaction data plus textual side information. Our setting (SP2): exposure frequency as a proxy for ranking quality — specifically the LLM-era re-ranking half: how LLM rankers/recommenders determine what gets exposed, and whether exposure frequency tracks true relevance under LLM-based ranking.
