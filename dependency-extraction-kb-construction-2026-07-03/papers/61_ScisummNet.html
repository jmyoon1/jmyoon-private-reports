ScisummNet: A Large Annotated Corpus and Content-Impact Models for Scientific Paper Summarization with Citation Networks

Michihiro Yasunaga et al. · AAAI 2019 · 2019 · 226 citations인용 226회

arXiv:1909.01716 · PDF · SP: SP5

P0Track A

Problem
해결하려는 문제

Scientific paper summarization lacked (1) a large manually-annotated training corpus — prior datasets (CL-SciSumm, TAC 2014) had only ~30 papers, too small for data-driven/neural methods — and (2) summaries that capture a paper's actual impact on the research community, not just the authors' self-described content. Abstracts miss impact; pure citation-based summaries miss authors' motivation.

논문의 실제 기여는 초록이 아니라 인용 문맥(citances)에 드러나며, 이를 추출·활용해야 한다.

Solution / Contribution
해결책 / 기여

Two contributions:

  1. ScisummNet corpus: 1,000 most-cited ACL Anthology Network (AAN) papers + their citation networks + expert gold summaries (avg 151 words). Fast annotation trick: annotators read abstract + citation sentences instead of full papers, cutting time to ~30% while covering >90% of major points (validated on 30-paper preliminary study; inter-annotator Kappa 0.75). ~600 person-hours total.
  2. Hybrid content-impact models: build input I = abstract ∪ cited text spans (~40 sentences), LSTM sentence encoding → tf-idf cosine similarity graph → 2-layer GCN → softmax salience (Eq. 2), trained with cross-entropy against ROUGE-1&2-derived target distribution (Eq. 3). Greedy non-redundant selection produces Hybrid 1 (extractive over I) or Hybrid 2 (augment abstract). Optional authority feature.

cited-text-span 국소화와 인용 수 authority 가중을 결합한 하이브리드 모델과 1,000편 AAN 코퍼스(ScisummNet)를 제공하여 재사용 가능한 span 국소화·edge 가중 primitive를 제시한다.

Key Results
주요 결과
  • Exp 1 (CL-SciSumm test, ROUGE): GCN Cited text spans trained on ScisummNet (1000) = 33.03 / 23.49 / 17.86 / 14.15 (2-R/2-F/3-F/SU4-F) vs. same model trained on CL-SciSumm (30) = 24.93/18.46/12.77/12.21 and best shared-task participant = 32.36/21.94/16.79/13.63. Corpus size gives large boosts (+5 ROUGE-3-F).
  • Exp 2 (all trained on ScisummNet): GCN Hybrid 2 w/ authority = 33.88/31.54/24.32/24.36 beats Abstract baseline (29.52/29.40/23.16/23.34) on all metrics; Hybrid 2 > Hybrid 1 > cited-text-spans-only.
  • Human eval of hybrid summaries: coverage 4.5/5, coherence 4.2/5 (5 = gold level).
Concepts Introduced도입된 개념

Citation sentence (citance)

  • What it computes: a sentence in a citing paper that references the reference paper (RP) → serves as a one-line summary of the RP written from the citing author's perspective. A collection of them reflects the RP's community impact.
  • Why it was created: the abstract conveys the authors' own view but may miss what the community actually found valuable (Fig. 1: Bergsma & Lin's abstract stresses pronoun-resolution technique, but citations reveal their noun gender dataset was the lasting contribution).
  • Concrete example: ~15 cleaned citation sentences per RP on average (sampled from 20, spanning oldest→latest citations).
  • How it is used in this paper: raw material for extracting cited text spans and building gold summaries.

Cited text spans

  • What it computes: for each citation sentence, the top-2 sentences in the RP most similar by tf-idf cosine similarity (stopwords excluded); union over all citations → set of RP sentences the community refers to.
  • Why it was created: citation sentences directly contain irrelevant content from the citing paper; mapping them back into the RP's own words keeps the summary faithful while reflecting community focus.
  • Concrete example: input set I = abstract ∪ cited text spans ≈ 40 sentences on average per RP.
  • How it is used in this paper: the input node set for the summarization graph.

Content-impact / hybrid summarization

  • What it computes: combines the RP abstract (authors' content view) with cited text spans (community's impact view). Hybrid 1 = extractive summary over abstract ∪ cited text spans; Hybrid 2 = start from full abstract, then augment with the most salient cited text spans.
  • Why it was created: pure citation-based summaries overlook authors' motivation; abstracts miss actual impact — integrate both.
  • Concrete example: Hybrid 2 added ~2 sentences on average to the abstract.
  • How it is used in this paper: the two proposed models, evaluated on CL-SciSumm 2016.

Authority feature

  • What it computes: per cited-text-span, sum of its citing papers' citation counts (abstract sentences get the RP's own citation count); appended as an extra feature dimension.
  • Why it was created: a citation from a highly-cited paper should weigh more than from an obscure one (Mei & Zhai 2008).
  • Concrete example: appended to the 200-dim LSTM embedding → D = 201.
  • How it is used in this paper: optional input feature; gives moderate ROUGE gains across all models.

GCN salience estimation — reuses Graph Convolutional Networks (Kipf & Welling 2017) and graph-based neural MDS (Yasunaga et al. 2017); not novel to this paper. LSTM encodes each sentence → node features; cosine-similarity sentence graph → GCN → softmax salience scores trained against ROUGE-derived target distribution.

5Cs Analysis5Cs 분석

Category: benchmark/dataset + new method (system) Context: extends CL-SciSumm (Jaidka et al. 2016) and AAN (Radev et al. 2013); model architecture from Yasunaga et al. 2017 neural MDS + GCN (Kipf & Welling 2017); builds on citation-context summarization lineage (Elkiss 2008, Qazvinian & Radev 2008, Mei & Zhai 2008). Correctness: annotation procedure validated (Kappa 0.75; human eval vs. CL-SciSumm gold on 15 overlapping papers, avg +0.02 → comparable). ROUGE results reported without CIs/significance tests. sample sizes for human eval small (5 students, 15 papers). Contributions: (1) first large (1,000-paper) manually-annotated citation-network summarization corpus; (2) hybrid content-impact summarization models; (3) demonstration that corpus size enables neural models to beat prior shared-task participants. Clarity: method reproducible — equations, hyperparameters (GloVe 100d, hidden 200/201, L=2, dropout 0.5, Adam lr 0.001), and greedy heuristics all given.

Contribution vs. Claim기여 대 주장

— abstract claims a "new framework" for scientific paper summarization and that hybrid summaries improve upon abstracts. Experiments (visible in full read) substantiate: Hybrid 2 does beat the abstract baseline on all ROUGE metrics on CL-SciSumm test, and the corpus enables neural models to surpass shared-task participants. Scope caveat: evaluated only on computational-linguistics (ACL/AAN) papers; generality to other scientific domains asserted but not shown.

Implementation Feasibility구현 실현가능성
  • Needs: AAN corpus with citation counts, GloVe embeddings, LSTM+GCN (Kipf-Welling), ROUGE toolkit; modest compute (batch 5, early stopping).
  • Code/data: dataset released at https://michiyasunaga.github.io/projects/scisumm_net/ (code availability ).
  • Modifications: for KB/dependency extraction, replace the salience-ranking + greedy-summary head with a relation-extraction/edge-typing head; keep the citance→cited-text-span localization and authority weighting.
  • Unknowns: missing link — how well tf-idf top-2 span mapping localizes fine-grained dependency claims (vs. coarse topical similarity); generalization beyond ACL/NLP domain.

Condition Delta — transfer to our setting
조건 차이 — 우리 환경으로의 전이

Paper's setting: inputs = reference paper abstract + incoming citation sentences (from AAN); outputs = 150-word extractive summary reflecting content + community impact; conditions = CL/NLP domain, 1,000 RPs, supervised via expert gold summaries, ROUGE evaluation. Our setting (SP5, dependency extraction / KB construction): inputs = papers + their citation graph; goal = extract inter-paper dependency/lineage relations to build a knowledge base, not produce readable summaries. Differences:

  • Output target: summary text vs. structured dependency edges — reference only (ScisummNet produces prose, not typed relations).
  • Cited-text-span extraction (tf-idf top-2 mapping of citance → RP sentence): resolved: reusable as a citation-context localization primitive for linking a citing statement to the specific claim it depends on.
  • Authority feature (citation-count weighting of edges): resolved: directly transferable as an edge-weighting signal for a citation KB.
  • Corpus/annotation method (abstract + citances as a cheap proxy for full-text reading): missing link — validated for summarization comprehensiveness, unverified for dependency-relation completeness.
  • Domain: CL/NLP only vs. our possibly broader corpus — reference only.
